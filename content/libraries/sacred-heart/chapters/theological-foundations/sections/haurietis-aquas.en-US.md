In 1956, Pope Pius XII published the encyclical *Haurietis Aquas* ("You Shall Draw Waters"), providing the definitive theological foundation for devotion to the Sacred Heart. Its title is drawn from Isaiah 12:3: *"You shall draw waters with joy out of the savior's fountains."*

The encyclical addressed those who considered the devotion merely sentimental or outdated. Pius XII demonstrated that it is rooted in Scripture, in the Fathers of the Church, and in the deepest truths of the Incarnation. The Sacred Heart, he taught, is not a private revelation added to the faith — it is a truth already contained in the deposit of faith, brought to fuller light through the devotion.

The Pope emphasized that the physical heart of Jesus is a natural symbol of His love, and that this symbolism is not arbitrary but willed by God. Because Jesus is true God and true man, His human heart is the heart of a divine Person. To worship the Sacred Heart is therefore to worship the Person of Christ Himself, considered under the aspect of His love.

Pius XII identified three distinct dimensions of the love symbolized by the Sacred Heart: the divine love Christ shares with the Father and the Holy Spirit, the human spiritual love of His created soul, and the sensitive love of His human heart. All three are real, all three are present in the one Heart.

The encyclical remains the most authoritative statement on the theology of the Sacred Heart and is essential reading for anyone who wishes to understand the devotion at its deepest level.
