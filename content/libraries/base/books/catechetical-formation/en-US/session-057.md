## Pius X asks

**250.** What are the evangelical counsels?

*The evangelical counsels are exhortations that Jesus Christ made in the Gospel to a more perfect life, by the practice of virtues that are not commanded.*

**251.** What are the principal evangelical counsels?

*The principal evangelical counsels are: voluntary poverty, perpetual chastity, and perfect obedience.*

## § 3. Moral Virtues and Vices — Evangelical Beatitudes

**252.** What is moral virtue?

*Moral virtue is the habit of doing good, acquired by repeating good acts.*

**253.** What are the principal moral virtues?

*The principal moral virtues are: religion, which makes us render to God the worship due to Him, and the four cardinal virtues — prudence, justice, fortitude, and temperance — which make us upright in our living.*

**254.** Why are the cardinal virtues so called?

*The cardinal virtues are so called because they are the hinge — that is, the support — of the other moral virtues.*

**255.** What is prudence?

*Prudence is the virtue that directs acts to their due end, and makes us discern and use good means.*

**256.** What is justice?

*Justice is the virtue that makes us give to each one what is due to him.*

**257.** What is fortitude?

*Fortitude is the virtue that makes us face, without rashness and without timidity, any difficulty or danger, and even death, for the service of God and for the good of our neighbor.*

**258.** What is temperance?

*Temperance is the virtue that bridles the passions and the desires, especially the sensual ones, and moderates the use of the goods perceived by the senses.*

> **Scripture.** *She teacheth temperance, and prudence, and justice, and fortitude, which are such things as men can have nothing more profitable in life.* — Wisdom 8:7

> *Lord, I am not yet brave, not yet just, not yet temperate, not yet wise. Today, let me act once like I am.*
