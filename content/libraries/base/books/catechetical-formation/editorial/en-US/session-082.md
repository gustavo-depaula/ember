Holman Hunt's *Light of the World* — Christ at the door overgrown with brambles, holding a lamp — has a famous detail: there is no handle on His side. The painting is the catechism of prayer in a single image. **God hears because He is faithful, not because we are persuasive**. The door has a handle on our side because His knocking is constant; the only question is whether we open.

Pius X gives the four reasons we sometimes feel unheard. Aquinas, in the chapters elsewhere in this book, gives the same diagnoses with characteristic precision:

  * **Confidence.** *Let him ask in faith, nothing wavering* (James 1:6). The prayer that begins by half-doubting whether prayer works is already half-not-prayer. Begin trusting.
  * **Perseverance.** Most prayers we describe as unanswered are prayers we abandoned before they had time to be answered. The Father is also forming us *through the asking*, not just answering at the end. Persist.
  * **In Christ's name.** Pius X is exact: only through Him do our prayers and good works have value. *Through your Son Jesus Christ our Lord* — the closing words of nearly every Catholic collect — is not a formality. It is the *channel* through which prayer ascends. Add the words, even silently. The clause is real.
  * **For what is good for us.** Aquinas: *many times our prayer is not heard because we seek that which is not good for us.* God is a Father, not a vending machine. He answers every prayer; sometimes the answer is *no*, *not yet*, or *I have something better in mind that you cannot yet imagine wanting*.

Two things follow.

The catechism then introduces the Lord's Prayer. Why is it *the most excellent of all prayers*? Aquinas's answer is plain: it is the only prayer Christ Himself dictated. Every petition in it is *guaranteed to be a suitable petition* because *He composed it.* When you do not know what to pray for, *He has handed you the words.* Use them. Slowly.

Today, identify one prayer you have stopped praying because you concluded God was not listening. Resume it — through Christ, with confidence, for what is good for you, with perseverance — and let Him answer in His way.
