No Catholic doctrine is more often misunderstood by Protestant Christians, and more often *unsupported* by Catholics under questioning, than this one. The catechism above has just said: **the First Commandment, which forbids worshipping any God but the One God, also *requires* the veneration of the saints and the Mother of God.** How can both be true?

The answer turns on a precise distinction the Church has held since the earliest centuries:

  * **Latria** — the worship owed to *God alone*. Adoration. The bow of the entire creature before the Creator. This is what we give to the Holy Trinity in the Mass, in the Eucharist, in private prayer.
  * **Dulia** — the *honor* given to the saints, friends of God, who reflect His holiness back into the world. We do not adore them. We honor them.
  * **Hyperdulia** — the highest form of *dulia*, given to *Mary alone*, because she alone is the Mother of God. Greater than the dulia of saints; *infinitely* less than the latria owed to her Son.

The First Commandment forbids only the first error: giving *latria* to anyone other than God. Honoring saints, asking their intercession, kissing relics, lighting candles before icons — these are not violations. They are **the natural movements of a soul that knows the saints are alive**. Pius X is exact: we honor them *as friends of God and powerful intercessors before His throne*.

The Protestant objection — *we go directly to Christ* — is theologically true *and* incomplete. Yes, Christ is the one Mediator (1 Timothy 2:5). But the saints' intercession does not bypass His mediation; it *participates* in it. Asking St. Joseph to pray for you is no different in kind from asking your sister to pray for you. The saints are *more* able to pray for you, not less, because they are now perfectly united to Christ.

A few practical Catholic devotions the Commandment authorizes and the Church recommends:

  * **Patron saints**. Find one whose life resonates with yours; befriend them in prayer. They are real persons, alive in glory, who *will* pray for you.
  * **The intercession of Mary**. Pius X singles her out: *we call upon Our Lady especially with the Hail Mary*. Liguori (Session 090) adds that her intercession reaches *every* living person, however accursed.
  * **The Litany of the Saints**. Prayed in every Easter Vigil, every priestly ordination, every monastic profession — the Church gathers the cloud of witnesses by name.

Today, name *one* saint and ask their prayers for *one* concrete intention. They will pray. Watch.
