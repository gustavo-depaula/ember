A sacrament is one of the words modern Catholics quote without quite knowing what it means. The catechism above gives the Tridentine definition: *a visible sign, instituted by Christ, to give grace*. Three pieces, all necessary.

  * **Visible.** A sacrament is a *physical* event. Water poured. Oil traced on the forehead. Bread held up. Hands laid on a head. Vows spoken aloud. **The Christian God works through matter, because in Christ He *became* matter**. Anyone who tells you sacraments are "merely external" has missed the Incarnation.
  * **Instituted by Christ.** Not invented by the Church. Not optional. The seven sacraments are *given* to the Church by Christ Himself, in identifiable scriptural moments — Baptism (Mt 28:19), Eucharist (Lk 22:19), Penance (Jn 20:23), Anointing (Mk 6:13, Jas 5:14), Confirmation (Acts 8:17), Holy Orders (Lk 22:19, Jn 21:17), Matrimony (Mt 19:6, Eph 5:32). The Church *administers* them; she did not *invent* them.
  * **To give grace.** The point of a sacrament is *not* primarily symbol or social ritual. The sacrament *causes* the grace it signifies. Aquinas, drawing on Augustine: *they accomplish what they signify*. Baptism *actually* washes original sin. The Eucharist *actually* delivers the Body and Blood of Christ. Confession *actually* forgives sins. The sacraments are not pictures of grace; they are grace *in action*.

Van der Weyden's altarpiece (today's image) shows the seven gathered under one roof — birth, growth, sin, healing, vocation, marriage, dying. **The arc of human life mapped to the seven channels by which grace meets it.** Two are *of the dead*: Baptism and Penance, which restore grace where it was missing or lost. Five are *of the living*: Confirmation, Eucharist, Anointing, Holy Orders, Matrimony, which strengthen grace where it already lives.

This is why the Catholic life is sacramental from beginning to end. There is no place a Catholic stands alone. The Church meets us at every threshold of being human, with grace that is not a mood or a memory but **God Himself reaching into time**.

For the next twenty-one sessions, you will walk the seven sacraments one by one. Read them not as *information* about the Church's rituals but as *the map of how God touches you*. By the end, you should be able to point to where grace has met you, by name.
