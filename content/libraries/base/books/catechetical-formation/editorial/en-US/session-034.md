The Apostles' Creed ends where the Christian life ends — and begins. *Life everlasting. Amen.* The catechism above unfolds what those four words mean and what they ask.

The doctrine, as Aquinas walks it:

  * **Life everlasting is *life*** — not merely existence prolonged. The soul in heaven is not bored; it is *fully alive* in every faculty at once. Sight that *sees* (the beatific vision); will that *loves* without obstruction; intellect that *knows* without limits; memory in which everything good is preserved; body (after the resurrection) glorified in the four dotes (Session 033). Heaven is the **maximum of being**, not its dimming.

  * **Life everlasting is *of God*** — its content is God Himself. *This is eternal life: that they may know thee, the only true God, and Jesus Christ, whom thou hast sent* (John 17:3). The blessed do not enjoy heaven by *getting* something other than God; they enjoy heaven by *seeing* and *loving* God face to face. The Trinity who has always been three-in-love opens its inner conversation to the saints. We become, in Peter's startling phrase, *partakers of the divine nature* (2 Peter 1:4).

  * **Life everlasting is *for everyone made for it*** — and we are all made for it. *God created us to know Him, love Him, and serve Him in this life, and afterward to enjoy Him in the next, in heaven* (Pius X Q13, the very first answer of this whole formation). The catechism that began with this answer ends with it. **You were made for heaven.** The whole of the Catholic life — Creed, Decalogue, Precepts, Virtues, Sacraments, Prayer — is the road home.

*Amen* is not a verbal full-stop. It is the *seal* of the entire Creed — the assent of the whole soul to everything that has been said. *Truly. Faithfully. So be it.* Aquinas: it is the strongest word a Christian has, the same word the assembly says at the doxology of the Mass. **You have just confessed twelve articles of doctrinal faith. The *Amen* is your signature.**

Van Eyck's *Adoration of the Lamb* (today's image) is the last word of the visible cosmos in pigment. *The saved at last gathered, the Lamb at the center, the New Jerusalem rising behind.* Every panel of the painting is a prophecy you can join. The angels, the patriarchs, the apostles, the martyrs, the virgins, the confessors, the just of every nation — *they have a place open*. So do you.

If you have walked the thirty-two-session arc on the Creed, you have professed the entire Christian faith. Not as a quiz answered correctly; as a life confessed. Tomorrow the catechism turns to *how you live what you have professed* — the Decalogue. The doctrine has feet. They walk into days and decisions.

Today, end the Creed by saying it once aloud. *I believe in God, the Father almighty, Creator of heaven and earth...* All the way to *Amen*. Thirty-two days have prepared you to mean it. **Mean it.**
