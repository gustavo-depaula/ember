Today's article of the Creed is brief and stunning: *I believe in the forgiveness of sins*. Five words holding the entire economy of mercy.

The doctrine has three layers, each grounded in scripture and unfolded by Aquinas:

  * **God *can* forgive sins.** This is not a small claim. Some philosophical schools held that even God could not undo the moral disorder of a wrong act — *what is done is done*. The Christian claim is that **God is not a cosmic accountant**; He is a Father, with the power to *blot out* what was done (Isaiah 43:25), to *cast it into the depth of the sea* (Micah 7:19), to *remember it no more* (Hebrews 8:12). The forgiveness is *real* — not bookkeeping forgiveness, but *destruction* of the guilt itself.

  * **Christ *won* the forgiveness of sins by His Cross.** Forgiveness was not free in the sense of being cheap. It was paid for in the Body of the Son. *He bore our sins in His own body upon the tree* (1 Peter 2:24). What we call mercy is *bought mercy* — and the price was infinite, so the supply is inexhaustible. There is no sin too large for the forgiveness Christ won, because the price He paid was infinite.

  * **The Church *administers* the forgiveness Christ won.** Through Baptism (which forgives original sin and all personal sin committed before it). Through Penance (which forgives sins committed after Baptism). Through Anointing of the Sick (which forgives, when contrition is present, even when the dying person can no longer confess). Through indirect application: the Eucharist itself is given for *the forgiveness of sins*; almsgiving covers a multitude of sins (1 Peter 4:8); the Mass offered for the dead reaches purgatory.

This article of the Creed is therefore the **doctrinal foundation of every Catholic confessional booth**, every priestly absolution, every Easter renewal of baptismal promises, every Sign of the Cross with holy water at the font.

What this asks of you:

  * **If you cannot believe God will forgive a particular sin, you have not yet understood Christianity.** *I believe in the forgiveness of sins* is the Creed's correction of every despair. Whatever you have done, the price has been paid. Whatever you cannot forgive yourself for, He has forgiven, if only you will receive it.

  * **Live as one forgiven.** Rembrandt's *Prodigal* — today's image — is the icon. The father's hands rest on the gaunt back of the son. *The mercy is already there.* The Christian who is loaded down with the memory of past sins is, in a real sense, refusing the gift of the forgiveness already won. **Lay it down.**

  * **Forgive others as you have been forgiven.** This is not optional (Mt 6:14-15). The conditional is exact: *if you forgive men their offences, your heavenly Father will also forgive you*. Forgive someone you have not forgiven. The mercy you give *flows from* the mercy you have received, and *opens you* to receive more.

*Stand inside the painting today.* The Father is running. The hands are open.
