**The Church is not the mood of a generation.** Today's ekphrasis is theologically exact, and the catechism above is asking the modern Catholic reader to recover the doctrine that scandal, division, and cultural pressure have eroded.

The Catholic doctrine of the Church holds the following four claims together — *they cannot be separated without dissolving the doctrine*:

  * **Christ founded the Church.** Not the Apostles after His death; not Constantine in the fourth century; not the Reformers in the sixteenth. *Thou art Peter, and upon this rock I will build My Church* (Matthew 16:18). The Church *as a visible society with structure, sacraments, and authority* was founded by Christ Himself. Aquinas insists: *the keys, the priesthood, the Eucharist, the bishopric* — all are given by Christ during His earthly life.

  * **The Church is *visible*.** Not invisible. Not the sum of all believers everywhere with no organizational expression. The Church is a *body* — *one body* (1 Corinthians 12:12) — and bodies are visible. You can find her in the Yellow Pages. She has a hierarchy, sacraments, doctrine, canon law. The Protestant move to make the Church *invisible* (an inward fellowship of the truly saved) is the move the Church has rejected since Augustine fought the Donatists.

  * **The Church is *one*.** Not many denominations united by a vague Christian sentiment. *One Lord, one faith, one baptism* (Ephesians 4:5). The fragmentation of Christianity into thousands of denominations is a tragedy the Church mourns and works to heal — through ecumenism, through prayer, through patient witness — but the Catholic Church does not *agree* that the fragmentation is the divine plan. **There is one Church, and the Church Christ founded *subsists* in the Catholic Church** (the formula of *Lumen Gentium* 8).

  * **The Church is *for everyone*.** *Catholic* means *universal*. The Church has no national identity, no ethnic boundary, no temporal expiration. *Go therefore and teach all nations* (Matthew 28:19). Every culture has a place in her. *You belong to a body older than empires* — and one that will outlast every empire still standing.

What this asks of you:

  * **Stay Catholic when scandals come.** The Church holds saints and sinners in the same fold. Christ said it Himself in the parable of the wheat and the tares (Mt 13). The presence of corrupt clerics, mediocre catechesis, dispiriting parishes — none of these dissolve the Church. *The gates of hell shall not prevail* (Mt 16:18) means *they have not, do not, will not*. **A bad bishop is still a bishop, in a Church Christ promised would last.**

  * **Belong, do not just believe.** A Catholic is *a member of a body*, not just an opinion-holder of certain doctrines. Showing up at Mass, joining a parish, knowing the priest by name, supporting the parish financially, serving in some way — these are not optional decorations on the spiritual life. They are how *the body* takes you in.

Today, name the parish you belong to. If you don't have one, *find one*. Walk in. Say hello. Begin.
