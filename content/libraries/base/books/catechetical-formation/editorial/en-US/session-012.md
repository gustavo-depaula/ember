**Sin is not abstract.** The catechism above and Masaccio's *Expulsion* (today's image) put the doctrine in shocking concreteness: *Eve cries out, mouth open, real*. The Fall is not a myth about the human condition; it is *what actually happened*, in time, to two real people, with consequences we live in.

What did the first sin actually consist of? Aquinas, following Augustine, identifies the deepest movement: **pride — the will's preference of self over God**. *You shall be as gods, knowing good and evil* (Genesis 3:5) was the serpent's promise. Eve and Adam reached not primarily for the fruit but for the **autonomy** the fruit promised — to *determine* good and evil for themselves rather than to *receive* them from God. The fruit was the occasion; pride was the act.

What we inherit from that act is called **original sin**, and the doctrine has been refined carefully over the centuries:

  * **Original sin is *not* personal sin.** A newborn has not committed any actual sin. *Original sin* is the *deprivation* of original justice into which every human being is born — the lack of the grace that ought to be there. We did not vote against God, but we are born outside the friendship Adam and Eve squandered.

  * **Original sin is *real*.** The Council of Trent against the Protestant Reformers and the Pelagians: *if anyone says that the sin of Adam injured only Adam himself and not his progeny... let him be anathema*. We *inherit* the consequences. Every page of human history confirms it.

  * **Original sin *can be remedied*** — by Baptism, which infuses sanctifying grace and restores the soul to friendship with God. Concupiscence, the disordered tug of desire, *remains* even after Baptism (it is the territory of struggle, not of sin), but the *guilt* of original sin is gone. *Where sin abounded, grace did more abound* (Romans 5:20).

The catechism above introduces a doctrine the Church holds with particular tenderness: **the Immaculate Conception of Mary**. By a singular grace and privilege, Mary alone among the descendants of Adam was preserved from original sin *from the first instant of her conception*. This was not because she did not need redemption — she did, more than anyone — but because Christ's redemption was applied to her *in advance*, in view of her divine motherhood. Mary is the new Eve: *the daughter of Adam who said yes where Eve said no*.

The angel's sword over Eden's gate, in Masaccio's painting, is not the last word. **The Cross is.** What was lost in Eden was won back, not in a paradise restored, but in a tree planted on Calvary. We do not return to the Garden; we are admitted to a *better one*. *The gate is not the last word.*

Today, take five minutes to admit one place where pride is the operating principle in your life — *I will determine what is good for me*. Hand that province over. The undoing of Eden begins, in your soul, with the *opposite* of Adam's choice: *Lord, You decide.*
