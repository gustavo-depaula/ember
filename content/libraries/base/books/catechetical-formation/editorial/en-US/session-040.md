The Second Commandment is the **most-broken commandment in modern Catholic life** — quietly and without anyone counting. *Oh my God*, *Jesus Christ*, *Christ Almighty* — used as exclamations of frustration, surprise, or comic effect, dozens of times a day, by people who would be horrified to think of themselves as blasphemers. The catechism above is asking us to pay attention.

The Commandment forbids three things, in increasing gravity:

  * **Disrespectful use** of the divine names. Casual exclamation, mocking imitation, the *OMG* texted in sarcasm. Not damnation, but a real venial sin we accumulate by the dozen.

  * **False or unnecessary oaths**. An oath, Pius X teaches, is *to call God to witness*. To swear *I swear to God I'll do this* about a triviality, or worse to swear falsely, is a misuse of the divine name as decorative emphasis. *Let your speech be Yes, yes; No, no* (Matthew 5:37) — Christ's own teaching.

  * **Blasphemy**. The grave use: a deliberate insult against God, the Mother of God, the saints, or holy things. Aquinas treats this with severity. Spoken blasphemy is mortal matter; blasphemy committed publicly, in mockery or as performance, is even graver.

But the Commandment is not only a list of negatives. The flip side is **positive reverence for the Holy Name**. The Church, especially after St. Bernardine of Siena's fifteenth-century preaching of the Holy Name and the rise of the Society of Jesus, made the *Holy Name of Jesus* a particular devotion — bowing the head at His name in the Creed, ending letters with *J.M.J.* (Jesus, Mary, Joseph), pausing at the *Adveniat regnum tuum* of the Pater Noster. The Bernini IHS in today's image is the visual culmination: the Name *blazes* because the Name **is light**.

What does this mean for daily life?

  1. **Catch one casual use today**. Don't beat yourself up; just notice. Repent silently. Begin again.
  2. **Replace, don't suppress**. When you would have said *oh God*, say *Lord have mercy* instead. The mind has a habit; redirect it, don't try to amputate it.
  3. **Honor the Name with deliberate use**. *Jesus*, said as a real prayer in a moment of fear, panic, gratitude, or longing — that *is* the Holy Name used rightly. The medieval hymn *Jesu Dulcis Memoria*, traditionally attributed to St. Bernard of Clairvaux: *Jesus is honey in the mouth, music in the ear, joy in the heart*. The Name becomes the air the soul breathes.

*Most of the cursing of the world is also unconscious prayer.* The ekphrasis is exactly right. The next time you hear someone say *Jesus Christ* in shock or anger, pray *Jesus Christ, have mercy on him* in your heart. Quiet repair. The Name will not be unmade by misuse, but it will be honored, by you, in advance.
