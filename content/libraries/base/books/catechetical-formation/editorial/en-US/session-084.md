The first petition is *not* about teaching God to value His own name. *Hallowed be Thy name* is something we ask **for our sake, not His**. God's name is already holy; it cannot become more so. What we are asking is that the holy name be **hallowed in us** — recognized, reverenced, and used rightly in our own speech and thought.

Reading Aquinas alongside the Roman Catechism, we can group the petition under three concrete asks:

  * **That God's name be known.** A holy name no one knows is a fire hidden under a basket. The first work of the Church — *every Christian*, not only missionaries — is that God's name reach those who do not yet say it. Your friend who calls themselves *spiritual but not religious* is, beneath the language, asking for a Name they have not been given. You may be the one who gives it.

  * **That God's name be loved and confessed.** That God be glorified by us, and we in turn glorified by Him. Confession is public. The Christian who hides their faith for the sake of social peace is *not yet* praying this petition seriously. There is a way to confess that is gentle, winsome, and never arrogant — but there is no way to confess silently.

  * **That we be holy ourselves.** The deepest sense of *hallowed* is *made holy*. We pray that *our names* become holy because *His* dwells in us. *You shall be holy, because I am holy* (Leviticus 19:2). The petition is a request for sanctification — your sanctification, today, in the quiet ordinary places where almost no one is watching.

The most subtle violation of this petition is the casual use of God's name when angry, surprised, in disappointment, in jest. *Oh my God* and its variants. *Jesus Christ* as an exclamation. The Second Commandment is the negative form of this petition; *hallowed be Thy name* is the positive. Today, **catch yourself once**. Not to be scrupulous. Just to notice: that name belongs to a Person, the Person who is holding you in being right now.

The Burning Bush is the true image: the One who reveals Himself first by saying *I AM* — the Name behind every name — orders Moses to **take off his shoes**. The minimum reverence due. Today, take off your shoes, even metaphorically, somewhere.
