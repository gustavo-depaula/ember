Aquinas's chapter on the Seventh Commandment is sneakily radical. He categorizes theft into five kinds — stealthy, violent, wage-withholding, fraud, and simony — and along the way casually quotes St. Augustine: *what are thrones but forms of thievery?* That is the saint canonized as *Doctor of the Church* writing in 1273 about *kings*. Aquinas takes the Commandment seriously enough to indict structural injustice with the same word he uses for a pickpocket.

Pius X above adds two precisions for modern life: the Commandment *orders* us to restore stolen goods, repair damages, pay our debts, and **pay workers a just wage**. The verb is *order*, not suggest. And he is unflinching about the consequence: *one who, being able, does not restore or repair will not obtain forgiveness, even if in words he declares himself repentant.* Restitution is part of repentance. Without it, the confession is incomplete.

Apply the Commandment to your life as it actually is:

  * **Wages.** If you employ anyone — a housekeeper, a contractor, a tutor, a driver — pay them on time, the agreed amount, with whatever benefits law or fairness require. Withholding wages is one of the four sins Scripture says *cries out to heaven* (cf. James 5:4).
  * **Debts.** A loan undertaken in good faith and not repaid is a slow theft. *Owe no man anything*, the Apostle teaches, *but to love one another* (Romans 13:8).
  * **Taxes.** Aquinas says explicitly that we owe rulers their due *for our safety*. Tax evasion beyond legitimate avoidance is a sin against the common good.
  * **Time and attention.** A modern theft Aquinas could not name: the deliberate consumption of another person's hours through bait, manipulation, or addictive design. To return repeatedly to a screen *engineered to steal hours from your life* is also to consent to a theft against yourself.
  * **Pirated work.** A book, a film, a course, a song made by someone who needs to be paid — to consume it without paying, when paying is possible, is theft. Aquinas's *treasures of wickedness shall profit nothing* applies.

If you owe restitution, today is the day. Even partial restitution is better than none. *Zacchaeus did not climb the tree to be cleared; he climbed it to begin restoring fourfold.* Begin yours.
