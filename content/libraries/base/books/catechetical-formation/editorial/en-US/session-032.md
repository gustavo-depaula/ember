**The little sins matter.** The catechism above and today's ekphrasis (the *Four Last Things* — death, judgment, hell, heaven — set in the corners of a medieval panel) are insisting on something modern Catholic life almost everywhere has lost: that **venial sins, *not* repented and *not* purified, are the path that ends at one of those corners**.

Aquinas distinguishes carefully:

  * **Venial sin** does not kill grace, but it *weakens* it. It is a *real wound* — small, perhaps, but cumulative. *Drop by drop, the stone is hollowed out.* The soul that habitually permits venial sin without contrition becomes *less responsive to grace*, and the path to mortal sin shortens. Aquinas: *no one becomes a great sinner all at once*.

  * **Sins against the Holy Spirit** are the most dangerous of all. The catechism above lists six classical kinds:
      1. *Despair of salvation* — refusing to hope in God's mercy.
      2. *Presumption of God's mercy without repentance* — taking forgiveness as license.
      3. *Resisting known truth* — refusing what the conscience has clearly seen.
      4. *Envy of another's spiritual good* — being grieved at someone else's holiness or graces.
      5. *Obstinacy in sin* — willful persistence after warning.
      6. *Final impenitence* — dying without repenting.

  Why are these called *sins against the Holy Spirit*? Because the Spirit is the One who gives the grace of repentance, of hope, of openness to truth. To set oneself against *Him* is to refuse the very means by which God's mercy reaches the soul. Christ's stark warning: *every sin and blasphemy shall be forgiven men, but the blasphemy of the Spirit shall not be forgiven* (Mt 12:31). Aquinas softens the harshness: it is not that God *cannot* forgive these sins, but that the sinner who clings to them refuses *the means of forgiveness*. The door is open; the sinner walks away from it.

What this asks of you:

  * **Do not despise the small sins.** The morning impatience, the third drink, the lazy half-prayer, the resentment held a week beyond its time — these are the gravel on which the path to bigger collapse is paved. Bring them to Confession. Bring them to the daily examen.

  * **Watch especially for the sins against the Spirit.** They are the slow corrosion of the soul's *capacity* to receive mercy. The despair that says *I am too far gone* — fight it. The presumption that says *I'll repent later* — refuse it. The resistance to truth — *bring me into it, even painfully*.

  * **Pray for the grace of *final perseverance*.** It is the most important grace any Christian asks for: that we be in the state of grace at the moment of death. Hail Mary's second half — *pray for us sinners now and at the hour of our death* — is exactly this petition.

*Today, the small choice. Tomorrow, the corner.* The medieval panel painters were not exaggerating; they were doing pastoral theology. **Choose the small thing today as if it determined which corner of the panel you stand in.** Because, slowly, it does.
