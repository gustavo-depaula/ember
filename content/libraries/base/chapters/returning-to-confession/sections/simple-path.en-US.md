Here is the whole shape of a confession, step by step. If it helps, practice the opening out loud on the way there.

**1. Before you go.** Take twenty quiet minutes and examine your conscience. The *Ten Commandments* or the *Examination by Commandments* in the app will walk you through it. Write down, if it helps, the things you want to confess — you can bring the list into the confessional and throw it away after.

**2. When you enter.** Most parishes offer both face-to-face and screen options. Choose what makes the confession honest for *you* — not what looks more reverent. A good confession is better than a performed one.

**3. The opening.** Make the sign of the cross and say:

> *"Bless me, Father, for I have sinned. It has been [X years / I do not remember exactly how long] since my last confession. These are my sins."*

**4. The confession itself.** Say the sins plainly, briefly, without commentary. *"I have…"* rather than *"I'm the kind of person who…"* Keep it concrete — *what* happened, *how many times* roughly (once / a few / many / daily). Don't explain. Don't justify. Don't accuse anyone else. Say the sin, then move on.

**5. If you get stuck.** Pause and say *"Father, help me."* He will.

**6. When you finish.** End with: *"For these and all the sins I cannot remember, I am truly sorry and ask for penance and absolution."*

**7. Listen.** The priest may offer a sentence or two of counsel. He will give you a penance — a short prayer, a small act of charity — and ask you to make an Act of Contrition. The app's `act-of-contrition` prayer will do; the short version is enough.

**8. The absolution.** The priest extends his hand and says the words of absolution. At the end, he will say *"Go in peace."* Say *"Thanks be to God"* — or simply *"Thank you, Father."*

**9. Your penance.** Do it before you leave the church if you can. If not, do it as soon as possible — that same day.

That is all. It is shorter than you think.
