If you have not been to confession in a long time — five years, fifteen, thirty — the one thing you need to hear first is this: **the Father is not waiting for you at the end of a long interrogation. He is running down the road to meet you.**

*"But when he was yet a great way off, his father saw him, and was moved with compassion; and running to him, fell upon his neck, and kissed him."* (*Luke 15:20*)

The son in the parable had rehearsed a careful speech. He got out only the first half before the father interrupted him with an embrace. That is how confession works. You do not have to get the speech right. You have to walk down the road.

The weight you are carrying — of things done, of years gone, of a relationship with God that feels strange because you have been away from it — is exactly what the sacrament is *for*. Christ did not institute confession for people with nothing to confess. He instituted it for the Prodigal: you, returning.
