Most returning Catholics are kept away from confession not by unbelief but by three specific fears. Name them, and they shrink.

**"I don't remember all my sins."** You are not asked to. You are asked to confess every mortal sin you can recall, in number and kind, to the best of your memory after an honest examination. Sins you genuinely forget — not the ones you are hoping to hide, but the ones truly gone from memory — are covered by the general absolution at the end. St. Francis de Sales puts it plainly: God asks for your sincerity, not a transcript.

**"The priest will be shocked."** He will not. A parish priest who has been hearing confessions for five years has heard everything — and the harder the confession, the more glad he is that you came. The sacrament is a place of relief for the priest as well as for you. You are not burdening him. You are giving him the reason he became a priest.

**"I don't remember how to do it."** The form is simple. It fits on a postcard. The next section walks through it line by line, and you may bring this chapter — or a printed note — with you into the confessional if you wish. Many parishes also have a small card taped inside the confessional for exactly this reason.
