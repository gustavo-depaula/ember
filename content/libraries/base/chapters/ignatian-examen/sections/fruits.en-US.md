## The Fruits

After a month of daily examen, you notice:

- **Gratitude becomes habitual.** You begin to see gifts before you see problems. This changes everything downstream.
- **You recognize your desolations earlier.** The mood that used to own you all afternoon is named by noon.
- **You recognize God's action.** The coincidences, the unasked graces, the quiet promptings — you begin to see the hand that was always there.
- **Resolutions accumulate, slowly, into change.** Not by force — by noticing, and by small concrete next steps.
- **Confession deepens.** You bring patterns, not lists.
- **You pray without ceasing, because prayer stops being an event and becomes an awareness.**

St. Francis de Sales — not a Jesuit, but a friend of the Society — wrote: *"It is at the end of the day that the soul sees what the day was."* The examen is the place where seeing becomes love, and love becomes change.
