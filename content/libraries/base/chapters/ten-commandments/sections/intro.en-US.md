God gave the Ten Commandments to Moses on Mount Sinai as the heart of his covenant with Israel. They are not arbitrary rules but the shape of the love that frees us: the first three direct us toward God, and the remaining seven toward our neighbor. Christ did not abolish them but fulfilled them in the law of love (Matt 22:37–40).

The Church teaches that the Decalogue, read in the light of the Gospel, binds every person of every time and place. It is the minimum of justice, not the maximum of sanctity — but without it, sanctity is a pretense.
