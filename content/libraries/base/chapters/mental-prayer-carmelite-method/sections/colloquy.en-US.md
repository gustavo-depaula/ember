## The Colloquy — The Point of It All

Everything else is preparation for this. *Colloquy* means conversation: you speak to the Lord, simply, as to a friend. Teresa insisted that mental prayer is not a monologue performed before God but a dialogue with Him.

**Speak to whichever Person your heart turns toward.** The Father, the Son, the Spirit. Or Our Lady, asking her to intercede. Let it be natural. Do not force a structured address.

**Say what is actually in your heart.** Not what you think you *should* say. The colloquy fails the moment it becomes pious performance. Teresa was blunt: *"If you do not know what to say, do not seek high-flown words. Say instead: 'Here I am, what do you want of me?' or 'Lord, I have failed again today, have mercy.'"*

**Listen.** After speaking, be silent. Let Him answer, if He wishes, in the depth of the heart — not in voices, but in a quiet settling, a conviction, a drawing. Often there is nothing you can name, and that is fine. You have spoken and been heard; the rest belongs to Him.

**Brother Lawrence** — the Carmelite lay brother of *The Practice of the Presence of God* — reduced the whole method to this: *"I keep myself in His presence by simple attentiveness and a loving gaze on God, which I may call the actual presence of God."* The colloquy is the training ground for this continuous gaze.

A half-hour of colloquy is not a half-hour of talking. It is a half-hour of *being with* — of attention, simple words, silence, attention again. What you speak can fit on one side of a bus ticket. What happens is beyond measuring.
