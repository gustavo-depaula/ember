St. Teresa of Ávila said it plainly: prayer *"is nothing else than an intimate sharing between friends; it means taking time frequently to be alone with him who we know loves us"* (CCC 2709). For the Carmelite tradition, mental prayer is not optional equipment for the advanced — it is the daily oxygen of anyone serious about God.

The Carmelite school — Teresa, John of the Cross, Thérèse of Lisieux, Elizabeth of the Trinity, Brother Lawrence — has shaped the Church's teaching on prayer more than any other. Its method is not a technique but a *way of being*: the soul learns to stand in God's presence, to speak simply, to listen, and above all to endure when consolation is withdrawn.

What follows is the classical Carmelite structure as distilled by Fr. Gabriel of St. Mary Magdalen, OCD, in *Divine Intimacy* — still the premier meditation book of the Carmelite tradition and a companion for countless souls who keep a daily half-hour of mental prayer.
