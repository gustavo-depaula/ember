## In Practice

**Choose a short passage.** Twenty minutes. Twenty verses is too many. *"Better a little well-digested than a lot swallowed whole."* The Gospel of the day is a classic place to start — it keeps you in rhythm with the Church. The Psalms, Pauline epistles, and the Prophets also lend themselves well.

**Prepare.** Two minutes of silence before opening the Bible. Place yourself in the presence of God. Ask the Holy Spirit to open your ears: *"Speak, Lord, your servant is listening"* (1 Sam 3:9).

**Read aloud, slowly.** Reading aloud slows the mind and involves the body. If you are in a place where you cannot, read with your lips moving silently.

**Expect distraction; return gently.** A wandering mind is not a failure — it is the weather. Bring the attention back to the word without scolding yourself.

**End with a resolution.** Before closing the Bible, name one concrete thing the passage asks of you today. One action. One attitude. A lectio that does not touch the day is half done.

**A simple rhythm:** 20 minutes, three or four times a week. *Ecclesia Dei* praised this cadence. Daily is better if you can — but a real, unrushed 20 minutes beats a guilty 5 minutes.
