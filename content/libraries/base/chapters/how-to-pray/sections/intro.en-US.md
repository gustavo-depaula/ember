St. John Damascene gave prayer its classical definition: *"the raising of one's mind and heart to God or the requesting of good things from God"* (quoted in CCC 2559). Not technique, not posture, not feeling — a raising. You lift what you are toward Him who made you.

Prayer is first God's work. *"We do not know how to pray as we ought, but the Spirit himself intercedes for us with sighs too deep for words"* (Rom 8:26). You do not begin prayer; you respond to a call already sounding in you. This is why the poorest beginner is not behind — he has already been called, and his halting words are already carried by the Spirit.

Learn to pray by praying. Reading about prayer, like reading about swimming, only goes so far. What follows is a map, not a substitute.
