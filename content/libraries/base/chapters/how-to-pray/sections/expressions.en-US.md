## Five Expressions

Within any prayer — vocal or mental — five movements of the heart keep returning. The *Catechism* names them (CCC 2626–2643). A balanced prayer life touches each.

**Blessing and adoration.** You kneel. God is God; you are creature. *"O come, let us adore him."* Without this first movement, prayer curdles into a transaction. Start with it.

**Petition.** Asking God for what you need — forgiveness first, then everything else. *"Ask, and it shall be given you"* (Mt 7:7). Concrete petitions please God; vague ones drift.

**Intercession.** Praying for others, as Christ intercedes for us. List them by name. Pray for those you love, those you struggle with, the dying, the Church, the world. An intercessor stands in the gap.

**Thanksgiving.** The Eucharist itself — *eucharistia* — means thanksgiving. Gratitude dissolves self-pity; it is the oxygen of joy. Count specific graces, not abstractions.

**Praise.** *"To sing is to pray twice,"* St. Augustine said. Praise asks nothing and needs nothing. It is the purest form — the prayer of heaven.

Weave these together. A short Our Father already contains all five.
