Finding your predominant fault takes a few weeks of attention, not a quiz. Sit with these questions across ten or fifteen nights of examen:

1. **What sin do I confess most often?** If the same matter has come up in three consecutive confessions, the root is almost certainly there.
2. **When I am wronged or frustrated, where does my reaction come from?** A surge of anger reveals pride. A collapse into self-pity reveals vanity. A retreat into fantasy reveals sloth or lust.
3. **What am I most defensive about?** The thing you *can't laugh at about yourself* is often exactly it.
4. **Where do I secretly feel superior — or secretly feel small?** Both are often branches of the same pride, in different postures.
5. **What does the spiritual counsel I resist most sound like?** "Just apologize." "Just give it away." "Just rest." The rule you will not accept is pointing at the fault you have not yet surrendered.

The classical catalog is the **seven capital sins**: pride, envy, anger, sloth, avarice, gluttony, lust. Every predominant fault fits somewhere in that list, though the particular shape differs. Vanity is pride wearing a mirror; people-pleasing is pride afraid of the mirror. Acedia is sloth's spiritual form. Over-scheduling can be avarice in disguise.

**Write it down.** Once a candidate fault surfaces, name it in one concrete sentence — *"I need to be seen as competent, and I do harmful things to sustain that need"* — rather than a label. Labels deflect. Sentences convict.

Then take it to confession, to a spiritual director if you have one, or to a trusted priest. Having another person confirm or refine the diagnosis is nearly always useful.
