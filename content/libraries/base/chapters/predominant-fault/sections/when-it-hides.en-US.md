The predominant fault is rarely gross. The obvious sins — the ones you confess in your first year of seriousness — are usually its **children**, not itself. The fault itself is subtler, older, and quieter. Three warnings:

**It hides under virtue.** Pride hides under humility the moment you start to notice your own humility. Sloth hides under moderation ("I shouldn't overdo it"). Greed hides under prudence. Anger hides under honesty ("I'm just being truthful"). If one of your virtues is suspiciously easy, that is the one to examine — because it may be the fault in reverse clothing.

**It shifts when surrendered.** Win against anger, and vanity may rise to take its place. This is not defeat; it is the pattern. Traditional writers called it *the secondary fault* waking up. When the bouncer is knocked out, the next one steps up. Each is a deeper uncovering, and each is real progress — even if the subjective experience is "new problems keep appearing."

**It returns in a new form in every season of life.** The fault of your twenties (perhaps impurity) may re-appear in your fifties as *nostalgia for your twenties*. The same root, the same surrender needed. Do not be surprised. Saints at the end of long lives still named their defect dominante. The difference is that they knew it intimately and feared it less.

The aim of this whole labor is not perfection. It is **the Beatitudes**. *"Blessed are the poor in spirit"* — blessed, that is, the one who has come to know how empty-handed he is before God. The predominant fault, named and fought across decades, is the doorway to that poverty. And that poverty is the doorway to the Kingdom.

*"Unless the Lord build the house, they labour in vain that build it."* (*Psalm 127:1*)
