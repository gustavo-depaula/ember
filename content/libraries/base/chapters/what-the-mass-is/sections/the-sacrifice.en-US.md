The Mass is first a **sacrifice** — and that word, which embarrasses modern ears, is essential.

In the Old Covenant, the priests offered the blood of bulls and goats, again and again, because no merely animal offering could take away sin (*Hebrews 10:4*). What Christ did on the Cross was offer **himself** — Priest and Victim at once — and that single offering was sufficient for all sin, for all time. *"By one offering he hath perfected for ever them that are sanctified."* (*Hebrews 10:14*)

How then does the Mass "re-offer" a sacrifice that was already perfect? It does not. The same Christ, who is eternally a Priest after the order of Melchizedek (*Hebrews 7:17*), is eternally offering. The Mass does not repeat Calvary; it **makes Calvary present**. There is one Sacrifice — Christ's. The altar in your parish is not a second Calvary, it is *that* Calvary, uncovered for you under the veil of bread and wine.

The Council of Trent spoke plainly: the Mass is a *"true and proper sacrifice"* — *"one and the same is the victim, the same now offering by the ministry of the priests, who then offered himself on the cross, only the manner of offering being different."* (*Council of Trent*, Session 22, ch. 2)

This is why the Mass cannot be optional entertainment. It is not a concert, not a fellowship hour, not a weekly lecture. It is the Cross, here, for you — and the greatest prayer the Church has to offer, because it is not finally *our* prayer at all. It is Christ's.
