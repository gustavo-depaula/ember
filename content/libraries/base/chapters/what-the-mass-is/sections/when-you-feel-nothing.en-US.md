Some Sundays you will go to Mass and feel absolutely nothing. The readings will glance off. The music will irritate you. The homily will be forgettable. You will come home flat.

**Go anyway.** What just happened at that Mass happened whether you felt it or not. Christ was made present on the altar. The one Sacrifice of Calvary was offered — for you, for the world, for the dead. Graces you will not know about for decades were poured out on your soul. The Mass does not require your feelings to work. It works because Christ works.

St. Thérèse of Lisieux, in one of her last letters, described years of dryness in prayer and at Mass. *"I accept not feeling,"* she wrote, *"for Jesus."* This is not spiritual failure. It is the long obedience of the saints.

If the Mass feels dry, do these things:

- **Offer the dryness itself** as your gift. Jesus was abandoned on the Cross; your tedium is a small participation in His. *"My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?"* was also prayed from a dry place.
- **Don't parish-shop looking for feeling.** The Mass is valid at the boring parish, the bad-homily parish, and the out-of-tune parish. Feeling is not the measure. Presence is.
- **Make one concrete act of faith** at the consecration and one concrete thanksgiving after Communion. Even one sentence. *"Thank you, Lord, for the gift of yourself."*
- **Keep going.** Perseverance across dry seasons is itself a form of love. *"Whoever perseveres to the end will be saved."* (*Matt 24:13*)

The feelings will return. Or they won't — and that will be its own mercy. What does not change is that at every Mass, at every altar, Christ is offering Himself for you, and you are invited to offer yourself along with Him. That is enough.
