The Second Vatican Council called the Eucharistic celebration *"the source and summit of the Christian life"* (*Lumen Gentium* §11). That is not pastoral flourish. It is structural: everything in Catholic life flows from the Mass and everything returns to it. The Rosary, the sacraments, the works of mercy, the Catechism itself — all are arranged around this one act.

What is the Mass, then? In the simplest answer: **it is Christ's own Sacrifice on Calvary made sacramentally present for us, now.** Not a new sacrifice each Sunday. Not a reenactment, not a pageant. The *one* offering Christ made, two thousand years ago on a hill outside Jerusalem, is made present on the altar — not as a memory, but as a reality — so that we can stand at the foot of the Cross and receive what was won there.

*"As often as the sacrifice of the Cross by which 'Christ our Pasch has been sacrificed' is celebrated on the altar, the work of our redemption is carried out."* (*Lumen Gentium* §3, quoting 1 Cor 5:7)

This is what the Mass is. Everything else — the vestments, the hymns, the readings, the kneeling and standing — is the *how* of a reality that is much simpler and much more shocking than most of us remember.
