From the mountainside, Jesus opened his preaching with eight blessings (Matt 5:3–12). The Beatitudes are the self-portrait of Christ and the charter of the Christian life. They do not replace the Ten Commandments but reveal their inner spirit: what the Decalogue demands, the Beatitudes transfigure into love.

They are paradoxes to the worldly mind — blessed are the poor, the mourning, the meek — but they describe the only happiness that does not pass. Every saint is a person in whom some beatitude has taken flesh.
