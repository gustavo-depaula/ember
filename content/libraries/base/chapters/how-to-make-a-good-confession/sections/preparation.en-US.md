## Preparation

Ten to twenty minutes of quiet before you go.

**1. Ask the Holy Spirit to show you your sins.** Not your general wretchedness (the devil specializes in vague self-disgust). Specific acts, specific omissions. *"Send forth your light and your truth"* (Ps 43:3).

**2. Examine your conscience.** Go through the Ten Commandments, or the Beatitudes, or the seven capital sins. A structured examination prevents the scatter that forgets half and invents the other. Since your last confession, consider:

- Duties to God — prayer neglected, taking His name in vain, missing Mass, skipping obligations
- Duties to neighbor — anger, gossip, impurity, dishonesty, uncharity, failures of mercy
- Duties to self — intemperance, laziness, despair, presumption
- Omissions — the good you did not do

**3. Be specific about grave matter.** Mortal sins (grave matter, full knowledge, deliberate consent) must be confessed by *kind and number* insofar as memory allows. *"I committed adultery once"* is a confession; *"I have been unfaithful in my thoughts"* is not quite enough.

**4. Ask for true contrition.** Perfect contrition is sorrow *for having offended God who is good.* Imperfect contrition (attrition) is sorrow out of fear of punishment — still enough for the sacrament. If you cannot muster feelings, ask Christ for the grace of contrition. Coming to confession *is itself* a sign of it.

**5. Resolve to amend.** Not a vow to never sin again — a real intention, here and now, to turn from the sins you are confessing. Without this, the sacrament is void.

**6. Pray a short preparation prayer.** *"My God, I am heartily sorry for having offended you..."* — the Act of Contrition is the traditional text.
