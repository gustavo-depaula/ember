## How Often

The precept of the Church requires confession at least once a year, and always before receiving Communion if one is in a state of mortal sin.

But that is the floor, not the aim. The tradition — and the pastors — recommend:

- **Monthly confession** for anyone serious about the spiritual life. One of the most consistent pieces of advice from confessors across traditions.
- **Weekly or biweekly** for those in seminary, consecrated life, or in a season of particular spiritual battle.
- **Whenever a mortal sin has been committed**, before the next Communion. Do not postpone.
- **Before major decisions, spiritual retreats, or feast days.** A soul freshly absolved prays and hears differently.

St. Josemaría Escrivá called frequent confession *"the soul's shower."* Not because it is always for grave sins — most confessions are not — but because the soul, like the body, is not kept clean by occasional deep cleaning. It is kept clean by rinsing.

**Find a confessor, not a confessional.** A regular priest who knows you, if possible, will help you see patterns you cannot see alone. This is how spiritual direction often begins.
