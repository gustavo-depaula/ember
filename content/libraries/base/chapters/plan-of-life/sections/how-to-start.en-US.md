## How to Start

**Start small.** The most common mistake is an ambitious plan kept for two weeks, then abandoned. A small plan kept for a year will transform you. A maximal plan kept for a month will not.

**Three rules of thumb:**

1. **If you are just starting** — daily morning offering, daily Angelus, daily 10 minutes of mental prayer, weekly Sunday Mass, monthly confession. That is a real plan.
2. **If you already pray daily** — add a fixed time and form (lectio or meditation), daily examen, daily Rosary, spiritual reading.
3. **If you live a solid plan and want more** — Holy Hour, daily Mass, annual retreat, Marian consecration, spiritual direction.

**Write it down.** Unwritten rules get renegotiated hourly. Paper, a note on the phone, this app — wherever, but written. *Ember's plan-of-life feature exists for this.*

**Tie each practice to a time and a trigger.** *"After coffee, mental prayer. After dinner, Rosary walk. Before bed, examen."* Practices glued to an existing routine survive; practices floating in the day do not.

**Expect to fail.** You will miss days. The plan is not broken; your plan is the standard you return to. Do not add a new practice to compensate for a missed one — just resume.

**Review quarterly.** Every three months, in a day of recollection: Is this still the right plan? Is it bearing fruit? Is it too much? Too little? Adjust with a director if you have one.

**Make Mary the author.** End the plan with an act of trust: *"Mary, I place this plan in your Immaculate Heart. Keep me in it."* A rule under her protection is a rule that holds.
