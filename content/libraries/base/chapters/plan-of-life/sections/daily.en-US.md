## The Daily Norms

A classic lay plan of life includes some combination of these. Do not copy a list — discern with a confessor or director what belongs in *yours*.

**Morning offering.** The first conscious act of the day: give it to God. A few seconds. The *Offering to the Sacred Heart* or the *Morning Offering* from the app's prayer collection.

**Mental prayer.** The non-negotiable core. 15–30 minutes daily, at a fixed time. Carmelite method, Ignatian meditation, or lectio divina — see the chapters *Mental Prayer (Carmelite Method)* and *Lectio Divina*.

**Holy Mass.** On Sundays always; daily if your state of life allows. Nothing you can do in the day is greater than what happens on the altar.

**Spiritual reading.** 10–15 minutes of a spiritual classic. Not the news. Not social media. The Gospels, *Imitation of Christ*, the saints, the Catechism — something that *forms* rather than *informs*.

**The Angelus.** Noon (or morning and evening). 30 seconds of remembering the Incarnation in the middle of the workday.

**The Rosary.** Five decades. Walking, driving, before bed — but prayed, not rushed. See *How to Pray the Rosary Well*.

**Examen of conscience.** Before bed. Five minutes. See *The Ignatian Examen*.

**Acts of presence of God through the day.** Aspirations — brief, repeated darts of love: *"Jesus, I trust in you." "My Lord and my God." "Mary, help me now."* Scattered through commute, work, and waiting.
