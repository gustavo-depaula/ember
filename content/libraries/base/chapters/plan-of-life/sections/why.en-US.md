## Why You Need One

**Feelings are a bad driver.** Consolation comes and goes. If prayer depends on mood, it will not survive the week, let alone a season of aridity. A plan of life is what prays when you do not feel like praying — and it is in exactly those moments that God does His most hidden work.

**The soul is shaped by repetition, not intensity.** A five-minute daily examen forms the conscience more than a two-hour retreat four times a year. This is why the Church's liturgy is daily, why the Divine Office is *hours*, why the Rosary is *repeated*. Repetition is how love learns.

**Without structure, urgency wins.** There is always one more email, one more errand, one more thing that feels like it cannot wait. If prayer is not scheduled, it does not happen. *"When you fail to plan, you plan to fail."*

**A plan protects against pride and sloth.** It keeps the zealous from burning out on prayers they cannot sustain; it keeps the lukewarm from quietly starving. It is a rule — meaning it rules *you*, so that you are not ruled by appetite.

**It makes spiritual direction possible.** A director cannot help you in the abstract; he needs something concrete to look at. A plan gives the director a point of contact.

*"Everyone who has made some progress in the spiritual life has had a rule."* — common observation of the masters.
