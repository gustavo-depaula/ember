Now the practical questions. Most people who were baptized as infants and fell away have one or more of these gaps: they were never confirmed, they never made First Communion, or they have not been to confession in so long they do not remember how. All of this is repairable, and none of it should stop you from walking into a parish.

**If you were never confirmed.** Confirmation completes what baptism began. The Catechism says it *"perfects baptismal grace"* (CCC 1285) and seals the soul with the Holy Spirit in a fuller way, strengthening the baptized for the adult life of a Christian. If you missed it as a teenager — because your family stopped going to Mass, or because you did not take the classes seriously, or for any other reason — you can still be confirmed as an adult. Most parishes run a short preparation track for adults in your situation: a few sessions, usually spread over a few weeks or months, culminating in the sacrament, often at the Easter Vigil or on Pentecost, or sometimes when the bishop visits the parish. It is not a long process, and it is not an exam.

**If you never made First Communion.** The same conversation handles it. The preparation overlaps; often the priest will prepare you for Confirmation and First Communion together, with a first confession beforehand. A Catholic who is baptized but has not yet received the Eucharist or Confirmation is called, in the current pastoral language, a *candidate* (not a catechumen — catechumens are unbaptized). The distinction matters because the path for you is shorter.

**If you have not been to confession since childhood — or ever.** This is the most common situation and the one people dread most. In practice, it is the smallest obstacle. Tell the priest in the confessional, or when you meet with him, *"It has been a long time; I do not remember how."* He will walk you through it. Priests do this constantly. You do not need to have a complete list. You need to be honest about what you know.

What to say when you walk into a parish office or catch a priest after Mass: **"I was baptized Catholic, I was never confirmed [or: I never made First Communion; or: I haven't been to confession in years], and I'd like to start practicing. Can we talk?"** That sentence is all you need. The priest has heard it before, and he has a path. You do not have to design the path. You have to show up for the first conversation.
