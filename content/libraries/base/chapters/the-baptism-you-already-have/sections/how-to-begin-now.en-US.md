One week. That is the scope. Not a program, not a ten-step plan, not a reading list. One week, four small things, and then you assess.

**1. Find your baptismal certificate.** The parish where you were baptized keeps a permanent record. If you know the parish, call or email the office and ask for a copy of your baptismal certificate — they do this routinely. If you do not know the parish, ask the older relatives most likely to remember: a mother, a grandmother, a godparent. A photograph of the event is sometimes enough to identify the church. You will need this document later, for Confirmation, for marriage, for everything. Start the hunt now; it may take a few days, and that is fine.

**2. Go to a Sunday Mass, without planning to receive Communion.** Choose a parish near where you live. Arrive a little early. Sit toward the back if you want; nobody is keeping score. When Communion comes, either stay in the pew, or go forward with your arms crossed over your chest, which is the signal for a blessing rather than the Host. No one will know, and no one will ask. Your job this week is only to be present — to let the Mass happen over you, to hear the prayers of the Church said around you, to let the room and the light and the bells do their work. The Mass does not require that you be ready. It requires that you come.

**3. Make one appointment.** Send one email, or make one phone call, to a parish priest. The message is simple: *"I'm a baptized Catholic who never practiced. Can we talk?"* That is enough. Do not write a long explanation. Do not apologize. The priest will answer. When you meet, let him lead. He has done this more times than he can count, and he knows the path from where you are.

**4. The Our Father, once a day, slowly.** Not a rosary. Not a long meditation. One Our Father, in the morning or before you sleep, said without rushing. If a line stops you, stay with it. If nothing happens, pray it anyway. You are not performing. You are showing up. Seven Our Fathers over the week is a genuine beginning.

That is the whole week. Find the certificate, go to one Mass, send one email, pray one Our Father a day. If at the end of the week something in you wants more, add more slowly. If not, do the same week again. There is no rush that Christ is imposing on you.

Remember what he said about the ones like you — not the ones who had it all figured out, but the ones coming back from the long road: *"Let the little children come to me, and forbid them not: for the kingdom of God is for such as these."* (Mark 10:14)

The kingdom is not withheld from you while you assemble the proper identity. It is opened to the child who simply walks toward him. You were made a child of God at your baptism. That has not changed. You are being invited, this week, to walk as one.

*"I will arise, and will go to my father, and say to him: Father, I have sinned against heaven, and before thee."* (Luke 15:18)
