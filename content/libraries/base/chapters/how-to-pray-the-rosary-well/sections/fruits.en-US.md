## The Fruit of Each Mystery

The tradition attaches a *spiritual fruit* to each mystery — a virtue to ask for while meditating. Praying the rosary with a specific request sharpens attention and unifies the decade.

**Joyful Mysteries**
1. The Annunciation — *humility*
2. The Visitation — *love of neighbor*
3. The Nativity — *poverty of spirit*
4. The Presentation — *obedience*
5. The Finding in the Temple — *piety, joy in finding Jesus*

**Luminous Mysteries**
1. The Baptism — *openness to the Holy Spirit*
2. The Wedding at Cana — *to Jesus through Mary*
3. The Proclamation of the Kingdom — *conversion*
4. The Transfiguration — *desire for holiness*
5. The Institution of the Eucharist — *active participation at Mass*

**Sorrowful Mysteries**
1. The Agony — *contrition, conformity to God's will*
2. The Scourging — *purity, mortification of the senses*
3. The Crowning with Thorns — *moral courage, reign of Christ over the mind*
4. The Carrying of the Cross — *patience in trial*
5. The Crucifixion — *perseverance, the grace of a holy death*

**Glorious Mysteries**
1. The Resurrection — *faith*
2. The Ascension — *hope of heaven*
3. Pentecost — *zeal, charity*
4. The Assumption — *devotion to Mary, grace of a happy death*
5. The Coronation — *trust in Mary's intercession*

Before each decade, ask for that grace by name. *"Give me, Lord, through Mary's hands, the grace of humility."* Then pray. What you ask for in faith during the rosary is rarely left unanswered.
