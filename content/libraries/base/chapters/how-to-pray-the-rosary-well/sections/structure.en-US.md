## The Structure

**Opening.** The Sign of the Cross. The Apostles' Creed on the crucifix. An Our Father, three Hail Marys (for faith, hope, charity), a Glory Be on the short chain.

**Five decades.** Each decade: announce the mystery, an Our Father, ten Hail Marys, a Glory Be. St. John Paul II recommended the Fatima prayer (*"O my Jesus..."*) after each Glory Be — an act of intercession that widens the rosary beyond oneself.

**Closing.** The Hail, Holy Queen, the closing prayer, the Sign of the Cross.

**The four sets of mysteries.** Joyful (Monday, Saturday), Sorrowful (Tuesday, Friday), Glorious (Wednesday, Sunday), Luminous (Thursday — added by John Paul II in 2002). Five mysteries per set, each a scene from the life of Christ or Mary, each twenty Hail Marys long in prayer time.

A full rosary is all fifteen traditional mysteries (or twenty, with the Luminous). Most Catholics pray one set daily, or even a single decade, consistently.

**Posture matters.** Kneel if you can. Sit if you cannot kneel. Walk, if that helps you stay awake. The Cura d'Ars said you can pray a whole rosary in a single *Ave Maria* well prayed — and he also walked miles with the beads.
