The Sacraments are "efficacious signs of grace, instituted by Christ and entrusted to the Church, by which divine life is dispensed to us" (CCC 1131). They are not symbols only; they *do* what they signify. Water poured in Baptism really cleanses the soul. The words of absolution really forgive. The bread consecrated at Mass really becomes the Body of Christ.

The Church counts seven, grouped by their purpose:

- **Sacraments of Initiation:** Baptism, Confirmation, Eucharist.
- **Sacraments of Healing:** Penance (Confession) and Anointing of the Sick.
- **Sacraments at the Service of Communion:** Holy Orders and Matrimony.
