The Precepts of the Church are the minimum obligations that every Catholic undertakes by baptism — the "indispensable minimum in the spirit of prayer and moral effort" that guarantees the faithful "the very necessary minimum" for growth in love of God and neighbor (CCC 2041).

They are not the peak of Christian life; they are the floor beneath it. Missing them gravely can be a matter for the confessional. Keeping them is the beginning, not the end.
