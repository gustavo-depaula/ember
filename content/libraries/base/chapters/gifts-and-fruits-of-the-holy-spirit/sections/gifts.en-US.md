## The Seven Gifts

Drawn from Isaiah 11:2-3, the seven gifts have been listed by the Church since the Fathers:

**Wisdom** (*sapientia*). To see and taste divine things as they are — to judge all by the light of God's own truth.

**Understanding** (*intellectus*). To penetrate the meaning of what is believed — to grasp the mysteries of faith from within.

**Counsel** (*consilium*). To discern, in concrete action, what God is asking now. The supernatural perfection of prudence.

**Fortitude** (*fortitudo*). Strength to endure and to act for the good, especially when natural strength fails.

**Knowledge** (*scientia*). To judge rightly about creatures and human things, seeing them in their relation to God.

**Piety** (*pietas*). Filial affection toward God as Father, and reverence toward all that is his.

**Fear of the Lord** (*timor Domini*). Not servile fear of punishment, but the son's fear of grieving his Father — the first step of wisdom (Ps 111:10).

The gifts are given with sanctifying grace and grow with it; they flower as the soul grows quiet enough to hear the Spirit move.
