Most people who want to begin the Rosary and cannot quite begin it are blocked by the same thing: the count. Fifty Hail Marys, five decades, a string of beads that looks, to the beginner, like a punishment. If that is the picture you have of the Rosary, it will go unprayed, and you will carry a small quiet shame about it. Put the picture down.

Start with **one decade**. Ten Hail Marys. A single mystery. One Our Father, ten Hail Marys, one Glory Be. That is a decade. You can pray it in under five minutes, while walking to the bus, while washing the dishes after dinner, while waiting for sleep. This is enough. It is not a lesser Rosary — it is the Rosary in seed form, which is how the Rosary was first prayed anyway, before the count became standard.

When you pray the decade, remember what the beads are *for*. They are a frame. You are not meant to be concentrating on the beads any more than a reader is meant to concentrate on the margin of a page. You are meant to be looking through the frame at the picture — at a scene from the Gospel. The Annunciation. The Visitation. The finding of the child Jesus in the Temple. The agony in the garden. The empty tomb. While your lips say *Hail Mary, Hail Mary*, your mind is quietly inside a Gospel scene, watching.

This is the point St. John Paul II made, in a letter on the Rosary he wrote late in his life: *"The Rosary, though clearly Marian in character, is at heart a Christocentric prayer."* You are meditating on Christ *through* the eyes of his mother, because she is the soul in Scripture who kept these things best, *pondering them in her heart*. The Rosary is Mary lending you her memory of her Son.

If you already know the full Rosary but have been praying it as a chore, and you feel this chapter is too small for you, treat this section as permission — *permission to shrink back to a decade* for a season, until the prayer is honest again. A devotion that has turned into a performance is not, by that fact, holy; it is only loud. Better one decade prayed with love than five decades mumbled to reach a number. The Rosary is a prayer, not a quota. If the quota is killing the prayer, kill the quota.

For the full form — the five mysteries, their order, the traditional prayers, how to actually sit inside a whole Rosary — the chapter **How to Pray the Rosary Well** is the one to read next. This section is only for the soul who needs to cross the first line. Cross it today. One decade. One mystery. One Gospel scene, seen through her.

*"And Mary kept all these things, pondering them in her heart."* (*Luke 2:19*)
