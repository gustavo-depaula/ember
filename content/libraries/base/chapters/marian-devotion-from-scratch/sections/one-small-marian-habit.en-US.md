Do not leave this chapter with a resolution to love Mary more. That is too large to keep. Leave it with *one small habit*, chosen honestly, that will bring her quietly into your days. Pick one. Only one. For a season — a month, a Lent, whatever you can actually stand behind.

**The Angelus at noon.** Thirty seconds. Three Hail Marys, three versicles about the Annunciation, one short collect. You can pray it silently in a parking lot. For centuries the bells of Catholic towns rang at noon to call people to this prayer; for you, a phone alarm set to 12:00 will do. If you can also pray it at 6 a.m. and 6 p.m., as the tradition does, all the better, but one is enough to start.

**One Memorare in distress.** The *Memorare* is the prayer of the soul with her back against the wall — *"Remember, O most gracious Virgin Mary, that never was it known that anyone who fled to thy protection was left unaided."* Not daily. Only when distress comes. When you cannot sleep. When the test results are waiting. When the marriage is on the edge. One Memorare, from the heart, in the dark. She has an old habit of hearing those.

**One Hail Mary on waking, one at bedtime.** Two prayers, fifteen seconds each. Before you reach for the phone in the morning, before you put out the light at night. This is the smallest possible Marian rule of life, and it is enough. It bookends the day with her. In a year you will have prayed her name seven hundred times in your most unguarded moments.

**A decade of the Rosary on the commute.** One mystery, ten Hail Marys, while you drive or sit on the train. Put the podcast down. You do not need another opinion today. You need one Gospel scene and ten beads, and the beads are even optional.

**The Sub Tuum Praesidium.** This is the oldest preserved prayer to Mary in Christian history, found on a third-century Egyptian papyrus — older than most of the creeds, older than the canon of the New Testament was formally closed. Before there was a Church in Europe, the desert Christians were already asking her for shelter. A small phrase from it, in English: *"We fly to thy protection, O holy Mother of God."* That is enough to hold in the mouth on a hard day. Say it when the day is hard.

Pick one. Not five. **One**. Whatever you pick, keep it small enough that you will actually do it on the worst day of the month, because the days you most need this are the worst days. The point is not to be impressive. The point is that a mother was given to you at the cross, and she is slowly, patiently, being let into your house.
