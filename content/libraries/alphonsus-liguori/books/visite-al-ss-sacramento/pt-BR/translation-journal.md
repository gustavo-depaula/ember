## Translation Decisions

1. **"Voi" in prayers**: Translated as "vós" (formal second person plural) per project guidelines, maintaining the traditional Catholic register in Portuguese.
2. **Latin phrases**: Kept inline in italics exactly as in the original.
3. **Scripture references**: Preserved as-is (e.g., Io. VII, 37; Is. XII, 3).
4. **Editor footnotes**: Dropped entirely — only Liguori's own text is preserved.
5. **Saints' names**: Standardized to Portuguese forms (São Francisco, Santa Teresa, São Bernardo, etc.).
6. **"il Venerabile"**: Translated as "o Santíssimo" or "o Santíssimo Sacramento" depending on context — this is the standard Portuguese Catholic term for the exposed Blessed Sacrament.
7. **"custodia"**: Translated as "sacrário" (tabernacle) or "custódia" (monstrance) depending on context.
8. **Paragraph structure**: Preserved exactly as in the Italian original.
9. **"Comunione spirituale" instruction lines**: Kept as brief directives (e.g., "A comunhão espiritual.").
10. **"Dice S. X" / "dice il divoto Y"**: Translated naturally as "Diz São X" / "diz o devoto Y".
11. **"ciborio"**: Translated as "cibório" (standard Portuguese Catholic term for ciborium/tabernacle area).
12. **"ostensorio"**: Translated as "ostensório" (monstrance for exposition).
13. **"Preghiera a Maria Santissima" (recurring closing prayer)**: Translated as "Oração a Maria Santíssima" and appears in full in visita-01; subsequent visits use shorter Marian prayers from various saints.
14. **Saints' prayers attribution**: Maintained the pattern "Oração de São X:" / "Oração do mesmo São X:" as in the original.
15. **"spedali / spedale"**: Translated as "hospitais / hospital" (modern Portuguese form).
16. **"Suor / Irmã"**: Both translated as "Irmã" (Sister) for religious women.
17. **"Alfonso de Liguori"**: Rendered as "Afonso de Ligório" in the dedication — standard Portuguese name form.
18. **Tone consistency**: Liguori's passionate, exclamatory style ("Oh!", "Ah!", "Deh!") preserved with Portuguese equivalents ("Oh!", "Ah!", "Ah!").
19. **"V'amo" contractions**: Expanded in Portuguese to "Amo-vos" for clarity while preserving devotional register.
20. **"dar gusto" / "far gusto"**: Consistently translated as "dar gosto" / "agradar" depending on context.
