## Translation Decisions

1. **Latin quotations**: Kept inline in italics exactly as in the original. Liguori's text is saturated with Latin — Scripture, Church Fathers, liturgical phrases. These are integral to his style and the reader's experience.

2. **Saints' names**: Standardized to English forms (St. Denis the Areopagite, St. Teresa, St. Augustine, St. Bernard, St. Francis Borgia, St. Francis de Sales, St. Catherine of Siena, St. Catherine of Genoa, St. Gertrude, St. Bonaventure, St. Gregory, etc.).

3. **"Uniformità" vs. "Conformità"**: Liguori explicitly distinguishes these two terms. "Conformity" means joining our will to God's; "uniformity" means making our will one with God's. This distinction is preserved carefully.

4. **Tone**: Liguori writes in a warm, direct, exhortative pastoral style — addressing the reader personally ("you," "my devout reader"). This is preserved. He alternates between scholarly citation and affectionate spiritual counsel.

5. **"Amor proprio"**: Translated as "self-love" throughout, in the traditional ascetical sense of disordered self-regard.

6. **Editor footnotes**: All [^N] markers and footnote definitions have been dropped per instructions.

7. **"Divoto/a"**: Rendered as "devout soul" or "devout one" depending on context, preserving Liguori's affectionate address to the reader.

8. **Paragraph structure**: The original has very long, dense paragraphs with minimal section breaks. This structure is preserved exactly as in the Italian source.

9. **"Beneplacito"**: Translated as "good pleasure" (divine good pleasure), the standard term in English ascetical theology.

10. **Scripture references**: Kept in the same abbreviated format as the original (e.g., Col. 3.14, Jo 6.38, Matth. 12.50).
