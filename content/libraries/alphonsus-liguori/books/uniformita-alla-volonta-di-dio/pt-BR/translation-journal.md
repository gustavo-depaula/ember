## Translation Decisions

1. **"Vós" for addresses to God** — All prayers and direct addresses to God use "vós" (2nd person plural formal), following traditional Brazilian Portuguese liturgical usage.

2. **Latin citations** — Kept inline in italics exactly as in the original. These are central to Liguori's method and must be preserved.

3. **Footnotes dropped** — The original has no footnote markers ([^N]) in the body, so nothing to strip.

4. **Paragraph structure** — The Italian text uses very long paragraphs (some spanning entire pages). Preserved exactly as in the original, as these represent Liguori's flowing ascetical argument style.

5. **"travaglio/travagli"** — Translated variously as "tribulação/tribulações," "sofrimento/sofrimentos," or "provação/provações" depending on context, to capture the range of meaning (suffering, trial, labor, trouble).

6. **Register** — Maintained a formal but warm devotional register, matching Liguori's style: scholarly (dense citations) yet pastoral (direct address to the reader as "alma devota").

7. **Section structure** — The original uses "Per 1.", "Per 2.", etc. for the practical section. Translated as "Em 1.o lugar", "Em 2.o lugar", etc., following Portuguese convention.

8. **"divoto/divota"** — Translated as "devoto/devota" (standard Portuguese).
