## Translation Decisions

1. **Prayers use "vós"**: All addresses to God use the formal "vós" form (vós, vosso, etc.) as is traditional in Brazilian Portuguese Catholic prayer.
2. **Editor footnotes dropped**: All [^N] markers and footnote definitions at file bottoms are removed. These are editorial/scholarly apparatus, not Liguori's original text.
3. **Latin phrases kept inline**: Scripture quotes and Latin phrases preserved exactly as in the original.
4. **Saints' names**: Standardized to Portuguese forms (Santo Agostinho, São Tomás, etc.)
5. **Dramatic style preserved**: Liguori's exclamatory, vivid meditation style maintained — rhetorical questions, direct address to reader, graphic imagery of death and judgment.
6. **"Cristiano mio"**: Translated as "Meu cristão" — Liguori's direct address to the reader.
7. **"Fratello mio"**: Translated as "Irmão meu" — Liguori's fraternal address to the reader.
8. **"Lettor mio"**: Translated as "Leitor meu" — direct address to reader in ch. 35.
9. **Bible abbreviations**: Book of Job = "Jó" (not "Jo", which is João/John).
