## Translation Decisions

- Numbered paragraphs (1-78) preserved exactly as in the French source.
- Latin quotations kept inline with italics, matching the original's pattern.
- "Esclave/esclavage" rendered as "slave/slavery" -- this is Montfort's deliberate theological language, not a casual term. The concept of voluntary slavery of love to Mary is central to his spirituality.
- "Mahométans" does not appear in this text (it does in True Devotion).
- "Les Turcs" in paragraph 33 rendered as "the Turks" -- historical reference to Ottoman slavery practices, kept as-is.
- Scripture references preserved in their original form.
- Montfort's rhetorical style -- exclamatory, passionate, with long flowing sentences -- preserved in translation rather than broken into shorter modern sentences.
- "Oraisons" (chapter title) rendered as "Prayers" rather than the more technical "Orations."
