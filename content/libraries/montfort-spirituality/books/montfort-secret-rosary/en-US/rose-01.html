The Prayers of the Rosary

The Rosary is made up of two things: mental prayer and vocal prayer. In the Rosary mental prayer is nothing other than meditation of the chief mysteries of the life, death, and glory of Jesus Christ and of His Blessed Mother.

Vocal prayer in the Rosary consists in saying fifteen decades of the Hail Mary, each decade headed by an Our Father, while at the same time meditating on and contemplating the fifteen principal virtues which Jesus and Mary practised in the fifteen mysteries of the Holy Rosary.

In the first five decades we must honour the five Joyful Mysteries; in the second five decades, the five Sorrowful Mysteries; and in the third group of five decades, the five Glorious Mysteries. So the Rosary is nothing other than a method of contemplation of the actions and virtues of Jesus and Mary.
