## 360 — TRANSFORMING LOVE

### Presence of God

My God, may Your love invade and penetrate me until it transforms me wholly in You.

### Meditation

As a tiny flame plunged into a vast fire becomes one with it, burning and shining with it so that it can no longer be distinguished from the great blaze, so the soul united to God by love is lost in Him, enveloped and transformed in such a way that it seems almost God Himself, becoming "divine and God by participation, as far as possible in this life" (St. John of the Cross).

God remains God, essentially distinct from the soul; yet love unites creature and Creator so intimately that they are "two natures in one spirit and love." The perfection of charity and grace makes the Holy Spirit dwell in the soul with singular fullness, and in this Spirit - bond of love - the soul remains totally united to the Most Holy Trinity.

Thus is fulfilled, in the highest way possible on earth, Jesus' ardent prayer: "As Thou, Father, in Me, and I in Thee, that they also may be one in Us" (Jn 17:21).

Being so united, the soul is transformed in God through love. This transformation extends to all powers: the intellect, united to the divine, becomes illuminated by supernatural light; the will becomes one with God's will and wholly inflamed by holy love; memory, affections, and appetites are likewise divinized according to God.

Not only the soul's being and powers are divinized, but also its actions, because God Himself moves and directs them according to His Spirit and will. Hence the soul's acts become divine in their mode. Spiritual marriage brings the supernatural life to fullness and realizes most perfectly grace's privilege: to make man a "partaker of the divine nature" (2 Pt 1:4).

Here we see again the marvelous continuity between the growth of grace and these highest states, which are its final consequence and splendid crown. Why then linger among the miserable things of earth, when God has created us for such divine greatness?

2 — "The lover," says St. John of the Cross, "cannot be satisfied unless he feels he loves as much as he is loved." The one who truly loves cannot bear to be surpassed in love. Yet how can a weak and finite creature equal God in love?

This holy and bold longing is fulfilled precisely in total transformation. Because the soul is transformed into God's will, there is equality of love: the soul loves God with God's own will, which has become its own. Therefore it loves Him as much as it is loved by Him, since it loves with the very love with which God loves it - the Holy Spirit.

The Holy Spirit, eternal Love of the Father and the Son, is given to us so that, kindling divine charity in us, He enables us to love God not by our poor powers alone, but together with infinite Love Himself. The flame lit at Baptism and increased by correspondence to grace is finally immersed and lost in the infinite flame of the Spirit when the soul becomes one spirit with God.

Then the soul truly loves God as it is loved by Him, because it loves in union with the Holy Spirit. Its capacity for love becomes, in a participated way, almost limitless; only thus is its love satisfied, because it can repay love with parity of love.

What joy and comfort for the soul that suffers from the poverty of its love compared with God's infinite lovableness: to know that the Holy Spirit can and wills to supply what is lacking, provided the soul lets itself be wholly taken and absorbed in His immense flame.

### Colloquy

"O my soul, created for such greatness and called to it! What are you doing, where are you lingering? Your ambitions are littleness, your possessions misery. Lamentable blindness, not to see so great a light nor hear so great a call!" (St. John of the Cross)

"O Holy Spirit, You are the Mediator between the soul and God; You stir the soul with ardent desires and set it aflame with that sovereign fire to which it is so near.

"What mercies You use with souls, O Lord! Blessed and praised forever be the great love with which You love us. Is it possible that anyone should not love You? Woe to me, who for so long did not love You!

"How good You are, my Lord! Forever blessed be Your Name, and let all creatures praise You. You have loved us so much that, in truth, we can speak of the communications You deign to grant souls even in this exile. O infinite liberality of my God, how great are Your works!" (cf. St. Teresa of Jesus)
