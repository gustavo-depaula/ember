## 357 — THE MUTUAL GIFT

### Presence of God

O Lord, may I be all Yours, and may You be all mine.

### Meditation

1 — "God does not give Himself wholly except to those who give themselves wholly to Him" (St. Teresa of Jesus). God so respects human freedom that, although He wills to communicate His divine life, He gives Himself according to our consent. But when consent is full, He does not hesitate to give Himself entirely.

Thus, to the soul's perfect yes, God answers with the "true and entire yes of His grace" (St. John of the Cross). To the full gift of our will corresponds the full communication of grace, together with infused virtues and the gifts of the Holy Spirit.

Grace and love go together. Since perfect adherence to God's will is the sign of perfect love, God grants an abundance of grace to the soul wholly conformed to Him. St. John of the Cross says that when God's will and the soul's will are one in free consent, the soul possesses God by grace: to her yes, God has answered with His complete yes.

This is the triumph of grace. Grace, planted in Baptism and increased through every stage of spiritual life, reaches maturity when the soul places itself entirely in God's hands. It dies to self in order to live in God and of God: "You have died, and your life is hidden with Christ in God" (Col 3:3).

The Saint adds that if the soul does what lies in its power, emptying and stripping itself of all things, it is impossible that God should fail to communicate Himself—at least in secret silence. It is more impossible than that the sun should fail to shine in a clear sky. As sunlight enters a house when the window is opened, so God enters the emptied soul and fills it with divine goods.

For a long time the divine Sun has shone on your soul and knocked at your door: "Behold, I stand at the door and knock" (Rev 3:20). Every confession, every communion, every Mass, every occasion to practice virtue, every inspiration, every call to obedience is another knock of God upon your heart.

Why make Him wait? Awake from lethargy and throw the soul's door wide open. Do not open halfway, nor three quarters: remove every obstacle, for the King of glory must enter.

If it costs you to deny your will in all things, think how beautiful it is to be guided in all things by God's will. If it pains you to renounce self-love and earthly affections, think how sweet it is to possess God's love. If dying to self repels you, think how glorious it is to live for God.

The Lord wishes to communicate Himself to your soul, to give Himself wholly to you, to dwell with you: "If anyone hears My voice and opens the door, I will come in to him and sup with him, and he with Me" (Rev 3:20). Oh, do not lose so immense a gift through negligence.

### Colloquy

"Lord of heaven and earth! Is it possible to enjoy You so intimately even in this mortal life? What delights You reserve for souls that give themselves wholly to You!

"In this life, my Lord, I ask only one thing: that You kiss me with the kiss of Your mouth. But do so in such a way that my will remains united to Yours, never to be separated.

"For love of God, my soul, awake from sleep: God does not wait for the next life to reward your love. The reward begins already here below.

"My Lord, my Mercy and my Treasure, what other good can be desired on earth than to remain so near You that separation is no longer possible? Since love permits me, I will repeat without ceasing: 'My Beloved to me, and I to my Beloved.'" (cf. St. Teresa of Jesus)
