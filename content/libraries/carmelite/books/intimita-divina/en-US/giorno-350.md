## 350 — HUMBLE AND REVERENT LOVE

### Presence of God

O God, who are so great, deign to raise up to Yourself my soul so small and miserable.

### Meditation

1 — The love that boldly urges the soul toward conquest of divine union is, at the same time, full of reverence and respect, for the soul understands better than ever how sublime and exalted God’s Majesty is.

If, on one hand, love makes it impatient to unite with Him, on the other, continual and clear awareness of its misery makes it more solicitous than ever to watch itself carefully so that in its conduct there be nothing that can displease so exalted a Majesty.

«The soul,» says the Mystical Doctor, «soon sees in itself a true and effective determination not to do what it understands to offend God, nor to omit anything that concerns His service.... It has a very vigilant and solicitous care about what it must do or leave undone to please the Lord, examining and searching itself a thousand times to see whether in any way it may have offended Him.»

Evidently this is far above simple flight from sin: it is firm resolve to flee every knowingly recognized imperfection, omission, or negligence.

And because the soul knows by experience that, despite all good will, many such faults may still escape through inadvertence or fragility, it wants to intensify vigilance so as to avoid even these as much as possible.

This solicitude comes from love, not from scruple: true loving solicitude, like that which made St. Teresa Margaret of the Heart of Jesus continually repeat: «Now, in this action, what am I doing? Do I love my God?»

Or like that which Blessed Angela of Foligno expressed with burning words: «See, Lord, whether there is in me anything that is not love!»

If you want a sure sign of your love for God, see how firm you are in the purpose of fleeing even the least thing that displeases Him.

This purpose must be so rooted in your will that not only is it continually present—as truly important things are—but also effectively restrains you from every imperfection as soon as you perceive it.

This is absolutely necessary because, as St. John of the Cross teaches, «for the soul to be perfectly united with God in will and love, it is required that it consent knowingly and advertently to no imperfection whatever, and that it have power and freedom not to consent to it when it perceives it.»

2 — Awareness of one’s lowliness keeps the soul humble in love, banishing every presumption.

Far from counting on merits and good works, it sees clearly that, whatever it does, all is nothing in comparison with God’s exalted Majesty; «consequently it judges itself useless in all it does, and seems to itself to live in vain.»

The Gospel words «we are unprofitable servants» (Lk 17:10) are for it vividly present and express well its habitual state of soul.

The light the Holy Spirit infuses is too great for it to fall into illusion about its value or vainglory over its works. On the contrary, «with great conviction it judges itself worse than all: first, because love teaches what God deserves; second, because being many the works it does in His service and knowing them defective and imperfect, from all of them it draws profound confusion and pain, understanding that its low way of proceeding is too unworthy of so high a Lord.»

It is beautiful to see how this deep humility is fruit not only of light but also of love. Love makes the soul so esteem God that, while ardently desiring to possess Him, it is deeply convinced that by itself it is wholly incapable of attaining Him.

Yet though humble and reverent, love keeps its characteristic boldness, and the soul does not cease aspiring to divine union.

Precisely in this sense St. Therese of the Child Jesus wrote: «Despite my extreme weakness I dare to fix my gaze on the divine Sun, the Sun of Love.»

The Saint, who with simplicity compares herself to a little fledgling unable to fly, sees clearly she can never rise so high by herself; yet she does not lose hope.

If she cannot count on her strength, she knows she can count on Jesus’ love, the divine Word made flesh precisely to seek us poor sinners, who suffered and died to draw souls «to the center of the eternal Hearth of the blessed Trinity.»

She is sure that one day Jesus will be moved by her weakness and descend upon her to make her the prey of His love: «One day, I hope, You will come to seek your little bird, and rising with it to the Hearth of Love, You will plunge it for eternity into the eternal Abyss of this Love.»

Yes, Jesus is ready to come to every soul of good will, ready to come to your soul too, to raise it to desired union; but He wants you to know how to wait for Him with fidelity in generous and full dedication to His service.

### Colloquy

«O divine Word, You are the adored Eagle whom I love and who draws me. You are He who, swooping down to this land of exile, willed to suffer and die to draw souls to the center of the eternal Hearth of the blessed Trinity.

«You are He who, ascending again to the inaccessible Light that henceforth is your dwelling, still remain in this valley of tears, hidden under the appearance of a white host.... Eternal Eagle, You wish to nourish with your divine substance me, poor little being, who would return to nothingness if your divine gaze did not give me life each instant....

«O Jesus, let me tell You in the excess of my gratitude how far your love goes, even to folly! And how could my heart not fly toward You before such folly? How could my confidence have limits?

«For You, I know, even the saints committed follies, did great things, because they were eagles.... I am too little to do great things, and my folly is to hope your love accepts me as a victim....

«Wait as long as You wish, my Beloved; your little bird will remain without strength and without wings, yet always with eyes fixed on You. It wants to be fascinated by your divine gaze, to become prey of your love.

«One day, I hope, adored Eagle, You will come to seek your little bird, and, rising with it to the Hearth of Love, You will plunge it for eternity into the eternal abyss of this Love to which it has offered itself as victim» (St. Therese of the Child Jesus).
