## 361 — DIVINE INTIMACY

### Presence of God

Grant me, O Lord, a full and stable intimacy with You, that I may love You more and more.

### Meditation

1 — A soul begins to enter the path of divine intimacy on the day it resolves to come out of itself and all things, and with ardor seeks the living God present within. From this first step to the deep intimacy of total union, the road is long and demanding.

As the soul, sustained by grace, detaches itself from self and creatures, frees itself from imperfections, strips itself of its own will to be clothed in God's will alone, and lets love blaze within, its intimacy with God grows ever more intense and loving, until at the heights of transforming love it becomes continuous and complete: a divine embrace uniting creature to Creator.

Then Jesus' great promise is fulfilled in the highest way possible on earth: "If anyone loves Me... My Father will love him, and We will come to him and make Our dwelling with him" (Jn 14:23).

St. John of the Cross says this is not incredible in a soul purified, tried in tribulations and temptations, and found faithful in love: the Most Holy Trinity truly comes to dwell stably there - enlightening intellect in the wisdom of the Son, delighting will in the Holy Spirit, and absorbing the soul in the Father's sweetness.

At the highest moments of transforming union, the soul perceives God living, present, and acting within; it feels His paternal embrace, the splendor of His Wisdom, and the fire of His Love penetrating all. Even when this sensible awareness is not so vivid, the soul remains conscious of being deeply united to God, moved and governed by Him.

2 — Divine intimacy, especially in its higher degrees, is in itself profoundly joyful and beatifying. Yet the loving soul does not seek it for enjoyment, but to love God more, to be wholly united to Him, wholly possessed and governed by Him, so as to serve Him better and glorify Him in every act.

St. Teresa of Avila teaches that the purpose of God's communications and favors, even the highest mystical ones, is not merely to caress souls with consolations, but to strengthen their weakness so they can imitate Jesus in much suffering. This is the goal of prayer; this is what spiritual marriage aims at: producing works, and works.

The sweetness of intimacy with God is meant to make the soul more courageous in divine service, more generous in self-gift, stronger in carrying the cross. As long as we are on earth, suffering remains. Even amid delights of union, we must conform ourselves to the Crucified and follow Him to complete immolation for the Father's glory and the salvation of our brothers and sisters.

The works union must produce are works of love: intense activity of pure love by which the soul gives itself incessantly to God, longing to draw many souls with it. Thus, from divine intimacy and total union springs the most fruitful apostolate.

The loving soul forgets itself. It thinks neither of enjoyment nor suffering, but only of loving and serving God, contributing as much as possible to His glory, and associating itself with Jesus' redemptive work. If it longs for ever more intimate union, on earth and in heaven, it is to love with greatest intensity and to make Love loved by as many souls as possible.

### Colloquy

"Lord God, my Beloved, if the memory of my sins still holds You back from granting what I ask, then in this too, my God, let Your will be done, for that is what I desire above all.

"But if You await my works in order to answer me, then give me those works Yourself; accomplish them in me, and let Your will be fulfilled in whatever sufferings You ask me to accept.

"And if You do not await my works, what are You waiting for, most clement Lord? Why do You still delay? If, in the end, what I ask in Your Son is grace and mercy, then receive my poor coin and give me this Good that You Yourself will to give.

"Who can be freed from low ways of acting unless You raise him in purity of love? How shall man rise to You unless You lift him with the hand that formed him?

"You will not take from me, my God, what once You gave me in Your only-begotten Son Jesus Christ, in whom You gave me all that I desire. Therefore I rejoice, certain that You will not delay if I hope.

"Why then, my soul, do you delay, when from this very moment you can love God in your heart? The heavens are mine, the earth is mine, peoples are mine, the just are mine, sinners are mine, angels are mine, the Mother of God is mine; and God Himself is mine and for me, because Christ is mine and all for me. What then do you seek, my soul? All this is yours and for you." (St. John of the Cross)
