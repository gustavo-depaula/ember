## 71 — THE PRECURSOR

### Presence of God

O Jesus, eternal light, infuse into my soul a strong desire to prepare Your ways.

### Meditation

1 — In the Christmas liturgy we paused to admire Jesus as the Incarnate Word, God-with-us, and rejoiced thinking of the immense riches of divine life, holiness, and grace He came to bring us. It is good now to consider what our conduct should be in order to welcome Jesus' great gift fittingly.

Today's Gospel invites us to place ourselves in the school of John the Baptist, the Precursor. Jesus Himself called him "a burning and shining lamp" (Jn. 5, 35), and his first appearance in the desert is already a lesson in austere, penitent, mortified life. The whole life of the precursor is nothing but a preparation for the coming of the Messiah. John understood that Christ is the Holy One, the Savior, God-with-us, and that to receive Him worthily one must prepare oneself by interior purification and by ascesis. He teaches us to await Jesus not passively, but with diligent and generous commitment to order our whole life and make it worthy of Him.

John's words vibrate with energy and admit no compromise. To the people who go to listen to him he preaches penance; to tax collectors he imposes that they extort no more than what is just; to soldiers that they not mistreat neighbor and be content with their pay; to Pharisees and Sadducees he asks a sincere conversion without evasions: "Bear fruits worthy of penance" (Mt. 3, 8). Appearance is not enough, desires are not enough; deeds are needed.

2 — Two other virtues of the precursor indicate to us how we must await Jesus: humility and charity.

"After me comes One mightier than I, and I am not worthy to untie the strap of His sandals" (Mk. 1, 7). John not only recognizes before Jesus all his own littleness and unworthiness, but rejoices in annihilating himself that Christ may be known and glorified by all: "He must increase and I must decrease" (Jn. 3, 30).

The Precursor's mission and joy is to prepare the way for the Savior and then disappear. Of no one else can it be said as Jesus said of him: "Among those born of women there has not arisen one greater than John the Baptist" (Lk. 7, 28). John teaches us not to seek ourselves but Christ; not to live in function of our interests but His. He teaches us to love Jesus with absolute dedication: every one of his preachings, words, actions, and sacrifices are nothing but a witness given to the Messiah. He is wholly committed, to the end, to leading his disciples to Jesus.

And here is yet another lesson in charity. To lead souls to Jesus and prepare His ways, it is not enough to think and work for our personal sanctification; one must dedicate oneself also to the sanctification of neighbor.

After all, how could we glorify the Lord more than by spending life for His good, for the expansion of His Kingdom?

### Colloquy

O Jesus, to prepare Your ways and draw many souls to You, purify my heart and make it humble.

John the Baptist had the task of preparing Your way in the soul of the crowds and in each individual, but first he had to prepare it in himself. He clearly understood that he could never call others to conversion and penance if he did not personally lead a life of penance and austere mortification. Here is his great example: no one can draw others to good without first doing good for himself; no one can lead others to virtue and holiness without beginning by being virtuous and holy.

O Lord, I often fall into the error of agitating and worrying myself over others, as though wanting to force You to regulate Your grace according to my views. Instead You teach me that the first work to do to prepare Your way is within me, namely in mortifying my passions, correcting my defects, denying myself in order to conform my life fully to Yours. And You also teach me that a life of ascesis cannot be reduced to practicing some external mortification, but must be an assiduous and intense work of interior conversion, capable of changing a selfish soul into a soul open to love of You and neighbor.

O Jesus, since all comes to me from You, all is Your grace, make me understand that I must live like John the Baptist, in most sincere humility, recognizing my powerlessness and littleness, and entrusting myself to You alone.

Since You are the center, the sun of my life, help me never to seek myself but always and only You. Help me to be disposed at any moment to annihilate myself so that You may be known, loved, and glorified.

Yes, O Jesus, it will be joy and glory for me too to draw souls to You and then, like the Baptist, disappear and remain in shadow so that You alone may appear in full light.
