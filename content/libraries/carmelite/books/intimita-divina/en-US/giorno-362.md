## 362 — OUR LADY OF THE ROSARY
*October 7*

### Presence of God

May your blessed Rosary, O holy Virgin, be for me a weapon of defense and a school of virtue.

### Meditation

The feast of the Holy Rosary is thanksgiving for the great victories granted to the Christian people through Mary's Rosary and, at the same time, a solemn witness to the value of this prayer. The liturgy of the day is, in a sense, a commentary and expansion of the Rosary.

Today's feast tells us that honoring the Rosary is honoring Mary, because the Rosary is meditation on her life united to the devout repetition of the Hail Mary. Precisely under this aspect the Church praises and strongly recommends it: "O God... grant that, meditating these mysteries in the most holy Rosary of the Blessed Virgin Mary, we may imitate what they contain and obtain what they promise."

The Rosary, well prayed, is both prayer and school. Its mysteries teach that in Mary's life everything is judged in relation to God: her joys are in what pleases Him; her sorrows are united to those of Jesus suffering for the sins of humanity.

Mary's one joy is Jesus: to be His Mother, hold Him in her arms, present Him to the world, contemplate Him risen in glory, and be united to Him in heaven. Mary's one sorrow is Jesus' Passion: to see Him betrayed, scourged, crowned with thorns, and crucified for our sins.

One first fruit we should draw from the Rosary is to judge the events of our own life in relation to God: to rejoice in what pleases Him and unites us to Him, and to grieve over sin, which separates us from Him and caused Jesus' Passion and death.

Another fruit of daily Rosary is penetration of Christ's mysteries. Through Mary and with Mary, who opens the door to them, the Rosary helps us enter the ineffable grandeur of the Incarnation, Passion, and glory of Jesus.

Who better than Our Lady understood and lived these mysteries? Who better can give us their meaning? If, while praying the Rosary, we truly place ourselves in spiritual contact with Mary and accompany her through the stages of her life, we can receive something of her heart's sentiments before these mysteries. Then the Rosary becomes a true time of meditation, almost contemplation, under her guidance.

This is what Mary desires, not rosaries prayed only with the lips while the mind wanders. The repeated Hail Marys should express the soul's effort to rise toward the Virgin and be led by her into understanding of divine mysteries.

Such prayer requires recollection. As St. Teresa says, before beginning, the soul should ask itself with whom it is going to speak and who it is that speaks, so as to know how to behave. Rosaries recited in haste cannot nourish interior life. But when prayed with true devotion, the Rosary is a very effective means to cultivate Marian piety and enter more deeply into intimacy with Mary and her divine Son.

### Colloquy

"O Mary, just as among the blessed spirits no one loves God more than you, so after God we have, and can have, no one who loves us more than you, our most loving Mother.

"Even if one could gather all the love of all mothers for their children, all spouses for their spouses, all saints and angels for their devotees, it would never equal the love you bear for a single soul - and therefore also for mine.

"O Mary, since you love me, make me like you. You have power to change hearts; take my heart and transform it. Make me holy; make me worthy to belong to your Son.

"Let others ask what they wish: health, riches, earthly success. I ask you, my Mother, for what you yourself desire from me and what is most pleasing to your heart: humility, patience in trials, pure love of God, and charity toward all, especially toward those who oppose me.

"In short, O Mary, most holy among creatures, make me holy. In you there is no lack of love or power; you can obtain everything for me. Only my negligence in turning to you, only my lack of confidence in your help, can hinder me from receiving your graces." (St. Alphonsus)
