## 364 — FEAST OF CHRIST THE KING
*Last Sunday of October*

### Presence of God

O Jesus, Prince of ages, King of nations, be the only King of my mind and my heart.

### Meditation

Today's liturgy celebrates Christ's Kingship. From First Vespers, Jesus appears in majesty, seated on a royal throne over all the world: His kingdom is everlasting, and all kings shall serve and obey Him. He reigns and proclaims peace to the nations.

The Mass begins with an apocalyptic vision of this unique King, whose royalty is inseparably linked to His immolation for human salvation: "Worthy is the Lamb that was slain to receive power and riches and wisdom and strength and honor... to Him be glory and power forever".

In the Epistle (Col 1:12-20), St. Paul lists the titles that make Christ King of kings. As God, He is image of the invisible Father, firstborn of all creation, and Creator of all things visible and invisible; all things were created through Him and for Him, and in Him all hold together.

Then come the titles of His kingship as Man: He is Head of the Body, the Church; through Him all things are reconciled to God by the Blood of His Cross. Already our King by divine nature, He is also our King by Incarnation and by Passion, through which He redeemed the souls already His by creation.

Jesus is our King in the fullest sense: He created us, redeemed us, gives us life by grace, feeds us with His Body and Blood, governs us by love, and by love draws us to Himself. Spontaneously we cry with St. Paul: let us give thanks to the Father, who delivered us from darkness and transferred us into the kingdom of the Son of His love, in whom we have redemption and forgiveness of sins.

In today's Gospel (Jn 18:33-37), the most authoritative proclamation of Christ's Kingship comes from His own lips, in the solemn hour before His Passion. Pilate asks: "Art Thou the King of the Jews?" Jesus does not answer directly, for He is not king of a single people and His kingdom is not earthly.

But to the clearer question, "Art Thou a king then?" He replies without reserve: "Thou sayest that I am a king." He affirms His royalty before the highest civil authority, not in triumph, but bound in chains, before one about to condemn Him. Soon on Calvary, above His thorn-crowned head, the title appears: "Jesus of Nazareth, King of the Jews" (Jn 19:19).

He who fled when crowds wished to make Him king now proclaims Himself King amid Passion's humiliations, showing that His kingdom is not of this world and that no insult can obscure His royalty. Yet this also reveals that He wills His kingship to shine above all as a conquest purchased by His Blood.

To this divine King - so human in appearance, so loving and welcoming, stretching out His arms on the Cross and showing His pierced side as sign of love - we must go with all the momentum of our soul.

We do not wish to escape His rule; rather, we invoke and beg it, that He may have primacy in mind and heart, full dominion over our will, and that we with all we are may submit ourselves "to His most sweet empire."

### Colloquy

"You are King, O my God, eternal and immense King, of a kingdom not borrowed. When I recite in the Creed that Your kingdom will have no end, I tremble with joy. Yes, Your kingdom is eternal; I praise and bless You." (St. Teresa of Jesus)

"O divine King, most lovable Jesus, my Redeemer, my Savior, my Spouse, my Master and model, today I renew the total consecration of my being. I beg You, take absolute dominion over me.

"Be my Sovereign, my Lord, my Guide; direct and govern me entirely, that everything in me may be ordered to Your greater glory. Reign in my memory, intellect, will, and sensibility, which I desire to subject completely to You.

"Your kingdom is a kingdom of truth, love, justice, and peace.

"Establish Your kingdom of truth in my intellect, destroying error, deceit, and illusion; enlighten me with Your divine wisdom.

"Establish Your kingdom of love in my will; move, urge, and direct it always, so that I may no longer be moved by self-love or creatures, but only by Your Spirit. Make this weak and reluctant will strong, generous, and constant.

"Establish Your kingdom of justice in all my actions, that all may be holy works done with purity of intention and great fidelity to please You.

"Establish Your kingdom of peace not only in my soul, but also in my sensibility, that harmonized with the higher part it too may give You glory and never hinder my union with You." (Sr. Carmela of the Holy Spirit, OCD)
