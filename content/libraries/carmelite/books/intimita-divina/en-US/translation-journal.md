## Translation Decisions

- [Initial] Section headings: Presenza di Dio → Presence of God, Meditazione → Meditation, Colloquio → Colloquy (standard English Carmelite terminology)
- [Initial] Keep Latin phrases inline (e.g., alter Christus, Pater Noster), with English rendering following the original's pattern
- [Initial] Scripture references preserved as-is (book, chapter, verse)
- [Initial] Liturgical formulas: keep well-known Latin (Ave Maria, Magnificat) but render less recognizable ones in English
- [Initial] Saints' names in standard English form (e.g., S. Giovanni della Croce → St. John of the Cross)
- [Initial] Italian abbreviations: S. → St., N.S. → Our Lord, B.V. → Blessed Virgin, SS.ma → Most Holy
- [Giorni 004-010] Preserved classical ascetical vocabulary (e.g., "divine good pleasure," "motion of the Holy Spirit," "way of nothingness") to maintain Carmelite theological precision.
- [Giorni 004-010] In block prayer excerpts with dense rhetoric, retained long periodic sentence flow while smoothing only where needed for intelligible modern devotional English.
- [Giorni 011-014] Rendered San Giovanni della Croce's "nulla" language as "nothing/nothingness" consistently, preserving paradoxical ascent (death to self -> all in God).
- [Giorni 011-014] For Marian passages, preserved elevated devotional cadence while avoiding archaic over-inflation; retained `fiat`, `ecce`, `Magnificat` in Latin where source does.
- [Giorni 015-018] Liturgical Sunday subtitles translated directly (II/III domenica d'Avvento -> Second/Third Sunday of Advent) while preserving italics line placement under title.
