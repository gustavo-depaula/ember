## 333 — SPIRITUAL FATHERHOOD AND MOTHERHOOD

### Presence of God

O my God, unite me to You with the bond of intense love, and by the power of this union grant that I may beget souls for You.

### Meditation

God calls man to be His collaborator in the work most proper to Him and essentially belonging to Him alone: the communication of life—not only natural life, but supernatural life as well.

On the natural plane, which we may call the plane of creation, collaborators of God are fathers and mothers of families, to whom is entrusted the lofty mission of communicating life to new human beings, raising them and educating them for divine glory.

On the supernatural plane, the plane of Redemption, collaborators of God are all who, dedicating themselves to apostolate, have the even nobler and wider mission of communicating to men the life of grace, without which they are unhappy and diminished creatures, in a certain sense unable to attain eternal life.

In the encyclical *Menti Nostrae*, Pope Pius XII declares: «The priest is ... the organ of communication and growth of the life of the Mystical Body of Christ. By the law of celibacy the priest, rather than losing the gift and office of fatherhood, increases it to infinity, since if he does not beget offspring for this earthly and passing life, he begets them for that heavenly and eternal life.»

With due proportion, the same can be said of every apostle, because the ultimate end of all apostolate is precisely to beget souls to supernatural life.

«My little children, of whom I am in labor again until Christ be formed in you!» exclaims St. Paul to the Galatians (4:19).

Likewise every apostle, in regard to the souls for whom he sacrifices himself by giving all he has, rightly feels himself father and mother together—fatherhood and motherhood that are a reflection, or rather a participation, in God’s fatherhood.

In the natural order God has established that fecundity, source of life, be the fruit of union between two creatures; in the supernatural order too fecundity is fruit of union, but of an immeasurably higher and wholly spiritual union: that of the soul with God.

The more a creature is united to God by love, the more it shares in His inexhaustible fecundity, whose end is the communication of divine life to men.

Whoever has renounced natural fatherhood or motherhood to consecrate himself to God has not impoverished or suffocated life in sterility, but, by virtue of union with God, is raised to a fatherhood and motherhood far superior.

This is true not only for those working in external apostolate, but also for those devoted to pure contemplative life. St. Therese of the Child Jesus, though totally separated from the world, felt the sense of spiritual motherhood grow beyond measure in her heart and, in the solitude of Carmel, wrote: «To be your spouse, O Jesus ... and by my union with You, mother of souls!»

This was the fundamental aspiration of her spirit, the ideal that drew, sustained, and impelled her into a life of continual and painful immolation.

The humble cloistered nun is perfectly aware that she must give herself and sacrifice herself for souls, must place herself totally at their disposal, just as a loving mother gives herself wholly for her children.

One day, seeing a novice go unwillingly to work, the Saint, full of fervor, reproved her: «Is this the care one should have who has children to feed and is obliged to work to support them?»

The true apostle knows he has children to nourish, that he must spend his whole life for them, that he must sustain them by his sweat, prayers, labors, and above all by his love.

Precisely from love—the same love that unites him to God—he draws strength to sacrifice himself for them and also that spiritual fecundity by which he becomes God’s collaborator in communicating to them the life of grace.

The more love grows, the more union with God deepens, and from this there follows greater fecundity, greater power to communicate divine life to an immense number of souls. Who can say to how many souls the spiritual fatherhood and motherhood of the saints has extended?

There is no interior life, no authentic holiness, that is not crowned with the aureole of spiritual fatherhood and motherhood.

But just as, in the natural order, a mother does not bring forth children without pain, so in the spiritual order one does not become father or mother of souls without suffering.

Jesus begot us to divine life by dying on the Cross. From Him we learn that, if we wish to be associated with His redemptive work, we must fear neither persecution, nor mockery, nor scourges, nor thorns, nor nails, nor cross; we must be ready to give for souls all that we have and are, even to consuming our substance for them, even to nourishing them with our blood.

### Colloquy

«O Eternal Father, You cannot fail to know that poor sinners are your creatures, and by the supreme title of creation they belong to You.

«O Son, O blessed King, You cannot deny that these miserable ones are yours, made worthy by You through the unassailable title of Redemption. Hear me, O most obedient Son, hear me and show Yourself favorable to my prayers, for when I present myself thus to the Eternal Father, holding in my hand the pledge of your Blood and Passion, He cannot cast me away before hearing my requests.

«Help me, O eternal Holy Spirit! These souls, however abominable by the enormity of their sins, still belong to You, for You made them yours by admitting them to participation in your goodness.

«O Lord, I have no other consolation here than seeing my neighbors converted to You; and for this alone I bear patiently your absence. If You do not grant me this joy, what shall I do? Do not cast me away, most merciful Lord!

«You are already resolved and disposed to hear me, because turning your benign eyes toward me, I see my spiritual sons and daughters, my brothers and sisters, and all those whom day by day I gain for You by the desire to see them always faithful to You, clothed in your light.

«I commend to You, supreme and eternal Father, my most beloved children whom You have placed on my shoulders, and I beg You to visit them with your grace; make them live dead to the world with true and perfect light; bind them together with the sweet bond of charity. I beg You, eternal Father, let none be taken from my hands, and forgive all our iniquities. I offer and commend to You my most beloved children, because they are my soul» (St. Catherine of Siena).
