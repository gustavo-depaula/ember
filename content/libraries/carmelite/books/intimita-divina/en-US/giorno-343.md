## 343 — PASSIVE PURIFICATION

### Presence of God

My God, illumine my path, so that I may not lose my way amid the darkness of tribulations.

### Meditation

1 — Although by yourself, through generous practice of total self-denial and intense exercise of theological virtues, you can enter the night of the spirit, you cannot penetrate where it is densest unless God Himself places you there.

He alone can gather the darkness of this night over your head in such a way as truly to reduce you to nothing, annihilate you in all things, and bring you to the poverty and nakedness of spirit required for union.

Then, more than taking initiatives, your task is reduced to accepting with love, and enduring with patience and humility, all that God disposes for you.

To avoid the danger of resisting divine action, keep in mind that, in general, God purifies souls through ordinary circumstances of life.

In the life of every Christian, every apostle, every religious, there is always a measure of suffering sufficient for purification of spirit. They are sufferings chosen and disposed by God Himself in the most fitting way according to each soul’s needs.

But the problem is that very few know how to profit from them, because few know how to recognize in life’s sorrows the hand of God that wants to purify them.

Illnesses, bereavements, detachments, separations from loved ones, misunderstandings, struggles, difficulties sometimes coming precisely from those from whom one expected greater help and support, collapse of cherished works sustained at the price of great labor, abandonment by friends, material and spiritual solitude—these are among the sufferings found, more or less, in every human life, and therefore in yours.

Know, then, that all this is positively willed, or at least permitted, by God to purify you to the innermost fibers of your being.

In face of these things, never blame human malice; never stop to examine whether they are just or unjust. Look only at God’s blessed hand, offering you these bitter remedies to lead you to perfect health of spirit.

«It is very necessary,» says St. John of the Cross, «that the soul bear with great patience and constancy all tribulations and pains that God may send. Whether internal or external, spiritual or bodily, great or small, it must receive them all from the Lord’s hand, as directed to its good and medicine for its health.»

2 — Consider how much spirit of faith you need to accept from God’s hand all circumstances that afflict, humiliate, oppose, and mortify you.

At times it may be easier to bow before grave trials that come directly from the Lord—illness, bereavement, and the like—while perhaps you feel greater repugnance before lighter ones in which creatures are involved.

The immediate action of creatures, especially when their malice is involved, makes it harder for you to perceive the divine hand. Here you need greater spirit of faith to go beyond the human side of circumstances, beyond the defective way this or that person acts, and see, beyond all this, the dispositions of divine Providence, which wants to use those very creatures—and even their defects and errors—to file down your self-love and annihilate your pride.

In such cases, very useful is the counsel St. John of the Cross gave a religious: «Consider that all [who live around you] have the office, as indeed they have, of trying you—some by word, others by deed, others by thought—and that in this you must be subject to all, as an image is to the painter and sculptor who paint or gild it.»

A soul of faith, deeply convinced that God guides and governs all things and disposes everything for the good of those who love Him, does not rebel nor grow indignant over unkind or even unjust treatment it receives; it bows its head and accepts it humbly as the most fitting cure to free itself from defects and imperfections.

This is exactly the conduct you must keep if you want to profit from all the trials God places on your path.

Beware, then, in such cases, of posing as victim, of taking a tone of protest, complaint, or revenge. Whatever suffering may come to you from creatures, one alone is its true explanation: the Lord wants to purify you and begins doing so precisely through these exterior tribulations.

Be convinced that everything greatly helps your spiritual progress, because one does not reach union with God unless one first comes to be reduced to nothing, that is, established in profound humility.

### Colloquy

«Teach me, my God, to suffer in peace the afflictions You send me, so that my soul may come out of the crucible of trial more refined and purified, to contemplate You better, You who dwell within me. Then the troubles that now seem unbearable will become as nothing to me, and I will desire to suffer them again, if You so will, since even the greatest trials and persecutions are never without profit. But I am too imperfect and do not bear them as I should» (St. Teresa of Jesus).

«O greatness of my God! Whatever You permit in us by way of temptation and tribulation, all, all is ordered to our good; and if in temptation on earth we remembered nothing but your goodness, this alone would suffice us to overcome every temptation.

«O my loving and sweet Spouse, the Word, You who have all power in heaven and on earth, are the One who confounds all and puts all enemies to flight. For my part I am weak, very weak; I see nothing, being full of miseries and sins. But You, Word, with one little glance put all enemies to flight like straw in the wind; first, however, You let them fight with your servants in order to make these stronger.

«And the greater grace and light You want to give them, so that they may love and know You more, the more You test by fire and purify their hearts like gold, so that their virtues may appear as worthy and precious stones.

«In your strength, O Word, grant us strength to fight; and whoever wishes to fight manfully for your glory must descend lower in self-knowledge, while always lifting the heart to You, lest he be confounded» (St. Mary Magdalene de’ Pazzi).
