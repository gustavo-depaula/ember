## 354 — DIVINE HELP

### Presence of God

O Lord, who precede, accompany, and sustain me with your grace, grant that this grace in me may not be in vain.

### Meditation

1 — «If the soul seeks God, much more does its beloved Lord seek it. And if it sends Him loving desires,... He too sends it the fragrance of His ointments, by which He draws it and makes it run toward Him» (St. John of the Cross).

In its effort to reach union, the soul is never alone. God Himself comes to meet it, as it were extending His hand and drawing it to Himself through holy inspirations that enlighten the mind and interior touches that inflame the will.

These divine inspirations and touches are nothing else than the operation of the gifts of the Holy Spirit, through which God takes direction of the soul and works in it first to purify and dispose it for union, and then to unite it effectively to Himself through love.

It is most consoling to think that this wealth of divine helps belongs to the normal line of growth of grace-life and is therefore found also on the ordinary way of holiness.

This is the patrimony God has prepared for every soul that truly gives itself to Him.

We must conclude with St. John of the Cross that if so few truly reach perfect union, «this is not because God wills that few spirits be raised high» or because He is sparing of His helps, «but because He finds few vessels capable of so sublime a work,» that is, He finds few generous souls.

If, after many years of spiritual life, we still find ourselves far from union with God, we cannot really attribute this to scarcity of divine help. We must blame only our lack of generosity and fidelity to grace.

St. Teresa declares strongly: «True union can always be attained by striving to obtain it through subjecting one’s own will to God’s will.»

And though she acknowledges «this cannot be done except at the cost of great struggles,» she assures us: «there is no doubt one can arrive there, provided union with God’s will is true.»

2 — «To arrive at union it is not necessary that the Lord give us great consolations: it is enough what He has given us in having sent His Son to teach us the road» (St. Teresa of Jesus).

Jesus is enough for us: He who not only showed us the way of divine union, but also obtained for us the means to attain it.

Jesus washes and purifies our souls with His Blood; nourishes them with His Flesh; teaches them with His doctrine. Jesus every day and thousands of times each day renews on the altar His Sacrifice for us.

Jesus, glorious at the right hand of the Father, always intercedes for us, obtaining and distributing graces according to our needs.

Jesus sends us the Holy Spirit, His Spirit, that He may guide us on the way of holiness.

Jesus gives us His Mother, the Blessed Virgin Mary, that she may be our Mother too, our refuge and support in difficult hours.

What more could we ask? Should we judge these graces less precious because they belong to the common graces granted to every soul?

Oh, if we were truly convinced of the efficacy of these means of sanctification, we would seek nothing else. Instead of waiting for extraordinary favors to decide to give ourselves wholly to God, we would apply ourselves to correspond with great fidelity to the graces He offers each day with immense generosity, and thus we would surely reach the goal.

«Let us take courage,» exhorts Teresa of Avila, «and since we can enjoy a little heaven even in this life, let us beg the Lord to grant that we not remain deprived of it through our own fault.»

The heaven we can enjoy already here below is precisely the state of union with God, in which the soul, perfectly conformed to the divine will, enjoys deep peace even amid life’s inevitable sorrows, always resting abandoned in the arms of divine Providence.

To this happy state we can all arrive, provided we are firmly resolved to follow the road Jesus Himself showed us, saying: «If anyone loves Me, he will keep My word.... You are My friends, if you do the things that I command you» (Jn 14:23; 15:14).

This is the road He walked, willing no other food than to do His Father’s will and always doing what pleased Him.

Let us follow Jesus, entrust ourselves to His guidance, and He, who is the Way, the Truth, and the Life, will lead us to the longed-for union.

### Colloquy

«O Jesus, in those words by which You said that doing your Father’s will was your food, You showed us that your will was His and His will was yours; and having one will with Him, You declared Yourself equal to the Father and one with Him.

«But more: You taught us how we too can become, by grace and in a certain way, equal to God and one with Him. We can do so by doing His will, which must be the rule and pole toward which our will, like a magnetized needle, always points; and when we depart even slightly from the divine will, we lose this equality and this union.

«O Lord, deign to unite me wholly to You as a new bride. Remove from me my will and all my desires, so that I may desire and will nothing except what You will.

«Make my will so conformed and united to Yours that I can will nothing more of myself and care no longer whether to die or live, but only will what is your will.

«My God, when I have offered You my will in everything and entirely, You will return it to me; for when my will is no longer mine, but wholly given to You, then You will be pleased that I do it in all things, since it will no longer be mine, but wholly Yours» (cf. St. Mary Magdalene de’ Pazzi).

«Receive, O Lord, all my liberty. Receive my memory, intellect, and entire will. Whatever I have and possess You have given me. I restore all to You and surrender it wholly to the governance of your will.

«Give me only your love with your grace, and I am rich enough and ask nothing more» (St. Ignatius of Loyola).
