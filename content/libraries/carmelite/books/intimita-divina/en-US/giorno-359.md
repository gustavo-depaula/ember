## 359 — THE TRIUMPH OF LOVE

### Presence of God

O my God, may there be only love in me; may everything come from love and return to love.

### Meditation

The life of a soul in total union can be described as one continuous, intense exercise of love, by which it gives itself ceaselessly to God. All its powers, purified and harmonized, are employed in divine service: the intellect to know what gives God greater glory and to do it; the will to love what pleases Him; the memory to keep attentive to all that concerns His service.

Not only this: even the sensitive part, even the body and senses, join this concert of love. The soul can truly say that all its "capital" - spiritual and sensible powers - is invested in holy love. The body is treated according to God; the internal and external senses are directed to Him; the passions themselves are ordered to Him.

It rejoices only in God, hopes only in God, fears only to offend God, and sorrows only according to God. Its desires and cares tend toward Him alone.

The flame of divine wisdom has taken such possession of this soul, purified it so deeply, and enamored it of God alone, that its whole being vibrates only for Him. Even first movements become movements of love: intellect, will, memory, affections, senses, desires, hopes, and joys rise immediately to God.

Love becomes the atmosphere in which the soul breathes and lives. The hard sacrifices and struggles of earlier days - when love meant stripping away what is not God - now seem little compared with the good attained. Truly, everything is little when it is a matter of acquiring pure and true love of God.

This total gift of self is pure love: pure because no self-seeking remains, no attachment to creatures; pure because it passes through all circumstances without stopping in anything created and rises directly to God. In every event, duty, and action, the soul seeks only to love and serve Him as most pleases Him.

At this point the soul no longer needs to apply itself separately to one virtue and then another; having acquired them in maturity, its exercise in temporal matters and spiritual matters alike is simply to love. Its law is no longer an external pressure but the great love within, which impels it in all things toward the divine good pleasure.

This does not abolish law, duty, or obedience. Rather, true love fulfills and perfects every law, carrying the soul to highest perfection. Of this pure and total love St. John of the Cross writes that a little of pure love is more precious before God and more useful to the Church than all other works together.

There is no activity more intense or sublime than one that gathers all human energies and fixes them in God. It is the eternal occupation of angels and saints - and, in a foretaste, the occupation already begun on earth by souls arrived at total union.

### Colloquy

"As the bride places her love, thought, and work in none but her bridegroom, so grant, O Lord, that my soul may have no affection, no thought, no concern, no desire that is not directed to You.

"Grant that I know how to do nothing but love You, divine Bridegroom. Seeing that You esteem nothing but love, help me to apply myself entirely to pure love of You, for I desire to serve You perfectly.

"Do not permit me to seek my own interest, follow my tastes, or occupy myself with things foreign to You. Let my whole soul be engaged in loving You. Let all my actions be acts of love; let everything in me move by love and in love. In acting, I want to do all things with love; in suffering, I want to suffer all with love.

"Grant that I may say with the spouse of the Canticle: 'All fruits, old and new, I have kept for You' - that is, for love of You I desire what is hard and toilsome for myself, and what is sweet I desire for You.

"O Jesus, my heart seeks neither riches nor glory, not even the glory of heaven. What I ask is love. I know now only one thing: to love You, O Jesus. I love Your Church, my Mother, and I remember that the smallest movement of pure love is more useful to her than all other works together. Grant, Lord, that pure love may truly be found in my heart.

"O Jesus, let love penetrate and surround me; let Your merciful love renew and purify me at every instant and leave no trace of sin within me." (cf. St. John of the Cross; St. Therese of the Child Jesus)
