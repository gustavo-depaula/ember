## 130 — LOVE OF THE CROSS

### Presence of God

I come again to You, my crucified God, with the desire to penetrate more deeply into the mystery of the Cross.

### Meditation

1 — The cross is suffering seen in supernatural light as an instrument of salvation and sanctification, therefore as an instrument of love. Seen in this
light, the cross is certainly lovable: it is the great means
of our sanctification. Our union with God
is not realizable except by means of suffering. St. John of the Cross showed how the soul must be
purified, scraped to the depths, in order to attain even in
this life union with God. A total work of mortification is necessary to tear out all our attachments, because in us there are many resistances that prevent us from being totally moved by God; and it is not possible that all
this be done without suffering. But active suffering is not enough; that is, those mortifications and
penances we can procure on our own initiative are not sufficient;
rather passive suffering is needed above all, that is,
the Lord Himself must make us suffer, and not only in body,
but also in spirit, precisely because we are so rusty,
so full of miseries that our total purification is not possible unless God Himself intervenes with His
action. To place us in passive suffering is therefore one of the greatest works of His mercy, one of the
greatest proofs of His love: when God acts thus
in a soul, it is a sign He wants to bring it to high perfection.
And it is precisely in these passive purifying sufferings
that the concept of cross is realized in a particular way. In the Living Flame of Love (stanza II), St. John of the Cross asks
why so few souls arrive at the fullness of spiritual life.
And he answers: the reason
is not that God wishes to reserve this state to some
privileged soul, but that He finds few souls disposed to accept a profound work of purification,
therefore He ceases to purify them, and thus souls condemn themselves to mediocrity; they no longer walk, they do not go forward. It is impossible to unite oneself to God without these spiritual sufferings, without this weight of God. Only through suffering and interior desolation are the soul's faculties enlarged, and the soul becomes capable of embracing God Himself.


2 — "O souls who desire to walk secure and consoled in the things of the spirit! If you knew how much
you must suffer to obtain this security!" (J.C.
F. 228). Yet suffering is necessary not only for
the soul's good, but also so that the soul may glorify God and show Him its love; in fact, it is not a matter
of attaining perfection to enjoy it - for
the perfect soul never thinks of itself - but to dedicate itself totally to God's glory. In this very sense,
at the summit of Mount Perfection is written: "only the honor and glory of God dwell here." As Jesus'
Cross was the great means by which He gave
the Father that glory sinful man had denied Him,
so must our cross be; through suffering
we must atone for and repair our own faults
and those of others, to give God all the glory
that is due to Him.

Moreover, as Jesus' Cross was the supreme
proof of His love for us, so our cross is the
most beautiful proof of our love for Him. Precisely in the fact
that the Son of God came to die for us on the cross,
His infinite love for us is revealed; and thus,
in the fact that for Him we embrace suffering,
the reality of our love for God is shown. The cross is therefore an instrument
and work of love: it is instrument and work of God's
love toward us and of our love toward God. The more God
sanctifies us, the more He shows us His love and gives us the means
to glorify Him; but He sanctifies us precisely through the
cross, the great Cross of Jesus to which we must unite
our little cross. The more the Lord, through
suffering, gives us a way to share in Christ's Passion,
the more He sanctifies us and therefore loves us. Sufferings are, then, in this sense too, a proof
of His love for us.
If all this were understood, how one would love the cross!

### Colloquy

"O Lord, the way of the cross is the one You reserve
for Your beloved: the more You love them, the more You load them with trials...
since You admit to Your intimacy only souls in love
with sufferings. If You asked me which I prefer
of these two things: to remain on earth until the end of the
world amid every kind of trial, and then go up
to heaven with a little more glory; or to go immediately
to heaven without suffering anything, but with one degree of glory
less, I would gladly accept all the torments of the world,
in order to have that little more glory which allows me
to understand You better. For I see, O Lord, that one
who understands You better, loves You better and praises You better.

"No, I never want to care about sufferings that end,
when Your service is at stake, when it is a matter of honoring You,
who have suffered so much for us.

"If I want to know, my God, how You deal with
those who sincerely ask You to accomplish in them Your will, I must ask it of Your glorious Son who in the garden of olives offered You this prayer... You fulfilled in Him Your will by flooding Him with sufferings,
insults, persecutions, and leaving Him at last to die on
a tree of the cross. This is what You gave to Him
whom You loved most. As long as we are here below, these are Your
gifts. You give them according to the love You bear us:
You give more to those You love more, and less to those You love less. Another
rule is the courage You see in us and the love we bear You, because if we love You much, we shall also be capable of
suffering much, but little if we love You little" (T.G. Cam. 18, vv. 2; Vi. 37, 2; Cam. 3, 6-7).

O my God, increase then my love, enlarge my
poor heart and make it capable of suffering much for You.
Yes, willingly I want to accept suffering to attest to You
in deeds the reality of my love.
