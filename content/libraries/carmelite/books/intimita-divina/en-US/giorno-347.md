## 347 — THE GROWTH OF LOVE

### Presence of God

My God, who from eternity have preceded me with your infinite love, increase my love for You.

### Meditation

1 — «Who can prevent God from doing what He wills in a resigned soul, stripped and annihilated?» (St. John of the Cross).

This saying helps you understand that God ardently desires to work in your soul to lead you to holiness and union with Him, provided you place yourself in His hands stripped of every attachment, annihilated in self-love, totally docile to His action, totally pliable and movable.

Through purifying trials the Lord helps you empty yourself, detach from creatures, and sink into true humility; but at the same time He helps you grow in love, the great force that must unite you to Him.

All the work God does in your soul aims at making you progress in this virtue. Exterior and interior trials, humiliations, impotence, dryness, struggles, tempests, all have in the divine plan the purpose of extinguishing in you the false fires of self-love, pride, earthly affections, and every disordered passion, so that one fire alone may blaze in you ever more intensely and vigorously: the fire of charity.

The more the Lord purifies you, the more your heart is freed from dross and becomes able to concentrate all its affections on Him.

Walk, then, in this direction, accepting purification in view of fuller love and directing your whole spiritual life to the exercise of love.

What you suffer, suffer for love: suffer willingly, without rebelling, without complaining. Then, as your soul is humbled, stripped, and mortified, it will also be clothed in charity.

The trials God sends you aim not only at making your heart purer, but also at enlarging it in charity. They seek to carve in you a greater capacity for love—not sensitive love, but strong love of will that tends toward God with pure benevolence, independent of personal comfort, seeking only His glory and good pleasure.

2 — «[God] wonderfully instructs the soul in perfection of love» (St. John of the Cross).

First, He teaches it to love independently of all joy, even depriving it of that joy—so legitimate and spontaneous—which comes from awareness of loving.

A soul not yet fully purified could cling to this joy; therefore God withdraws it. Dense darkness takes away awareness of loving; dry aridity quenches all sweetness and delight, and the soul is constrained to proceed by pure act of will.

Instead of delighting in its love, now no longer felt, the soul remains deeply afflicted by the tormenting doubt of not knowing how to love; to fight this doubt it has no other means than to apply itself with all its strength to works of love, that is, to embrace generously whatever pleases God, whatever sacrifice He asks.

Precisely thus its love develops, becoming purer and stronger: purer, because no personal comfort is mixed in; stronger, because it is driven to more generous acts.

In this state the soul adheres to God by the sole act of will, wherein the substance of love consists. It loves God solely because He is the supreme Good, infinitely lovable.

It wants Him alone and serves Him alone by fulfilling all His divine wills, without turning back on itself, without seeking joys or spiritual tastes. The soul is no longer concerned with enjoying its love, no longer anxious to receive; its only concern is to give, to give itself, to please God.

From this you see how aridity and darkness, far from suffocating love, make it grow marvelously, provided the soul is willing to walk seeking only God’s pleasure and totally forgetting itself.

«Learn to love as God wills to be loved, and leave your own ways,» St. John of the Cross tells you: learn to love God with pure and strong act of will, without concern for feeling, consolation, or joy of heart.

Perhaps your way of loving still depends too much on feeling; then thank the Lord if He makes you walk in darkness and aridity. In this way He will help you free yourself from such weakness.

### Colloquy

«O Lord of my soul and my only Good, why do You not will that as soon as a soul resolves to love You and does all it can to detach itself from everything in order to serve You better, it should at once have the consolation of seeing itself in possession of true perfect love? But I spoke badly. I should complain thus: why do we not will it? Yes, the fault is ours if we do not reach so great an honor at once.

«If we succeeded in perfectly attaining your true love, we would possess every other good besides. But, my God, we are so miserly and so slow in giving ourselves to You that we never truly put ourselves in the dispositions required to receive it. This is also because, since this love is so precious, You require that it be enjoyed only at great price.

«There is nothing on earth that can buy such a treasure. Yet we would quickly become possessors of it if only we did our utmost not to attach ourselves to things of the world, placing all our care and conversation in heavenly things.

«Instead, Lord, it happens that we believe we give You everything, while in reality we give You only the income and fruits, keeping for ourselves the capital and property. A fine way to seek your love! And then we want it at once, and in full measure.

«O Lord, if You do not give us this treasure all at once, it is because we too do not give ourselves wholly to You. Deign at least to give it to us drop by drop, even if for this we had to undergo all the hardships of the world.

«No, my God, love does not consist in tears, nor in those consolations and tendernesses that one so often desires and in which one so delights. It consists rather in serving You with justice, with strength of soul, and with humility. Without this, it would seem to be always receiving and never suffering.

«Do not permit, Lord, that something so precious as your love be given to persons who serve You only from desire for spiritual consolations» (St. Teresa of Jesus).
