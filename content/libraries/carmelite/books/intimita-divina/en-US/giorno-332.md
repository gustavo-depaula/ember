## 332 — APOSTOLIC MATURITY

### Presence of God

May your love, O God, mature my soul and make it capable of giving itself fully in the service of souls.

### Meditation

1 — Once the soul has reached that union with God in which the flame of zeal bursts forth spontaneously, the apostle can now give himself freely to apostolate.

It is a fact that, having reached this point, the soul cannot and must not exempt itself from self-gift: whether dedicated to contemplation or action, whether living in the solitude of the cloister or amid the turbulence of the world, its life now consists in unceasing self-donation: giving itself to God for the benefit of neighbor; giving itself to neighbor for the glory of God.

To stifle this tendency would be to regress and impoverish one’s spiritual life. This is precisely the period in which the soul must enrich itself through the gift of self lived in an intense interior or exterior apostolate.

Yet the saints teach us that prudence is still necessary, and one must not cease to be vigilant, for having received the interior grace of apostolate does not mean being confirmed in grace.

St. Teresa says explicitly: «I have known some very advanced souls who, after reaching this point, were still won over by the devil through subtle snares and stratagems» (*Interior Castle* V, 4, 6). Shortly before she had said: «How many must there be who, called by God to apostolate, honored like Judas with His very communications... end by losing themselves through their own fault!»

Spontaneously one recalls the cry full of humility and distrust of self that rose from the heart of Paul, the Apostle caught up to the third heaven: «Lest perhaps, when I have preached to others, I myself should become reprobate» (1 Cor 9:27).

As long as we are on this earth, we have reason to fear. Sadly, we always retain the possibility of not corresponding to grace, of separating ourselves—even in little things—from God’s will, and therein lies our ruin, so that little by little we regress.

«Christian souls whom the Lord has led this far,» exclaims Teresa of Jesus, «I beg you for love of Him never to neglect yourselves, and to flee occasions!» She adds: «I think [the devil], to succeed in causing an apostle to deviate, must mobilize all hell, because he knows that to ruin one such soul is to ruin a multitude.»

On the contrary, if the apostle remains faithful to the grace of apostolate, he not only becomes an instrument for the salvation of many, but he immensely enriches his interior life.

2 — In this phase, three cautions are especially necessary.

First, one must always jealously guard humility, defending oneself from the flattering voices of praise and success by setting against them the picture of one’s own miseries, as well as one’s mistakes and failures in apostolate.

If Lucifer, a pure spirit, fell through pride, it is not impossible that an apostle, being human and burdened by matter, should also fall through pride.

Second, delicate care is needed to maintain intimate contact with God, because just as iron glows and shines only in contact with fire, so the apostle spreads divine light and warmth only if he remains united to the One who is their sole source.

Finally, it is of utmost importance to persevere in perfect detachment from one’s own judgment and one’s own will. On this point St. John of the Cross warns «that among the many stratagems used by the devil to deceive spiritual persons, the most common is to delude them under appearances of good and not of evil, because he already knows they will scarcely touch manifest evil» (*Ascent* 10).

Therefore, not to fall into the snare, the Saint urges us never to undertake «an action, however good and full of charity» it may seem, without the approval of obedience.

This counsel applies not only to religious but to all who work in apostolate, for all must remain subject to ecclesiastical authority.

Even if a work, initiative, or method of apostolate gave the best results, if ecclesiastical authority, for any reason, did not approve it, the apostle should be ready to renounce it at once, without criticism, without complaint, without maneuvers to impose his own point of view.

Protected by deep humility, sustained by sincere detachment and intimate union with God, the apostle can tread the roads of the world without any fear for his spiritual life.

### Colloquy

«O Lord, the souls that were closest to You—like your most holy Mother and your glorious Apostles—were those that suffered most and labored most for You, allowing themselves no rest.

«My God, how neglectful of personal rest the soul must be when it lives deeply united to You! How little it must care for honor! How far it must be from desiring to be esteemed in anything! Yes, if it converses often with You, as it should, it ends by forgetting itself entirely, spending all its concern in seeking to please You more and in knowing in what things and by what ways it can show You the love it bears You.

«O Lord, You teach me that this is the end of prayer, and this is what union with You tends to: to produce works and works.

«If I fix my eyes on You, divine Crucified One, everything will become easy for me. If You have shown me your love by such great works and such dreadful torments, why should I be content with words alone?

«Grant that I may know how to give myself to You as your slave, so that, marked with your brand—the Cross—You may sell me as a slave of the whole world! This is what it means to be truly spiritual.

«United to You, divine Strong One, I shall share in your strength, as the saints shared in it, in order to labor with great zeal for your glory, and to suffer and die for You, to win for You many souls» (cf. St. Teresa of Jesus).
