## 334 — MARTHA AND MARY

### Presence of God

O Lord, may I love You with Mary’s heart and at the same time serve You with Martha’s dedication.

### Meditation

In the soul that has given itself seriously to God, two attractions are found: attraction to solitary and silent prayer in order to plunge into God, listen to His voice, penetrate His mysteries, and above all unite itself more intimately to Him; and at the same time attraction to apostolate, to active and generous sacrifice for the salvation of brethren.

To recollect itself in God, the soul «would like to flee men and greatly envies those who live and have lived in deserts. At the same time it would like to throw itself into the midst of the world so that even one soul might praise God more.»

This is the double movement of charity which, being at once love of God and love of neighbor, impels both toward union with God and toward service of neighbor. If one of these movements were lacking, charity would not be complete.

The development of interior life brings with it this twofold attraction, which is both sign and means of progress, and at the same time a torment for the soul that does not yet know how to balance the two tendencies well.

To which of the two should it give prevalence—action or contemplation?

In practice, the problem must be resolved on the basis of the demands of the duties of one’s state, the dispositions of obedience, and the concrete circumstances permitted by God.

The desire for contemplation that distracts or turns the soul away from fulfillment of duty would not be in conformity with God’s will; and God has every right to ask even renunciation of this desire in order to send us to the service of neighbor.

To Magdalene, happy to have finally found Him again and wishing to remain at His feet, the risen Jesus commanded: «Go to my brethren» (Jn 20:17); and she, docile, went to announce the Resurrection.

On the other hand, the opposite is also true. To the Apostles returning from preaching, Jesus said: «Come apart into a desert place» (Mk 6:31), thus inviting them to suspend apostolic activity to restore their spirit in silence and prayer, alone with Him.

The right path, preventing deviation in either direction, is always that of duty, God’s will, and interior inspiration, examined by one who has authority to guide souls.

2 — To settle the conflict between attraction to apostolate and attraction to union with God, a deeper solution is needed, one that cannot come from outside but only from within: greater progress in the ways of the spirit, to attain a higher degree of love.

Love is the only root from which both action and contemplation spring; it is the only force that, nourishing both activities at once, finally succeeds in blending them in full harmony so that they bear the best fruits.

Born from the single stock of an already mature love, action and contemplation are fused only in perfect love.

Perfect charity makes the soul recollected in contemplation at the Lord’s feet supremely active and fruitful for the good of brethren: «A little of this pure love [solitary love that flowers in intimate contact with God] brings more benefit to the Church than all other works together.... Therefore,» declares St. John of the Cross, «if a soul had a little of this solitary love, great harm would be done to it and to the Church if one wanted to occupy it in exterior or active things, however important.»

In the regions of pure love, that is, perfect charity, contemplation and apostolate are identified, complement each other, and require each other.

When the contemplative soul has reached this degree, it is supremely apostolic, and the greatest activity it can exercise for brethren is precisely its solitary prayer nourished by love, sacrifice, and immolation.

On the other hand, perfect charity also makes the soul engaged in apostolic works at the same time more united with God than ever, more contemplative than ever.

Love has so fixed it in God that even during work its interior gaze is always turned to Him: to nourish itself with His divine presence, to reflect His infinite perfections in conduct, and to regulate all things according to the expression of His good pleasure.

Thus, at the summit of spiritual life, action and contemplation blend in unity and perfect harmony.

«Believe me,» writes St. Teresa of Jesus, «to receive the Lord, to have Him always with us, to treat Him well and offer Him food, Martha and Mary must agree.... One gives food to the Lord when one does what one can to win many souls, so that, being saved, they may praise Him forever» (*Interior Castle* VII, 4, 12).

From this we understand how all great contemplatives have been great apostles, and great apostles have been great contemplatives.

### Colloquy

«O Lord, the desire to listen to your divine word, the need for silence, is sometimes so strong that I would wish to know nothing else but to remain at your feet, like Magdalene, to penetrate ever more deeply the mystery of love You came to reveal.

«But You teach me that if the soul never departs from You, it can remain always absorbed in contemplation, even when outwardly it carries out Martha’s office.

«In this way, O Lord, I understand and want to exercise apostolate: I can radiate You, I can give You to souls, only if I never move away from You, divine source. Help me, sweet Master, to stay very close to You, to place myself in communion with your Soul, to make all your affections mine, and then to go like You to fulfill the Father’s will.

«What power the apostle exercises over souls who never detaches himself from the source of living waters! Grant, O Lord, that I may be one of these. Then I may let the wave overflow and spread around without danger that my soul be found empty, because it remains in continual communication with You, the Infinite.

«My God, deign to invade all my powers; let everything in me be divine and bear your seal, so that I may be another Christ working for your glory.

«O Lord, I want to labor greatly for your glory! I want to give myself entirely to You, let myself be permeated by your divine sap; be You the life of my life, the soul of my soul, and grant that I may remain always vigilant and conscious under your divine action» (cf. E.T.).
