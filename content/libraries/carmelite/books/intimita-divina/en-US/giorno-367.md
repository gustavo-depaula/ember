## 367 — PRESENTATION OF MARY IN THE TEMPLE
*November 21*

### Presence of God

O Mary, present to the Lord my offering and my life.

### Meditation

Although Sacred Scripture does not recount the presentation of the Blessed Virgin in the Temple, this event is solidly rooted in ancient Christian tradition, and the Church has officially recognized it by instituting a specific Marian feast.

Mary as a little child, leaving home and parents at a tender age to live in God's house, speaks to us of detachment, separation from the world, and complete dedication to the service of God; she speaks of virginal consecration to the Most High.

After her, countless virgin souls would present themselves to God, but no offering would be so pure, so total, so pleasing as Mary's.

From the first moments of her existence she heard the divine call: "Hear, O daughter, and see, and incline your ear; forget your people and your father's house" (Ps 44:11). The Most High was enamored of her beauty and wanted her wholly for Himself. Mary responded with readiness and fullness.

So must souls called by God to the altar, to religious life, or to virginal consecration in the world respond. They too must separate from the world, leave family and friends. Sometimes this is not material separation, but it must always be spiritual separation in affection.

The heart must detach itself and stand apart, for the Lord's elect can no longer belong to the world: "They are not of the world" (Jn 17:14). To live in the world without being of the world is not easy, yet absolutely necessary for fidelity to the call.

Many consecrated souls fail in this response because they remain attached to worldly maxims, vanities, curiosities, and softnesses. This can happen both to those living in the world and to those in cloister, because the world can enter everywhere and invade hearts not fully detached.

Mary teaches total consecration. She gives herself wholly to God, without reserve, forever. Such were the dispositions of the holy Child, and she lived them with a fullness and coherence that confound our poverty.

Not for an instant did she retract her gift. God could do in her whatever He wished, without finding the least resistance. Her life was filled with painful circumstances: Joseph's trial, the difficult journey, Bethlehem's poverty, flight into Egypt, hidden hardships of Nazareth, hostility against Jesus, betrayal, ingratitude, condemnation, Calvary, and Crucifixion.

Yet in Mary there is no resentment, protest, or complaint. She gave herself totally to God and let Him exercise all His rights as Sovereign. She had offered herself without reserve; now, as the offering was consumed, she repeated only: "Fiat! Ancilla Domini!"

How different our own consecrated life often is. How easily we take back what we once gave: the heart, when we allow it to be reoccupied by human attachments; the will, when we resist obediences that mortify us; the gift of self, when we complain and defend our rights.

Yet the one true right of a soul consecrated to God is to let itself be wholly used and consumed for His glory.

Let us ask Mary presented in the Temple to take our poor offering into her maternal hands, heal and complete it with her own, hide it within her pure and generous offering, so that purified and renewed it may become pleasing to God.

### Colloquy

"O beloved of God, most lovable child Mary, would that today I could offer You the first years of my life and dedicate myself wholly to Your service, as You presented Yourself in the Temple and consecrated Yourself entirely to the glory and love of God.

"But I am no longer in time, having lost many years serving the world and my whims, almost forgetting You and God. Woe to that time when I did not love You! Better late than never to begin.

"See, O Mary, today I present myself to You and offer myself wholly to Your service for whatever little or much remains for me to live on earth; and like You I renounce creatures and dedicate myself entirely to the Love of my Creator.

"I consecrate to you, O Queen, my mind, that it may always think of your love; my tongue, that it may praise you; my heart, that it may love you. Accept, O most holy Virgin, this offering of a poor sinner.

"Accept it, I beg you, for the consolation your heart felt when in the Temple you gave yourself to God. And if I come late to your service, grant that I may make up for lost time by redoubling my homage and love.

"O Mother of mercy, help my weakness by your powerful intercession. Obtain from Jesus perseverance and strength to be faithful to you until death, and grant that by serving you always in this life I may come to praise you forever in Paradise." (St. Alphonsus)
