**Confession is not therapy.** The day's image of Christ giving Peter the keys is theologically exact: confession is the *Tribunal of Mercy*, a *court* in which Christ Himself sits as Judge — but the Judge here is *the One who paid the debt being judged*. The verdict is mercy because the price has already been paid; the judicial form remains, because what is at stake is real.

Aquinas walks the doctrine carefully. Penance is a sacrament of *the dead* — that is, it restores grace where mortal sin has killed it. Christ instituted it on Easter evening, breathing the Holy Spirit on the Apostles and saying *whose sins you forgive, they are forgiven; whose sins you retain, they are retained* (John 20:23). The *power of the keys*, given first to Peter (Mt 16:19) and then to all the apostles (Mt 18:18), is the authority to bind and loose sins **in the Person of Christ**. Every priest you confess to wields the same power; he is, in that moment, the very channel through which Christ's mercy reaches you.

Three modern misunderstandings the catechism is asking you to put down:

  * ***I can confess directly to God.*** True — God forgives mortal sin in any soul that turns to Him with perfect contrition (sorrow from love, not just fear). But the Church teaches: *one who is even thus reconciled is bound to confess sacramentally as soon as possible*. The sacrament is not redundant with private prayer; it is the *normative* way grace enters back into the soul. The peace that comes from absolution audibly spoken is *part of the grace*. Use it.

  * ***I'm not Catholic enough to go.*** This sentence describes someone who *needs to go*. The sacrament does not require you to be holy first; it makes you holy. The priest is not there to be impressed by you; he is there to apply the mercy of Christ. Most priests have heard worse sins than yours, with calm. Walk in.

  * ***I can't remember when I last went.*** Then go this week. The Church requires confession of mortal sins at least once a year (Session 054); the saints encouraged frequent confession (monthly, even biweekly) for venial sins as well. **Frequent confession is medicine, not penance.** It cleanses the soul before sin has time to take root.

Christ's hand in Rubens's painting (today's image) is *raised, open, by an open window*. The window stands for the door of the confessional. **Walk in.** What is on the other side is not a verdict you fear. It is a Father waiting.
