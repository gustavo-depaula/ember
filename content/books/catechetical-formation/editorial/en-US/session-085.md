*Thy kingdom come* is the petition modern Catholics are most likely to dilute into a vagueness. We hear it as *let things be a bit better, please.* Aquinas refuses the dilution. The petition asks for **a real kingdom — a sovereign reign with a real King, real laws, real subjects, real territory.**

He distinguishes the kingdom in three concentric senses:

  * **The kingdom of grace within the soul.** The first place the kingdom comes is *here*, in the baptized soul. Christ rules where He is *let* to rule. *Thy kingdom come* is therefore the prayer for the surrender of the inner provinces still in revolt — the area of life I have not yet handed over. *Today*, what province is unsurrendered?

  * **The kingdom of the Church on earth.** Aquinas calls the Church *the kingdom in time*. Not yet glorified, mixed with weeds among the wheat, often scandalous, real. To pray *Thy kingdom come* is to pray for the Church visible — for vocations, for holy bishops, for the conversion of the world to the gospel. To pray it well is to *roll up sleeves* for the Church one is praying for.

  * **The kingdom of glory in heaven.** Aquinas says the petition is, finally, *a request for the consummation of all things*: that Christ return, that the dead rise, that what we now believe be then seen. The early Christians prayed *Maranatha* (*Come, Lord*) — and meant it. They were not afraid of the end of the world. They were waiting for the **bridegroom**.

A modern reader will want to ask: *but doesn't a "kingdom" sound authoritarian, anti-democratic, premodern?* Aquinas's answer is exact: this is not the kingdom of any earthly ruler. *His yoke is sweet and His burden light.* The Christian asks for the rule of Christ because every other rule — of the algorithm, the market, the self, the strongman — is *worse for human flourishing*. It is freedom, finally, to be ruled by Truth in person. The Pantocrator on the dome — the Christ enthroned of today's image — is not a tyrant. He is the One whose hand was nailed for us.

To pray *Thy kingdom come* is therefore to renounce, today, the kingdom of self, even by inches. **Find one decision in your day where the kingdom of self would say one thing and the kingdom of Christ another. Choose the second.** That is what the petition is asking you to do.
