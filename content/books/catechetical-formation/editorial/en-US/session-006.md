The doctrine of the Trinity is not solvable. It is *believable*, but it is not *demonstrable* by reason alone, the way the existence of God is demonstrable. The Trinity is **revealed** — disclosed by Christ Himself, especially at the moments when the curtain of His own person opens.

Three of those moments anchor the doctrine in Scripture, and Aquinas walks them carefully:

  * **The Baptism of Christ** (Matthew 3:16–17). The Son stands in the Jordan, the Spirit descends as a dove, the Father's voice speaks from heaven. Three Persons, in the same scene, distinguishable, equally present. **The doctrine is on the page.**
  * **The Last Supper discourse** (John 14–17). Christ speaks of the Father from whom He proceeds, of the Spirit who will be sent, of His own oneness with both. *I and the Father are one* — and yet *I will pray the Father, and He shall give you another Paraclete*. The grammar of the Trinity is here in the most personal language Christ ever used.
  * **The Great Commission** (Matthew 28:19). *Baptize them in the name* — singular, *name* — *of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit*. One name, three Persons. The Church's first act of catechesis already presupposes Trinitarian doctrine.

The catechism above is honest about the limit. *We do not understand, nor can we understand, how the three divine Persons, though really distinct, are one God: it is a mystery.* Aquinas is unflinching: this is not philosophical hand-waving but the doctrine being *honest with itself*. **A god small enough to be fully understood would not be God.**

Rublev's *Trinity* (today's image) is the icon of this. Three figures at one table, leaning toward each other, *resting in each other*, with an empty fourth seat at the front of the table — the seat *prepared for the viewer*. The Christian claim is that you were made *for* that seat. The communion of love that has always been three-in-one is not a closed circle; it is a circle that turns its face toward you and **invites you in**.

Today, when you make the Sign of the Cross, *make it slowly*. *In the name of the Father* — touch the forehead, name where you have come from. *And of the Son* — touch the heart, name what holds you up. *And of the Holy Spirit* — touch each shoulder, name what carries you outward. *Amen.* You belong to this Three. They have a place ready for you.
