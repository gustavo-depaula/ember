The doctrine of *ex opere operato* (Session 060) tells you the sacrament is real *regardless of your disposition*. Today's session names the rest of the truth: **what you bring still shapes what you receive**. The sacrament gives the grace it signifies; whether that grace *fructifies* in your soul depends on whether you have prepared yourself to receive it.

The Catholic tradition has always distinguished two kinds of preparation:

  * **The *sine qua non*** — the disposition without which the sacrament cannot bear its proper fruit. For Confession: contrition. For Communion: the state of grace. For Marriage: free, intentional, full consent of the parties. These are not optional. To receive a sacrament *while placing an obstacle to its grace* is sacrilege.

  * **Devout preparation** — the further disposition that allows the grace to flow more deeply. For Communion: a few minutes of recollection beforehand; the Eucharistic fast; a deliberate intention; thanksgiving afterward. None of these are *required* for the sacrament's validity, but each *enlarges* its fruit.

Aquinas: *the more disposed the recipient, the more abundant the grace*. The sacrament's power is fixed; what varies is **how much the soul drinks**.

Modern Catholic life makes the second kind of preparation hard. The Mass is approached late, the Communion line is moved through quickly, the post-Communion thanksgiving is drowned by the closing announcement. Three small recoveries the saints have always counseled:

  1. **Arrive early.** Five minutes of silence before Mass changes the entire interior shape of what follows. Use the prayers in the missal, or simply sit and address Christ directly — *Lord, prepare me to receive you well today.*
  2. **Make a fresh act of faith and contrition before Communion.** *Lord, I believe You are here. I am sorry for what I have done. I receive You.* Three sentences. Sufficient.
  3. **Stay after.** Even three minutes of silent thanksgiving after the dismissal does what no homily can. Christ is sacramentally present *within you* for at least the time it takes the species to dissolve. **Speak to Him while He is closer than your breath.**

Leonardo's Last Supper (today's image) is the model. Twelve men around the table, the Lord at the center. *Communion is not a private moment with a private God; it is a meal you cannot eat unless you arrive ready and willing.* The catechism is asking you to come awake.
