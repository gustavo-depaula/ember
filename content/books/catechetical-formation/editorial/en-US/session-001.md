If you are reading this, **you have begun**.

The catechism of St. Pius X is one of the most respected catechetical instruments the Church has produced — a 1912 simplification of Trent for the working faithful, in the form of 433 questions and answers, doctrinally complete and pedagogically clear. The Catechetical Instructions of St. Thomas Aquinas above are among the last sermons he ever preached — given in Naples during Lent 1273 to ordinary working people, less than a year before his death. The Catechism of Trent (when it appears as *Going Deeper*) is the formal pastoral instrument the Council of Trent gave parish priests in 1566 — the same book the Curé of Ars relied on. **You are sitting down, day by day, with three of the great catechetical voices of the Latin Church.**

But you are also being given something else. The opening question is profoundly personal: *Who created us?* — *God created us.* The catechism does not begin with arguments for God's existence; it begins with *whose you are*. **You exist because Someone wanted you to.** Aquinas, in the teaching above, walks the four effects of *faith* — the lens by which all 90 days of this formation will be seen. Faith unites the soul to God as a marriage. Faith begins eternal life now, because eternal life is to know God. Faith teaches us how to live when reason cannot reach far enough. And faith overcomes temptation, because what we believe about God reorders what we want.

Read the next ninety days knowing two things.

  1. **You will not master this material; it will master you.** That is the entire point. The Christian life begins not when you decide what is true, but when you let what is true decide you.

  2. **Take it one session at a time.** Aquinas above is dense; the Pius X questions can feel like a list; the Trent material at the end of some sessions is the deeper treasure for those who want to dig. None of it is meant to be skimmed. Read one session in the morning. Sit with one sentence afterward. Let it work.

The day's image is Michelangelo's first creation — *light arriving where there had been none, the Spirit hovering*. The same Word that spoke then is, at this moment, holding you in being. Today's catechism is asking you to *notice that this is the case*. Tomorrow it will ask you to *live as if it were*.

Welcome.
