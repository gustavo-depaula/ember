# Protestation of the Author

Should anyone judge that any proposition written in this book is too far-reaching, I declare that I have stated and understood it in the sense of the holy Catholic Church and of sound theology. For example, in calling Mary *Mediatrix*, I have intended to call her such only as Mediatrix of Grace, in distinction from Jesus Christ, who is the first and only Mediator of Justice. In calling Mary *Omnipotent* (as she has likewise been called by St. John Damascene, St. Peter Damian, St. Bonaventure, Cosmas of Jerusalem, and others), I have intended to name her such inasmuch as she, being the Mother of God, obtains from him by her prayers whatever she asks for the benefit of her devoted servants; for neither this nor any other divine attribute can ever belong to a creature, such as Mary is. In calling Mary our *Hope*, I have intended to call her such because all graces (as St. Bernard holds) pass through her hands.
