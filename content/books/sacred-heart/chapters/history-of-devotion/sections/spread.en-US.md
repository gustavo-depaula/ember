The devotion spread slowly at first. Margaret Mary faced significant opposition from within her own community, but found a crucial ally in **St. Claude de la Colombière**, a Jesuit priest who became her spiritual director in 1675. He recognized the authenticity of her revelations and consecrated himself to the Sacred Heart.

Through the Jesuit order, the devotion gradually spread across Europe. Pope Clement XIII approved the feast in 1765 for Poland and the Archconfraternity of the Sacred Heart in Rome. Pope Pius IX extended it to the universal Church in 1856.

The defining moment came in 1899, when **Pope Leo XIII** consecrated the entire human race to the Sacred Heart, calling it *"the great act"* of his pontificate. In 1928, Pius XI issued the encyclical *Miserentissimus Redemptor*, establishing the Act of Reparation. And in 1956, Pius XII published *Haurietis Aquas*, providing the definitive theological foundation for the devotion.
