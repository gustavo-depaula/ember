The most significant revelation came in June 1675, during the octave of Corpus Christi. Our Lord appeared to St. Margaret Mary and, showing her His Heart, said:

> Behold this Heart which has so loved men that it has spared nothing, even to exhausting and consuming Itself, in order to testify Its love. And in return, I receive from the greater part only ingratitude, by their irreverence and sacrilege, and by the coldness and contempt they have for Me in this Sacrament of Love.

He asked that a feast of reparation be established on the Friday after the octave of Corpus Christi — what we now celebrate as the **Solemnity of the Sacred Heart**. He also asked for the practice of Holy Hours on Thursday nights, in memory of His agony in Gethsemane, and for Communion on First Fridays in reparation for sins.

These requests form the core of Sacred Heart devotion as practiced to this day.
