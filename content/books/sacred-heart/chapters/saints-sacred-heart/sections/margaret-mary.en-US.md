Margaret Mary Alacoque was born in 1647 in Burgundy, France. From childhood she experienced a deep attraction to prayer and the Blessed Sacrament. After a prolonged illness in her youth, she made a vow to the Blessed Virgin to enter religious life, and was healed.

She entered the Visitation monastery at Paray-le-Monial in 1671. Her mystical experiences began almost immediately, but it was the series of great revelations between 1673 and 1675 that would define her mission: to make known the love of the Sacred Heart and establish the devotion throughout the Church.

She faced years of opposition, doubt, and humiliation from her superiors and fellow religious. Only the support of her confessor, St. Claude de la Colombière, sustained her through these trials. She was eventually vindicated, and the devotion she championed spread throughout the world.

She died on October 17, 1690, whispering: *"I need nothing but God, and to lose myself in the Heart of Jesus."* She was canonized by Pope Benedict XV in 1920.
