The teaching of *Haurietis Aquas* on the three loves united in Christ's Heart deserves special attention, for it reveals the extraordinary depth of the Sacred Heart devotion.

**The first love is divine**: the infinite love of the Word, the Second Person of the Trinity, for the Father and for all creation. This is the love that brought the universe into existence and that sustains it at every moment. It is the same love by which the Son eternally proceeds from the Father in the life of the Trinity. When we honor the Sacred Heart, we honor this divine love made present in human flesh.

**The second love is the spiritual love of Christ's human soul**: a created love, but one infused with grace beyond all measure. From the first instant of the Incarnation, Jesus' human soul possessed the fullness of charity — a love for God and for every human person that surpassed that of all the angels and saints combined. This is the love that moved Him to pray for His executioners, to forgive the repentant thief, to weep over Jerusalem.

**The third love is the sensitive love of Christ's human heart**: the natural, spontaneous emotional life that belongs to a true human nature. Jesus experienced real human affections — tenderness for His Mother, friendship for Lazarus, anguish in Gethsemane, joy at the faith of the centurion. These were not pretenses or mere appearances. They were the authentic movements of a human heart.

These three loves are not separate. They are united in the one Person of Christ and symbolized by His one Heart. This is why the Sacred Heart devotion is so rich: it reaches from the depths of the Trinity to the most intimate movements of human feeling. In one devotion, we encounter the whole mystery of Christ's love.
