## 38 — JESUS MEDIATOR
*January 3*

**Presence of God.** — *Grant me, O Lord Jesus, to penetrate the grand mystery hidden in Your Incarnation.*

### Meditation

**1** — The Christmas feasts have concentrated our gaze on Jesus, revealing to us somewhat of the great "mystery hidden
for ages in God" (Eph. 3, 9), a mystery that we now want to consider in synthesis so as better to admire its "unsearchable riches."

Between God and man an infinite distance, an unsurmountable abyss, a rupture of every relation of friendship: such is the tremendous
consequence of sin. But between God and man behold the sweet
Child of Bethlehem; suddenly and completely, the situation changes aspect: the distance is bridged and over the abyss

rises a marvelous bridge that unites earth to heaven,
that re-establishes relations of intimacy between God and men and women. This bridge is Jesus who, "the one Mediator between God and men,
joins earth to heaven in a truly admirable way"
(Myst. Corp.). In His function as Mediator He stands
truly "in the middle" between Divinity and humanity: His
mediation has every title to be perfectly pleasing
to God, because He Himself is true God, and has every requisite
to fully satisfy the debts of sinful humanity,
because He is also true man and as such represents
the whole human race.

In Jesus, Divinity, all Divinity that He possesses
as Word, meets humanity that He possesses
as man; not only meets it, but embraces it, indeed,
even more, unites with it in one person, the Person of the Incarnate Word: Jesus Christ, our Lord. And in Him and through Him all men and women are readmitted to friendship
with the heavenly Father; in Him all can find again the way to
arrive at union with the Trinity.

The Eternal Father deigned to reveal this marvelous mystery to St. Catherine of Siena: "I desire that you look
at the bridge I built for you in my only-begotten Son and
that you observe its greatness, which goes from earth to heaven,
since in Him the greatness of Divinity is united to the earth
of your humanity. This was necessary to remake the way that
was broken and to permit arriving, through the bitterness
of this world, at eternal life" (Dialogue).

**2** — "In Him [Jesus] it pleased (the Father) that all fullness should dwell and through Him all things should be reconciled to Himself,
having made peace through the Blood of His cross,
whether things on earth or those in heaven" (Col. 1, 19 and 20).
The work of Jesus Mediator is fulfilled on Calvary, where
He pours out all His Blood, price of our ransom;
but this work begins in Bethlehem, where the Word accomplishes,
so to speak, that ineffable "giant's stride" that makes Him

descend from heaven to earth, that from true God makes Him also
true man.

The terrible abyss that sin had produced between God
and men and women is already bridged by the Child who stretches out His
arms to us from the manger. Everything that sin had ruined and destroyed is thus, by God's will, saved and "re-established in Christ" (Eph. 1, 10). Oh, how tenderness is joined
to immense admiration, how spontaneous arises the
need to praise and adore when one contemplates the Child Jesus
in this light!

That grace which Adam had received directly
from God, we now receive only through Jesus Mediator;
everything supernatural that reaches our souls always comes from His hands. And if we want to reach God,
we have no other means than to cling to
Jesus, than to pass through Him: our Mediator, our
bridge, our way. Jesus said it: "I am the way"
(Jn. 14, 6); "I am the door. Whoever enters through me will be
saved" (Jn. 10, 9). Here is the only condition, the only way of
salvation, of holiness.

### Colloquy

"O eternal Deity, O high Eternal Father, in You I see
inestimable love... I see the love that constrained You to open
the eye of Your pity upon us poor miserable ones, wherefore
You sent the Word, Your only-begotten Son,
Word, incarnate utterance, veiled in our poor flesh, clothed in
our mortality. And You, Jesus Christ, our reconciler,
restorer, and Redeemer, made Yourself mediator, Word,
love; and from the great war man had with God You made
great peace; You punished our iniquities and Adam's disobedience on Your body, being obedient unto the shameful death of the cross!...

"Whichever way I turn, I find ineffable love; and I cannot excuse myself from not loving, because You
alone, God and man, are He who loved me without being loved by me; for I was not, and You made me. All
that I want to love, I find in You, except sin...
If I want to love God, I have Your ineffable Deity;
if I want to love man, You are man...;
if I want to love the Lord, You have paid the price of Your Blood, drawing me from
the slavery of sin. You are Lord, Father, and our Brother

by Your benignity
and boundless charity... You, Deity,
supreme wisdom, and I, ignorant miserable creature;
You, supreme and eternal goodness. I, death, and You are life; I, darkness, and You light; I,
folly, and You wisdom; You infinite, and I finite; I sick, and You physician; I fragile sinner who never loved You;
You purest beauty, and I vilest creature. You by
ineffable love drew me from You, and draw us all to You by
grace and not by debt, if we wish to let ourselves be drawn to You,
that is, if our will does not rebel against Yours" (St. Catherine of Siena).

"O Christ God, sweet lover of men, I invoke You,
I pray You, I beseech You, so that through You I may walk, to You I may come,
in You I may rest, You who are way, truth, and life; without whom no one
comes to the Father" (St. Augustine).
