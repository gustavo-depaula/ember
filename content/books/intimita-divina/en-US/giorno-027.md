## 27 — THE WORD WAS MADE FLESH
*December 23*

**Presence of God.** — *O Jesus, eternal Word, allow me to penetrate more deeply into the sublime mystery of Your Incarnation, so that my heart may be ever more seized by Your infinite love.*

### Meditation

**1** — The Word is the second Person of the Most Holy Trinity.
In the bosom of the one divine nature there are three Persons, three
subsisting terms. We too are "subsisting"; "subsistence" is what allows us to say "I" and to attribute
to this I the various actions we do. In God, in the
divine nature, there are three Terms that can say "I"
with regard to divine operations, operations common to all
three, because proceeding from one nature possessed by
all three divine Persons. The Word possesses the
divine nature as the Father and the Holy Spirit do; He possesses their same divine properties of infinity,
eternity, omnipotence, omniscience, etc.; all divine greatness and perfections are
of the Word, as of the other two Persons. The Word performs

the same divine actions as the Father and the Holy Spirit:
intimate actions of knowledge and love that constitute
the very life of the Most Holy Trinity; exterior actions of creation,
of preservation of creatures, etc. The Word is God.
St. John the Evangelist, beginning his Gospel, before
speaking of Jesus' temporal birth, presents to us the

eternal generation of the Word existing "ab aeterno" in
the bosom of the Father, altogether equal to the Father, yet distinct from Him.
"In the beginning was the Word and the Word was with God and the
Word was God" (1, 1). The Word is the one word of the Father
that expresses the whole Father; the Father, giving to Him all His
essence and divine nature, also communicates to Him all divine
action. Thus the Word is efficient cause and principle of every natural and supernatural life: "All things
were made through Him, and without Him was made
nothing," but the Word, splendor of the Father, is not only
life; He is also light, light that reveals to men and women God's greatnesses
and mysteries: "in Him was life, and the life was the light
of men" (Jn. 1, 3-4). Natural life, life of grace,
light, knowledge of God: everything comes to us from the Word who is
God, together with the Father and with the Holy Spirit.

**2** — "The Word was made flesh." Being God, the Word
is eternal and immutable, therefore He necessarily remains
always what He was: *manet quod erat*! But nothing prevents Him, together with the Father and with the Holy Spirit,
from creating in time a human nature that, instead of having a
limited and weak I like ours, remains totally under
the government of His divine I. Thus He did;
the human nature assumed by Him is the same as
ours, but, instead of belonging to a human I, it belongs to the
divine I, to the subsisting Person of the Word, and therefore the operations
and passions of this human nature are also of the Word. The
Word, after the Incarnation, has a twofold nature: the
one divine nature, which He possesses in common with the Father and with
the Holy Spirit; the human nature, which is of the same kind
and has the same properties as ours.

The Word remained what He was and yet, while being
God, did not disdain to assume human nature fallen
through sin, "but emptied Himself, taking
the nature of a servant and becoming like man; and in all
His outward bearing recognized as a man" (Phil. 2, 7). This is what the immense charity of God has worked,
He who, full of mercy for His poor creature
sunk in the abyss of sin, did not hesitate to decree the redemptive Incarnation of His Only-Begotten. Thus the
eternal Word comes to us exactly like the good shepherd
who leaves everything to descend into the valley in search of the lost sheep. This is the fruit of the great love with
which God has loved us!

### Colloquy

"O divine Word, You are the adored Eagle whom I love
and who draws me. You are He who, plunging toward the land
of exile, willed to suffer and die to draw souls

all the way to the center of the eternal Hearth of the blessed Trinity. You
are He who, rising again toward inaccessible Light,
which from now on will be Your dwelling, remain still in the valley of tears, hidden under the appearance of a white Host...
O Jesus, allow me, in the excess of my gratitude,
to tell You that Your love goes so far as madness! How
can it be that before this madness my heart does not rush
toward You? How could my confidence have limits?

"Ah, for You, I know it, even the saints committed follies,
did great things because they were eagles... Jesus, I
am too small to do great things... My folly
consists in begging the eagles, my sisters, to obtain for me to
fly toward the Sun of Love with the same wings of the divine
Eagle. As long as You will, O my Beloved, I shall keep my
eyes fixed on You; I want to be fascinated by Your divine gaze,
I want to become prey of Your love" (T.B.G.
Lt. 175).

Yes, my Jesus, I ardently desire to become prey
of Your love; I desire that it invade me, purify
and transform me, so that You may delight in fully carrying out
in me Your merciful redemptive, sanctifying work.

You come to us to heal us of our misery, to transform us from children of sin into children of God. But how
many times, alas, Your infinite charity is forced to halt before ungrateful hearts that close their doors to You.
"He came to His own and His own did not receive Him" (cf. Jn. 1, 11). Those do not receive You who do not
believe in Your love, those who distrust Your infinite mercy, those who have no confidence in You. And then,
that excessive charity which brought You down from heaven to us,
which found no unseemliness in You, eternal Word,
assuming our poor human nature, finds an obstacle to
its course in that very creature, creature of Yours, whom You
came to benefit.

O eternal Word, my Savior, may I never place
an obstacle to Your work; may my soul open itself wholly to the invasion of Your infinite merciful love, so that You
may totally accomplish in me Your office as Savior, as Sanctifier.
