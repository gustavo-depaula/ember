# Station VIII — Jesus Speaks to the Women Who Weep

V). Adoramus te, Christe, et benedicimus tibi.

R). Quia per sanctam crucem tuam redemisti mundum.

Consider how the women, seeing Jesus Christ so distressed and leaving a trail of blood along the way, wept out of compassion; but Jesus said to them: Do not weep for me, but weep for your children. — My sorrowing Jesus, I weep for the offenses I have committed against you, for the punishments I have deserved, but even more for the displeasure I have given you who have loved me so much. It is not so much the fear of hell as your love that makes me weep for my sins. My Jesus, I love you more than myself. I repent of having offended you. Do not permit me to offend you again. Grant that I may always love you, and then do with me as you will.

Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be. Dear Jesus, etc.
