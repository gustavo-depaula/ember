## Translation Decisions

1. **"SS. Sacramento"** -- Rendered as "Blessed Sacrament" throughout, the standard English Catholic term.
2. **"Venerabile"** -- When referring to the exposed Eucharist, rendered as "Blessed Sacrament" rather than the archaic "Venerable" to match standard English Catholic usage.
3. **"custodia"** -- Rendered as "tabernacle" in most contexts (the container on the altar holding the Eucharist).
4. **"divozione/divoto"** -- Modernized spelling in English: "devotion/devout" (not "devozione" -- Liguori uses the older Italian spelling).
5. **Footnotes** -- All editor footnotes ([^N] markers and definitions) are dropped per instructions. Only Liguori's own text is retained.
6. **Saints' names** -- Anglicized to standard English forms: S. Bernardo -> St. Bernard, S. Teresa -> St. Teresa, S. Tommaso -> St. Thomas, etc.
7. **Latin quotations** -- Kept inline in italics exactly as in the original.
8. **Scripture references** -- Kept in original format (e.g., Io. VII, 37; Is. XII, 3).
9. **Tone** -- Preserving Liguori's passionate, intimate, devotional voice. His long sentences and exclamatory style are characteristic and retained.
10. **"palazzo"** -- Context-dependent: "palace" when referring to a king's palace, but the metaphor is preserved.
11. **Orazione di S. Efrem / S. Germano etc.** -- "Prayer of St. Ephrem" / "Prayer of St. Germanus" etc.
12. **"Preghiera a Maria Santissima che se le deve replicare..."** -- Rendered as a subtitle in the same style as the original.
13. **"La comunione spirituale"** -- Instruction line kept as "Spiritual Communion." (referring the reader to the act of spiritual communion).
14. **"In fine farà la comunione spirituale"** -- "At the end, make a Spiritual Communion. Then make the Visit to the great Mother Mary before one of her images."
15. **"ciborio"** -- Rendered as "tabernacle" (synonymous with "custodia" in this context).
16. **"ostensorio"** -- Rendered as "monstrance" (the vessel used for Eucharistic exposition).
17. **"Idiota"** -- Kept as "the Idiota" (a medieval author, Raymond Jordan, known by this pen name).
18. **"Discepolo"** -- Rendered as "the Disciple" (referring to the medieval spiritual writer).
19. **"Abbate Cellense"** -- "Abbot of Celles" (Thomas of Citeaux/Celles).
20. **"paciera"** -- Rendered as "peacemaker" (mediatrix of peace between God and men).
21. **"B. Amedeo"** -- "Bl. Amadeus" (Amadeus of Lausanne).
22. **Saints encountered across all 31 visits**: St. Bernard, St. Teresa, St. Thomas, St. Philip Neri, St. Peter of Alcantara, St. Mary Magdalene de Pazzi, St. Catherine of Siena, St. Ephrem, St. Germanus, St. Ildephonsus, St. Anselm, St. Peter Damian, St. Athanasius, St. Andrew of Crete, St. John Damascene, St. Bonaventure, St. Bernardine of Siena, St. Augustine, St. Bridget, St. Thomas of Villanova, St. Paulinus, St. Ignatius the Martyr, St. Lawrence Justinian, St. Francis Xavier, St. Aloysius Gonzaga, St. John Francis Regis, St. Francis of Assisi, St. Wenceslaus.
23. **Verse/poetry lines** -- Liguori's short devotional verses at the end of some visits are preserved as separate lines matching the original's paragraph structure.
