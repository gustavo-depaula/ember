## Translation Decisions

1. **Recurring prayer ending**: Each station ends with a personal prayer that follows a pattern but varies slightly. I preserve the variation rather than standardizing.
2. **V). / R).** versicle/response format kept as-is (Latin).
3. **"Pater, Ave, Gloria"** rendered as "Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be" since these are standard English prayer references.
4. **"Caro Gesù, etc."** rendered as "Dear Jesus, etc." — the abbreviation mirrors the original's convention of shortening the repeated hymn after Station I.
5. **Station XIII hymn variant**: The hymn changes from "a morire / ten vai" to "già morto / sei tu" — translated accordingly ("already dead / you are" vs. "you go to die").
6. **Addresses to Jesus**: Liguori uses many affectionate vocatives (Amato, Adorato, Dolcissimo, etc.) — each translated distinctly to preserve variety: "Beloved," "Adored," "Sweetest," etc.
7. **"Considera"** — rendered as "Consider" (direct, devotional imperative matching the original).
