# Station II — Jesus Takes Up His Cross

V). Adoramus te, Christe, et benedicimus tibi.

R). Quia per sanctam crucem tuam redemisti mundum.

Consider how Jesus Christ, walking on this journey with the cross upon his shoulders, was thinking of you, and for you offered to God the death he was about to suffer. — Most lovable Jesus, I embrace all the tribulations you have destined for me until death. I beg you, by the merit of the pain you suffered in carrying your cross, to give me the help to carry mine with perfect patience and resignation. I love you, Jesus my love. I repent of having offended you. Do not permit me to separate myself from you again. Grant that I may always love you, and then do with me as you will.

Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be. Dear Jesus, etc.
