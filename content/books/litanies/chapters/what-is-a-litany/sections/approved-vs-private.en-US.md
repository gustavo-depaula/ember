The Church distinguishes between litanies approved for public liturgical use and those intended for private devotion.

**The six approved litanies** — approved by the Holy See for public recitation — are:

1. **Litany of All Saints** — the most ancient, used at the Easter Vigil, ordinations, and religious professions
2. **Litany of Our Lady of Loreto** — approved in 1587 by Pope Sixtus V
3. **Litany of the Holy Name of Jesus** — approved in 1886 by Pope Leo XIII
4. **Litany of the Sacred Heart** — approved in 1899 by Pope Leo XIII
5. **Litany of St. Joseph** — approved in 1909 by Pope St. Pius X, updated by Pope Francis in 2021
6. **Litany of the Precious Blood** — approved in 1960 by Pope John XXIII

These six litanies may be used in public worship and carry a special authority within the Church's prayer life.

**Private litanies** — such as the Litany of Humility, the Litany of the Holy Spirit, and the Litany of the Blessed Sacrament — are not approved for public liturgical celebrations, but they are perfectly lawful and meritorious for private prayer, whether alone or in groups outside of liturgical functions. Many of these have been beloved by saints and spiritual directors for centuries, and they remain powerful instruments for growth in holiness.

All the litanies in this collection — both approved and devotional — are treasures of the Church's tradition, each illuminating a different facet of the mystery of God's love and the communion of saints.
