St. Louis Marie Grignion de Montfort (1673–1716) was a French Catholic priest and missionary who became one of the Church's greatest writers on Marian devotion. Born in Montfort-sur-Meu, Brittany, he was ordained in 1700 and spent his short life preaching popular missions throughout western France.

His masterwork, *True Devotion to the Blessed Virgin Mary*, was not published until 1843 — over a century after his death — when the manuscript was discovered hidden in a chest. It has since become one of the most influential spiritual works in Catholic history, inspiring popes from Pius IX to John Paul II, who took his papal motto *Totus Tuus* ("Totally Yours") directly from Montfort's consecration formula.

Montfort was canonized in 1947 by Pope Pius XII. His feast day is April 28.
