# Article 27

In the twenty-seventh article, the question is raised whether it can be conjectured without danger that after the common resurrection, the moon will shine as much as the sun now shines, while the sun will shine sevenfold more than it does now and the bodies of the good will shine seven times more than the sun. See *De 30 Articulis,* a. 15, in which he responds that there is no danger in doing so and *De 43 Articulis,* a. 37, in which he gives a more complete answer.  

I see no danger if the assertion is devoid of that which could be attributed to presumption.
