# Article 34

In the thirty-fourth article, the question is raised whether the body of Christ in the sacrament is not moved when the host is moved. *(See also, *S.Th.,* III, q. 76 a. 6.)*  

It is true to say that his body is not moved as bodies are moved *per se* or accidentally in a place because it is not in the place in which the sacrament is in the way in which bodies are in a place *per se* or accidentally. His body has another relationship to the sacramental dimensions than a body has to its own dimensions (according to which it is moved *per se* or accidentally). But in the way in which it belongs to the body of Christ to be in a place by reason of the sacramental dimensions, it belongs to it to be moved in place.
