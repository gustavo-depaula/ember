# Chapter 40 — Accusations Levelled Against Religious, on the Grounds That They Recommend Themselves and the Orders to Which They Belong

AS we have before said, religious are calumniated for performing actions which, in themselves are perfectly indifferent. We will now, therefore, enquire into the grounds on which these false charges are based. Religious are accused:

Of commending themselves and their order, and of procuring from others letters of recommendation.
Of refuting, instead of bearing with, the detractions of their enemies.
Of going to law.
Of causing their persecutors to be punished.
Of desiring to please men.
Of rejoicing in the good which God wonderfully does through them.
Of frequenting the courts of kings and princes.
