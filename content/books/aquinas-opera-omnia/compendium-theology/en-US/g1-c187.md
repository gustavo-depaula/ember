# Chapter 187 — That that perfect state may be called original justice

This wonderfully ordered state of man is called original justice. By it man himself was submissive to what was higher than him, and to him all lower creatures were subjected, as is indicated in Genesis 1:26: *Let him have dominion over the fishes of the sea and the fowls of the air*. And among man’s component parts, the lower were subservient to the higher without any conflict. This state was granted to man, not as to a private individual, but as to the first principle of human nature, so that through him it was to be handed down to his descendants together with human nature.  

Moreover, since every one ought to have a habitation befitting his condition, man thus harmoniously constituted was placed in a most temperate and delightful region, so that all inconvenience, not only of internal annoyance, but also of external surroundings, might be far removed from him.
