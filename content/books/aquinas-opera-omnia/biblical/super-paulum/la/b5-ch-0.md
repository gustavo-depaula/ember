# Caput 0 — All of these cross-currents were to show up in his biblical exegesis. He was neither an Alexandrian nor an Antiochene, perhaps because he was both. No one has successfully categorized his approach to the Bible. Advocates of allegory claim him as their own and defenders of strictly literal interpretation praise him for asserting the sufficiency of the letter. A noted Oxford historian admires the revolutionary quality of his exegetical principles and a prominent Jesuit theologian finds them simply traditional.

THE GREETING

THE NARRATIVE: meant to fortify the Ephesians in good:

I. By reason of Christ and his blessings

a. The blessings offered to all men

b. The unique gifts given the Apostles

c. The blessings bestowed on the Ephesians

1. Paul’s affection for them has grown

2. He prays that they be blessed even more

d. Christ, the form and exemplar of all blessings:

1. Especially in his Resurrection and Exaltation

2. In which he has power over the universe and the Church

By reason of the Ephesians own past experience

a. Their sinful state and unmerited justification and salvation in Christ

b. Their Gentile condition and Christ’s goodness

1. In making them, with the Jews, one New Man in himself

2. Now both share in God’s blessings

III.By reason of Paul’s solicitude for them and the special graces he has received

a. The dignity of his mission because of

1. His knowledge of divine mysteries

2. His part in carrying these mysteries into effect and their astonishing outcome

b. Paul’s trials and his prayer that they remain strong in faith and love

c. If they do, a knowledge of divine mysteries will be granted to them also

d. A Thanksgiving to God for giving us Christ and the Church

AN EXHORTATION: urging them on to higher goods, especially unity

I. An admonition to preserve ecclesial unity

a. The admonition itself

b. The form of this ecclesial unity:

1. That which is common to every member

2. What is particular to each individual:

a) The source of these different gifts

b) The fruit of this diversity, Christ’s body

How they are to remain in Christian unity

a. General norms for everyone:

1. Christ’s teaching is contrary to pagan sins

2. The conditions for knowing Christ’s doctrine

b. General precepts applicable to everyone:

1. Avoid spiritual sins and follow Christ’s exampel

2. Avoid carnal sins; Paul speaks of their punishment and warns against them

3. Exhortation to newness of life and thanksgiving

c. Particular precepts for different groups

1. For husbands and wives, the mystery of Christ and the Church

2. For children and parents

3. For slaves and their masters

THE CONCLUSION: indicating by whose power these precepts will be fulfilled:

I. Trust in God, he will provide spiritual armour for the struggles Christians must undergo

II. A final prayer, farewells and doxology
