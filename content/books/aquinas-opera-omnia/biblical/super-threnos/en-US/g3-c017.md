# Lecture 17

^1:17^ Phe. Zion has spread forth her hands, there is none to comfort her: the Lord has commanded against Jacob, his enemies are round about him: Jerusalem is as a woman polluted with menstruating among them.  

> **17**. Here he weeps over the second captivity, which took place under Zedekiah, when all the people were taken captive; and concerning this, he does three things.

First, he weeps over the siege;  

second, he weeps over the captivity, at ***the Lord is just*** (Lam 1:18);  

third, he weeps over the wasting away by starvation, at ***I called for my friends*** (Lam 1:19).  

Concerning the first, he does three things.  

First, he presents the defection of her friends who should have come to her rescue and prevented the siege: ***Zion has spread forth her hands***, as if calling for help from the Egyptians. ***We have given our hand to Egypt, and to the Assyrians*** (Lam 5:6). Or from God. *When you stretch forth your hands, I will turn away my eyes* (Isa 1:15).  

Second, he presents the arrival of his enemies: ***the Lord commanded***, in dispensing justice; ***round about him***, to besiege; ***his enemies***, the Chaldeans. *I will give him a charge against the people of my wrath, to take away the spoils* (Isa 10:6).  

Third, the vileness of the besieged: ***Jerusalem is as a woman polluted***—no one goes to her to defend her. *All that seek her shall not fail* (Jer 2:24).
