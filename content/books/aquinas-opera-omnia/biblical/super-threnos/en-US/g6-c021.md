# Lecture 21

^4:21^ Sin. Rejoice, and be glad, O daughter of Edom, who dwells in the land of Hus: to thee also shall the cup come, you shall be made drunk, and naked.  

> **87**. Here he threatens those insulting them, and especially the Edomites.

First, he threatens them;  

second, he consoles the Jews: ***your iniquity is accomplished*** (Lam 4:22).  

Concerning the first, he does two things.  

First, he excludes joy, saying mockingly: ***rejoice***. *Tell it not in Geth, publish it not in the streets of Ascalon* (2 Sam 1:20). ***Daughter of Edom***, people of the Edomites; ***in the land of Hus***.  

On the contrary: *I have given mount Seir to Esau, for a possession* (Deut 2:5).  

Furthermore. Hus was the firstborn of Nahor (Gen 22:21).  

But it should be said that it is not spoken about the man called Hus, but about the land called Hus, which is also called Ausitis, as the Gloss says. *(Jerome, *In Hieremiam prophetam libri vi* 5.308 (CSEL 59.308; PL 24:679 [705]).)* *All the kings of the land of Ausitis* (Jer 25:20), which is on the border of the Edomites and the Arabs.  

Second, he threatens punishment: ***to you also shall the cup come***, the wrath of God; ***you shall be made drunk***, you will be filled with misery; ***and naked***, stripped of all your goods. *Behold they whose judgment was not to drink of the cup, shall certainly drink* (Jer 49:12).
