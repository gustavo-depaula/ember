# Lecture 13

^1:13^ Mem. From above he has sent fire into my bones, and has chastised me: he has spread a net for my feet, he has turned me back: he has made me forsaken, wasted with sorrow all the day long.  

> **13**. Here he presents the severity of the discipline, inasmuch as he educated them like a schoolmaster by harsh punishments.

And first, he presents the severity in correcting sins committed: ***from above***, that is, in his high council; ***he has sent fire*** of affliction, or literal fire, by which the city and camp were burned; ***into my bones***, that is, in her strong places and fortifications; and so by painful lashes ***he has chastised me***. *And vexation alone shall make you understand what you hear* (Isa 28:19).  

Second, he shows severity in caring for the future: ***he has spread a net***, that is, an obstacle by which he diverted them from sin*. I will hedge up your way with thorns* (Hos 2:6).  

Third, he shows severity in suppressing the benefits of consolation: ***he has made me forsaken***, as if to say, “He gave no consolation after the lashes,” as a schoolmaster usually does with children. *God has brought upon me great mourning* (Bar 4:9).
