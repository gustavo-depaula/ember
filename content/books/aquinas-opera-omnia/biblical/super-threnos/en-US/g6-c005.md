# Lecture 5

^4:5^ He. They that were fed delicately have died in the streets; they that were brought up in scarlet have embraced the dung.  

> **71**. Here he presents the hunger of those who ate luxuriously.

And first, he presents the affliction of their hunger;  

second, he presents a comparison, at ***and the iniquity of the daughter of my people is made greater than the sin of Sodom*** (Lam 4:6).  

Their hunger is shown by two signs: death, ***they have died***; and the consumption of coarse foods: ***they that were brought up in scarlet***, when the food was withdrawn from their mouths, ***have embraced the dung***, that is, they threw themselves on coarse food. Or literally (2 Kgs 6:24–29). Or as a simile: *a soul that is hungry shall take even bitter for sweet* (Prov 27:7).
