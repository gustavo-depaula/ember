# Lecture 19

^1:19^ Coph. I called for my friends, but they deceived me: my priests and my ancients wasted away from hunger in the city: because they sought their food to revive their souls.  

> **19**. Here he laments the wasting away of those who have starved to death during the siege.

And first, he places the disappointment caused by friends, ***my friends***, the Egyptians, because they have not helped, as I had hoped. *For Egypt shall help in vain, and to no purpose: therefore have I cried concerning this* (Isa 30:7).  

Second, he sets forth the wasting away occasioned by this: ***priests***, for whom it seems more unbecoming, ***wasted away from hunger***. *Their nobles have perished with famine* (Isa 5:13).  

Third, he gives the reason for this wasting away: ***because they sought*** simply to be revived, not to be filled, and they did not find it. Or he assigns here the reason for their fault: because they searched for themselves and not for the people. *Woe to the shepherds of Israel, that fed themselves* (Ezek 34:2).
