# Lecture 21

^3:61^ Sin. You have heard their reproach, O Lord, all their imaginations against me.  

> ^3:62^ Sin. The lips of them that rise up against me: and their devices against me all the day.

> ^3:63^ Sin. Behold their sitting down, and their rising up; I am their song.

> **65**. Here he denounces his enemy’s sin.

And first, as regards the infliction of reproaches: ***you have heard their reproach***, as if to say, “There is no need for proof.” *Jerusalem, and your people are a reproach to all that are round about us* (Dan 9:16).  

Second, as regards their threats: ***the lips of them that rise up***. *They that sought evils to me spoke vain things* (Ps 37:13).  

Third, as regards their mockery and insults: ***their sitting down***, because they sit in the assembly to ruin me; ***their rising up***, in exaltation; ***their song***, because they make songs to mock me.  

And all this can be understood to be said in the person of the prophet or of the people, as was said above. *Now I am turned into their song, and am become their byword* (Job 30:9). *They that drank wine made me their song* (Ps 68:13).
