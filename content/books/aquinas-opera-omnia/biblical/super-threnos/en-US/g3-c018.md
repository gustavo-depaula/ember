# Lecture 18

^1:18^ Sade. The Lord is just, for I have provoked his mouth to wrath: hear, I pray you, all you people, and see my sorrow: my virgins, and my young men are gone into captivity.  

> **18**. Here he weeps over captivity; and concerning this, he does three things.

First, he confesses the justice of the judge: ***the Lord is just***. *The Lord our God is just in all his works which he has done* (Dan 9:14).  

Second, he captures the benevolence of his hearers, provoking them to be compassionate: ***hear, I pray you***. *Give ear, all you that dwell near Zion* (Bar 4:9).  

Third, he weeps over the misery of captivity: ***my virgins and my young men***, who, thanks to the robustness of their age, were able to escape death from hunger, ***are gone into captivity***. *He has filled his belly with my delicate meats, and he has cast me out* (Jer 51:34).
