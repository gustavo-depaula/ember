# Lecture 14

^1:14^ Nun. The yoke of my iniquities has watched: they are folded together in his hand, and put upon my neck: my strength is weakened: the Lord has delivered me into a hand out of which I am not able to rise.  

> **14**. Here he shows the rigor of God’s justice, inasmuch as he is a judge; and concerning this, he does three things.

First, he presents the careful consideration of the sins: ***watched***, that is, he considered vigilantly; ***the yoke***, of affliction, to lay hands on me for my sins. *What do you see, Jeremiah? And I said, I see a rod watching* (Jer 1:11).  

Second, he presents the hard affliction of punishment: ***they are folded together***, namely, my iniquities, like a chain, in an indissoluble bond; ***and put upon my neck***, while I suffer punishment for them. *For my iniquities are gone over my head: and as a heavy burden are become heavy upon me* (Ps 37:5).  

Third, he excludes deliverance, sometimes because of weakness: ***my strength is weakened***; sometimes because of the power of the enemy: ***the Lord has delivered me into a hand***, namely, of the Chaldeans. *My strength is weakened through poverty* (Ps 30:11).
