# Lecture 21

^1:21^ Sin. They have heard that I sigh, and there is none to comfort me: all my enemies have heard of my evil, they have rejoiced that you have done it: you have brought a day of consolation, and they shall be like unto me.  

> **21**. Here he denounces the fault of others; and concerning this, he does three things.

First, he denounces the failure of her allies: ***they have heard***, namely, the Egyptians and others, in whom I had trust, have heard. *A voice was heard on high of lamentation, of mourning, and weeping* (Jer 31:15).  

Second, he denounces the joy of her enemies: ***all my enemies have heard of my evil, they have rejoiced that you have done it***. *They that trouble me will rejoice when I am moved* (Ps 12:5).  

Third, as if secure of divine justice, he predicts their ruin: ***you have brought***, that is, you will bring, on account of his certitude, ***a day of consolation***; they will be ***like*** me, sorrowful and afflicted. *Behold my servants shall eat, and you shall be hungry* (Isa 65:13).
