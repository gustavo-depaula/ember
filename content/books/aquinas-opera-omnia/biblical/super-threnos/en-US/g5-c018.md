# Lecture 18

***^3:52^ Sade. My enemies have chased me and caught me like a bird, without cause.***

> ^3:53^ Sade. My life has fallen into the pit, and they have laid a stone over me.

> ^3:54^ Sade. Waters have flowed over my head; I said: I am cut off.

> **62**. Here is presented the affliction in which they have fallen.

And first is presented the capture itself: ***have chased***, as if working by ambushes, stratagems, and force; ***enemies***, namely, the Chaldeans; ***without cause***, for I had not offended them in anything, nor had they been useful to me. *The Assyrian has oppressed them without any cause at all* (Isa 52:4)*. After this I will send them many hunters, and they shall hunt them* (Jer 16:16).  

Second is presented the imprisonment of the captives: ***my life has fallen into the pit***, of prison and captivity. *They have laid me in the lower pit: in dark places, and in the shadow of death* (Ps 87:7).  

Third, the affliction of the prisoners: ***have flowed over***, they have multiplied; ***the waters***, of tribulations; ***I said***, despairing and impatient. *All your billows, and your waves have passed over me* (Jonah 2:4).
