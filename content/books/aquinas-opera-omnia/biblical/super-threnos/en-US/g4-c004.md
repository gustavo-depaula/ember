# Lecture 4

^2:4^ Daleth. He has bent his bow as an enemy, he has fixed his right hand as an adversary: and he has killed all that was fair to behold in the tabernacle of the daughter of Zion, he has poured out his indignation like fire.  

> **26**. Here he weeps over the destruction of those things which pertain to the use of the people.

And first, over what pertains to beauty;  

second, over what pertains to defense, at ***the Lord has become as an enemy*** (Lam 2:5).  

Concerning the first, he does three things.  

First, he shows the divine indignation: ***he has bent his bow***, that is, his judgment, or the army of the Chaldaeans, in order to strike, as it were, from afar; ***he has fixed***, for striking a blow, ***his right hand***, in order to strike from close by. *Except you will be converted, he will brandish his sword: he has bent his bow and made it ready* (Ps 7:13).  

Second, he presents the infliction of the punishment: ***he has killed***, destroyed, ***all that was fair***: men, buildings, and the like. *How beautiful are your tabernacles, O Jacob!* (Num 24:5).  

Third, he shows the greatness of the punishment. As regards its immeasurable abundance: ***he has poured out***; and as regards its severity: ***like fire***; or this in regard to the kind of punishment, because the city was burned. *A fire is kindled in my wrath, and shall burn even to the lowest hell* (Deut 32:22). *Pour out your indignation upon them* (Ps 68:25).
