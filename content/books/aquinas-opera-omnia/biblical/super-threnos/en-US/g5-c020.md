# Lecture 20

^3:58^ Res. You have judged, O Lord, the cause of my soul, the Redeemer of my life.  

> ^3:59^ Res. You have seen, O Lord, their iniquity against me: judge you my judgment.

> ^3:60^ Res. You have seen all their fury, and all their thoughts against me.

> **64**. Here he commends justice to the judge: ***you have judged***, in past times; do so also now. *Judge me, O God, and distinguish my cause* (Ps 42:1).

***You have seen***. Here, having captured the goodwill of the judge, he sets forth his accusation against his enemy.  

And first, he denounces the iniquity of their works: ***you have seen, O Lord***, as if to say, “They cannot deny what is known to you.” Above: ***let all their evil be present before you: and make vintage of them, as you have made vintage of me*** (Lam 1:22).  

Second, he denounces the fury of their hearts: ***you have seen all their fury***. *Come, and let us invent devices against Jeremiah* (Jer 18:18).  

Third, he denounces the sin of their mouths: ***you have heard their reproach*** (Lam 3:61).
