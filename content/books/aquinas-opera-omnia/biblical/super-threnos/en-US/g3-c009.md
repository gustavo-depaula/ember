# Lecture 9

^1:9^ Teth. Her filthiness is on her feet, and she has not remembered her end: she is wonderfully cast down, not having a comforter: behold, O Lord, my affliction, because the enemy is lifted up.  

> **9**. Here he puts forward the progression of sin.

And first, he presents the blame: ***her filthiness***, namely, of sins; ***is on her feet***: namely, in her affections, as if for advancing in sin; ***her end***: namely, of death or divine judgment. *In all your works remember your last end, and you shall never sin* (Sir 7:40).  

Second, he presents the punishment: ***she is cast down***, from her state of dignity, ***wonderfully***, that is, into extreme misery. *Scatter them by your power; and bring them down* (Ps 58:12).  

Third, he begs for mercy: ***behold, O Lord***, namely, a feeling of pity*. See my humiliation and deliver me: for I have not forgotten your law* (Ps 118:153).
