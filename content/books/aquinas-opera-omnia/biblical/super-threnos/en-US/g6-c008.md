# Lecture 8

^4:8^ Heth. Their face is now made blacker than coals, and they are not known in the streets: their skin has stuck to their bones, it is withered and has become like wood.  

> **74**. Here he presents the disfigurement following upon their hunger. As regards their change of color, ***their face is now made blacker than coals, and they are not known***, on account of their disfigurement; and as regards the drying out of their fatness, ***their skin has stuck to their bones***. *The faces of them all are as the blackness of a kettle. Where is now the dwelling of the lions, and the feeding place of the young lions* (Nah 2:10–11)*. My skin is withered and drawn together* (Job 7:5).
