# Lecture 5

***^3:13^ He. He has shot into my reins the daughters of his quiver.***

> ^3:14^ He. I am made a derision to all my people, their song all the day long.

> ^3:15^ He. He has filled me with bitterness, he has inebriated me with wormwood.

> **49**. Here, in the second place, he presents the affliction of the punishment: ***into my reins***, which signifies the wantonness of the people; ***the daughters***, arrows, or the various punishment coming forth from his providence. *He has opened his quiver, and has afflicted me* (Job 30:11).

Third, he presents the delusion of the one punished: ***I am made a derision to all my people***. This is fulfilled in Jeremiah: *I am become a laughing-stock all the day, all scoff at me* (Jer 20:7); likewise, the people were also derided by others.  

***He has filled me***. Here he shows that it is done abundantly.  

And first, as regards the multitude of pains: ***he has filled me with bitterness***, with various anxieties; ***he has inebriated me***, brought it upon me abundantly (Jer 41:3–10).  

Second, at ***and he has broken*** (Lam 3:16), as regards the number of people punished.
