# Lecture 7

***^3:19^ Zain. Remember my poverty, and transgression, the wormwood, and the gall.***

> ^3:20^ Zain. I will be mindful and remember, and my soul shall languish within me.

> ^3:21^ Zain. These things I shall think over in my heart, I shall hope in God.

> **51**. Here he puts forward arguments to exclude despair.

And first, from the divine mercy;  

second, from the divine justice, at ***to crush under his feet all the prisoners of the land*** (Lam 3:34);  

third, from the divine power, at ***who is he that has said a thing should be done, when the Lord commands it not?*** (Lam 3:37);  

Concerning the first, he does two things.  

First, he shows the divine mercy as regards a comparison of benefits;  

second, as regards the relaxation of punishments, at ***for the Lord will not cast off forever*** (Lam 3:31);  

Concerning the first, he sets forth three things.  

First, the memory of past blessings;  

second, the experience of present blessings, at ***the mercies of the Lord that we are not consumed*** (Lam 3:22);  

third, the expectation of future blessings, at ***the Lord is good to them that hope in him*** (Lam 3:25).  

Concerning the first, he does three things.  

First, the prophet leads God to consider the misfortunes they have endured, saying, “O Lord, you who appear to hide and forget us,” ***remember***, freeing us; ***my poverty***, as to the plundering of our goods; ***and transgression***, our fault, which is the cause of so much misfortune; ***the wormwood***, as to the bitterness and affliction of men. Below: ***remember, O Lord, what is come upon us*** (Lam 5:1).  

Second, he encourages himself to remember the benefits they have received: ***I will be mindful and remember***, namely, the benefits; ***and my soul shall languish within me***, fainting from admiration or from desire. *These things I remembered, and poured out my soul in me* (Ps 41:5).  

Third, he concludes with confidence: ***these things I shall think over***, namely, these benefits. *I remembered your mercy, O Lord* (Sir 51:11).
