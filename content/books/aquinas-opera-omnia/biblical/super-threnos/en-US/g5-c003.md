# Lecture 3

^3:7^ Ghimel. He has built against me round about, that I may not get out: he has made my fetters heavy.  

> ^3:8^ Ghimel. Yea, and when I cry, and entreat, he has shut out my prayer.

> ^3:9^ Ghimel. He has shut up my ways with square stones, he has turned my paths upside down.

> **47**. Here he excludes the remedy of escape.

First, because of the siege of the enemy: ***he has built against me round about***, that is, an army of besiegers; ***fetters***, which signifies the same thing as the siege, as those who are thrown into prison so that they are unable to flee. *I was delivered up, and came not forth* (Ps 87:9). *You have put my feet in the stocks, and have observed all my paths* (Job 13:27).  

Second, because of the shutting out of their prayer: ***yea, and when I cry, and entreat, he has shut out my prayer***. *I shall cry by day, and you will not hear* (Ps 21:3).  

Third, because of the impediment to their plans: ***he has shut up my ways***, my plans for escape; ***with square stones***, serious obstacles*. I will hedge up your way with thorns* (Hos 2:6).
