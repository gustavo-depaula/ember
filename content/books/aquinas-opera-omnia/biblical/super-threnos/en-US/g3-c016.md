# Lecture 16

^1:16^ Ain. Therefore am I weeping, and my eyes run down with water: because the comforter is far from me, changing my soul: my children are desolate because the enemy has prevailed.  

> **16**. Here he weeps over the withdrawal of consolation.

And first, he speaks of the mourning of sorrow, ***weeping***. *Who will give water to my head?* (Jer 9:1).  

Second, the matter of the sorrow: ***because the comforter is far from me***, the Egyptian; ***changing***, from the misery which I now suffer, into my original prosperity. *You have put away my acquaintance far from me* (Ps 87:9).  

Third, he speaks of the effect of this: ***my children are desolate***. For the consequence of the lack of help is the power of the enemy and the captivity of the people. *My people have been a lost flock* (Jer 50:6).
