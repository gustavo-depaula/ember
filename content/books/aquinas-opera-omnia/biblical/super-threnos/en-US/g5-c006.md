# Lecture 6

***^3:16^ Vau. And he has broken my teeth one by one, he has fed me with ashes.***

> ^3:17^ Vau. And my soul is removed far off, I have forgotten good things.

> ^3:18^ Vau. And I said: my end and my hope is perished from the Lord.

> **50**. Here he describes the afflictions: ***teeth***, the warriors by whom they were defended, as a beast is defended by his teeth; ***one by one***, so that nothing would remain; ***with ashes***, humiliation. *The teeth of the whelps of lions are broken* (Job 4:10). *For I did eat ashes like bread* (Ps 101:10).

***And my soul is removed far off***. Here he takes up the reprobation. ***Removed far off***, from the mercy of God. *But you have rejected and despised* (Ps 88:39). ***I have forgotten good things***, through the experience of evils. *The affliction of an hour makes one forget great extravagances* (Sir 11:29).  

***And I said***. Here he concludes with despair, as if saying: “Because I am in anguish, and the Lord has rebuffed me in order not to help me,” ***I said***, in my heart, in the person of those despairing. ***My end is perished***, as if saying: “I will not receive that for which I was waiting.” *I have lost all hope, I will not do it* (Jer 2:25)*. You have said: he labors in vain that serves God* (Mal 3:14).
