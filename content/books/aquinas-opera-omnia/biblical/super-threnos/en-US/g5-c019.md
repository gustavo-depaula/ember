# Lecture 19

***^3:55^ Coph. I have called upon your name, O Lord, from the lowest pit.***

> ^3:56^ Coph. You have heard my voice: turn not away your ear from my sighs, and cries.

> ^3:57^ Coph. You drew near in the day, when I called upon you; you said: fear not.

> **63**. Here he seeks to capture goodwill from the person of the judge.

And first, he shows his mercy towards the unfortunate;  

second, he shows his justice, at ***you have judged, O Lord, the cause of my soul*** (Lam 3:58).  

Concerning the first, he sets forth three things.  

First, the prayer of the unfortunate: ***I have called upon your name***, others being in distress; ***from the lowest pit***, from the greatest tribulations, as in Egypt and often in the book of Judges. *I called upon the Lord, the father of my Lord, that he would not leave me* (Sir 51:14).  

Second, he presents the merciful hearing of the prayer: ***you have heard my voice***, so too, now, ***turn not away***. *The Lord has heard my supplication: he has received my prayer* (Ps 6:10).  

Third, he recalls comfort after the prayer has been heard: ***you drew near in the day***, strengthening me by your help; so too now. *Set me beside you* (Job 17:3). *Fear not, my servant, for I am with you: turn not aside* (Isa 41:10).
