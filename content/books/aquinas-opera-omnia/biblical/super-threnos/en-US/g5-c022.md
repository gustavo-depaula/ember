# Lecture 22

^3:64^ Thau. You shall render them a recompense, O Lord, according to the works of their hands.  

> ^3:65^ Thau. I shall give them a buckler of heart, your labor.

> ^3:66^ Thau. You shall persecute them in anger, and shall destroy them from under the heavens, O Lord.

> **66**. Here he asks for revenge against his enemies. And because of his confidence in his prayer being heard, he sets forth more a prediction than a prayer; whence, he does two things.

First, he predicts the just retribution of sinners: ***you shall render them***, namely, punishments. *According to the works of their hands give to them: render to them their reward* (Ps 27:4).  

Second, he sets forth the determination of their punishment:  

and first, as to its duration: ***I will give them your labor***, that is, the sin in which they made you suffer. *I am weary of bearing them* (Isa 1:14)*: **a buckler of heart***, that is, for their sins you will reward them by putting on their hearts a shield, namely, a hardness, so that they are not penetrated by the arrows of your grace, in the sword of your word. *His body is like molten shields* (Job 41:6).  

Second, as to the punishment of the body: ***you shall persecute them***, with various afflictions, and then, ***you shall destroy***, by death and eternal damnation; ***under the heavens***, those who wanted to be in the heavens. *Bring upon them the day of affliction, and with a double destruction, destroy them* (Jer 17:18). *So shall you pursue them with your tempest: and shall trouble them in your wrath* (Ps 82:16).
