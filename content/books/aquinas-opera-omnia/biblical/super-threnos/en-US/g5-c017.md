# Lecture 17

^3:49^ Ain. My eye is afflicted, and has not been quiet, because there was no rest:  

> ^3:50^ Ain. Till God regarded and looked down from the heavens.

> ^3:51^ Ain. My eye has wasted my soul because of all the daughters of my city.

> **61**. Here the compassion of the prophet is presented.

And first, as regards external mourning: ***my eye is afflicted***, crying; ***and has not been quieted***, from tears; ***rest***, from tribulations for the people. *Let my eyes shed tears, for a voice of wailing is heard out of Zion* (Jer 9:19–20).  

And the end of mourning, ***till God regarded***, with an eye of mercy. *Because he has looked forth from his high sanctuary* (Ps 101:20).  

Second, as regards the spur of inner pain: ***my eye***, seeing the pillaging of the earth; ***has wasted my soul***, depriving it of joy, or weeping, he has pulled all the pain out of his heart.
