# Lecture 9

^3:25^ Teth. The Lord is good to them that hope in him, to the soul that seeks him.  

> ^3:26^ Teth. It is good to wait with silence for the salvation of the Lord.

> ^3:27^ Teth. It is good for a man, when he has borne the yoke from his youth.

> **53**. Here he shows the divine mercy from the expectation of future benefits.

And first, he puts the expectation;  

second, the condition of the one waiting, at ***he shall sit solitary, and hold his peace*** (Lam 3:28).  

Concerning the first, he does three things.  

First, he presents the fruit of waiting: ***the Lord is good***, as it were, spreading his own goodness. *How good is God to Israel, to them that are of a right heart!* (Ps 72:1).  

Second, he presents the manner of waiting: ***it is good to wait with silence***, patiently, without murmuring. *In silence and in hope shall your strength be* (Isa 30:15).  

Third, he presents the time of waiting: ***it is good for a man, when he has borne the yoke***, of the fear of the Lord and of love, ***from his youth***, so that the age of turbulence be calmed and more easily accustomed to good. *A young man according to his way, even when he is old he will not depart from it* (Prov 22:6).
