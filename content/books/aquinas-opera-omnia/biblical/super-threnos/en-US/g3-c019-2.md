# Lecture 19

^1:20^ Res. Behold, O Lord, for I am in distress, my bowels are troubled: my heart is turned within me, for I am full of bitterness: without, the sword destroys, and at home, there is death alike.  

> **20**. Here he begins to complain to the judge himself; and concerning this, he does three things.

First, he exposes his own misery;  

second, he denounces the fault of his enemies, at ***they have heard that I sigh*** (Lam 1:21);  

third, he seeks vengeance, at ***let all their evil be present before you*** (Lam 1:22).  

Concerning the first, he does three things.  

First, he draws attention: ***behold, O Lord***. *Have mercy on me, O Lord, for I am afflicted* (Ps 30:10).  

Second, he presents his sorrow under the metaphor of internal pain and anguish of heart: ***troubled***, which he explains: ***for I am full of bitterness***. *My bowels are in pain* (Jer 4:19). *For the Almighty has quite filled me with bitterness* (Ruth 1:20).  

Third, he gives the reason for this sorrow: ***without, the sword destroys***. *Without, the sword shall lay them waste, and terror within* (Deut 32:25).
