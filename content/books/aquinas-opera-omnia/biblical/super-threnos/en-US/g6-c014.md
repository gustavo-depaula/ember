# Lecture 14

^4:14^ Nun. They have wandered as blind men in the streets, they were defiled with blood: and when they could not help walking in it, they held up their skirts. *(The sense of the passage in Hebrew is different: *they are so defiled with blood that no one dares to touch their garments*.)*  

> **80**. Second, he presents their lack of amendment, because they thought they were doing enough only by keeping away from the entrance of the temple: ***they have wandered***, roving through the streets; ***as blind men***: *these things they thought, and were deceived: for their own malice blinded them* (Wis 2:21); ***walking in it***, the temple; ***their skirts*** (*lacinias*), cut in the style of jesters, which is called “lacerated” from *lacero*: in which it is shown that they did not give up their pleasures.

Or, according to a variant text, ***their wantonness*** (*lascivias*), and this is clearer. *For your hands are defiled with blood, and your fingers with iniquity* (Isa 59:3).  

Or it can be explained concerning those captives who were physically blinded by their enemies, who could not enter their homes by themselves without holding the end of the garment of a guide; and this fits with what follows.
