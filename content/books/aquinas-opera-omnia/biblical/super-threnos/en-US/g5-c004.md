# Lecture 4

^3:10^ Daleth. He has become to me as a bear lying in wait: as a lion in a secret place.  

> ^3:11^ Daleth. He has turned aside my paths, and has broken me in pieces, he has made me forsaken.

> ^3:12^ Daleth. He has bent his bow, and set me as a mark for his arrows.

> **48**. Here he shows the mode of their affliction.

And first, that it is done deceitfully;  

second, that it is done openly, at ***he has bent his bow*** (Lam 3:12).  

third, that it is done abundantly, at ***he has filled me*** (Lam 3:15).  

Concerning the first, he does two things.  

First, he presents the deceit of their enemies: ***he***, that is, Nebuchadnezzar, who fights against me with ambushes, ***has become to me as a bear***, in which his cruelty is signified, and as ***a lion***, in which is designated his power. *And I will be to them as a lioness, as a leopard in the way of the Assyrians* (Hos 13:7).  

Second, he excludes ambushes, or their plans for them, in which they repel the ambushes of their enemies: ***he has turned aside my paths***. Above: ***he has made me forsaken, wasted with sorrow all the day long*** (Lam 1:13).  

***He has bent his bow***. Here he shows that he afflicted him publicly and openly; and concerning this, he does three things.  

First, he presents the proposition: ***bow***, the judgment, or the army of their enemies; ***as a mark***, towards which all publicly shoot arrows. *He has bent his bow* (Ps 7:13). *He has broken me, and has set me up to be his mark* (Job 16:13).
