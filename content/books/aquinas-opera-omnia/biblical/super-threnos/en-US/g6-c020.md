# Lecture 20

^4:20^ Res. The breath of our mouth, Christ the Lord, is taken for our sins: to whom we said: under thy shadow we shall live among the gentiles.  

> **86**. Fourth, he excludes the protection of kings: ***the breath of our mouth***, by which we breathed in the midst of our anguish; ***Christ the Lord***, Josiah; ***is taken***, he was killed by the Egyptians (2 Kgs 23:29); ***for our sins***, because he himself was just. Or it can be expounded of Zedekiah, according to what follows in this history. Or better, of Christ. *He was wounded for our iniquities* (Isa 53:5). ***Under thy shadow***, protection. *I sat down under his shadow, whom I desired* (Song 2:3).
