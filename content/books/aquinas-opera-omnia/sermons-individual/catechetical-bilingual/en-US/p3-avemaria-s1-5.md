# AveMaria.S1.5

We must now consider concerning the first part of this prayerThat is, the first part of the salutation. that in ancient times it was a great thing even when angels appeared to men; and for man to show them reverence was especially praiseworthy. Thus, it is written to the praise of Abraham that he received the angels with all courtesy and showed them reverence.See Genesis 18:2 and following. But for an angel to show reverence to a man was never heard of until the angel reverently greeted the blessed Virgin, saying: ***hail***. In olden times, an angel would not show reverence to a man, but a man would revere an angel.Parallel texts: *ST* III.27.1, 4, 5; *In III Sent*. 3.1.1, qa., 3; *In III Sent*. 3.2, qa., 2, 3; *In III Sent*. 13.1.2, qa. 1; *Quodlibet VI*, q. 5, a. 1; *Compendium theologiae*, ch. 224; *Exposition on the Lord’s Prayer*, a. 5; *Commentary on Psalms*, at Psalm 45; *Commentary on Jeremiah*, ch. 1; *Commentary on Matthew*, ch. 12. This is because angels are greater than men, and indeed in three ways.
