# Prologue

The miracles of God cannot be scrutinized by man, as we read in Job 37:5: *who works great wonders and things that cannot be scrutinized*. *(Also, Job 5:9.)* Some of the miracles God performs in his saints. Augustine teaches this, saying that justifying man is more than creating, *(Augustine, *Tractates on the Gospel of John*, tract. 72, para. 3 (CCSL 36:508; PL 35:1823); cf. Aquinas, *Catena Aurea on John*, at 14:12, ch. 14, lect. 4; *ST* I-II.113.9, resp.; III.43.4, ad 2. It is even more than creating another world: *ST* I.25.3, obj. 3; *On Truth*, q. 27, a. 3, s.c. 4.)* because creation passes whereas justification remains (cf. Matt 24:35). Thus *God is wondrous in his saints* (Ps 68:36). We cannot scrutinize these wonders of God in his saints, unless he who is the scrutinizer of hearts and reins (cf. Ps 7:10) teaches us. *(Because it is through grace; cf. *ST* III.2.10; Jeremiah 17:10; Revelation 2:23.)* Therefore, let us take refuge with him in prayer and ask him at the beginning that he may give me something to say, et cetera.
