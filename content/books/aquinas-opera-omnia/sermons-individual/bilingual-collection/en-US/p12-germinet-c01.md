# Prologue

Isaiah says about Christ: *who will tell of his generations?* (Isa 53:8). The generation of Christ rests in a way on the generation of Mary—I speak here of Christ’s generation in time.“In a way” (*quodammodo*), for not in an absolute way; God did not depend on her, but he had chosen her to be the mother of Christ. Cf. how Thomas distinguishes between different meanings of necessity: in *ST* III.46.1 in the context of the Passion in view of God’s almightiness. Hence the human understanding is not enough for telling the whole story of the generation of Mary.Although she is a human being born from an earthly father and an earthly mother, her birth is a mystery: *ST* III.27.1–5. Let us, therefore, call upon the grace of the Holy Spirit, by whichLatin: *quam*, the antecedent of which is “grace” (*gratiam*). Mary is sanctified, and let us ask the Lord himself that he may give me something to say.
