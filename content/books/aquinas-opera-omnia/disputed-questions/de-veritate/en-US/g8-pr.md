# Prologue — On the knowledge of angels

***1. Whether the angels see God through his essence?***

- 2. Whether the intellects of beatified angels and men comprehend the divine essence?
- 3. Whether an angel, by means of his own natural powers, attains the vision of God through his essence?
- 4. Whether an angel, seeing God through his essence, knows all things?
- 5. Whether the vision of things in the Word is through likenesses of them existing in the angelic intellects?
- 6. Whether an angel knows himself?
- 7. Whether one angel knows another?
- 8. Whether an angel knows material things through forms or by knowing his own essence?
- 9. Whether the forms by which angels know material things are innate or received from things?
- 10. Whether higher angels know more through universal forms than those by which lower angels know?
- 11. Whether angels know singulars?
- 12. Whether angels know the future?
- 13. Whether angels know the heart’s secrets?
- 14. Whether angels know many things at the same time?
- 15. Whether angels’ knowledge of things is discursive?
- 16. Whether morning knowledge be distinguished from evening knowledge in angels?
- 17. Whether an angel’s knowledge is adequately divided into morning and evening knowledge?
