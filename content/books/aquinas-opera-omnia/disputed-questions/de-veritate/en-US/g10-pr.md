# Prologue — On the mind

1. Whether the mind, as containing within itself the image of the Trinity, is the essence of the soul or one of its powers?  

- 2. Whether there is memory in the mind?
- 3. Whether memory is distinguished from understanding as one power from another?
- 4. Whether the mind knows material things?
- 5. Whether our mind knows material things in their singularity?
- 6. Whether the human mind receives knowledge from sensible things?
- 7. Whether the image of the Trinity is in the mind as it knows material things or only as it knows eternal things?
- 8. Whether the mind knows itself through its essence or through some species?
- 9. Whether its through their essence or through some likeness that our mind knows habits which exist in the soul?
- 10. Whether one knows that he has charity?
- 11. Whether the mind in this life can see God through his essence?
- 12. Whether God's existence is self-evident to the human mind, just as first principles of demonstration, which cannot be thought not to exist?
- 13. Whether the Trinity of persons in God can be known by natural reason?
