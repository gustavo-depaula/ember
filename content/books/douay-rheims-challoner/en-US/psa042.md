# Psalms 42

*The prophet aspires after the temple and altar of God.*

*(Does this psalm differ from what you were expecting? Don't be confused. There are two systems for arranging the psalms: the **Septuagint** and the **Masoretic**. We use the Septuagint system here. In the Masoretic system, which is used in most modern Bible translations, this psalm is called **Psalm 43**, and the **previous psalm** is known as **Psalm 42**.)*

A psalm for David.

> **1** Judge me, O God, and distinguish my cause from the nation that is not holy: deliver me from the unjust and deceitful man.
> **2** For thou art God my strength: why hast thou cast me off? and why do I go sorrowful whilst the enemy afflicteth me?
> **3** Send forth thy light and thy truth: they have conducted me, and brought me unto thy holy hill, and into thy tabernacles.
> **4** And I will go in to the altar of God: to God who giveth joy to my youth.
> **5** To thee, O God my God, I will give praise upon the harp: why art thou sad, O my soul? and why dost thou disquiet me?
> **6** Hope in God, for I will still give praise to him: the salvation of my countenance, and my God.
