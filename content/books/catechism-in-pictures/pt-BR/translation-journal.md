# Translation Journal — Catéchisme en Images (pt-BR)

Source: fr-FR (original 1908, Maison de la Bonne Presse, Paris)
Target: pt-BR (Brazilian Portuguese, modern post-1971 orthography)

This is a working document for the translator. It is **not** rendered in the app.

## Translation register

A Brazilian Portuguese rendering aimed at the modern lay reader, faithful to the 1908 French original. Register: clear, dignified, warm — like a well-edited modern catechism. The author's voice is doctrinal, paternal, and concise — preserve that. Numbered paragraphs are kept exactly as in the source.

The 1910 Lisbon edition (*Catecismo Illustrado*) was consulted as a reference but **not** used as the source — it is in pre-1911 European orthography (*introducção*, *christã*, *Padre Nosso*) and would need extensive modernization. The French is the canonical source for this translation.

## Decalogue couplets

Plates 27–51 in the French source open with a French rhyming couplet of the commandment, e.g.:

> Un seul Dieu tu adoreras,
> Et aimeras parfaitement.

These are 17th-century French versified Decalogue. **Render as a traditional Brazilian Portuguese versified Decalogue** (Mons. Inácio José de Sousa Bandeira version is widely used in Brazil):

| French couplet (plate) | pt-BR rendering (Brazilian versified Decalogue) |
|------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| Un seul Dieu tu adoreras, / Et aimeras parfaitement. (27–28) | Amar a Deus sobre todas as coisas. |
| Dieu en vain tu ne jureras, / Ni autre chose pareillement. (29–31) | Não tomar seu santo nome em vão. |
| Les dimanches tu garderas, / En servant Dieu dévotement. (32–33) | Guardar domingos e festas de guarda. |
| Tes père et mère honoreras, / Afin de vivre longtemps. (34–37) | Honrar pai e mãe. |
| Homicide point ne seras, / De fait ni volontairement. (38–40) | Não matar. |
| Luxurieux point ne seras, / De corps ni de consentement. (41) | Não pecar contra a castidade. |
| Le bien d'autrui tu ne prendras, / Ni retiendras à ton escient. (42–43) | Não furtar. |
| Faux témoignage ne diras, / Ni mentiras aucunement. (44–46) | Não levantar falso testemunho. |
| L'œuvre de chair ne désireras, / Qu'en mariage seulement. (47) | Não desejar a mulher do próximo. |
| Bien d'autrui ne convoiteras, / Pour les avoir injustement. (48) | Não cobiçar as coisas alheias. |

Render the commandment formula as `> Amar a Deus sobre todas as coisas.` (blockquote) just below the article subhead and before the body. Drop the second French line — the Brazilian formula is one-line and self-contained.

## Translation Decisions

(Decisions appear here as translation proceeds. Date format: YYYY-MM-DD.)

- **2026-05-17 — Source choice.** Translating from the French 1908 canonical, **not** from the 1910 Lisbon Portuguese edition (which is pre-1911 European orthography in a scanned, OCR-less PDF). Modern pt-BR rendering, post-1971 orthographic reform.
- **2026-05-17 — Quadro vs Tableau.** The French *tableau* means both "table/picture" and the lesson-illustration unit. In pt-BR, **Quadro** captures both senses and matches Brazilian catechetical usage. (The Lisbon edition uses "gravura" but "Quadro" is closer to the original sense and to the lesson-as-unit nuance.)
- **2026-05-17 — Decalogue couplets.** French has 10 rhyming couplets across plates 27–51. Brazilian Catholic tradition uses single-line prose formulations (Mons. Inácio José de Sousa Bandeira, widely diffused). Substituting the Brazilian formula preserves the catechetical function without forcing a forced Portuguese rhyme; noted here so subsequent translators don't second-guess.
- **2026-05-17 — Article subheads.** The French source has italic-rendered article subheads at the top of each plate (e.g., `*Premier article : Je crois en Dieu le Père tout-puissant…*`). These stay italic in pt-BR (`*Primeiro artigo: Creio em Deus Pai todo-poderoso…*`). Numbered as 1º, 2º, etc. with the abbreviation, not spelled out.
- **2026-05-17 — Pai-Nosso (plate 53).** Whenever the French quotes a phrase of the Pater (e.g., *Notre Père qui êtes aux cieux*, *Que votre nom soit sanctifié*, *Donnez-nous aujourd'hui notre pain de chaque jour*), use the **standard Brazilian Catholic liturgical** version word-for-word ("Pai nosso que estais nos céus", "Santificado seja o vosso nome", "O pão nosso de cada dia nos dai hoje", "Perdoai-nos as nossas ofensas, assim como nós perdoamos a quem nos tem ofendido", "E não nos deixeis cair em tentação, mas livrai-nos do mal"). Do not invent intermediate phrasings.
