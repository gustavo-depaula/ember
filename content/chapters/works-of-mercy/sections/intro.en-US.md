The Works of Mercy are charitable actions by which we come to the aid of our neighbor in his bodily and spiritual needs. They flow directly from the two great commandments: love of God and love of neighbor made concrete.

Christ himself names the corporal works in Matthew 25, where the Son of Man judges the nations not by what they said, but by whether they fed the hungry, clothed the naked, and visited the prisoner: *"Whatever you did for one of the least of these brothers of mine, you did for me."*
