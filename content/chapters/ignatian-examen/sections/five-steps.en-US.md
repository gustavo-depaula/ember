## The Five Steps

Take ten to fifteen minutes. Find a quiet place. Place yourself in God's presence — not talking to a distant judge, but sitting with a Father who already loves you and already knows the day.

**1. Gratitude.** Begin with thanks. Look back over the day and notice gifts — a good conversation, a meal, a mercy you did not earn. Gratitude opens the heart and changes the key of the whole prayer. Without it, the examen curdles into scrupulous self-analysis.

**2. Petition for light.** Ask the Holy Spirit to show you the day as He sees it. Your own eyes are biased toward pride or discouragement; you need His. *"Send forth your light and your truth, let them lead me"* (Ps 43:3).

**3. Review of the day.** Walk through the hours. Not every event — the significant ones. Where did you feel drawn to God? Where did you feel pulled away? What moods and movements were in you? Ignatius calls these *consolation* (what drew you toward God, faith, hope, love) and *desolation* (what drew you away). Name them.

**4. Contrition.** For the failures you see, ask forgiveness. Not self-flagellation — honest sorrow. "Lord, I was impatient with her. I turned away when I could have helped. Forgive me." If something is grave, resolve to bring it to confession. Otherwise, receive the Father's mercy simply.

**5. Resolution for tomorrow.** One concrete thing. Not a heroic plan — a single next step. *"Tomorrow I will begin with morning offering before the phone."* "*Tomorrow I will call him."* Close with the Our Father or a short prayer into Mary's hands.
