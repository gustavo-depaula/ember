St. Ignatius of Loyola was asked what prayer he would keep if he could keep only one. He chose the examen. In his *Spiritual Exercises* (§43) he gave it five steps, and he asked every Jesuit to make it twice a day — noon and evening — no matter how busy.

It is not an examination of conscience in the penitential sense, though it includes one. It is a daily review of the day with God — looking at the hours together, asking where grace moved, where you turned away, what the day is asking of tomorrow. Done faithfully, it teaches the most elusive of all spiritual skills: *noticing God in the ordinary.*

The examen is the anchor of the Ignatian spiritual life. Even the lay retreatant, after three days of silent Exercises, is told: if you keep nothing else, keep this.
