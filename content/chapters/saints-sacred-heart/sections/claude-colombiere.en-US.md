Claude de la Colombière was born in 1641 near Lyon, France. A brilliant student, he entered the Society of Jesus at age seventeen and quickly distinguished himself as a preacher and spiritual director.

In 1675, he was appointed superior of the small Jesuit residence at Paray-le-Monial, where Providence brought him into contact with Sister Margaret Mary. Recognizing the authenticity of her revelations, he became the first to consecrate himself to the Sacred Heart and began quietly promoting the devotion.

Sent to England as chaplain to the Duchess of York, he was arrested during the anti-Catholic persecutions of the Titus Oates plot, imprisoned, and eventually expelled from the country. His health broken by imprisonment, he returned to France and died at Paray-le-Monial on February 15, 1682, at the age of forty-one.

His personal writings reveal a man of extraordinary faith and trust in God's providence. He was canonized by Pope John Paul II in 1992.
