## In the Confessional

**Begin.** *"Bless me, Father, for I have sinned. It has been [time] since my last confession. These are my sins..."*

**Confess.** Name the sins. Briefly. Do not narrate — confess. *"I was angry with my wife three times and spoke harshly"* — not the whole story of the week. If you are unsure whether something is a sin, ask the priest.

**Do not omit out of shame.** The one thing you are most afraid to say is often the thing the sacrament exists to heal. Every priest has heard it. Christ knew it before you entered the room. *Nothing* a penitent can say will shock the confessional.

**End.** *"For these and all the sins of my past life, I am sorry."*

**Listen.** The priest may counsel you. Take it seriously; he is not performing a ritual but speaking in Christ's name. Accept the penance. If the penance is unclear or impossible, ask.

**Make the Act of Contrition.** Out loud, unless the priest indicates otherwise:

*"O my God, I am heartily sorry for having offended thee, and I detest all my sins, because I dread the loss of heaven and the pains of hell, but most of all because they offend thee, my God, who art all-good and deserving of all my love. I firmly resolve, with the help of thy grace, to confess my sins, to do penance, and to amend my life. Amen."*

**Receive absolution.** Listen. *"I absolve you from your sins, in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit."* Make the sign of the cross. *It is done.* Your sins are gone. Heaven rejoices. Believe it.
