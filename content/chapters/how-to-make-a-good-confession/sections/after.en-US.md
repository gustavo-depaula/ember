## After Confession

**Do the penance.** Soon. A penance unperformed is part of the wound left untended.

**Give thanks.** Five minutes of thanksgiving in the pew. You have been washed. Do not rush out of the church. Let the grace settle. A decade of the Rosary, or *Anima Christi*, or simply silence before the tabernacle.

**Believe it.** The most common post-confession temptation is to doubt that it worked — to pick at the scar. *"Did I confess well enough? Did I forget something? Did I really mean it?"* If you prepared honestly and the priest absolved you, **it is done**. Scrupulosity, not humility, is what keeps returning to the act. Humility trusts the sacrament.

**Do not go back and re-confess the same sins already absolved.** Unless you had deliberately concealed a mortal sin (in which case all sins since that confession must be re-confessed), the past is closed. Bring only new matter.

**Note what God showed you.** If the priest said something that struck you, write it down later. The Holy Spirit often uses confession to give the next step.

**Resume the plan of life.** A good confession does not end with the sacrament — it ends with the small, concrete change you carry out over the coming days.
