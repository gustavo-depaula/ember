## Fruits, Today and Over Years

The tradition is clear: the fruit of mental prayer is not what you feel during prayer. It is what your life looks like six months and six years later.

### The fruit you carry today

Francis de Sales asked his directees to leave every meditation with a **spiritual bouquet** — one word, one image, one phrase from the prayer, to smell again in the margins of the day. *"As those who have walked in a garden do not leave willingly without carrying away in their hand four or five flowers to smell and keep throughout the day, so too our soul, having walked in the garden of meditation among so many beautiful thoughts and virtuous affections, should choose one, two, or three that seem most useful, to breathe upon frequently during the remainder of the day."*

That bouquet is the first fruit. It is tiny. It is immense. A woman who remembers at three in the afternoon that Christ called her by name at seven in the morning is a different woman in the afternoon meeting.

Liguori adds the small **resolution** — one concrete fidelity: a word not spoken, a minute of silence before a meal, one sincere kindness to the colleague who grates. *"A single, specific, small resolution is worth more than many good intentions."*

### The fruits that grow over years

- **Patience under annoyances you did not used to bear.**
- **A steady turning-away from what once enthralled you.**
- **A growing ease in the presence of God during ordinary tasks** — what Brother Lawrence called *the practice of the presence of God.*
- **Peace that does not depend on circumstance.**
- **Love that no longer needs to be rewarded to continue.**
- **Self-knowledge in the light of Christ** — not introspection, but Teresa's slow realisation that *"by gazing at His grandeur we get in touch with our own lowliness; by looking at His purity we shall see our own filth; by pondering His humility, we shall see how far we are from being humble."* The soul becomes honest before it becomes holy.

*"A soul in prayer,"* Teresa wrote, *"is a soul that is growing. A soul that has given up mental prayer is a soul that is shrinking."* There are no plateaus. Only advance or retreat.

Therefore, begin. Fifteen minutes tomorrow morning. Same place the day after. After a month, a year, a decade, you will look back and not recognize the person who started. That is the quiet miracle of the half-hour.
