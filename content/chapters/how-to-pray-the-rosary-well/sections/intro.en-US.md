The rosary is simple enough for a child and deep enough for a saint. But it is easily prayed badly — run through as a quota to fill, a chain of Hail Marys with the mind three rooms away.

Pope St. John Paul II, who prayed the rosary daily, wrote in *Rosarium Virginis Mariae*: *"The Rosary, though clearly Marian in character, is at heart a Christocentric prayer."* You say "Hail Mary, Hail Mary" with your lips; with your heart you gaze at Christ through her eyes. The beads are a frame. The picture is Jesus.

This is why the rosary tires the inattentive and rests the attentive. Pray it as a meditation, not a recitation, and fifteen minutes with the beads becomes the most restorative silence of your day.
