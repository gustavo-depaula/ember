## How to Meditate the Mysteries

This is the part that separates the rosary from a counter. The Hail Marys are the heartbeat; the mystery is the scene you inhabit.

**Before the decade.** Read the mystery aloud or in the heart. Pause. See it. John Paul II recommended *"announcing the mystery, and dwelling on it as if a biblical icon."* You are not summarizing a story you know; you are standing inside it.

**Use your imagination.** St. Ignatius's *composition of place*: picture the stable at Bethlehem, the dust on Mary's feet, the smell of hay, the silence. At the Agony in the Garden, kneel with Christ under the olive trees. At the Resurrection, stand beside the women at the empty tomb.

**Let one word hold the mystery.** Pick a single phrase or image — *"Let it be done," "They have no wine," "Father, forgive them"* — and let it quietly return as the Hail Marys unfold.

**Speak to Mary.** She was there. She remembers. Ask her to let you see what she saw. The rosary is a conversation with her about her Son; she is not a mechanical bead-counter at your side.

**Do not force feelings.** A meditation is not a performance of piety. Sometimes the mystery opens and you weep; sometimes it is a shut door and you pray dryly. Both are real prayer. What you offer is attention, not emotion.

**One decade done well beats five done poorly.** If fifteen minutes has slipped past in one decade, rejoice. That is the rosary praying you.
