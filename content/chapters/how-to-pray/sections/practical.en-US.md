## A Practical Rule

**Fixed time.** Choose a time, not "when I feel like it." The saints are unanimous: a short prayer faithfully kept beats a long one attempted occasionally. Morning before the day begins is the classical recommendation — the first act belongs to God.

**Fixed place.** A chair, a corner, a small icon or crucifix. The body remembers places; going to the same spot each day shortens the distance to prayer.

**Fixed form.** A plan of life (see the chapter) gives prayer a structure you do not have to reinvent each morning. Typical daily minimum: a morning offering, fifteen minutes of mental prayer, a decade of the Rosary, the Angelus, an examination of conscience at night.

**Attitude.** Reverence, but not tension. St. Francis de Sales: *"A thousand times a day I draw my heart back to its center."* Returning is itself prayer.

**Perseverance.** There will be days when it feels like nothing. Pray anyway. What God looks at is the will that shows up, not the feeling that fails to.
