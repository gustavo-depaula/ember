A virtue is "a habitual and firm disposition to do the good" (CCC 1803). It is not a single act but a settled inclination — the soul trained to act rightly without every decision becoming a fresh struggle.

The Catholic tradition distinguishes two families of virtues. The **theological virtues** — faith, hope, and charity — are infused by God at Baptism; their object is God himself. The **cardinal virtues** — prudence, justice, fortitude, temperance — are acquired by repeated good acts (and deepened by grace); they govern human life before God, neighbor, self.

Together they are "the seven": the core virtues that shape a holy life.
