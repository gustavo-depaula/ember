## The Cardinal Virtues

**Prudence.** Right reason in action — the virtue that discerns the true good in every circumstance and chooses the right means to attain it. It is the "charioteer" of the other virtues, guiding them.

**Justice.** The constant will to give each person his due — to God, the worship owed to him; to neighbor, what belongs to him in truth and charity.

**Fortitude.** Firmness in difficulty and constancy in pursuing the good. Fortitude resists fear, even the fear of death, and fortifies one's resolve when the good is hard.

**Temperance.** Moderation in the use of created goods — food, drink, pleasure, honor. Temperance is not rejection of the good but its right ordering; it keeps desire within the bounds of reason and truth.

The cardinal virtues are called "cardinal" from the Latin *cardo*, the hinge — because upon them the moral life turns.
