## Fruits

**Scripture begins to speak to you.** Verses rise unbidden during the day — in traffic, at the sink, in a hard conversation. The Word has moved from memory to heart.

**Christ becomes a face, not a concept.** Reading the Gospels slowly, day after day, the Lord stops being an idea and becomes someone you recognize — His tone, His silences, His way of answering with a question, His eye for the outsider.

**Life becomes narrative again.** Without daily contact with Scripture, the imagination defaults to whatever the world is selling. With lectio, the soul's background story quietly rewrites itself: exodus, covenant, cross, resurrection.

**Prayer becomes easier.** The Psalms become your own — lament, praise, trust, pleading. The words you did not have are already in the Psalter.

**Judgment sharpens.** You start to recognize what comes from the Lord and what does not. Cultural noise loses a little of its grip.

*"Ignorance of Scripture is ignorance of Christ."* — **St. Jerome**

You are not reading to become a biblical scholar. You are reading to learn Christ's face, voice, and mind — so that when He speaks, you know Him.
