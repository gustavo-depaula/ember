## Weekly, Monthly, Yearly

### Weekly

- **Sunday Mass** — the axis of the week. Non-negotiable.
- **Confession** — for many, weekly or biweekly is the right rhythm; for most, once a month. See *How to Make a Good Confession*.
- **A Holy Hour** — one hour before the Blessed Sacrament if possible. *"Could you not watch one hour with me?"* (Mt 26:40)
- **Fasting / penance** — the traditional Friday, and whatever penance your state bears (a small food fast, a silence fast, a device fast).

### Monthly

- **Day of recollection** — a few hours of silence, prayer, and review of the plan itself. Is it working? Am I keeping it? Has my state of life changed?
- **Confession** (at minimum) — even if nothing grave. Monthly confession is the "shower of the soul" (cf. *How to Make a Good Confession*).
- **Review with a spiritual director** — if you have one. If you do not, pray for one.

### Yearly

- **Annual retreat** — three to eight days in silence, if your state of life permits. Ignatian or Carmelite retreat houses are classic options. If not possible, a full *day* of silent retreat at home or a parish.
- **Marian consecration** (e.g., Montfort's 33-day preparation) — to be done once, then renewed.
- **Major feasts as landmarks** — Christmas and Easter especially, but also Pentecost, Corpus Christi, the patron saint's day, the anniversary of baptism.

### Lifelong

- **Spiritual direction** — a trusted priest who knows you.
- **A fixed Marian devotion** — the Rosary, the Brown Scapular, the Miraculous Medal.
- **A fixed apostolate** — concrete service: teaching catechism, visiting the sick, a work of mercy you own.
