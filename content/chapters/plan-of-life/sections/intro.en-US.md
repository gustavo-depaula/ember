## What a Plan of Life Is

A **plan of life** is a simple, written rule: a small, fixed set of practices you commit to, day in and day out, as the spine of your spiritual life. Daily Mass when possible. A fixed time for mental prayer. Rosary. Examen. Spiritual reading. Confession monthly. Whatever your state of life and season can bear — *and no more*.

It is not a to-do list. It is a **rhythm**. The saints, with astonishing consistency, recommend one — and every serious interior life has had something like it, named or not.

*"Nothing great is ever built without a plan."* — **St. Josemaría Escrivá**, who made the plan of life central to the spirituality of Opus Dei.

St. Benedict gave Western monasticism *The Rule*. St. Francis de Sales gave lay Christians the *Introduction to the Devout Life* — a plan of life for the married, the merchant, the student. The genius of both was the same: holiness does not happen by fits and starts. It happens through fidelity to small, repeated acts.

You are not writing a contract with God. You are building a trellis. The Holy Spirit grows the vine.
