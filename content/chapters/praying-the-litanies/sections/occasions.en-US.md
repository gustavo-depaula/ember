Each litany has its own character and its own natural home in the rhythm of the Catholic year. Here is a guide to when each litany is most fittingly prayed:

**Litany of All Saints** — In times of great need, at the beginning of important undertakings, during Rogation Days, and whenever the Church processes and prays for God's protection. Liturgically, it is sung at the Easter Vigil and at ordinations.

**Litany of Our Lady of Loreto** — During May (the month of Mary) and October (the month of the Rosary), on Marian feast days, and as a daily Marian devotion. It pairs beautifully with the Rosary.

**Litany of the Holy Name of Jesus** — In January (the month of the Holy Name), on the feast of the Holy Name of Jesus, and as a daily prayer of adoration. It is a powerful prayer against temptation and for deepening love of Christ.

**Litany of the Sacred Heart** — On First Fridays, during June (the month of the Sacred Heart), and as a prayer of reparation. It is especially fitting during Eucharistic adoration.

**Litany of St. Joseph** — During March (the month of St. Joseph), on the solemnity of St. Joseph (March 19), on the feast of St. Joseph the Worker (May 1), on Wednesdays (traditionally dedicated to St. Joseph), and for the grace of a holy death.

**Litany of the Precious Blood** — During July (the month of the Precious Blood), on the feast of the Most Precious Blood (July 1), and as a prayer of reparation for sins.

**Litany of Humility** — As a daily prayer for growth in virtue, during examinations of conscience, in spiritual direction, and whenever pride or vanity tempts the heart. Many saints and spiritual directors have recommended it as a regular practice.

**Litany of the Holy Spirit** — During the novena before Pentecost, in preparation for Confirmation, and whenever one seeks the guidance, wisdom, and consolation of the Holy Spirit.

**Litany of the Blessed Sacrament** — During Eucharistic adoration, on Corpus Christi, on Holy Thursday, and as a preparation for or thanksgiving after Holy Communion.
