The capital sins are called "capital" from the Latin *caput*, head — not because they are the worst sins, but because they are *heads* or sources from which other sins flow. A single root, untreated, bears a whole harvest.

The Church's tradition numbers seven: pride, avarice, envy, wrath, lust, gluttony, and sloth (CCC 1866). St. Thomas Aquinas, following Gregory the Great, teaches that each has an opposing virtue — a habit that, cultivated, starves the sin of its soil.

The spiritual life is not merely the avoidance of sin but the deliberate cultivation of its opposite. To fight pride is to practice humility; to fight avarice is to practice liberality. The remedy is never only negative.
