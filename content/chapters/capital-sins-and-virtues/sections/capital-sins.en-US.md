## The Seven Capital Sins

**Pride** (*superbia*). Inordinate love of one's own excellence. The first and deepest sin — the refusal to receive oneself as a gift. Pride hides behind humility, behind service, behind even apparent piety; it is the hardest to see in oneself.

**Avarice** (*avaritia*). Inordinate love of earthly goods, especially money. Not wealth itself but the grasping — the inability to let go, to give, to trust providence.

**Envy** (*invidia*). Sorrow at another's good, as if his flourishing diminished one's own. Envy hates what love rejoices in; it corrodes communion.

**Wrath** (*ira*). Disordered desire for revenge or injury. Not the just anger that hates sin, but the anger that hates the sinner — that lingers, broods, and plots.

**Lust** (*luxuria*). Disordered desire for sexual pleasure detached from its true ends — the self-gift of spouses and the gift of life. Lust uses persons; love honors them.

**Gluttony** (*gula*). Disordered attachment to food and drink — eating not to live but to escape, to numb, to fill a hunger no meal can reach.

**Sloth** (*acedia*). Sorrow at the divine good — a weariness of the things of God, a resistance to the labor of love. The desert Fathers called it the "noonday devil" for its tendency to strike the soul at the middle of its course.
