## The Opposing Virtues

Each capital sin has a corresponding virtue that, practiced deliberately, displaces it.

| Sin | Opposing Virtue |
|-----|-----------------|
| Pride | **Humility** — truth about oneself before God |
| Avarice | **Liberality** — free and generous giving |
| Envy | **Kindness** (or brotherly love) — rejoicing in another's good |
| Wrath | **Patience** (or meekness) — steady love under provocation |
| Lust | **Chastity** — love rightly ordered toward the other's whole good |
| Gluttony | **Temperance** — moderation that frees the soul |
| Sloth | **Diligence** — zeal for the things of God |

St. Paul gives the rule in a word: *"Do not be overcome by evil, but overcome evil with good"* (Rom 12:21). Examination of conscience should not stop at naming the sin — it should move to asking which virtue to plant in its place, and what single act of it I will make today.
