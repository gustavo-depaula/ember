When you come out, the temptation — almost universal — is to now do *everything* at once. A daily Rosary, daily Mass, three novenas, the Divine Office, a strict plan of life, and the resolution never to sin again.

**Don't.** The fervor is real, but the fall will be harder than the return.

Keep it simple. One week at a time. Start with exactly three things:

- Sunday Mass. Every Sunday. Even when it's inconvenient. This is the hinge of the whole Christian life.
- A short daily prayer. The *Our Father* at night, slowly, is enough. Add one decade of the Rosary if that feels natural — not five, not yet.
- A short nightly examen. Two minutes. *Where did I fail today? Where did I love? Give thanks, ask pardon, go to sleep.*

That is a sustainable rule for a soul coming back. It is also — if you are honest — the bar that most of the saints kept for years before doing more.

**And return to confession.** Not in twenty years. Not in five. *Within a month.* Monthly confession is the traditional counsel for serious Catholics; it is also the best antidote to the slow drift that brought you here the first time. Put the date in your calendar before you forget.

*"He that is faithful in that which is least, is faithful also in that which is greater."* (*Luke 16:10*)

The Father who ran to meet you is not finished. He has slain the fatted calf and set the table. You came home. Now stay.
