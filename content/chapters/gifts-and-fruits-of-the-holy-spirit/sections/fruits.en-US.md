## The Twelve Fruits

*"The fruit of the Spirit is charity, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, long-suffering, mildness, faith, modesty, continence, chastity."* — Gal 5:22-23 (Vulgate)

1. **Charity** — love of God above all and of neighbor for his sake.
2. **Joy** — the Spirit's own peace overflowing, even in trial.
3. **Peace** — tranquility of order, the soul settled in God.
4. **Patience** — enduring trial without bitterness.
5. **Kindness** — sweetness in speech and demeanor toward others.
6. **Goodness** — readiness to do good, generously.
7. **Long-suffering** (*longanimity*) — patience stretched over years.
8. **Mildness** — meekness, not easily roused to anger.
9. **Faith** (*faithfulness*) — reliability and trust in dealings with God and neighbor.
10. **Modesty** — restraint that honors the dignity of self and others.
11. **Continence** — self-mastery in desires.
12. **Chastity** — purity of heart and body.

These fruits do not come from striving alone but from abiding: *"He who abides in me, and I in him, bears much fruit"* (Jn 15:5). The examination of fruits is a gentle, long-term one — a year later, am I more patient? more peaceful? more given to kindness? The Spirit's work is rarely dramatic; it is cumulative.
