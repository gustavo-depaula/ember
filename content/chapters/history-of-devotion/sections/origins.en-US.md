The devotion to the Sacred Heart of Jesus, though rooted in the earliest Christian contemplation of the wound in Christ's side, took its definitive form through the mystical experiences of a young Visitandine nun in seventeenth-century France.

**Margaret Mary Alacoque** entered the Monastery of the Visitation at Paray-le-Monial in 1671, at the age of twenty-four. Between 1673 and 1675, she received a series of private revelations in which Our Lord appeared to her and revealed His Heart — burning with love for humanity, yet wounded by ingratitude and indifference.

The first great revelation occurred on the feast of St. John the Evangelist, December 27, 1673. Christ invited Margaret Mary to rest her head upon His Heart, as St. John had done at the Last Supper, and revealed to her the wonders of His love and the desire that these be made known to all the world.
