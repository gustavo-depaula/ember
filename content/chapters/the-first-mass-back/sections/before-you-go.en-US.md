The night before the first Mass back is usually not peaceful. You may find yourself rehearsing how to sit, trying to remember whether you stand or kneel after the Sanctus, wondering whether the parish will notice you, whether anyone will recognize you, whether the priest will say something that undoes you. **None of that is the point.** The point is that you are going. The rehearsal is not required.

A few small counsels, from many who have walked this walk before you.

**Set expectations low.** You are not going to have a great experience. You are not going to feel rivers of grace. You may feel almost nothing, or you may feel too much. What you are going to do is **be there** — and being there, with a baptized soul present in the nave, is already more than your feelings can weigh.

**Do not plan a performance.** You are not going to impress God, and the priest is not grading you. You do not need to know the responses in the new translation. Mumble where you forget. Stand when others stand. Kneel when others kneel. You will pick it up again faster than you think.

**Wear what you would wear to a job interview you are not anxious about.** Not a suit, not sweatpants. Clean, ordinary clothes. This is not a fashion matter; it is a small act of respect that quiets the mind. If dressing well is already a burden on a hard morning, wear whatever lets you actually leave the house. Leaving the house is the sacrament of the morning.

**Arrive five minutes early. Sit in the back.** The back is fine. The back is holy. Half the saints of the last century prayed from the back. From there you can see what everyone is doing, you do not block anyone's sightline, and you can leave without drama if you need to. Nobody is watching you. The few who might notice a new face are glad you came; they are not taking attendance.

**If you cry, you cry.** Bring a handkerchief. Do not be embarrassed. Tears at the first Mass back are ancient and ordinary; they are often the body saying what the mind has not yet caught up to. Let them come. If they do not come, that is also fine. Your return does not depend on tears.

One more thing. Before you leave the house, make a small sign of the cross and say, *"Lord, I am going."* That is the only prayer you need. Everything else, the Church will pray for you.

*"The Lord is my shepherd; I shall not want."* (*Psalm 22:1*)
