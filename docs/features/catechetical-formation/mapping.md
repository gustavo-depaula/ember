## Why some days bundle plates

The Catechism in Pictures uses several plates per commandment (4 plates for the Fourth Commandment, 3 each for the Fifth and Eighth, 3 for the Capital Sins, 3 for the Precepts of the Church). Most continuation plates are short and tightly coupled — the second plate of the Second Commandment, for instance, finishes the same exposition the first plate began. Where two or three continuation plates carry one coherent topic, they are merged into a single 15-minute day. The result:

- **All 68 plates of the source book are referenced** (none dropped).
- **89 reading days**, not 68 — because Compendium-only days are added where the 1908 plates leave a topic thin (faith, Revelation, Tradition and Scripture, the mysteries of Christ's public life, the marks of the Church, the Marian dogmas, the rite of the Mass, the moral foundations, the wellsprings and battle of prayer, the petitions of the Our Father).
- **8 days bundle continuation plates** to stay inside the 15-minute cap without losing any plate:

Days 70–80 follow the *Catechism in Pictures* book ordering (Vanity → Death → Particular Judgment → Original Sin revisited → Capital Sins → Virtues → Works of Mercy) rather than the Compendium's strict Part-3 ordering — the Catechism in Pictures gathers these meditative topics at the end, after the Decalogue, and this program follows that pedagogical sequence. Compendium paragraphs from Part 1 (eschatology), Part 3 (virtues, capital vices), and other parts are cited where relevant on those days.
