## One plate per day

Every plate-day pairs with **a single plate** of the Catechism in Pictures. The 1908 book used "(continuation)" plates whenever a topic needed more space than one page — but each plate stands on its own and was meant to be read by itself. Splitting them keeps each daily reading inside the 15-minute cap once the Compendium citations are added on top.

All 68 plates are covered: 68 plate-days + 30 Compendium-only days = 98 days.

## Compendium ranges by structure

Tracing the Compendium's four parts through the program:

| Compendium part | Range | Days |
|---|---|---|
| Part 1 — The Profession of Faith | §§ 1–217 | 1–32 |
| Part 2 — The Celebration of the Christian Mystery | §§ 218–356 | 33–44 |
| Part 3 — Life in Christ | §§ 357–533 | 45–78 |
| Part 4 — Christian Prayer | §§ 534–598 | 90–98 |

Days 79–89 follow the *Catechism in Pictures* book ordering (Vanity → Death → Particular Judgment → Original Sin revisited → Capital Sins → Virtues → Works of Mercy) rather than the Compendium's strict Part-3 ordering — the Catechism in Pictures gathers these meditative topics at the end, after the Decalogue, and this program follows that pedagogical sequence. Compendium paragraphs from Part 1 (eschatology), Part 3 (virtues, capital vices), and other parts are cited where relevant on those days.
