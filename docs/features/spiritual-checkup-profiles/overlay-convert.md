# Overlay — Convert

> **Not a profile.** Modifies any of 01–09. Triggered by one yes/no at L4.5.

## Signal

L4.5 question (paraphrase): "Were you received into the Catholic Church as an adult from another Christian tradition (Protestant, Orthodox, Anglican)?"

Yes → overlay set.

## What this overlay marks

A soul received as an adult — typically through RCIA or a similar rite — **from a living faith tradition**, not from no faith at all. (Someone with no prior religious background routes to profile 01 at L1; this overlay is not needed there.) Converts bring:

- A real prayer life — sometimes more articulate than many cradle Catholics.
- A real scriptural formation — often *deeper* than the modal Catholic's.
- A sometimes-unresolved relationship with their prior tradition — grief, bitterness, nostalgia, or unfinished theological arguments.
- Distinctive gaps — sacramental vocabulary absorbed intellectually but not yet felt, Marian devotion encountered as an abstraction, a saints-calendar they didn't grow up with.
- Frequently a more *traditional* instinct than the cradle Catholics around them (converts often came in for what the Church is, and find contemporary Catholic life disappointingly thin).

## Modifications to the host profile

1. **Credit the formation they bring.** Scripture reading, personal prayer, the Apostles' Creed — these are not new to them. Starter content should name this: "You came in with real formation. We're not going to recap what you already know."
2. **Marian content as bridge, not immersion.** Converts often struggle with Marian devotion in the first years. Offer a short bridge chapter (hypothetical — see authoring notes) before the Rosary becomes a primary starter practice. The `how-to-pray-the-rosary-well` chapter is suitable, but a pre-read is valuable.
3. **Sacramental catechesis, even for those who think they have it.** Transubstantiation is easy to assent to intellectually and slow to *feel* as the center of the day. Offer `what-the-mass-is` even to converts who would mark themselves "well-formed."
4. **The confession move.** Converts often have no practical experience of sacramental confession. The `how-to-make-a-good-confession` chapter is nearly universal in the first year.
5. **Saint discovery.** The Catholic calendar is unfamiliar. Recommend encountering saints *liturgically* — let the calendar surface them — rather than through topical saint biographies that miss the rhythm.
6. **Honor the grief of leaving.** Do not mock, caricature, or dismiss the tradition the convert left. Even if its formal positions are wrong, the grace received through it was real, and the convert's memory of it is often tender. Any starter copy that treats Protestantism/Orthodoxy dismissively will fail this profile.
7. **Language of "completion," not "conversion-from."** The Catholic framing, especially for ex-Protestants, is that what they already had has been brought to fullness, not that they abandoned it. Small language choices carry this.

## Warnings specific to the overlay

- **The "professional convert" trap.** A small minority turn their conversion story into an identity and a vocation — apologist-converts, speakers, podcasters. This can be a real service, but it can also trap the soul in explaining her move rather than living into her destination. The app can honor the conversion without feeding the identity; copy should generally talk about *the Catholic life to come*, not *the return from elsewhere*.
- **The Orthodox case is different.** Ex-Orthodox converts to the Catholic Church are rare and often come with particular sensitivities — the filioque, papal authority, icons, married priesthood. Do not pretend these disputes don't exist; do not re-litigate them.
- **Unfinished marriage canonical questions.** Many adult converts come with prior marriages, annulments in process, situations requiring a pastor's help. The checkup cannot navigate these; the overlay copy should name the possibility and point toward pastoral care without probing.
- **The Rosary timing issue.** Starting daily Rosary in the first year of conversion is often premature. Offering an Angelus (short, Scriptural, Christocentric) first is frequently the better move — it is a Marian practice built on a Gospel event rather than on doctrinal Marian content. `base/practices/angelus` exists for this.

## Reading for the overlay

- *The Soul of the Apostolate* (Chautard) — not convert-specific, but addresses the subtle danger of an active Catholic life lacking interior fuel; many converts are more activist than contemplative.
- Short selections from *Newman's Apologia* — for the ex-Protestant who wants a companion to their own move.
- A *Catechism-centered* approach, not an *apologetics-centered* one — for the first year. Converts coming in through apologetics benefit from being pointed back to the prayer of the Church.
- Frank Sheed's *Theology and Sanity* or *A Map of Life* — short, formative, not apologetic in tone, and widely accessible.
