# 06 — The Sacramental Baseline

> **Primary archetype:** Disciple
> **Secondary (tension pair):** `{ Seeker — "there are gaps I want to shore up", Grower — "I want to go deeper than I have" }`
> **Overlays possible:** scrupulosity (moderate), convert (moderate), traditional-leaning (moderate)

## Who this is

A soul who **attends Sunday Mass reliably, goes to confession several times a year, and prays occasionally** — morning/evening vocal prayers, the Rosary now and then, a novena when a specific need arises. The faith is present and practiced, not an ornament and not a performance. The defining feature is *steadiness at the foundation* without yet a structured interior life above it.

This is probably the **modal profile** — the most common single answer — for cradle Catholics in the middle of life, adults who were catechized reasonably well, married, raising a family or settled in work. They are not in crisis, not in conversion, not dry, not falling. They are the parish. They are here because they sense (or a friend suggested) that there is more to Catholic interior life than what they are doing, and they want a gentle on-ramp.

Signals: "I go to Mass every Sunday" — "I pray the Rosary sometimes" — "I'd like to pray more but I don't know how" — "my kids are at a Catholic school / I teach CCD" — "I'm fine, but I want to grow."

## Route through the tree

L1=D ("I practice regularly") → L2 branches on prayer life. This profile is reached by **"Occasional — Mass, maybe a Rosary sometimes."**

L3 asks: "How often do you examine your conscience?" The answer routes to the emphasis of the starter track:

- **Rarely / never** → Disciple (examen-starter) — lead with the examen as the first new habit.
- **Sometimes, before confession** → Disciple (confession-starter) — lead with a monthly confession rhythm and a short daily examen.
- **Most days** → Disciple (mental-prayer-starter) — they are ready for mental prayer; lead there.

L4 tension pair picks the secondary.

## Starting tension

The sacramental baseline soul's characteristic mistake is to **treat the current rhythm as either sufficient (complacency) or insufficient (scrupulosity)**, and to move in one of those two directions rather than deepening. The correction is to say plainly: *the rhythm you have is a real foundation, and the next step is specifically a small upgrade on it — not a replacement, not an indictment.*

This profile responds well to **one new practice per month**, not per week. The temptation to over-offer is strong (the catalog is large, the soul is agreeable) and the cost of overwhelm is a quiet fade.

## What to offer first

- **Morning offering** — `base/practices/morning-offering`. Under a minute. Anchors the day.
- **Night examen** — five minutes, based on `base/chapters/ignatian-examen`. Introduce in week 1 or 2 depending on L3.
- **Daily Rosary** — one decade minimum. Full Rosary if the user already does the occasional one, with the day-of-week mystery rotation. `base/chapters/how-to-pray-the-rosary-well` is the formation read.
- **Monthly confession** as the new baseline if not already. `base/chapters/how-to-make-a-good-confession` — week 2 read.
- **One daily Gospel passage**, short. The `scripture-*` scheme-practices in base provide a structured reading sequence if desired, but a simple "one chapter every three days" is fine.
- **`base/chapters/plan-of-life`** — week 3 or 4 read. Introduces the framework the app is built around.

## What NOT to offer first

- **Not the full Liturgy of the Hours.** Introduce Compline or Vespers later, one hour at a time.
- **Not a large book-length reading.** *Introduction to the Devout Life* is the classic prescription here and is well-suited eventually, but reading it in parallel with adopting three new practices is too much. Offer it after the rhythm is stable, month 3+.
- **Not mental prayer of any length in week 1.** Mental prayer belongs at weeks 3–4 once the foundation is steadier.
- **Not consecration practices immediately.** Montfortian consecration is a strong candidate for month 4 or 5 — but after the rhythm is real.
- **Not multi-devotion onboarding.** Chaplet of Divine Mercy, Stations, Sacred Heart consecration — these are all good; picking one and saving the rest for later is the whole skill.

## Two-week shape

**Week 1 — "one more anchor":**
- Morning: add the Morning Offering (1 min).
- Evening: keep existing practice; add 5 minutes of examen (`base/chapters/ignatian-examen` is the method).
- Chapter: `ignatian-examen`.
- Sunday Mass (existing).
- One Rosary decade.

**Week 2 — "the rhythm forming":**
- Same morning/evening.
- Chapter: `how-to-pray-the-rosary-well`.
- Move to one full Rosary per day OR (if that is too much) keep one decade + a Gospel passage.
- `how-to-make-a-good-confession` read; schedule monthly confession.

**Weeks 3–4:**
- Introduce 10 minutes of mental prayer (morning or evening, whichever is calmer).
- Chapter: `mental-prayer`.
- Read `plan-of-life` and adopt the app's Plan of Life feature — fill in daily/weekly/monthly norms.

**Month 2+:**
- Stable rhythm review. Consider adding: a weekly Adoration hour, Compline, a devotional Saturday (Mary), First Friday (Sacred Heart), etc. One at a time.

## Pastoral warnings

- **The "overload and fade" pattern.** Every app and every retreat tends to offer this profile twelve practices and lose them at three. Ruthless pacing is a feature. If the track produces a stable rhythm of four small practices after eight weeks, that is a *success*, not a floor.
- **The "I'm fine" plateau.** The soul who is steadily doing what this profile prescribes for a year without adding anything is not necessarily plateaued — consistency over novelty is a mark of growth. But after ~12–18 months at steady baseline, Grower territory is ready, and the checkup should suggest retaking if offered.
- **Family and state-of-life integration.** This profile most often has small children, a spouse, a job that wants attention. The starter track must honor this: 5 minutes actually said beats 30 minutes aspired to. `plan-of-life` chapter already addresses this explicitly.
- **The scrupulosity risk.** Some baseline souls, once given an examen, become scrupulous — reviewing the day for minor offenses and internalizing them as grave. Apply the scrupulosity overlay.
