# 01 — The Curious Unbaptized

> **Primary archetype:** Quaerens
> **Secondary (tension pair):** `{ Redivivus — "I've been Catholic-adjacent before", Discipulus — "I want structure from day one" }`
> **Overlays possible:** convert (if from another Christian tradition), scrupulosity rare but possible

## Who this is

A soul who has **not been baptized**, or is not sure whether they have been. They are here because something is pulling them — a person, a book, a grief, a conversion story, or an unnameable longing. They may have been raised secular, or in a Protestant denomination they no longer find sufficient, or culturally exposed to Catholicism without ever entering it. They often arrive with surprisingly specific knowledge about one narrow thing (the Rosary, the Sacred Heart, St. Francis, Mary) and almost nothing about the wider structure of the faith.

## Route through the tree

L1=A ("I'm not baptized / I'm not sure I've been baptized"). The question is short because this answer is definite for those it applies to. No L2 or L3 — we go directly to L4 (tension pair). Total: consent + L1 + L4 = 3 questions.

## Starting tension

This profile is *almost always* mis-treated by apps and well-meaning guides in two opposite ways at once:
1. **Too much, too fast.** Novenas, consecration, rule of life — things that presume a baptized, catechized soul.
2. **Too little, too slowly.** "Come back when you've been through RCIA" — which turns away the very moment of drawing.

The pastoral sweet spot is **one real practice they can do today** plus **a warm, specific direction to an actual parish priest**.

## What to offer first

- **The Our Father, prayed slowly, once a day.** Not as a rote warm-up — as *the* prayer the Lord gave to name God as Father. The catechumenate itself has historically been shaped around the Our Father and the Creed.
- **A single Gospel passage a day, starting with Mark.** Short chapters, fast pace, plain speech. Matthew is for later; John is for later. Mark first.
- **The "How to Pray" chapter from `base/chapters/how-to-pray/`** — it assumes no prior formation.
- **A strong, warm nudge to find a parish and speak to a priest.** The checkup cannot baptize. RCIA exists. Tell them plainly.

## What NOT to offer first

- **Not the Rosary yet.** The Rosary without the Gospels is beads without story. Offer it in week 3 after Mark is familiar.
- **Not mental prayer / lectio / the examen.** These assume a Christian interior life that is not yet present. Offering them at this stage produces either intimidation or a counterfeit interior life built on self-reflection rather than on Christ.
- **Not any consecration (Montfortian, Sacred Heart, etc.).** These are for the baptized. Offering them now risks a sentimental response that is not sacramental.
- **Not the Divine Office.** Not yet. The Office without the sacraments is a literary exercise.
- **Not catechism reading plans covering the whole CCC.** The compendium form might work later; right now the CCC in full is the wrong length.

## Two-week shape

**Week 1:**
- Morning: Our Father, said slowly (2 min).
- Evening: 5 verses of Mark (start at 1:1).
- One chapter read from `base/chapters/`: `how-to-pray` or `what-the-mass-is`.
- Action: find a local Catholic parish's website; note its Mass times.

**Week 2:**
- Same morning Our Father.
- Evening Mark continues (5–10 verses).
- One additional chapter: `seven-sacraments`.
- Action: attend a Sunday Mass (sit in back, no reception — the user will not know what "reception" means; this is addressed in the chapter).

At week 3, the primary next action is **contact a priest about RCIA**. The app's job becomes secondary from that point.

## Pastoral warnings

- **Do not allow the app to substitute for a priest.** The fail-mode is a catechumen who prays a daily Rosary for two years and never goes to a parish. Every leaf action in weeks 1–2 should nudge toward an actual Catholic in an actual place.
- **Beware syncretism.** Some in this profile come from exposure to meditation, yoga, astrology, Wicca-adjacent practice. The "How to Pray" chapter names Christian prayer as *not* a technique; be gentle but clear.
- **Watch for the aesthetic trap.** Catholicism is beautiful. Souls can mistake aesthetic attraction for conversion. Aesthetic attraction is a *gift* (Augustine's "late have I loved thee") — but its completion is sacramental, not curatorial.
- **Do not use language of "already Catholic."** A soul who has not been baptized is not yet Catholic, and telling them they already are cheapens both their future baptism and the truth. Use language of *drawing* and *seeking* — they are Quaerens in the most literal sense.

## Notes for the chapter author

If more chapters are written for this profile specifically, candidates:
- **"Who God Is" / "Who Jesus Is"** — very short, prayer-shaped, not apologetic.
- **"How to Find a Parish"** — practical.
- **"What RCIA Is, and Why It's Worth the Year"** — addressing the common objection that it takes too long.
