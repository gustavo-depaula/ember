# Overlay — Scrupulosity

> **Not a profile.** Modifies any of 01–09. Triggered by one yes/no at L4.5.
> **Pastoral note:** scrupulosity has been recognized as a distinct spiritual affliction since at least St. Alphonsus Liguori, who wrote at length on it and named it as a disorder of conscience rather than a virtue.

## Signal

L4.5 question (paraphrase for the eventual copy): "Do you ever worry excessively about sin, or return to confess the same thing many times because you're not sure the last confession 'counted'?"

Yes → overlay set. Subsequent copy shifts.

## What this overlay marks

A soul who is **more likely to receive any spiritual direction as an additional weight than as a relief**. The characteristic pattern:

- Re-confessing sins already absolved.
- Reading every pastoral guideline as tighter than the Church teaches it.
- Inability to make a decision about minor matters without fearing sin.
- Hours spent in examination for a 20-minute examen.
- Reading about the faults of saints and internalizing them as commands for oneself.
- Taking isolated harsh passages (from Escrivá, or the desert fathers, or older manuals) as the whole teaching.

This is genuine suffering, frequently rooted in temperament, sometimes comorbid with OCD or anxiety. It is not holy caution. The saints who struggled with it (Thérèse, early Ignatius, Alphonsus Liguori himself) named it as an enemy.

## Modifications to the host profile

Regardless of which profile this overlay rides on:

1. **Reduce, do not add.** The overlay-set version of any track offers *fewer* practices, not more. One new thing per month at most.
2. **Specific language adjustments.** Strip words like "fight," "combat," "ascetical discipline" from copy where reasonable alternatives exist. Replace with "companionship," "trust," "letting God do the work."
3. **Confession rhythm.** Whatever the host profile says about confession, this overlay caps it: *not more than every two weeks*. A scrupulous soul going to confession more frequently than that is, with rare exception, feeding the affliction.
4. **No examen in week 1.** Delay the examen by at least 2 weeks past the host profile's recommendation. When introduced, it is cap-limited to 3 minutes and phrased as gratitude + one thing to offer tomorrow — not as sin-enumeration.
5. **No "predominant fault" work.** Classically, scrupulous souls cannot do this work unaided without intensifying the disorder. It is direction-only territory.
6. **Shorter chapter list.** Prune the host profile's chapter list to the most consoling; defer ascetical material.
7. **Spiritual direction is named as necessary, not optional.** More forcefully than in non-overlay profiles.
8. **A specific reading included: Alphonsus on scrupulosity.** Short and direct.

## Warnings specific to the overlay

- **"Just tell them to stop being scrupulous" is not the answer.** The scrupulous soul can't stop any more than an anxious person can stop being anxious by being told to. Copy must be warm, not corrective.
- **Harsh spiritual literature is actively dangerous.** Older manuals (pre-1960) sometimes use language that reads as demanding to a scrupulous soul in a way the authors did not intend. Even classics like *The Spiritual Combat* can be weaponized against oneself. Curate.
- **The director is essential.** Name it. A parish priest who knows the user *as a person* is worth more here than any content the app can offer. If a priest is not available, suggest a trusted confessor encountered regularly (even without formal direction).
- **Medical comorbidity.** OCD is common comorbid with scrupulosity and responds to specific clinical treatment. A single sentence: "If this sounds like how you experience daily non-religious decisions as well, please also consider speaking with a doctor or therapist. Grace works through nature."
- **Apps amplify scrupulosity.** Streaks, completion percentages, reminders about missed practices — all fuel the affliction. For any profile with this overlay, the app must quiet its own gamification. Currently none is aggressive; keep it that way.

## Which profiles does this most often attach to

In rough order:
- **03 Lapsed Returner** — very common; the return triggers over-confession.
- **02 Nominal Catholic** — common once catechesis begins and the moral framework sharpens.
- **04 Apologetics Nerd** — occasional; intellectualized scrupulosity (fixating on fine doctrinal distinctions).
- **07 Committed Practitioner** — occasional; scrupulosity dressed as zeal.
- **06 Sacramental Baseline** — occasional; often triggered by a new examen habit.
- Rarely on 01, 05, 08, 09.

## Reading for the overlay

- St. Alphonsus Liguori, short selections on scrupulosity (from his pastoral writings; short passages, not full treatises).
- Thérèse of Lisieux, *Story of a Soul* — her own scruples and how she was freed of them are a direct pastoral resource.
- Padre Pio's letters on the subject are also strong.
- **Avoid**: full *Spiritual Combat*, full *Ignatian Exercises* without direction, long examen manuals, anything on mortification.
