# Novenário — source comparison (FishEaters vs PrayMoreNovenas)

> Working doc to choose a text source per novena **before** importing into Ember. 
Fill the **Preferred** column with `FE` (FishEaters — traditional, largely public-domain), `PMN` (PrayMoreNovenas — modern, copyrighted), or `orig` (author original). Leave blank to skip.

## Summary

- **Book novenas:** 83 — on FishEaters: **50**, on PrayMoreNovenas: **70**, on **both: 46**, on **neither: 9**.
- **FishEaters total:** 115 novenas · **PrayMoreNovenas total:** 505 novenas.
- **FishEaters novenas beyond the book:** 65 (Section B).
- **PrayMoreNovenas long tail** (not in book, not on FishEaters): ~402 (Appendix).

**Structure signal:** PrayMoreNovenas is uniformly *9 distinct daily prayers* (modern compositions). FishEaters varies — many are a *single traditional prayer repeated 9 days* (often public-domain), some have *distinct days*. The FE column notes which.

## My recommendation per overlap (45 novenas evaluated)

Both full texts were read and scored against the rubric below. **Result: FishEaters 33, PrayMoreNovenas 8, 4 ties.** FishEaters wins most because it carries the *established, often indulgenced* traditional prayer of each saint; PrayMoreNovenas wins where no historic novena exists (modern saints) or where its distinct 9-day build clearly outweighs a bare FE collect.

**Rubric (priority order):** 1) fidelity & provenance (traditional/indulgenced > ad-hoc modern; also better licensing) · 2) saint-specific spiritual substance · 3) structure for a 9-day journey (distinct days, unless the single repeated prayer is itself a rich classic) · 4) theological soundness & reverent tone (gate) · 5) literary beauty · 6) practical completeness. Criteria 1–4 decide; 5–6 break ties.
