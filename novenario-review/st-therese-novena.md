# St. Thérèse (Rose Novena) — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** St. Therese, Little Flower of Jesus, teach me your Little Way of trust, humility, and love. Present my intentions to the Lord and help me offer each moment with generous charity. Amen.

**Day 1 — Spiritual childhood and confidence in God:** St. Therese teaches that holiness begins by becoming little before God. Like a child in a father's arms, we entrust ourselves to divine mercy with simplicity and peace. _(Intention: For childlike trust in God's fatherly love.)_

**Day 2 — Humility and littleness:** Therese did not seek greatness in the eyes of the world, but the hidden place where God is found. True humility receives everything from the Lord and gives Him all the glory. _(Intention: For a humble heart free from pride and self-seeking.)_

**Day 3 — Hidden charity in ordinary duties:** In Carmel, Therese sanctified small tasks and quiet sacrifices. She reminds us that ordinary duties become extraordinary when done with patient and generous love. _(Intention: For fidelity and charity in daily responsibilities.)_

**Day 4 — Love for Jesus in prayer and sacrifice:** Therese offered prayer, suffering, and joy to console the Heart of Jesus. She shows us that every sacrifice united to Christ can become an act of pure love. _(Intention: For deeper love of Jesus in prayer and sacrifice.)_

**Day 5 — Trust through dryness and suffering:** Even in spiritual darkness and physical suffering, Therese trusted God without reserve. Her witness teaches us to cling to Jesus when we feel weak, dry, or forgotten. _(Intention: For perseverance and trust in every trial.)_

**Day 6 — Merciful love and conversion of sinners:** Therese prayed fervently for souls far from God, especially those in danger of despair. She invites us to intercede with confidence in the triumph of divine mercy. _(Intention: For conversion of sinners and return of the fallen away.)_

**Day 7 — Zeal for priests, missionaries, and souls:** Though hidden in cloister, Therese became Patroness of the Missions through prayer and sacrifice. She teaches us that contemplative love strengthens the Church's evangelizing mission. _(Intention: For priests, missionaries, and all who labor for the Gospel.)_

**Day 8 — Joyful fidelity in daily life:** Therese lived with spiritual joy rooted in love, not in comfort. She encourages us to embrace each day with gratitude, turning small acts into offerings pleasing to God. _(Intention: For joyful fidelity in family, work, and prayer.)_

**Day 9 — Hopeful perseverance under St. Therese's intercession:** At the end of this novena, we entrust our future to God with hope. With St. Therese, we ask for the grace of final perseverance and a life wholly offered in love. _(Intention: For final perseverance and holiness of life.)_

**Closing prayer:** St. Therese of Lisieux, Little Flower, obtain for us the grace to do small things with great love, to trust Jesus in every trial, and to persevere in holiness. Pray for us now and at the hour of our death. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas opens each day with the Sign of the Cross and prays the same core prayer every day: "Dearest Saint Therese of Lisieux, you said that you would spend your time in heaven doing good on earth. Your trust in God was complete. Pray that He may increase my trust in His goodness and mercy as I ask for the following petitions…" — then "(State your intentions)" — then "Pray for me that I, like you, may have great and innocent confidence in the loving promises of our God." Each day adds a short day-specific aspiration: Day 1 "I love you, Lord. Help me to love you more!"; Day 2 "I trust you, Lord. Help me to trust you more!"; Day 3 "I see you, Lord. Help me to see you more!"; Day 4 "I am humble, Lord. Give me more humility!"; Day 5 "I try to forgive, Lord. Help me to forgive 70 times 7 times!"; Day 6 "I accept your will, Lord. Help me to accept your will every day!"; Day 7 "I rely on you, Lord. Help me to rely on you more!"; Day 8 "I reflect you to the world, Lord. Help me to reflect you more clearly!"; Day 9 "I love your people, Lord. Help me to love them more!"

## Fidelity note
Ember's St. Thérèse novena is an original composition, not a transcription of PrayMoreNovenas (the only source available; it is not on FishEaters). It does not use PMN's repeated "Dearest Saint Therese… spend your time in heaven doing good on earth" prayer or the daily "Help me to love/trust/forgive…" aspirations. Instead it builds nine themed meditations around authentic Theresian topics — the Little Way, spiritual childhood, humility, hidden charity, trust through dryness, mercy for sinners, zeal for the missions — which are faithful to her spirituality and overlap thematically with PMN (trust, humility, forgiveness/mercy). The result is doctrinally sound and well-aligned with St. Thérèse's "Story of a Soul" themes, but wording-wise it is a fresh paraphrase rather than the familiar Rose Novena text.
