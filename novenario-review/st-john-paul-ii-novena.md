# St. John Paul II — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** Loving Father, who gave St. John Paul II to Your Church as a fearless witness to Christ, grant us through his intercession the courage to open wide the doors of our hearts to the Redeemer. Help us, like him, to belong entirely to Jesus through Mary. Amen.

**Day 1 — Be not afraid!:** At the beginning of his pontificate, John Paul II cried out to a fearful world, 'Be not afraid! Open wide the doors to Christ!' He teaches us that fear loses its power over a heart that has welcomed the Redeemer.  _(Intention: For the courage to open every part of our lives to Christ.)_  (every day)

**Day 2 — Totus Tuus — wholly Mary's:** John Paul II took as his motto 'Totus Tuus' — 'wholly Yours, Mary.' He shows us that consecration to Our Lady does not lessen our love for Christ, but carries us straight to her Son.  _(Intention: For the grace to entrust ourselves entirely to Mary.)_  (every day)

**Day 3 — Defender of every human life:** In an age that grew cold to the weakest, John Paul II proclaimed the Gospel of Life, defending the child in the womb and the dying alike. He reminds us that every person, from conception to natural death, bears the image of God.  _(Intention: For the protection of human life at every stage and a renewed culture of life.)_  (every day)

**Day 4 — The beauty of married love:** Through the Theology of the Body, John Paul II revealed that the human body itself speaks of God's love and the gift of self. He restored to marriage and chastity their true splendor as paths to holiness.  _(Intention: For the strengthening of marriages and families in self-giving love.)_  (every day)

**Day 5 — Apostle of Divine Mercy:** John Paul II canonized St. Faustina and gave the world Divine Mercy Sunday, urging souls to trust in God's boundless forgiveness. Fittingly, he himself died on its vigil, commending the Church to the mercy he had preached.  _(Intention: For deep trust in God's mercy and the grace to extend mercy to others.)_  (every day)

**Day 6 — A father to the young:** John Paul II loved the young and gathered millions of them at World Youth Days, challenging them to be saints. He believed in the goodness hidden in every young heart and called them to settle for nothing less than greatness in God.  _(Intention: For young people, that they may discover their call to holiness.)_  (every day)

**Day 7 — Forgiveness and peace:** After surviving an assassin's bullet, John Paul II went to the prison to forgive the man who shot him. He showed the world that mercy is stronger than vengeance and that peace begins in a reconciled heart.  _(Intention: For the grace to forgive those who have wronged us and to be peacemakers.)_  (every day)

**Day 8 — Suffering offered to God:** In his final years, the once-vigorous pope grew frail and bore his illness before the eyes of the whole world. He turned weakness into a pulpit, teaching that suffering united to Christ has redeeming power.  _(Intention: For the grace to unite our sufferings to the cross of Christ.)_  (every day)

**Day 9 — Let me go to the Father's house:** As death drew near, John Paul II whispered his desire to go to the house of the Father, serene in the hope he had always preached. His holy passing crowned a life spent entirely for Christ and His Church.  _(Intention: For the grace to live and die in joyful hope of eternal life.)_  (every day)

**Closing prayer:** St. John Paul II, friend of the young and defender of life, you taught us that man cannot live without love. Obtain for us a heart wholly given to Christ, unwavering hope in His mercy, and the strength to proclaim the Gospel without fear. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
Nine days on one repeated core prayer with a single changing petition. Each day: "In the name of the Father..." → "Most holy servant of God, Pope St. John Paul II, we pray today for the youth and for World Youth Day." → "We ask you to grant us your blessing from heaven!" → "Most holy St. John Paul II, you were a witness of Jesus Christ for the whole world." → a day-specific "Pray for us, that..." petition → "Hail Mary… Glory be… In the name of the Father..." Daily petitions: (1) the unhappy filled with joy; (2) convey forgiveness to those who have done wrong; (3) guided/protected on difficult paths; (4) grace to see goodness in our experiences; (5) bear witness to God's mercy; (6) share faith with those in doubt; (7) give hope to the discouraged; (8) love those who feel indifferent; (9) the love sparked in us transforms hearts.

## Fidelity note
Both are nine-day novenas to JPII, but they are independent compositions. PrayMoreNovenas repeats one short core prayer (heavily centered on youth / World Youth Day) with a rotating petition line. Ember instead builds a distinct biographical meditation for each day that traces the actual landmarks of his life and teaching — "Be not afraid," Totus Tuus, the Gospel of Life, the Theology of the Body, Divine Mercy (and his death on its vigil), World Youth Days, his forgiveness of his would-be assassin, his final illness, and his death. Ember's version is substantially fuller and more historically specific; the only real overlap with PMN is the shared devotion to the young (Ember's Day 6) and the general theme of mercy. Faithful in spirit, clearly an original work rather than a reproduction.
