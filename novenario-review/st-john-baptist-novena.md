# Novena to St. John the Baptist — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** Glorious St. John the Baptist, voice crying in the wilderness, you prepared the way of the Lord with fearless preaching and a pure heart. As I begin this novena, obtain for me a spirit of repentance and a love of the truth. Help me to point others always to Christ. Amen.

**Day 1 — The voice in the wilderness:** St. John went out into the desert, eating locusts and wild honey, that nothing might dull his hearing of God. He teaches us that the soul must be emptied of noise before it can speak God's word. _(Intention: For the grace of silence and solitude in which to hear God's voice.)_ (every day)

**Day 2 — Preparing the way of the Lord:** He called the people to make straight the paths, leveling the high places of pride and filling the valleys of despair. His whole life was to clear the road by which the Savior would come. _(Intention: That I may remove from my heart whatever blocks the coming of Christ.)_ (every day)

**Day 3 — A call to repentance:** John baptized in the Jordan, urging hearts to turn from sin and bear worthy fruit. He reminds us that conversion is not a single moment but a daily turning back to God. _(Intention: For true contrition and a steadfast purpose of amendment.)_ (every day)

**Day 4 — Behold the Lamb of God:** Seeing Jesus approach, John cried, 'Behold the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world.' His joy was to disappear behind the One he announced. _(Intention: That I may recognize Christ present in the Eucharist and in the poor.)_ (every day)

**Day 5 — He must increase, I must decrease:** When his own disciples grew jealous of Jesus, John answered serenely, 'He must increase, but I must decrease.' He shows us the freedom of the soul that desires only God's glory. _(Intention: For deliverance from vanity and the desire to be seen.)_ (every day)

**Day 6 — Humility before Christ:** John confessed he was unworthy to loosen the strap of the Lord's sandal, though crowds came to him as a prophet. True greatness, he teaches, knows its place before God. _(Intention: For a humble heart that takes the lowest place gladly.)_ (every day)

**Day 7 — Courage to speak the truth:** John did not flatter the powerful, but told Herod plainly that it was unlawful to take his brother's wife. He teaches that charity sometimes requires the courage to wound in order to heal. _(Intention: For the courage to speak the truth with charity and without fear.)_ (every day)

**Day 8 — Faithful in prison:** Cast into Herod's dungeon, John sent his disciples to Jesus and waited in the darkness without bitterness. He teaches us to hold fast to faith even when God's purposes seem hidden. _(Intention: For perseverance in trials and trust when God seems distant.)_ (every day)

**Day 9 — Martyr for the truth:** John was beheaded for the sake of righteousness, sealing his witness with his blood. The Forerunner who prepared the way in life now leads the way of the martyrs to the throne of God. _(Intention: For the grace of final perseverance and fidelity unto death.)_ (every day)

**Closing prayer:** St. John the Baptist, who decreased that Christ might increase, teach me to seek not my own glory but His. Strengthen me to bear witness to the truth even at great cost, and to await the Lamb of God with a watchful heart. Intercede for me, that I may share one day in your joy. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
FishEaters carries a traditional Novena to St. John the Baptist (https://www.fisheaters.com/novenas.html#baptist), prayed over nine days especially June 15–23 (eve of the Nativity of St. John). Each day has its own prayer, each followed by "Pray one Pater, one Ave, and one Gloria." Sample texts: Day One — "O glorious St. John the Baptist, of those born of women the greatest of prophets; thou... didst nevertheless will to retire into the desert, there to practise austerities and penance; obtain for us... the grace to be detached... from all earthly treasure, and to practise Christian mortification with interior recollection and a spirit of holy prayer." Day Two — "O most zealous Apostle, who, without working any miracle on others, but solely by the example of thy life of penance and the power of thy words, didst draw after thee crowds... to worthily receive the Messiah..." Day Three — "Unconquered martyr, who for the honour of God and the salvation of souls, didst, with heroic constancy, and at the cost of life itself, withstand the impious Herod..." Closing each day: V. "Pray for us, St John the Baptist." R. "That we may be made worthy of the promises of Christ," followed by the collect "O God, Who hast made this day worthy of honour in the nativity of St John..."

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas has a Saint John the Baptist novena (https://www.praymorenovenas.com/saint-john-the-baptist-novena), Trinitarian opening, a unique daily meditation and intention, a closing petition ("Lord Jesus, Your cousin John prepared the way for Your coming..."), "St. John the Baptist, pray for us!", and a Trinitarian blessing. Daily themes there lean on the infancy narrative and ministry: Day 1 miraculous conception; Day 2 recognition of Christ in the womb / protecting the unborn; Day 3 his naming and Zechariah's restored speech; Day 4 desert preparation and repentance; Day 5 Jesus's baptism and John's humility; Day 6 "Behold the Lamb of God"; Day 7 the forerunner / humble rejoicing in God's glory; Day 8 repeats Day 7; Day 9 the greatest prophet.

## Fidelity note
Ember's text is an original composition, not a copy of either source. It is closest to the FishEaters novena in spirit — both stress the desert/penance, fearless rebuke of Herod, and martyrdom — though the wording is entirely Ember's and does not reproduce FishEaters' archaic "thou/didst" prayers. Against PrayMoreNovenas the structural match is strong (fixed opening + unique daily meditation + intention), but the day themes diverge: PrayMoreNovenas weights the infancy narrative, whereas Ember organizes around John's mission and Gospel sayings ("make straight the paths," "Behold the Lamb of God," "He must increase, I must decrease"). All meditations are scripturally accurate; a faithful, well-grounded original.
