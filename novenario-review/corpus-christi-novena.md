# Novena for Corpus Christi — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O Jesus, truly present in the Most Blessed Sacrament, we kneel before You hidden under the veil of bread. Saving Victim who opens wide the gate of heaven, we adore You with all our heart. Increase in us faith, hope, and love for this gift of Your abiding presence. Amen.

**Day 1 — The Real Presence:** Under the humble appearances of bread and wine, the whole Christ is truly, really, and substantially present — Body and Blood, soul and divinity. The eyes are deceived, but faith alone suffices to make the heart sure. Here is the same Jesus born of Mary and risen from the tomb. _(Intention: For a living and unshakable faith in the Real Presence of Christ.)_

**Day 2 — The gift of the Last Supper:** On the night before He suffered, Jesus took bread, gave thanks, and gave Himself to His apostles, commanding them to do this in His memory. From that hour the Church has never ceased to obey. Every Mass makes present anew the love of that upper room. _(Intention: For deep gratitude for the gift of the Eucharist instituted at the Last Supper.)_

**Day 3 — Adoration before the Host:** As St. Thomas Aquinas sang, we devoutly adore the hidden God who lies truly concealed beneath these figures. To kneel in silence before the tabernacle is to keep company with the King of heaven. Adoration asks nothing but to love and be loved. _(Intention: For the grace to adore the Blessed Sacrament with reverent love.)_

**Day 4 — Thanksgiving for so great a gift:** The Eucharist is itself thanksgiving, the great act of praise that the Church offers to the Father through the Son. For God to give Himself as our food surpasses every other gift He could bestow. A grateful heart is the first response to such love. _(Intention: For a heart overflowing with thanksgiving for the Holy Eucharist.)_

**Day 5 — Reparation for irreverence:** So great a Sacrament is often received coldly, forgotten, or profaned by the world. With love we seek to console the Heart of Jesus and to make amends for the indifference shown to Him. Reparation is the work of friends who will not leave their Lord neglected. _(Intention: For the grace to make reparation for sins and indifference toward the Eucharist.)_

**Day 6 — Bread of life and food for the journey:** Jesus declared Himself the living bread come down from heaven, that whoever eats this bread may live forever. Holy Communion is the strength of pilgrims, sustaining us along the road to eternal life. We do not walk to heaven on our own strength but on His. _(Intention: For the grace to draw strength from Holy Communion for the journey of life.)_

**Day 7 — Worthy reception of Holy Communion:** To receive the Lord worthily, the soul must come cleansed by repentance and adorned with charity. A pure heart welcomes its King as into a prepared and festive home. Let us never approach the altar carelessly, but with reverence and longing. _(Intention: For the grace to receive Holy Communion always in the state of grace.)_

**Day 8 — Union with Christ in Communion:** In Holy Communion the Lord enters into us so that we may abide in Him and He in us. This is the most intimate union possible on earth, a foretaste of the union of heaven. We become what we receive, members ever more deeply of His Body. _(Intention: For the grace of ever deeper union with Christ in the Eucharist.)_

**Day 9 — Pledge of future glory:** The Eucharist is the pledge of the glory that is to come, the seed of the resurrection planted within us. What we now receive under sacramental veils we hope one day to behold unveiled in heaven. The Bread of angels prepares us for the banquet that has no end. _(Intention: For the grace to long for heaven nourished by the Bread of angels.)_

Each day also prays one Our Father, one Hail Mary, and one Glory Be.

**Closing prayer:** O sacred banquet in which Christ is received, the memory of His Passion renewed, the soul filled with grace, and a pledge of future glory given to us. Down in adoration falling, we hail this Sacrament so great. Grant that we may one day pass from the veiled Host to the unveiled vision of Your face. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
A nine-day Eucharistic novena. Each day opens with the Sign of the Cross and a thanksgiving prayer: "Most Holy and Almighty God, we thank you and praise you for giving us the most precious body and blood of Your only son, Jesus Christ." Then an intention insertion point, a unique daily petition, a closing reflection on Christ's sacrifice, a Glory Be, and the Sign of the Cross. Daily petitions: 1 the sacred mysteries / grace of Redemption; 2 strength to approach the throne of mercy; 3 praise of Christ as the greatest gift; 4 happy eternal life with Him; 5 perseverance in His love; 6 remembrance of His Passion and sacrifice; 7 spiritual and temporal benefit from receiving the Eucharist; 8 Christ living in our hearts and minds; 9 sharing in His Cross and Resurrection.

## Fidelity note
Ember's novena is an original composition that converges with PrayMoreNovenas on the universal Eucharistic themes — Real Presence, thanksgiving, the Passion remembered, worthy reception, union with Christ, and the Eucharist as pledge of future glory (Ember's day 9 closely matches PrayMoreNovenas' day 9). Ember's distinctive contribution is to weave in St. Thomas Aquinas' Corpus Christi hymns: the opening's "Saving Victim who opens wide the gate of heaven" (O Salutaris), day 3's "we devoutly adore the hidden God" (Adoro Te / Pange Lingua), and the closing built from the antiphon O Sacrum Convivium plus the Tantum Ergo's "Down in adoration falling." This is faithful to the feast's own liturgy. FishEaters could not be verified this pass. Independent, theologically dense wording, well-rooted in the proper texts of the day.
