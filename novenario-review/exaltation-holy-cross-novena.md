# Novena for the Exaltation of the Holy Cross — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** We adore You, O Christ, and we bless You, because by Your holy Cross You have redeemed the world. Through this sacred sign of our salvation, hear the prayers I bring before You, and let the Cross be my strength, my refuge, and my glory. Amen.

**Day 1 — The triumph of the Cross:** What looked like Christ's defeat was in truth His victory, for on the Cross He conquered sin and death. The Church teaches us to call this wood not a gallows but a throne, the standard of the King who reigns from the tree.  _(Intention: For the grace to see victory hidden within the Cross.)_

**Day 2 — We adore You, O Christ:** Before the crucifix the Church bows and prays, 'We adore You, O Christ, and we bless You.' The Cross is never honored apart from the One who hung upon it, and our reverence rises always to the living, risen Lord.  _(Intention: For a deeper love of Christ crucified.)_

**Day 3 — The price of our redemption:** On the Cross Christ paid a debt He did not owe to free a people who could not pay. Each time we look upon it we are told the measure of our worth in the eyes of God, who counted us worth His own blood.  _(Intention: In thanksgiving for the redemption won on Calvary.)_

**Day 4 — St. Helena and the True Cross:** In her old age St. Helena journeyed to Jerusalem and, according to tradition, uncovered the True Cross beneath the rubble of centuries. Her zeal teaches us to seek out and treasure the relics and memory of our salvation.  _(Intention: For zeal to seek Christ where the world has buried Him.)_

**Day 5 — Glory only in the Cross:** St. Paul resolved to glory in nothing except the Cross of our Lord Jesus Christ. He teaches us to set aside every other boast and to find our whole dignity in belonging to the Crucified.  _(Intention: For freedom from worldly pride and vanity.)_

**Day 6 — Taking up our own cross:** Christ asks each of us to take up our cross daily and follow Him. The Cross teaches that suffering joined to His is no longer barren, but becomes the very road that leads to life.  _(Intention: For strength to carry our daily crosses with love.)_

**Day 7 — The Cross as our healing:** As Moses lifted up the serpent in the desert that the people might look and live, so Christ was lifted up to heal the wound of sin. We need only turn our gaze to Him to be made whole.  _(Intention: For healing of body and soul through the power of the Cross.)_

**Day 8 — The Cross planted in every land:** From Calvary the Cross has been raised over every nation, marking churches, graves, and the brows of the baptized. It reminds us that no place and no person lies beyond the reach of Christ's saving arms.  _(Intention: For the spread of the Gospel to the ends of the earth.)_

**Day 9 — Hail, O Cross, our only hope:** The Church sings to the Cross as 'our only hope,' begging it to increase grace in the just and pardon for the guilty. By this sign we shall conquer, and by it we shall come at last to glory.  _(Intention: For the grace to hope in the Cross until we reach heaven.)_

**Closing prayer:** O Lord Jesus, lifted high upon the Cross to draw all things to Yourself, grant that I may never be ashamed of Your Cross but carry my own with love. May the wood of suffering blossom for me into the tree of life. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
A 9-day novena. Each day uses the same frame: Sign of the Cross; an introductory reflection ("Dear Jesus, your love for your Father and for the whole world brought you to the cross..."); a day-specific intention; the petition formula "Lord Jesus, in remembrance of your sorrowful Passion and Death, please look down upon us and grant the petition we ask for..."; a Litany to the Holy Cross (identical all nine days); a closing meditation; and Glory Be with a closing Sign of the Cross. The nine day-intentions are practical/moral virtues: be more loving; accept our sufferings; bear everyday pain; face trials with courage; be patient and endure insults; not be desperate; avoid domineering attitudes; accept situations out of our control; have servant's hearts.

## Fidelity note
Ember's version is an independent composition that does not follow PrayMoreNovenas' content. The two share only the genre and the liturgical opening "We adore You, O Christ, and we bless You" (the traditional Tenebrae/Good Friday antiphon, which Ember uses for its opening prayer and Day 2). The day structures diverge sharply: PMN's nine days are interior-disposition petitions (patience, humility, servant-heartedness), whereas Ember's nine are theological/historical meditations on the Cross itself (the True Cross, St. Helena, the bronze serpent, "glory only in the Cross" from Gal 6:14, the Vexilla Regis / "our only hope" hymn). Sound and traditional, but textually unrelated to the source.
