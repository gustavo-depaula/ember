# Novena to St. Lucy — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O glorious St. Lucy, virgin and martyr, whose very name proclaims the light of Christ, look kindly upon me as I begin this novena. By the steadfast faith that carried you through fire and sword, obtain for me the grace I seek and a heart enlightened by God. Amen.

**Day 1 — A name that means light:** Lucy bore in her very name the word 'light,' and her short life made that name true. She reminds us that the saints are not their own splendor but mirrors of the one Light that came into the world.  _(Intention: For the grace to let Christ's light shine through my ordinary days.)_

**Day 2 — Consecrated virginity:** While still young, Lucy gave her whole heart to Christ as her only Bridegroom, refusing the marriage her mother had arranged. Her purity was not coldness but an undivided love, the gift of a soul wholly given.  _(Intention: For purity of heart and an undivided love for God.)_

**Day 3 — The healing at Agatha's tomb:** Lucy brought her ailing mother to pray at the tomb of St. Agatha, and through that intercession her mother was healed. She teaches us to seek heaven's help with confident, persevering prayer for those we love.  _(Intention: For confidence in prayer for the sick and those I love.)_

**Day 4 — Generosity to the poor:** In thanksgiving for the healing, Lucy gave away her dowry to feed the poor, choosing the treasure of heaven over the security of earth. Her charity flowed straight from her love of Christ, who is hidden in the needy.  _(Intention: For a generous heart that gives freely to those in need.)_

**Day 5 — Courage before the judge:** Betrayed and dragged before the governor, Lucy answered with calm and fearless words, professing Christ without wavering. She shows us that true courage is not the absence of fear but love that will not be silenced.  _(Intention: For courage to confess my faith without shame.)_

**Day 6 — Unmovable in grace:** When the judge ordered her dragged away, tradition tells that Lucy could not be moved, held fast as if by the very hand of God. Her firmness reminds us that the soul rooted in grace cannot be torn from Christ.  _(Intention: For steadfastness in temptation and trial.)_

**Day 7 — Patroness of the eyes:** Honored from ancient times as the protector of sight, Lucy is invoked by all who suffer in their eyes and by those who long to see rightly. She turns our gaze from the things that pass to the One who alone deserves to be beheld.  _(Intention: For the health of my eyes and clear vision of soul.)_

**Day 8 — Faithful unto death:** Lucy was at last martyred by the sword, sealing her witness with her blood and receiving Communion before she died. She crowns her life with the fidelity Christ asks of us: to be faithful unto death, and to receive the crown of life.  _(Intention: For the grace of final perseverance in the faith.)_

**Day 9 — Light in the Advent night:** Lucy's feast falls in the darkest days of the year, lighting a candle as the Church awaits the Sun of Justice at Christmas. She invites us to carry the small, faithful flame of hope until the dawn of our Savior breaks.  _(Intention: For hope that watches faithfully for the coming of Christ.)_

**Closing prayer:** St. Lucy, bright lamp of the Lord, who preferred to lose your eyes rather than betray your Bridegroom, watch over my body and my soul. Keep the eyes of my faith clear and fixed on Christ, and lead me at last to the light that never fades. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
Each of the nine days opens with "In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen.", offers thanksgiving with a request to imitate St. Lucy's virtues, then a thematic reflection on her life, a petition ("Please bring my petitions before God"), space for personal intentions, and closes "St. Lucy, pray for us!" plus the Trinity invocation. Daily themes: Days 1–3 — her devotion and consecrated virginity; Days 4–5 — prayer and her mother's healing, and the betrayal by her rejected bridegroom; Days 6–8 — persecution, refusal to renounce the faith, and her suffering; Day 9 — courage in facing death. Emphasizes Lucy as patroness of the blind and example for the persecuted.

## Fidelity note
Ember closely follows the same biographical thread the PrayMoreNovenas version uses — consecrated virginity, the healing at St. Agatha's tomb, the betrayal and trial, her steadfastness, and martyrdom by the sword — and shares the signature themes of light (her name) and patronage of the eyes/sight. Ember adds material PMN does not foreground: her gift of the dowry to the poor (Day 4) and the Advent-light framing (Day 9, tying her Dec 13 feast to the awaited Sun of Justice), giving the set a stronger seasonal arc. Wording throughout is Ember's own, and the opening/closing prayers are not PMN's text. The FishEaters source could not be verified. A faithful retelling of the saint's life as an original composition.
