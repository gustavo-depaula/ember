# Novena to St. Catherine of Alexandria — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O glorious St. Catherine, virgin and martyr, who confessed Christ before kings and confounded the wise of this world, intercede for me before the throne of God. Obtain for me a share in your wisdom, your purity, and your unshakable courage. Amen.

**Day 1 — A heart seeking the truth:** Catherine devoted her early years to the study of philosophy and the sciences, but her restless mind found peace only when it found Christ. She teaches that all true learning leads at last to the One who is Truth itself.  _(Intention: For all who seek truth in their studies, that they may find Christ.)_

**Day 2 — Wisdom from above:** Summoned before fifty learned philosophers, Catherine spoke with a wisdom that was not her own but the gift of the Holy Spirit. She fulfilled the Lord's promise to give His witnesses words their adversaries could not resist.  _(Intention: For the gift of wisdom to know and defend the truth of the faith.)_

**Day 3 — Confounding the wise:** The philosophers who came to silence Catherine were instead converted by her arguments and embraced the faith they had been called to attack. God chooses the lowly to shame the strong and the simple to confound the proud.  _(Intention: That intellectuals and scholars may be led from error to faith.)_

**Day 4 — Courage before the powerful:** Catherine did not flatter the emperor but rebuked his cruelty to her face, fearing God more than any earthly throne. She gives courage to all who must speak the truth before those who hold power.  _(Intention: For the courage to confess my faith without fear of the world.)_

**Day 5 — Purity for the King of heaven:** Offered marriage and honors if she would renounce Christ, Catherine refused, having given her whole heart to her heavenly Bridegroom. Her purity was the freedom of a soul that loved God above every passing reward.  _(Intention: For the grace of purity and undivided love of God.)_

**Day 6 — The breaking wheel:** Condemned to be torn apart on a spiked wheel, Catherine prayed, and the instrument of her torture shattered at her touch. God showed that no power of this world can crush the soul that rests in Him.  _(Intention: For trust in God's protection amid suffering and trial.)_

**Day 7 — Faithful unto death:** When the wheel failed, Catherine was beheaded, sealing her witness with her blood. She received the martyr's crown for being faithful to the end, choosing death rather than the slightest betrayal of Christ.  _(Intention: For perseverance in the faith until my final breath.)_

**Day 8 — Patroness of students and scholars:** Because she joined deep learning to deeper holiness, Catherine became the patroness of students, teachers, and philosophers. She reminds the learned that knowledge without love of God can puff up, but wisdom with humility saves.  _(Intention: For students and teachers, that their learning may serve God.)_

**Day 9 — One of the Holy Helpers:** Among the Fourteen Holy Helpers, Catherine is invoked by the faithful in their needs and especially at the hour of death. She who broke the wheel by prayer is a powerful intercessor for all who call upon her.  _(Intention: For St. Catherine's intercession in all my needs and at my death.)_

**Closing prayer:** St. Catherine of Alexandria, faithful unto death and crowned with glory, lead me along the path of truth and keep me steadfast in the faith. By your powerful intercession, help me to love wisdom that comes from above and to remain Christ's to the end. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

FishEaters lists "Novena to St. Catherine of Alexandria" (anchor #catherineofalexandria) in its novena index, but the section text could not be retrieved — the fetched page consistently truncates partway through an earlier novena (St. Sebastian) before reaching this one, so the actual prayer text was unavailable for comparison.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
Each of the nine days opens with "In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen.", gives thanks to God for St. Catherine as an example with a day-specific focus, addresses a petition to her, and closes with "St. Catherine of Alexandria, pray for us!" plus space for personal intentions ("Please also pray for (mention your intentions here)"). Daily themes: Day 1 — active choice to love God despite persecution; Day 2 — bold defense of others / confronting injustice; Day 3 — courage facing torture and death; Day 4 — eloquent defense of the faith; Day 5 — fortitude amid suffering; Day 6 — faithfulness amid deprivation; Day 7 — inspiring conversions through suffering; Day 8 — rejecting worldly power for devotion; Day 9 — courageously facing martyrdom.

## Fidelity note
Ember tracks the same hagiographic arc as the PrayMoreNovenas version — Catherine's learning, her confrontation with the emperor, the conversion of the philosophers, her purity, the shattered wheel, and her martyrdom — and both attach a daily petition and personal intention. The ordering differs (Ember places wisdom/conversion early and martyrdom late, while PMN front-loads courage and ends on martyrdom), and Ember adds two distinctively traditional days PMN does not stress: patroness of students/scholars and her place among the Fourteen Holy Helpers. Wording is Ember's own throughout; the opening and closing prayers do not reproduce PMN's text. The FishEaters source could not be verified. Overall a faithful retelling of the same saint's narrative in original prose.
