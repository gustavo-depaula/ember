# Our Lady of the Rosary — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O Queen of the Holy Rosary, who delights to lead your children through the mysteries of your Son, gather me now beneath your mantle. Teach me to pray the Gospel with your own loving heart, and obtain for me the grace I implore in this novena. Amen.

**Day 1 — The Gospel in prayer:** The Rosary is the whole Gospel gathered into a garland of prayer, where we walk with Christ from Nazareth to Calvary to the glory of heaven. Mary teaches us that to pray the Rosary is to learn Jesus from the one who knew Him best.  _(Intention: For the grace to contemplate the life of Christ through Mary's eyes.)_  (every day)

**Day 2 — The joyful mysteries:** In the joyful mysteries Mary welcomes the angel, visits Elizabeth, gives birth in poverty, and finds her Son in the temple. She shows us how to receive God's coming with humble joy amid the ordinary events of life.  _(Intention: For joy in welcoming Christ into the daily moments of our lives.)_  (every day)

**Day 3 — The luminous mysteries:** The luminous mysteries shine upon Christ's public ministry, from His baptism to the gift of the Eucharist. At Cana, Mary's words still echo: 'Do whatever He tells you,' guiding us to obey her Son in all things.  _(Intention: For the grace to listen to Jesus and do whatever He tells us.)_  (every day)

**Day 4 — The sorrowful mysteries:** In the sorrowful mysteries we kneel with Mary at the foot of the Cross, sharing the agony, the scourging, and the death of her Son. She teaches us to unite our own sufferings to His and to find in them the price of our redemption.  _(Intention: For the grace to carry our crosses united to the Passion of Christ.)_  (every day)

**Day 5 — The glorious mysteries:** The glorious mysteries lift our hearts to the Resurrection, the Ascension, and Mary's own crowning in heaven. They remind us that sorrow is never the final word, and that the same glory is promised to those who persevere.  _(Intention: For unwavering hope in the resurrection and the glory to come.)_  (every day)

**Day 6 — The victory of Lepanto:** When Christendom faced ruin at Lepanto, Pope St. Pius V summoned the faithful to the Rosary, and the victory that followed was credited to Our Lady. She shows us that prayer is a true power that turns the course of history.  _(Intention: For confidence in the Rosary as a weapon against every evil.)_  (every day)

**Day 7 — Mary's school of contemplation:** Scripture says that Mary kept all these things, pondering them in her heart, and in the Rosary she shares that contemplation with us. Bead by bead she draws us into the silence where the soul gazes upon the face of Christ.  _(Intention: For a contemplative heart that ponders the mysteries of God in silence.)_  (every day)

**Day 8 — A remedy for the world:** At Fatima and elsewhere, Our Lady pleaded with her children to pray the Rosary daily for peace and the conversion of sinners. She offers this humble prayer as a gentle remedy for a wounded and restless world.  _(Intention: For peace in our world and the conversion of all sinners.)_  (every day)

**Day 9 — Our Lady's faithful child:** To take up the Rosary daily is to place one's hand in Mary's and let her lead the way to Jesus. She promises her constant protection to all who honor her through this faithful and persevering prayer.  _(Intention: For the grace of fidelity to the daily Rosary all our lives.)_  (every day)

**Closing prayer:** O Queen of the Holy Rosary, terror of demons and help of Christians, who at Lepanto turned defeat into triumph, fight now for me in every spiritual battle. Bind me ever closer to your Son through your most holy beads, that I may share at last in the glory of His mysteries. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
FishEaters' "Feast of the Most Holy Rosary" page is not a nine-day novena but a single Our Lady of Victory devotional service: three prayers (to Our Lady Help of Christians, to Our Lady of the Rosary, and to Our Lady of Victory — "terrible as an army in battle array"), all tied to the 1571 victory at Lepanto, followed by intercessions to various saints and a Glory Be. It supplies no per-day structure or daily meditations.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas opens each day with the Sign of the Cross and a fixed thanksgiving ("Dear Lord, we thank You for giving us Your mother as Our Lady of the Rosary. Help us to turn to her as our mother in all of our needs"), with a distinct daily meditation, a personal-intentions line, and the refrain "Our Lady of the Rosary, pray for us!" Daily themes: 1–2 growth in devotion to Mary and the Rosary, 3 faith, 4 perseverance in prayer, 5 persecuted Christians, 6 freedom to practice the faith, 7 peace in the world, 8 conversion of those hostile to Christianity, 9 the Church. Lepanto frames the historical reflections throughout.

## Fidelity note
Ember's novena is an original composition with a different organizing principle: it walks through the Rosary itself (Gospel-in-prayer, then the joyful/luminous/sorrowful/glorious mysteries on days 2–5) before turning to Lepanto, Mary's school of contemplation, Fatima as a remedy for the world, and fidelity to the daily Rosary. It shares the Lepanto/Pius V history and the petitions for peace and conversion of sinners with both sources, but the mystery-by-mystery structure is its own and is catechetically richer than PrayMore's theme list. The FishEaters page is not a true match in form. Wording is Ember's own throughout.
