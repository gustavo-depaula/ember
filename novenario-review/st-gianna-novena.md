# St. Gianna Beretta Molla — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** St. Gianna, faithful physician and devoted mother, you served Christ in the sick, the young, and the unborn with tender and competent love. Draw near to me now as I begin this novena, and teach me to make a gift of my whole life. Obtain for me from God the grace I seek and a heart wholly His. Amen.

**Day 1 — Day 1: Holiness in ordinary life:** Gianna was not a cloistered mystic but a busy doctor, wife, and mother who found God in clinics, kitchens, and cradles. She shows us that the path to sanctity runs straight through our daily duties faithfully fulfilled.  _(Intention: For the grace to seek holiness within the ordinary tasks God gives me each day.)_

**Day 2 — Day 2: Love of the sick:** As a pediatrician Gianna saw in every patient the suffering face of Christ, treating the poor with the same care as the rich. She teaches us that medicine and every honest profession can become an act of charity.  _(Intention: For all physicians, nurses, and caregivers, that they may serve the sick with reverence.)_

**Day 3 — Day 3: A vocation discerned in prayer:** Before her marriage Gianna prayed earnestly to know God's will, asking whether He called her to the missions, the consecrated life, or the home. She reminds us that every vocation is a holy answer to a divine invitation, discovered on our knees.  _(Intention: For the grace to discern God's call for my life with patience and trust.)_

**Day 4 — Day 4: Joyful married love:** Gianna's love for her husband Pietro was tender, faithful, and full of joy, expressed in letters that brimmed with affection and faith. She shows married couples that human love, lived in God, is a true path to heaven.  _(Intention: For married couples, that their love may be faithful, generous, and joyful.)_

**Day 5 — Day 5: Reverence for the unborn:** Gianna believed that the life of a child is a sacred gift entrusted by God, never a possession to be discarded. Facing her own danger, she chose to protect the smallest and most defenseless of all.  _(Intention: For the protection of unborn children and the conversion of all hearts to the gospel of life.)_

**Day 6 — Day 6: Courage in suffering:** When doctors revealed the tumor in her womb, Gianna calmly refused any remedy that would harm her child, accepting grave risk to herself. Her courage was not recklessness but a serene trust that God would not abandon her.  _(Intention: For courage to do what is right even when it costs me dearly.)_

**Day 7 — Day 7: The gift of self:** Knowing she might not survive the birth, Gianna asked that her child be saved if a choice had to be made. In laying down her life for her daughter, she imaged most perfectly the love of Christ, who lays down His life for His own.  _(Intention: For the grace to love others with a generous and self-giving heart.)_

**Day 8 — Day 8: Trust in divine providence:** Through her long final week of suffering, Gianna clung to Jesus and surrendered everything into His hands. She teaches us to entrust our fears, our families, and our futures to a Father whose providence never fails.  _(Intention: For deeper trust in God's providence amid uncertainty and trial.)_

**Day 9 — Day 9: A saint for our families:** Gianna died on Easter Saturday, 28 April 1962, and her daughter Gianna Emanuela lived to see her mother canonized. Her life proclaims that ordinary families can become forges of holiness when love is placed before self.  _(Intention: For my family, that we may grow together in faith, love, and holiness.)_

**Closing prayer:** St. Gianna, who in your final hour repeated, "If you must choose, save the baby," and offered your life without reserve, intercede for me before the throne of God. Help me to find Christ in the duties of each ordinary day and to love until the end. Through your prayers, may I one day join you in the joy of heaven. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
The PrayMoreNovenas novena uses a single recurring prayer prayed each day, with a unique daily petition inserted between the opening and closing sections.

Opening invocation (repeats daily): "Dear holy servant of God, Saint Gianna Beretta Molla, we pray to you because you were once the selfless and suffering mother who chose the life of her child over her own life. Your faithfulness to God brought you so close to God that you are now resting in His divine splendor. We humbly pray for your holy intercession."

Mid-prayer reflection (repeats daily): "In the midst of your suffering you continued your mission to care for the sick, needy and weak. And so we bring our needy hearts to you and ask that you pray for us to God our Father…"

Daily petition (varies by day): Day 1 — comfort for those experiencing infertility or miscarriage; Day 2 — that we may be filled with love for children; Day 3 — seeking God's will in all things; Day 4 — generosity toward those in need; Day 5 — patience in suffering; Day 6 — dignity in the medical professions; Day 7 — witnessing to the sanctity of life; Day 8 — an end to abortion; Day 9 — mothers as God's face within their families.

Closing petition (repeats daily): "Most holy Saint Gianna, you said 'yes' to the new life of your baby, generously sacrificing your own life. Show us how to say 'yes' as you did to God's will so that we may come to eternal rest with God in heaven." Each day concludes with a Hail Mary, a Glory Be, and "Saint Gianna Beretta Molla, pray for us!"

## Fidelity note
Ember's text is an original composition, not a reproduction of the PrayMoreNovenas prayer; the two share no wording. Both are nine-day novenas to St. Gianna built around the same themes (her motherhood, her medical vocation, her sacrifice for her child, the sanctity of life), and several daily focuses line up closely — Ember's Day 2 (the sick), Day 5 (the unborn), and Day 7 (the gift of self) echo PMN's care for the sick, end to abortion, and "yes" to her child's life. Ember's version is more devotional-meditation in structure (a fixed opening prayer, a per-day meditation plus intention, then Our Father / Hail Mary / Glory Be, then a closing prayer), whereas PMN repeats one long invocation daily with a swapped petition. It is a faithful, theologically sound novena in spirit but should be regarded as Ember's own text rather than the PMN source.
