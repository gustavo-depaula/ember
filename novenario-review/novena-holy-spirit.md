# Pentecost (Holy Spirit) — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** Come, Holy Spirit, fill the hearts of Thy faithful and enkindle in them the fire of Thy love.

**Day 1 — The Gift of Wisdom:** Come, O Spirit of Wisdom, and grant us the grace to see all things in the light of eternity. Detach our hearts from the passing vanities of this world and fix them upon the things that are above. Let us taste the sweetness of divine truth and find in God alone our lasting joy, so that we may order our lives according to His holy will. _(Intention: For the grace to judge all things wisely in the light of God's eternal truth.)_

**Day 2 — The Gift of Understanding:** Come, O Spirit of Understanding, and enlighten the eyes of our minds. Grant us a penetrating insight into the mysteries of faith, that we may grasp what God has revealed and hold it with unwavering conviction. Dispel the darkness of ignorance and error, and let the radiance of divine truth illuminate our hearts and guide our steps. _(Intention: For a deeper penetration into the truths of our holy faith.)_

**Day 3 — The Gift of Counsel:** Come, O Spirit of Counsel, and guide us in all our ways. In moments of doubt and uncertainty, whisper to our hearts the path that leads to God. Preserve us from rash judgments and hasty decisions, and grant us the prudence to discern Your will in all things. Be our guide in every difficulty, that we may never stray from the way of righteousness. _(Intention: For the light to know God's will and the courage to follow it.)_

**Day 4 — The Gift of Fortitude:** Come, O Spirit of Fortitude, and strengthen our souls in time of trial. Grant us the holy courage of the martyrs, that we may bear every cross with patience and overcome every temptation with steadfast faith. When the way grows dark and our strength fails, be our rock and fortress, that we may persevere unto the end and never falter in our love for God. _(Intention: For strength to bear the crosses of life and remain faithful to God.)_

**Day 5 — The Gift of Knowledge:** Come, O Spirit of Knowledge, and teach us to see the hand of God in all created things. Grant us the grace to use the goods of this world as stepping stones to heaven and never as obstacles to our salvation. Help us to recognize the emptiness of earthly treasures and to seek with all our hearts the imperishable riches of the kingdom of God. _(Intention: For the grace to see God's presence in all of creation and to use His gifts wisely.)_

**Day 6 — The Gift of Piety:** Come, O Spirit of Piety, and fill our hearts with a tender devotion to our heavenly Father. Enkindle within us a filial love that turns to God in all things — in joy and in sorrow, in abundance and in want. Grant us reverence for all that is sacred, compassion for our brothers and sisters, and a spirit of worship that transforms our every action into a prayer. _(Intention: For a childlike trust in God and a heart full of devotion.)_

**Day 7 — The Gift of Fear of the Lord:** Come, O Spirit of Holy Fear, and fill us with a profound reverence for the majesty of God. Grant us not a slavish terror, but the loving awe of children who dread above all else to offend their Father. Let this holy fear be the guardian of our souls, preserving us from sin and drawing us ever closer to the Heart of God, in whom alone we find our peace and security. _(Intention: For a holy reverence that keeps us from sin and draws us closer to God.)_

**Day 8 — The Fruits of the Holy Spirit:** Come, O Holy Spirit, and bring forth in us the abundant fruits of Your indwelling: love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, and self-control. As a vine brings forth fruit in due season, so let our souls, grafted onto Christ and animated by Your grace, bear fruit that will last for eternal life. Transform our hearts that others may see in us the living presence of God. _(Intention: For the grace to bear the fruits of the Holy Spirit in our daily lives.)_

**Day 9 — Come, Holy Spirit — Prayer for a New Pentecost:** Come, O Holy Spirit, descend upon us as You descended upon the Apostles and Our Lady in the Upper Room. Renew the face of the earth and send forth Your fire to enkindle in all hearts the flame of Your divine love. As You gave courage to the first disciples to proclaim the Gospel to all nations, so strengthen Your Church today. Grant us a new Pentecost — a fresh outpouring of grace, zeal, and apostolic boldness. _(Intention: For a renewed outpouring of the Holy Spirit upon the Church and the world.)_

**Closing prayer:** _(none distinct — the flow ends with one Our Father, Hail Mary, and Glory Be, then the Sign of the Cross.)_

## Source — FishEaters
FishEaters carries the classic Novena to the Holy Ghost (begun the day after Ascension Thursday, concluding on Pentecost). Day 1 begins "Almighty and eternal God, Who hast vouchsafed to regenerate us by water and the Holy Ghost…" and asks for the sevenfold Spirit. Days 2–8 each focus on one of the seven gifts, traditionally beginning with Holy Fear and ascending through Piety, Fortitude, Knowledge, Understanding, Counsel, to Wisdom (e.g. Day 3 "Come, O Blessed Spirit of Piety, possess my heart. Enkindle therein such a love for God…"). Day 9 turns to the fruits: "Come, O Divine Spirit, fill my heart with Thy heavenly fruits, Thy charity, joy, peace, patience, benignity, goodness, faith, mildness, and temperance…" Each day closes with one Our Father, one Hail Mary, and seven Glory Be's.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas opens each day with the Sign of the Cross, then a fixed invocation: "Let us bow down in humility at the power and grandeur of the Holy Spirit. Let us worship the Holy Trinity and give glory today to the Paraclete, our Advocate." Its common closing each day is the "Come Holy Spirit, fill the hearts of your faithful… Send forth your Spirit and they shall be created. And You shall renew the face of the earth." Rather than the seven gifts, PMN organizes the nine days around the fruits/virtues: Day 1 Charity, 2 Joy, 3 Peace, 4 Patience, 5 Kindness, 6 Faithfulness, 7 Gentleness, 8 Self-Control, 9 Goodness, each with a "grace of [intention]" petition.

## Fidelity note
Ember's Holy Spirit novena is an original composition that follows the older, traditional FishEaters structure much more closely than PMN's. Its daily opening — "Come, Holy Spirit, fill the hearts of Thy faithful and enkindle in them the fire of Thy love" — is the historic versicle (the same line PMN expands in its closing). The core architecture matches the classic seven-gifts novena: Days 1–7 take the gifts one by one and Days 8–9 turn to the fruits and a Pentecost prayer — exactly the FishEaters arc, though Ember orders the gifts Wisdom→Fear (descending) rather than the traditional Fear→Wisdom ascent, and ends each day with one Glory Be rather than seven. The day-prayers themselves ("Come, O Spirit of Wisdom…," etc.) are freshly written paraphrases in the same idiom, not the verbatim "Almighty and eternal God…" text. The fruits enumerated on Day 8 follow the Douay/traditional list (charity, joy, peace, patience, benignity, goodness, faith, mildness, temperance — rendered here in modern terms). Highly faithful in structure and spirit; paraphrased in wording.
