# Our Lady of Perpetual Help — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** Mother of Perpetual Help, with childlike confidence I come before your holy icon and place this novena in your hands. Obtain for me the grace to trust Jesus in every need, persevere in prayer, and follow His will with a faithful heart. Amen.

**Day 1 — Mother of Perpetual Help, icon of mercy:** In the holy icon, Mary points us to Jesus, the true source of every grace. Her gaze invites us to trust God's mercy in every circumstance, especially when we feel weak or afraid.  _(Intention: For renewed trust in God's mercy and Mary's maternal care.)_

**Day 2 — Help in anxiety and trials:** Mary is called Perpetual Help because she does not abandon her children in hardship. She teaches us to bring every burden to Christ with perseverance, believing that grace is stronger than fear.  _(Intention: For all who are anxious, burdened, or passing through difficult trials.)_

**Day 3 — Refuge of sinners and conversion:** At the foot of the Cross, Mary received us as her children. She intercedes for sinners and leads hearts back to Jesus with tenderness and truth.  _(Intention: For the conversion of sinners and those far from the Church.)_

**Day 4 — Mother of families and homes:** Mary lived the hidden life of Nazareth with Jesus and Joseph. She understands family joys and wounds, and helps homes become places of prayer, forgiveness, and faithful love.  _(Intention: For peace, healing, and unity in our families.)_

**Day 5 — Consolation of the sick and suffering:** The Mother of Perpetual Help is close to those who suffer in body or soul. She carries our pain to Christ and obtains strength, patience, and hope in the midst of affliction.  _(Intention: For the sick, the suffering, and all who care for them.)_

**Day 6 — Help of the Church and vocations:** Mary never ceases to intercede for the Church. Under her mantle we ask for holy pastors, generous vocations, and faithful disciples who witness to Christ with courage.  _(Intention: For the Church, the Pope, bishops, priests, and new vocations.)_

**Day 7 — Mother of hope for the poor and forgotten:** Mary's heart is especially near to the poor, abandoned, and oppressed. She teaches us to recognize Christ in those who suffer and to practice concrete charity.  _(Intention: For the poor, the abandoned, and all who seek dignity and daily bread.)_

**Day 8 — Guide in fidelity and mission:** Mary formed the disciples in prayer while they awaited the Holy Spirit. She forms us to remain faithful in daily duties and to serve the Gospel with humility and zeal.  _(Intention: For perseverance in vocation and fruitfulness in Christian mission.)_

**Day 9 — Lead us always to Jesus:** Every true Marian devotion leads us to Jesus. At the close of this novena, we entrust our whole life to Our Lady of Perpetual Help, asking for final perseverance in grace.  _(Intention: For final perseverance and lifelong fidelity to Jesus Christ.)_

(After every day: one Our Father, one Hail Mary, one Glory Be.)

**Closing prayer:** Our Lady of Perpetual Help, pray for us. Help us in every trial, protect our families, strengthen the Church, and lead us always to your Son, Jesus Christ. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Fidelity note
Both are nine-day novenas to the same Redemptorist Marian title, both bracketed by the Sign of the Cross and built on the Our Father / Hail Mary / Glory Be. Ember's closing invocation "Our Lady of Perpetual Help, pray for us" matches PrayMoreNovenas almost word-for-word, and several themes overlap (vocations, the Church, leading us to Jesus). The opening prayers differ in voice — PrayMoreNovenas addresses God in thanksgiving, Ember addresses Mary before her icon — and Ember's nine meditations are its own composition organized around the icon and Mary's titles. The history (Byzantine icon, 1866, Pius IX, the Redemptorists) is accurate. Faithful in form and devotion; original in wording.
