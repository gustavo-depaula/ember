# Our Lady of Lourdes — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O Immaculate Virgin Mary, who at Lourdes opened heaven to a humble child, turn your merciful eyes upon me as you turned them upon Bernadette. Receive the petition I bring to your grotto and present it to your Son. Amen.

**Day 1 — Day 1: The Lady appears:** On a cold February day Bernadette saw a beautiful Lady in the rocky niche, holding a rosary, and instinctively reached for her own. Heaven chose a poor, asthmatic girl gathering firewood to be its messenger.  _(Intention: For the grace to recognize God's presence in the humble and unexpected.)_

**Day 2 — Day 2: 'I am the Immaculate Conception':** When asked her name, the Lady joined her hands, lifted her eyes, and said, 'I am the Immaculate Conception.' Bernadette did not understand the words, yet they confirmed the dogma the Church had defined only four years before.  _(Intention: In thanksgiving for Mary's Immaculate Conception and her sinless love.)_

**Day 3 — Day 3: The hidden spring:** The Lady told Bernadette to drink and wash at a spring, and the child scratched the muddy ground until clear water welled up. From that small trickle has flowed a stream of healing for the world.  _(Intention: For all the sick who seek healing of body and soul.)_

**Day 4 — Day 4: Penance, penance, penance:** The Lady's message was simple and grave: penance for one's own sins and for the sins of others. She invited not despair but a humble turning back to God.  _(Intention: For the grace of true penance and amendment of life.)_

**Day 5 — Day 5: Prayer for sinners:** Again and again the Lady asked Bernadette to pray for the conversion of sinners. The Mother of Mercy weeps for her straying children and longs to bring them home.  _(Intention: For the conversion of sinners and of those far from God.)_

**Day 6 — Day 6: Bernadette's faithfulness:** Mocked, questioned, and pressured by authorities, Bernadette never altered her account or sought any reward. Her simple steadfastness made her a fitting witness to the Queen of Heaven.  _(Intention: For courage to bear witness to the truth despite opposition.)_

**Day 7 — Day 7: Health of the sick:** To Lourdes come the suffering of every land, carried to the grotto in hope. Many are healed in body; far more receive the deeper grace to bear their cross with Christ.  _(Intention: For those who suffer, that they may find strength and consolation.)_

**Day 8 — Day 8: The light of the candles:** Each night the faithful process at Lourdes with lighted candles, singing the Ave to Mary. The flame in each hand is a prayer made visible, joining countless hearts as one.  _(Intention: For perseverance in daily prayer to Our Lady.)_

**Day 9 — Day 9: Our Lady's enduring care:** What began at a poor grotto has drawn millions to conversion and trust for over a century and a half. Our Lady of Lourdes still bends down to her children with a Mother's tenderness.  _(Intention: That I may entrust my whole life to the care of Mary.)_

**Closing prayer:** Our Lady of Lourdes, health of the sick and refuge of sinners, obtain for me the grace I ask in this novena, if it be God's will, and above all the conversion and healing of my soul. Help me to do penance and to pray, as you asked at the grotto. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
FishEaters carries a Novena to Our Lady of Lourdes. Its structure is: a daily opening prayer (invoking Mary's appearance at Lourdes, her "Immaculate Conception" declaration, and asking healing of soul and body), followed by nine daily sections. Each day combines a meditation on Mary's virtues and her appearance to St. Bernadette, a space for personal intentions, and a closing prayer affirming Mary's compassion, including the petition: "O Brilliant star of purity, O Mary Immaculate, Our Lady of Lourdes, glorious in thine Assumption, triumphant in thy Coronation, show unto us thy mercy..." It closes with invocations to Our Lady of Lourdes and St. Bernadette. ## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
The PrayMoreNovenas Our Lady of Lourdes Novena opens each day with the Sign of the Cross, then a core daily prayer addressing Our Lady of Lourdes with childlike trust, acknowledging the sanctuary's spiritual and physical healing, a space to state intentions, and the Memorare ("Remember, O most gracious Virgin Mary, that never was it known that anyone who fled to thy protection..."). Each day adds a unique petition:
- Day 1: "Show us your mercy, O Mother of God"
- Day 2: "Comfort us in our affliction"
- Day 3: "Pray that we may have hope in times of trial"
- Day 4: "Pray for the grace of God to be our strength"
- Day 5: "Pray that we may reach the consolation of heaven"
- Day 6: "Manifest in us your sweetness and beauty"
- Day 7: "Shower us with the love of your pure heart"
- Day 8: "Fill our hearts with love for Jesus and for you"
- Day 9: "Give us what your motherly heart sees we need at this moment"

It closes each day with: "Our Lady of Lourdes, pray for us! Saint Bernadette, pray for us! Amen. In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen."

## Fidelity note
Ember's novena shares the broad shape common to both sources — a daily opening prayer, nine days with meditation plus intention, and a closing prayer invoking Our Lady of Lourdes — but its prayer and meditation wording is an original composition, not a transcription of either source. It does not carry the distinctive fixed texts of either tradition (FishEaters' "O Brilliant star of purity" petition, or PrayMoreNovenas' Memorare and "Our Lady of Lourdes / Saint Bernadette, pray for us" refrain). Where the public sources repeat the same prayer body each day with only a varying petition, Ember instead gives each day a unique narrative meditation tracing the apparition story (the Lady's appearance, the Immaculate Conception, the spring, penance, prayer for sinners, Bernadette's witness, the sick, the candlelight procession, enduring care). It is faithful to the Lourdes devotion's themes and spirit but is best characterized as a freshly written novena rather than a reproduction of the FishEaters or PrayMoreNovenas texts.
