# Novena to St. Maximilian Kolbe — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O St. Maximilian Kolbe, knight of the Immaculate and martyr of love, you laid down your life so that a father might return to his family. Kindle in me your fearless charity and your boundless devotion to Mary, that I too may live and die for others. Amen.

**Day 1 — The two crowns:** As a child Maximilian saw Our Lady hold out a white crown of purity and a red crown of martyrdom, and he answered that he would have them both. He teaches us to desire holiness boldly and to hold nothing back from God.  _(Intention: For the grace to desire holiness without fear or reserve.)_

**Day 2 — Total consecration to Mary:** Maximilian gave himself entirely to the Immaculate, calling himself her property and her instrument in all things. He shows that to belong wholly to Mary is the surest road to belonging wholly to Christ.  _(Intention: For the grace to entrust my whole life to Our Lady.)_

**Day 3 — The Militia of the Immaculate:** He founded the Militia Immaculatae to win the whole world for Christ through Mary, using every modern means, from printing presses to radio. He teaches us to be zealous and inventive in spreading the Kingdom of God.  _(Intention: For zeal to spread the faith by every honest means within my reach.)_

**Day 4 — A builder of community:** At Niepokalanów he gathered hundreds of friars into a 'City of the Immaculate' devoted to prayer, work, and the printed word. He shows that great works for God are built on humble obedience and shared life.  _(Intention: For the grace to build up rather than tear down those around me.)_

**Day 5 — Missionary to the East:** Though in fragile health, Maximilian sailed to Japan and founded a friary on a hillside in Nagasaki that survived the atomic blast unharmed. He teaches us that love of souls overcomes weakness, distance, and fear.  _(Intention: For generosity to answer God's call beyond my comfort.)_

**Day 6 — Charity behind the wire:** In the Auschwitz prison Maximilian shared his meager rations, heard confessions in secret, and comforted the despairing. He shows that even in the darkest place, a heart full of God becomes a light for others.  _(Intention: For the grace to be kind to others in my own hardships.)_

**Day 7 — Greater love has no one:** When a prisoner was condemned to starve and cried out for his wife and children, Maximilian stepped forward and said, 'I am a Catholic priest; let me take his place.' He fulfilled in his own flesh the words of Christ about laying down one's life for a friend.  _(Intention: For a love willing to sacrifice itself for the good of another.)_

**Day 8 — Singing in the bunker:** In the starvation cell Maximilian led the condemned in hymns and prayers to the Virgin until their voices fell silent one by one. He teaches that faith can transform even agony into worship.  _(Intention: For trust and peace in the face of suffering and death.)_

**Day 9 — Martyr of love:** Last to remain alive, Maximilian raised his arm in peace to receive the lethal injection on the vigil of the Assumption, going to the Mother he had served. St. John Paul II called him the patron of our difficult century, a witness that love is stronger than hate.  _(Intention: For the grace to overcome evil with love in my own life.)_

**Closing prayer:** St. Maximilian, who turned a death camp into a place of song and hope, intercede for me before the Immaculate to whom you gave yourself wholly. Win for me the grace I now ask and a love so great that it counts no cost. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas hosts a St. Maximilian Kolbe novena. It is bracketed by the Sign of the Cross and uses one repeated prayer for all nine days rather than distinct daily meditations. The core prayer opens on Christ's words — "O Lord Jesus Christ, who said, 'greater love than this no man has that a man lay down his life for his friends'" — recalls that Maximilian founded the Militia Immaculata and laid down his life for a stranger while forgiving his persecutors, and petitions: "Grant, O Lord Jesus, that we too may give ourselves entirely without reserve to the love and service of our Heavenly Queen." Each day closes with three Hail Marys, a Glory Be, and the Sign of the Cross.

## Fidelity note
Ember and PMN converge on the same two pillars of Kolbe's life — his total consecration to the Immaculate and his "greater love has no one" sacrifice for a fellow prisoner — but the texts are independent. PMN is a single repeated Christ-addressed prayer; Ember addresses the saint and unfolds nine distinct, biographically dense days (the two crowns, the Militia, Niepokalanów, Nagasaki, the Auschwitz bunker, the hymn-singing, the lethal injection on the Assumption vigil). Ember's added detail is historically accurate and the shared Marian/self-gift themes reflect the saint's own spirituality rather than borrowing. A faithful original composition.
