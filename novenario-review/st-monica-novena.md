# St. Monica — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O glorious St. Monica, mother of tears and of unfailing hope, you never ceased to pray for those entrusted to your love. Obtain for me a heart that does not grow weary in prayer, and present my petitions before the throne of God. Amen.

**Day 1 — Perseverance in prayer:** For seventeen years St. Monica prayed without seeing any fruit, yet she never abandoned her son to despair. She teaches us that God's delays are not refusals, and that love prays on through the long silence.  _(Intention: For the grace never to abandon prayer for those who have strayed from God.)_

**Day 2 — Patience with a difficult husband:** Married to the hot-tempered pagan Patricius, Monica bore his anger without bitterness and won him to baptism by her quiet goodness. She shows us that holiness lived steadily within the home can convert even a hardened heart.  _(Intention: For peace and conversion within troubled marriages and families.)_

**Day 3 — Tears that pierced heaven:** Monica wept so long for Augustine that a bishop told her, 'It is impossible that the child of so many tears should perish.' Her sorrow was not weakness but a prayer the body offered when words ran out.  _(Intention: For all parents who weep for the faith of their children.)_

**Day 4 — Following her son in love:** When Augustine fled by ship to Italy to escape her, Monica followed him across the sea rather than let him go. Her love was relentless yet never controlling, pursuing him only toward God.  _(Intention: For the courage to keep loving those who pull away from us.)_

**Day 5 — Docility to wise counsel:** Monica sought out holy bishops and humbly accepted their guidance, even when told simply to wait and pray. She teaches us that perseverance is wedded to humility, trusting the Church to direct our zeal.  _(Intention: For humility to seek and follow holy counsel in our trials.)_

**Day 6 — Hope against all evidence:** Through years of Augustine's heresy and pride, Monica clung to hope when every sign said her prayers had failed. She shows us that Christian hope is not optimism but a refusal to let God's mercy be smaller than our fears.  _(Intention: For hope to endure when conversion seems impossible.)_

**Day 7 — The conversion of Augustine:** At last in Milan, under the preaching of St. Ambrose, Augustine surrendered to grace and Monica's long labor bore its fruit. She reminds us that God works in His own hour, and the harvest, when it comes, exceeds the sowing.  _(Intention: For one soul, named in our heart, to return to God.)_

**Day 8 — Resting in God's will:** Near death at Ostia, Monica told Augustine she no longer cared where her body lay, for nothing was far from God. Having received the one thing she had begged for, she was ready to go home in peace.  _(Intention: For the grace to surrender our desires into the hands of God.)_

**Day 9 — Patroness of mothers and families:** Monica's only request of her sons was to be remembered at the altar of the Lord wherever they might be. She intercedes now for every family, drawing scattered hearts back toward the Mass and toward home.  _(Intention: For all families, that they may be reunited around the table of the Lord.)_

**Closing prayer:** St. Monica, who saw your prayers answered beyond all your hope, intercede for me and for all I carry in my heart. Teach me to wait upon God with patience and to trust that no tear shed for love of souls is ever wasted. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
A 9-day novena that repeats one identical prayer every day (no separate opening prayer, no per-day intentions or meditations). The daily prayer reads: "Dear Saint Monica, you were once the mournful mother of a prodigal son. Your faithfulness to prayer brought you and your son so close to God that you are now with him in eternity. Please take my request to God with the same fervor and persistence with which you prayed for your own son. (Mention your intentions here) ... Above all, dear Saint Monica, pray for me, that I may, like your son, turn from my sin and become a great saint for the glory of God." Framed around her 17-year intercession for Augustine; begins Aug 18.

## Fidelity note
Ember's version is an original composition, not a transcription of either source. It shares the universal St. Monica themes — 17 years of tears, perseverance in prayer, the bishop's "child of so many tears" line (which Ember quotes directly in Day 3), and her patronage of mothers — but PrayMoreNovenas uses a single repeated prayer with no daily structure, whereas Ember builds nine distinct biographical meditations. The wording is Ember's own, well-grounded in Monica's life from the Confessions. The match is thematic and structural-genre, not textual.
