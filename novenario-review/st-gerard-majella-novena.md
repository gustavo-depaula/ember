# St. Gerard Majella — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** St. Gerard Majella, faithful servant of God who hid great holiness beneath the humblest of duties, look kindly upon me. Teach me to love the will of God as you did, with simple and joyful surrender, and obtain for me the grace I ask in this novena. Amen.

**Day 1 — Abandonment to God's will:** St. Gerard surrendered himself so completely to God that he longed only to do the divine will in all things. He teaches us that true peace is found in trusting God's plan even when it is hidden from our eyes.  _(Intention: For the grace to abandon myself wholly to the will of God.)_  (every day)

**Day 2 — Hidden holiness:** As a lay brother St. Gerard served quietly as porter, sacristan, and infirmarian, content to be unnoticed by the world. He shows us that the smallest task done for love of God can become a path to great sanctity.  _(Intention: For the grace to seek holiness in humble and hidden service.)_  (every day)

**Day 3 — Heroic obedience:** St. Gerard obeyed his superiors so promptly that he saw in their commands the very voice of God. Even when falsely accused, he submitted in silence rather than defend himself against his superiors.  _(Intention: For the grace of humble obedience to God and lawful authority.)_  (every day)

**Day 4 — Patron of mothers:** Tradition recalls how St. Gerard left a handkerchief with a young woman, saying she would one day need it, and through it a mother was later saved in childbirth. He has become the loving protector of mothers and the children they carry.  _(Intention: For the safety of expectant mothers and the children in their wombs.)_  (every day)

**Day 5 — Friend of the poor:** St. Gerard gave generously to the poor, often emptying the convent stores in his confidence that God would provide. His charity reveals a heart that loved Christ in every needy and suffering person.  _(Intention: For a generous heart toward the poor and suffering around us.)_  (every day)

**Day 6 — Love of the Eucharist:** St. Gerard was often found rapt in adoration before the Blessed Sacrament, drawn by an ardent love for the hidden Christ. From the Eucharist he drew the strength for his penances and his tireless charity.  _(Intention: For a deeper love of Jesus present in the Holy Eucharist.)_  (every day)

**Day 7 — Innocence vindicated:** When falsely accused of grave sin, St. Gerard bore the disgrace without complaint, trusting God to defend his honor in His own time. His innocence was later revealed, and he had won great merit by his silence.  _(Intention: For the grace to bear injustice in silence, trusting God to defend us.)_  (every day)

**Day 8 — Penance and purity:** St. Gerard embraced severe penances and guarded the purity of his heart with great care, that nothing might come between him and God. He teaches us that self-denial frees the soul to love the Lord without division.  _(Intention: For the grace of self-denial and purity of heart.)_  (every day)

**Day 9 — A holy death:** St. Gerard died at twenty-nine, asking only that God's will be done, with a note on his door reading that here the will of God was carried out. He teaches us to meet death in peace, surrendered to the loving plan of our Father.  _(Intention: For the grace of a holy death, surrendered to the will of God.)_  (every day)

**Closing prayer:** St. Gerard, wonderworker and tender protector of mothers and the helpless, intercede for me before the throne of God. Obtain for me a heart obedient and abandoned to Providence, so that in all things I may say with you, 'Lord, let it be done as You will.' Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
Nine days, each with an identical structural frame and a distinct daily petition. Each day: "In the name of the Father..." → a day-specific opening prayer naming one virtue → Nine Hail Marys → V. "Pray for us, O Saint Gerard." R. "That we may be made worthy of the promises of Christ." → closing Sign of the Cross. Daily virtues: (1) Faith — "believing firmly all that the Church of God proposes"; (2) Detachment — "place all my confidence in Jesus Christ alone"; (3) Charity; (4) Purity; (5) Obedience — "cheerful admission to His Holy Will"; (6) Humility; (7) Patience in suffering — "bravely bear the cross"; (8) Devotion to the Eucharist; (9) Devotion to Mary.

## Fidelity note
Ember and PrayMoreNovenas share the nine-day-with-distinct-daily-theme structure and overlap heavily on themes (obedience/abandonment, humility/hidden holiness, the Eucharist, purity, patience in suffering, Marian/Providence trust). But the wording is independent: Ember writes a fresh narrative meditation on Gerard's life and gifts for each day plus a one-line intention, whereas PrayMoreNovenas uses a classic virtue-petition formula with fixed responsorial elements (nine Hail Marys, the "Pray for us" versicle). Ember also foregrounds Gerard's hallmark patronage of expectant mothers (Day 4), which the PrayMoreNovenas virtue list does not isolate. Overall: faithful in spirit and recognizable as the same devotion, but a distinct composition rather than a reproduction of the FishEaters/PMN text.
