# Novena to Christ the King — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** Lord Jesus Christ, King of kings and Lord of lords, I acknowledge You as the sovereign of all creation and the rightful ruler of my soul. Reign in my heart, my home, and my whole life, and let nothing in me resist Your gentle and saving rule. Amen.

**Day 1 — King of all creation:** All things were made through Christ and for Him, and in Him all things hold together. His kingship is not borrowed from men but flows from His very nature as the eternal Word and as our Redeemer.  _(Intention: For the grace to recognize Christ as Lord over all that exists.)_

**Day 2 — King of hearts:** Christ does not reign by force but by love, seeking the free surrender of each heart. He stands at the door and knocks, and asks only that we open and let Him enter as Lord.  _(Intention: That I may give Christ the throne of my own heart without reserve.)_

**Day 3 — King of families:** When Christ reigns in a home, He guards its peace and binds its members in charity. To enthrone Him in the family is to invite the King who blessed the household of Nazareth.  _(Intention: That Christ may reign in every Christian family.)_

**Day 4 — King of nations:** Pius XI taught that rulers and assemblies are bound to give public honor to Christ the King, for nations that exile God invite ruin upon themselves. The peace of peoples is found in submission to His just rule.  _(Intention: That rulers and nations may submit their laws to the law of Christ.)_

**Day 5 — A reign of truth and life:** Christ told Pilate that He came into the world to bear witness to the truth, and He is Himself the Way, the Truth, and the Life. His kingdom drives out the lies that enslave and bestows the life that never ends.  _(Intention: That the truth of Christ may dispel error in the world.)_

**Day 6 — A reign of holiness and grace:** The King who reigns from the Cross pours out grace to make His subjects holy. To belong to His kingdom is to be drawn, day by day, into the very holiness of God.  _(Intention: For the grace to grow in holiness as a true subject of the King.)_

**Day 7 — A reign of justice:** Christ the King gives to each his due and lifts up the lowly whom the world overlooks. Where He reigns, the poor are not forgotten and right ordering returns to a wounded world.  _(Intention: That the justice of Christ may heal the wounds of society.)_

**Day 8 — A reign of love:** The scepter of this King is His pierced Heart, and the law of His kingdom is love. He conquers not by crushing His enemies but by laying down His life for them.  _(Intention: That the love of Christ may rule all my dealings with others.)_

**Day 9 — A reign of peace:** Christ is our peace, who reconciles all things to the Father by the Blood of His Cross. Only when He reigns within us and among us does true and lasting peace come to the world.  _(Intention: That the peace of Christ may reign in hearts, families, and nations.)_

**Closing prayer:** Christ Jesus, King of all nations, may Your kingdom come and Your will be done on earth as it is in heaven. Subject all hearts to Your reign of truth and peace, that one day every knee may bend before You and every tongue confess that You are Lord. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
Once daily for 9 days, beginning 10 days before the Feast of Christ the King (last Sunday in October). Preceded by one Our Father, one Hail Mary, and one Glory Be. Main daily prayer:

"O Lord our God, Thou alone art the Most Holy King and Ruler of all nations. We pray to Thee, Lord, in the great expectation of receiving from Thee, O Divine King, mercy, peace, justice and all good things." The prayer continues with petitions for the protection of families and homeland, forgiveness of sins, and trust in God's mercy, and concludes: "O most awe-inspiring King, We bow before Thee and pray: may Thy reign, Thy Kingdom, be recognized on earth. Amen."

FishEaters uses the *same* prayer every day for nine days, with space for personal intentions; it has no per-day meditations or distinct daily themes.

## Fidelity note
Ember shares the source's central theme — the universal kingship of Christ over creation, hearts, families, and nations, grounded in Pius XI's *Quas Primas* — and its nine-day frame with Our Father / Hail Mary / Glory Be. But the structures diverge: FishEaters repeats one fixed petition-prayer daily, whereas Ember is a fully composed nine-day cycle with distinct daily titles and meditations (notably the six attributes of Christ's reign — truth, life, holiness, justice, love, peace — drawn straight from the *Quas Primas* preface). The opening and closing prayers are Ember's own wording, not the FishEaters text. This is best read as a faithful original elaboration on the same devotion rather than a transcription.
