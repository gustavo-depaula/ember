# Mary, Mother of God — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** Holy Mother of God, beneath your protection we take refuge. You who carried in your womb the eternal Word, look upon us with a mother's tenderness and lead us always to your Son. Amen.

**Day 1 — Day 1: The Mother of God:** At Ephesus the Church confessed that the child Mary bore is true God, and so she is rightly called Theotokos, Mother of God. This title does not exalt her above God, but proclaims the wonder that God willed to be born of a creature. _(Intention: For a deeper faith in the mystery of the Incarnation.)_

**Day 2 — Day 2: Her humble fiat:** When the angel announced God's will, Mary answered, 'Let it be done to me according to your word.' In that free consent she opened the door of salvation, teaching us to surrender our whole life to God. _(Intention: For the grace to say yes to God's will without reserve.)_

**Day 3 — Day 3: Mother of our Redeemer:** Mary gave human flesh to the One who would redeem the world by His Blood. By cooperating in His coming, she stands closest to the work of our salvation, after Christ Himself. _(Intention: For gratitude for the redemption won by Christ.)_

**Day 4 — Day 4: Beneath your protection:** From the earliest centuries Christians fled to Mary's protection, praying the Sub Tuum Praesidium amid persecution. She is a sure refuge who shelters her children in every storm. _(Intention: For protection in times of trial and danger.)_

**Day 5 — Day 5: The faithful heart:** Saint Luke tells us that Mary kept all these things, pondering them in her heart. She is the model of contemplation, treasuring the word of God in silence and love. _(Intention: For a recollected heart that treasures God's word.)_

**Day 6 — Day 6: Mother at the Cross:** Mary stood faithfully beneath the Cross as her Son offered His life. There, in His words 'Behold your mother,' He gave her to the whole Church as our Mother. _(Intention: For perseverance in faith amid suffering.)_

**Day 7 — Day 7: Our Mother:** Because she is Mother of the Head, Mary is Mother of all His members. She watches over each of her children with a love that never wearies and never forgets. _(Intention: For trust in Mary's constant maternal care.)_

**Day 8 — Day 8: Queen of peace:** She who gave us the Prince of Peace leads hearts and nations back to her Son. Mary intercedes that the peace of Christ may reign in our families and in the world. _(Intention: For peace in our homes and among the nations.)_

**Day 9 — Day 9: Assumed into glory:** Taken body and soul into heaven, Mary now reigns beside her Son as the first fruit of His victory. She is the sure hope of all who long to follow her into glory. _(Intention: For the grace of final perseverance and eternal life.)_

**Closing prayer:** O Mary, Mother of God and our Mother, despise not our petitions in our necessities, but deliver us always from every danger. Through your intercession may we one day behold the face of your Son in glory. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas, "Mary, Mother of God Novena." Each day opens with the Sign of the Cross and "Dear Lord, we thank You for the amazing gift that You have given us in Mary, Your Mother," then a day-specific petition to God and an invocation of Mary as "Mother of God" asking her intercession; each day closes "Mary, Mother of God, pray for us!" and the Sign of the Cross. The nine days are organized by petition intentions rather than by historical/theological meditations:
- Day 1: deeper devotion to Mary as our Mother through her role in salvation
- Day 2: Mary as model for all mothers in holiness and loving care
- Day 3: those who consecrate themselves to God through Mary
- Day 4: men and women religious who serve through Mary
- Day 5: those who do not yet know Mary's love as her children
- Day 6: submission to God's will after Mary's example
- Day 7: defense of the Church's teaching on Mary as Mother of God
- Day 8: strength and virtue for the Church's leaders
- Day 9: that the Church remain strong and vibrant

## Fidelity note
Ember is not a translation of the PrayMoreNovenas text; it is an independent composition. Both honor Mary under the Theotokos/Mother-of-God title and share the daily Our Father / Hail Mary / Glory Be frame, but the substance diverges sharply: PMN structures the nine days as intercessory petitions (for mothers, religious, the Church, Church leaders), whereas Ember structures them as catechetical-Marian meditations following salvation history (Ephesus, the fiat, the Cross, the Assumption) and explicitly grounds itself in the ancient Sub Tuum Praesidium. Same feast and title, but different genre and day-by-day content; Ember is the more doctrinally/historically themed of the two.
