# Candlemas / Purification — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O Lord Jesus Christ, presented in the Temple and received into the arms of just Simeon, you are the Light that no darkness can overcome. Enter the temple of my heart in these nine days and prepare me to welcome you. Amen.

**Day 1 — Day 1: The Law fulfilled:** Though the Lord of the Temple, Christ submitted to the law of presentation as a humble Hebrew child. He who owed nothing chose to fulfill all righteousness for our sake. _(Intention: For the grace of humble obedience to God's will.)_

**Day 2 — Day 2: The Purification of Mary:** The Immaculate Virgin, who needed no purifying, came to the Temple in obedience like any other mother. Her humility teaches us never to set ourselves above the lowly path of the law of God. _(Intention: For the grace of humility, after the example of Our Lady.)_

**Day 3 — Day 3: The offering of the poor:** Mary and Joseph offered two turtledoves, the gift the law allowed to the poor. The King of kings was redeemed with the offering of the lowly, sanctifying poverty forever. _(Intention: For the poor, and for a spirit of detachment in my own heart.)_

**Day 4 — Day 4: Simeon's long waiting:** Simeon had waited all his life for the consolation of Israel, faithful to the promise that he would not die before seeing the Christ. His patience reminds us that God keeps His word in His own hour. _(Intention: For perseverance and patience while awaiting God's promises.)_

**Day 5 — Day 5: Nunc dimittis — now you may dismiss:** Holding the Child, Simeon prayed that he might now depart in peace, for his eyes had seen salvation. Having beheld Christ, he asked nothing more of this world. _(Intention: For the grace of a peaceful and holy death.)_

**Day 6 — Day 6: A light to the nations:** Simeon proclaimed the Child a light of revelation to the Gentiles and the glory of Israel. In Christ the long night of the world gives way to an unfailing dawn. _(Intention: That the light of Christ may reach those still in darkness.)_

**Day 7 — Day 7: The sword foretold:** Simeon warned Mary that a sword would pierce her own soul, joining her even now to her Son's coming Passion. She received the prophecy in silence, treasuring it in her heart. _(Intention: For the grace to unite my sorrows to those of Christ and His Mother.)_

**Day 8 — Day 8: Anna the prophetess:** The aged widow Anna, who served God night and day in the Temple with fasting and prayer, came forward and spoke of the Child to all who awaited redemption. A life of prayer made her quick to recognize her Savior. _(Intention: For perseverance in prayer and faithful worship.)_

**Day 9 — Day 9: The blessed candles:** On this feast the Church blesses candles and carries them in procession, for Christ Himself is the true Light they signify. As wax is consumed to give flame, so the Christian is called to be spent for love. _(Intention: That my life may shine with the light of Christ for others.)_

**Closing prayer:** O Christ, the Light of the world, as the blessed candle is consumed in giving light, so let my life be spent in your service. Through the prayers of the Virgin Mother who presented you in the Temple, grant me the grace I seek and a holy death in your peace. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas, "Presentation of the Lord Novena." Each day opens with the Sign of the Cross and "Dear Lord, we thank You and praise You for the mystery of the Presentation. In honor of this mystery, we ask You to hear our prayers," and closes "Lord, hear our prayers!" with the Sign of the Cross, plus a personalized "we especially ask in this novena for (mention your intentions here)." The nine days are organized as petitions:
- Day 1: blessing for all families to be full of holiness and love
- Day 2: God's blessing and protection for all children in the world
- Day 3: blessing and protection for all mothers
- Day 4: growth in submission to God's will
- Day 5: developing the humility necessary for spiritual growth
- Day 6: growth in holy poverty and detachment from material things
- Day 7: increased virtue of hope (referencing the witness of Simeon and Anna)
- Day 8: grace to accept suffering willingly as Mary did
- Day 9: deeper trust in God despite future hardships

## Fidelity note
Ember is an independent composition, not a translation of the PrayMoreNovenas text. Both treat the same feast and overlap on several virtues — submission to God's will, humility, holy poverty/detachment, and the figures of Simeon and Anna. But the genres differ: PMN frames the days as intercessions (for families, children, mothers), while Ember walks through the Luke 2 narrative scene by scene — the fulfillment of the Law, Mary's Purification, the offering of the poor, Simeon's waiting, the Nunc dimittis, the light to the nations, the sword prophecy, Anna, and the blessing of candles. Ember is more scripturally and liturgically grounded (it alone develops the candle symbolism that names the feast). All content is doctrinally and historically faithful; the two are parallel devotions of the same feast rather than the same text.
