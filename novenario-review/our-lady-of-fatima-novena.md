# Novena to Our Lady of Fatima — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** Our Lady of Fatima, tender Mother who came to call your children back to God, I begin this novena trusting in your maternal love. Teach me to pray, to do penance, and to console the Heart of Jesus. Through your Immaculate Heart, present my petitions to your Son and obtain for me the grace I now ask. Amen.

**Day 1 — The angel's call to prayer:** Before Mary came, an angel appeared to the children at Fatima, teaching them to adore God and to pray for sinners. He prepared their hearts by leading them deep into prayer, showing that adoration is the soil in which every grace grows. _(Intention: For the grace to adore God with a humble and recollected heart.)_ (every day)

**Day 2 — The Lady from heaven:** On 13 May 1917, a Lady brighter than the sun appeared above a small holm oak and asked the children to return on the thirteenth of each month. She came not to frighten but to draw her children gently back to her Son. _(Intention: For the grace to welcome Mary's words into my own life.)_ (every day)

**Day 3 — Pray the Rosary daily:** At every apparition Our Lady asked that the Rosary be prayed each day for peace and the conversion of sinners. She placed this simple, powerful prayer into the hands of children, and through them, into the hands of the whole world. _(Intention: For the grace to pray the Rosary daily with faith and love.)_ (every day)

**Day 4 — Penance and reparation:** Mary asked the children to make sacrifices for sinners and to offer their daily hardships to God in reparation. The little shepherds embraced penance with generous love, teaching us that small offerings, given in love, console the Heart of God. _(Intention: For the grace to offer my daily sacrifices for the conversion of sinners.)_ (every day)

**Day 5 — The Immaculate Heart:** Our Lady revealed her Immaculate Heart, surrounded by thorns from the sins of men, and asked for consecration and reparation. She promised that her Heart would be a refuge and the sure way leading souls to God. _(Intention: For the grace to consecrate myself wholly to the Immaculate Heart of Mary.)_ (every day)

**Day 6 — The holiness of the children:** Francisco longed to console the hidden Jesus, while Jacinta burned with love for sinners and the Holy Father; both died young and were canonized as saints. They show that holiness is offered even to the smallest and the youngest. _(Intention: For children and young people, that they may grow in holiness and love.)_ (every day)

**Day 7 — The miracle of the sun:** On 13 October 1917, before a crowd of thousands, the sun seemed to dance and plunge toward the earth, confirming the children's testimony. God gave this sign so that all might believe and heed His Mother's appeal. _(Intention: For the grace of firm faith in God's signs and His holy will.)_ (every day)

**Day 8 — Consecration of the world:** Our Lady asked for the consecration of the world to her Immaculate Heart and the devotion of the First Saturdays in reparation. She desires to gather all nations and all hearts and present them safely to her Son. _(Intention: For peace in the world and the consecration of all hearts to Mary.)_ (every day)

**Day 9 — The triumph of the Immaculate Heart:** Our Lady promised that in the end her Immaculate Heart would triumph and an era of peace would be granted to the world. This promise gives us hope, calling us to trust that no darkness can outlast the love of our Mother. _(Intention: For unshakable hope in the final triumph of the Immaculate Heart.)_ (every day)

**Closing prayer:** Our Lady of Fatima, who promised that your Immaculate Heart would triumph, keep me faithful to your requests of prayer and penance. Help me to pray the Rosary each day and to offer my sufferings for the conversion of sinners. May I one day rejoice with you and the shepherd children in the glory of heaven. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas has a Novena to Our Lady of Fatima (https://www.praymorenovenas.com/novena-our-lady-fatima). It opens each day with a Trinitarian blessing and a fixed invocation ("O Most Holy Virgin Mary, you came to Fatima to reveal the graces that come from praying the Holy Rosary..."), then gives a distinct daily meditation/intention, closing petitions to Our Lady of Fatima, Our Lady of the Rosary, and the Immaculate Heart, and a Trinitarian blessing. Daily themes there: Day 1 overcoming doubts as Gospel messengers; Day 2 the oppressed and gratitude; Day 3 those suffering injustice; Day 4 humility/forgiveness/amendment; Day 5 offering daily actions in love; Day 6 trust amid fear; Day 7 constant prayer and listening; Day 8 peace through trustful faith; Day 9 thanksgiving and continued prayer and sacrifice.

## Fidelity note
Ember is an independent composition, not a transcription of the PrayMoreNovenas text — none of the wording matches. Both share the same nine-day, fixed-opening + daily-meditation structure and the core Fatima themes (Rosary, penance/reparation, the Immaculate Heart, peace). Ember's days are more historically narrative (the angel, the May apparition, the miracle of the sun, the First Saturdays, the promised triumph), whereas PrayMoreNovenas leans toward generic petition themes. Faithful to the Fatima devotion in substance, but original in language.
