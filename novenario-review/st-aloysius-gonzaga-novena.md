# Novena to St. Aloysius Gonzaga — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O angelic St. Aloysius, who in your youth loved God with an undivided heart, look upon me as I begin this novena. Win for me purity of mind and body, and a love of God that nothing in this world can dim. Amen.

**Day 1 — Early love of God:** While still a small child Aloysius began to seek God, and he made a private vow of perpetual chastity at the age of nine. He teaches us that the young heart is not too small to give itself wholly to the Lord. _(Intention: For all children, that they may know and love God early.)_ (every day)

**Day 2 — Purity of heart:** Aloysius guarded his eyes and senses so faithfully that his innocence shone like that of an angel. He shows us that purity is not coldness but a heart kept undivided and free for God. _(Intention: For the grace of purity in thought, word, and deed.)_ (every day)

**Day 3 — Renouncing nobility:** Heir to a marquisate, Aloysius signed away his title and lands to his brother to follow Christ in poverty. He teaches us that no inheritance on earth is worth the loss of the kingdom of heaven. _(Intention: For detachment from riches and worldly ambition.)_ (every day)

**Day 4 — Obedience over his father's will:** For years Aloysius patiently bore his father's anger and schemes to keep him from the Jesuits, never yielding his resolve. He shows us that we may honor our parents and still obey God's higher claim with steadfast love. _(Intention: For young people seeking to follow their vocation faithfully.)_ (every day)

**Day 5 — A life of penance:** Aloysius embraced fasting, long prayer, and hard mortification, so that his superiors had to restrain his fervor. He reminds us that love for God presses the soul to give, not to spare itself. _(Intention: For the grace to deny myself for love of God.)_ (every day)

**Day 6 — Devotion to the Eucharist:** Aloysius prepared for Holy Communion with three days of thanksgiving and three of longing, so great was his love for the Blessed Sacrament. He teaches us to approach the altar with awe and burning desire. _(Intention: For a deeper reverence and love for the Holy Eucharist.)_ (every day)

**Day 7 — Serving the plague-stricken:** When plague swept through Rome, Aloysius begged to nurse the sick, carrying the dying on his own shoulders to the hospital. He shows us that charity counts no danger too great when a neighbor is in need. _(Intention: For generous service to the sick and the suffering.)_ (every day)

**Day 8 — Joyful acceptance of death:** Stricken by the very disease he tended, Aloysius welcomed his early death with peace, certain he was going to God. He teaches us to meet our last hour without fear, as the gateway to the One we have loved. _(Intention: For the grace of a holy and peaceful death.)_ (every day)

**Day 9 — Patron of youth:** In only twenty-three years Aloysius reached a holiness the Church now holds up to every young person. He assures us that sanctity is not measured by length of days but by the fullness of love poured out. _(Intention: For the particular grace I now entrust to St. Aloysius.)_ (every day)

**Closing prayer:** O St. Aloysius Gonzaga, patron of the young, present my petitions to the Lord you served so faithfully. Help me to flee what offends God and to spend my strength in His service, that with you I may one day behold His face. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas has a St. Aloysius Gonzaga novena (https://www.praymorenovenas.com/st-aloysius-gonzaga-novena). Each day opens with a Trinitarian blessing and a thanksgiving for St. Aloysius as an example, gives a daily meditation on his life and a "Pray for me..." petition, space for personal intentions, and closes "St. Aloysius Gonzaga, pray for us!" with a Trinitarian blessing. Daily themes: Day 1 beginning holiness in youth (his turn to prayer in illness); Day 2 discerning God's will (drawn by Jesuit missionaries); Day 3 radical religious commitment despite family opposition; Day 4 renouncing his worldly inheritance; Day 5 persevering through opposition; Day 6 service through personal suffering / chronic illness; Day 7 courage in caring for plague victims; Day 8 patient endurance of suffering; Day 9 faithful service unto death.

## Fidelity note
Ember's text is original and shares no wording with PrayMoreNovenas, but the two cover the same biography of the young Jesuit and overlap heavily in theme: youthful holiness, renouncing nobility, persevering against family opposition, serving the plague-stricken, and a holy early death. Ember adds emphases the source treats only lightly — purity/chastity (his vow at nine), penance, and Eucharistic devotion — which are central to Aloysius's traditional cult, so they strengthen rather than distort the fidelity. A faithful, well-grounded original composition closely parallel to the source in structure and substance.
