# Novena to the Most Holy Trinity — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** Most Holy Trinity, one God in three Persons, we adore You with all the powers of our soul. Father who creates us, Son who redeems us, Spirit who sanctifies us, we bow before the abyss of Your love. Draw us into the communion that You are. Amen.

**Day 1 — The mystery of one God in three Persons:** We worship one God who is Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — not three gods, but a single divine nature in three distinct Persons. This mystery surpasses our minds, yet Christ revealed it that we might share His own life. Faith bows where reason cannot reach. _(Intention: For the grace to believe and adore the mystery of the Trinity with humble faith.)_

**Day 2 — God the Father, source of all:** The Father is the unbegotten source from whom all things proceed, who made us out of love and calls us His children. From His hands we received our being, and into His care we may entrust every fear. He is the Father to whom Jesus taught us to pray. _(Intention: For the grace to trust the providence of God our Father.)_

**Day 3 — God the Son, our Redeemer:** The Son, eternally begotten of the Father, became man and gave His life to redeem us. In Him the invisible God took on a human face and a human heart. Through the Son we know the Father, for whoever sees Him sees the One who sent Him. _(Intention: For the grace to know the Father through Jesus Christ the Son.)_

**Day 4 — God the Holy Spirit, our Sanctifier:** The Holy Spirit proceeds from the Father and the Son as their eternal bond of love, poured into our hearts to make us holy. He is the giver of life, the consoler, the breath of God within us. Without Him we cannot even call Jesus Lord. _(Intention: For the grace to be docile to the sanctifying work of the Holy Spirit.)_

**Day 5 — The Trinity dwelling in the soul:** Jesus promised that the Father and He would come and make their dwelling in those who love Him. In a soul living in grace, the whole Trinity abides as in a living temple. Heaven is not only above us; it begins within the baptized heart. _(Intention: For the grace to live always in the state of grace, a worthy temple of God.)_

**Day 6 — A communion of love:** The three divine Persons exist in an eternal exchange of self-giving love, for God is love itself. From this communion flows every true bond of charity among us. To imitate the Trinity is to live no longer for ourselves but for one another. _(Intention: For the grace to love others with the self-giving love of the Trinity.)_

**Day 7 — Created and signed in the Trinity:** We were baptized in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit, and our every prayer is sealed with that same sign. The Sign of the Cross traces our whole life into the Trinity. We belong, body and soul, to the God who is Three. _(Intention: For the grace to make the Sign of the Cross with living faith.)_

**Day 8 — All glory to the Triune God:** Every Glory Be we have prayed since childhood is an act of adoration of the Trinity, joining the unending praise of the angels. The whole purpose of our existence is to glorify God now and forever. Our small doxologies echo the eternal worship of heaven. _(Intention: For the grace to make our whole life a hymn of glory to God.)_

**Day 9 — Toward the vision of the Trinity:** The blessedness for which we were made is to behold the Holy Trinity face to face in heaven. What we now adore in faith we shall one day contemplate in glory, lost in wonder and love. This is the hope that gives meaning to every day of our pilgrimage. _(Intention: For the grace of final perseverance and the eternal vision of God.)_

Each day also prays one Our Father, one Hail Mary, and one Glory Be.

**Closing prayer:** Glory be to the Father, and to the Son, and to the Holy Spirit, as it was in the beginning, is now, and ever shall be. Most Holy Trinity, abide in us as in a living temple, and let our whole life become an offering of praise. Make us at last partakers of Your eternal blessedness. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
A nine-day novena built on the Gloria. Daily opening: "In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen." with a threefold Glory Be: "Glory be to the Father, Who created me in the image and likeness of God / to the Son, Who through his Passion and Cross, triumphed over hell / to the Holy Spirit, Who cleansed me of my original sin at Baptism." Days 1–7 ask the seven gifts of the Holy Spirit (wisdom, understanding, knowledge, counsel, fortitude, piety, fear of the Lord); days 8–9 ask fruits of the Spirit (joy, goodness). Each day closes with the doxology and the Sign of the Cross.

## Fidelity note
Ember's novena is an original composition with a markedly different organizing principle from PrayMoreNovenas. Where PrayMoreNovenas frames the nine days around the seven gifts and fruits of the Holy Spirit (effectively a Spirit-centered novena), Ember builds a properly Trinitarian catechesis: the mystery itself, then each Person in turn (Father, Son, Spirit), then the Trinity indwelling the soul, the communion of love, the Sign of the Cross, the Glory Be, and the beatific vision. Both share the Gloria as a structural anchor — Ember's closing prayer is the Glory Be expanded. Ember's content is doctrinally rich and orthodox, closely tracking the Catechism's treatment of the Trinity. Independent wording; arguably more faithful to the feast's actual object than the source.
