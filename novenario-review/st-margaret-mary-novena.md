# St. Margaret Mary Alacoque — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** St. Margaret Mary, faithful confidante of the Sacred Heart, who beheld the furnace of love burning for sinners, draw me close to that wounded Heart. Teach me to console Him by my love, and obtain for me the grace I implore in this novena. Amen.

**Day 1 — The Heart that loves us:** Jesus showed St. Margaret Mary His Heart as a burning furnace of love, surrounded by thorns and crowned with the cross. He revealed that His one desire is to be loved in return by the souls for whom He gave everything.  _(Intention: For the grace to return love to the Heart of Jesus who loves us first.)_  (every day)

**Day 2 — Chosen in humility:** Christ chose a hidden, unlearned nun to be the herald of His Heart, passing over the wise and powerful of the world. St. Margaret Mary teaches us that God delights to accomplish great things through the lowly and humble of heart.  _(Intention: For the humility to let God work through our lowliness.)_  (every day)

**Day 3 — The apparitions at Paray-le-Monial:** Over several years in the quiet convent of Paray-le-Monial, Jesus poured out His confidences to St. Margaret Mary, asking that devotion to His Heart be established throughout the world. She faithfully received and guarded this great mission for the Church.  _(Intention: For openness to the gentle invitations of Christ in our hearts.)_  (every day)

**Day 4 — Reparation for ingratitude:** Jesus lamented to St. Margaret Mary the coldness and ingratitude His love so often receives, and asked her to make reparation. She teaches us to console the Sacred Heart by offering our love in place of the world's indifference.  _(Intention: For a spirit of reparation for the offenses against the love of God.)_  (every day)

**Day 5 — The Holy Hour:** Jesus asked St. Margaret Mary to keep watch with Him in prayer, sharing in the agony He suffered in Gethsemane. The Holy Hour she received teaches us to remain with the Lord and to comfort Him in His sorrow.  _(Intention: For the grace to keep faithful watch with Jesus in prayer.)_  (every day)

**Day 6 — The First Fridays:** Through St. Margaret Mary, Jesus promised the grace of final repentance to those who receive Communion on nine consecutive First Fridays in His honor. This great promise reveals the boundless patience of His Heart for sinners.  _(Intention: For perseverance in the devotion of the Nine First Fridays.)_  (every day)

**Day 7 — The Twelve Promises:** Jesus made twelve great promises to those devoted to His Heart, among them peace in their families and comfort in their trials. St. Margaret Mary invites us to take these promises to heart with humble and grateful trust.  _(Intention: For confidence in the promises of the Sacred Heart for our families.)_  (every day)

**Day 8 — Patience under trial:** St. Margaret Mary endured years of misunderstanding, mockery, and suspicion from those around her before her message was believed. She teaches us to bear contradiction in silence, uniting our trials to the wounded Heart of Christ.  _(Intention: For patience and trust when we are misunderstood or rejected.)_  (every day)

**Day 9 — Refuge in the Sacred Heart:** St. Margaret Mary found in the Heart of Jesus a sure refuge for every weakness and every sorrow. She calls us to take shelter there, certain that the Heart pierced for our sake will never turn away a contrite soul.  _(Intention: For the grace to make the Sacred Heart our refuge in life and death.)_  (every day)

**Closing prayer:** St. Margaret Mary, apostle of the Sacred Heart and model of reparation, intercede that I may take refuge in the Heart of Jesus all the days of my life. May I love Him who has so loved us, and trust the promises He revealed through you for every faithful soul. Amen.

## Fidelity note
With no FishEaters or PrayMoreNovenas counterpart, there is nothing to measure verbal fidelity against. The content is doctrinally sound and faithful to St. Margaret Mary's life and the Sacred Heart revelations at Paray-le-Monial (1673–1675): the Heart as a furnace of love crowned with thorns, her humble hidden vocation, the call to reparation for ingratitude, the Holy Hour, the Communion of the Nine First Fridays, the Twelve Promises, and her years of being misunderstood. Themes track the established Sacred Heart devotion closely. Treat as an original, sourced-by-spirituality composition rather than a transcription.
