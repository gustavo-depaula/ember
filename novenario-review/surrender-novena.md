# Surrender Novena (Fr. Dolindo) — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** Lord Jesus, you who said, "Come to me, all you who are weary, and I will give you rest," I come now with my burdens and my fears. Teach me through these nine days the secret of true surrender, that I may stop struggling and let you act. I place my whole life in your hands. Amen.

**Day 1 — Why do you worry?:** Jesus asks, "Why do you confuse yourselves by worrying?" He promises that every act of true, blind, and complete surrender to Him produces the effect we desire and resolves all difficult situations. He does not ask us to understand, only to leave the care of our affairs to Him.  _(Intention: For my anxious heart, I pray: O Jesus, I surrender myself to You; take care of everything.)_

**Day 2 — What surrender truly means:** To surrender does not mean to fret, to be agitated, or to lose hope, as though I were still in charge. It means to close the eyes of my soul, to turn away from my troubles, and to leave them in Jesus' hands so He can act in peace.  _(Intention: To stop my agitation, I pray: O Jesus, I surrender myself to You; take care of everything.)_

**Day 3 — Turmoil from relying on self:** Jesus says I am sleepless because I want to judge everything by my own reasoning and provide for everything by my own strength. When I rely on myself, I only multiply my confusion; His grace flows only into hearts emptied of self-trust.  _(Intention: To let go of my own reasoning, I pray: O Jesus, I surrender myself to You; take care of everything.)_

**Day 4 — Do not plan the outcome:** How often, Jesus laments, would things have gone well had I not anxiously occupied myself with them and instead left them to Him. Surrender means to ask Him to act, and then to abandon myself entirely to Him without dictating how He must answer.  _(Intention: To stop dictating the answer, I pray: O Jesus, I surrender myself to You; take care of everything.)_

**Day 5 — Pray, but in surrender:** Jesus desires that I pray for the help of His intervention, yet always in a spirit of surrender, willing that He provide whatever is truly good. Prayer joined to surrender is rooted not in my plans but in His love, and so it is always heard.  _(Intention: To pray in true surrender, I pray: O Jesus, I surrender myself to You; take care of everything.)_

**Day 6 — Close your eyes and rest:** Jesus invites me to close my eyes and let myself be carried on the flowing current of His grace, as a child rests in its mother's arms. When I stop thinking anxiously about what concerns me, He will think of it for me in His own perfect way.  _(Intention: To rest like a child in His arms, I pray: O Jesus, I surrender myself to You; take care of everything.)_

**Day 7 — Miracles flow from surrender:** Jesus reveals that He works miracles in proportion to my full surrender to Him and my ceasing to trust in myself. When the soul abandons itself entirely, He pours out treasures of grace that anxious effort could never obtain.  _(Intention: To open the way for His grace, I pray: O Jesus, I surrender myself to You; take care of everything.)_

**Day 8 — He works while I rest:** When I am in deepest distress, Jesus tells me to close my eyes and surrender, and He will lead me as if I were carried over the threshold of a new life. He works most freely in souls that no longer wrestle Him for the wheel, but let Him do everything.  _(Intention: To let Him do everything, I pray: O Jesus, I surrender myself to You; take care of everything.)_

**Day 9 — Through Mary, wholly His:** On this final day I turn to Mary and say, "Mother, I am yours now and forever; through you and with you I always want to belong completely to Jesus." In her arms the surrender Jesus asked of me is made whole, for she carries every trusting child straight to her Son.  _(Intention: Through Mary's hands, I pray: O Jesus, I surrender myself to You; take care of everything.)_

(After every day: one Our Father, one Hail Mary, one Glory Be.)

**Closing prayer:** O most loving Jesus, I thank you for the peace you offer to every heart that trusts in you. Free me at last from the anxious need to control what only you can hold, and let your providence carry me. Mother Mary, help me to belong completely to your Son. Amen.

## Fidelity note
The Surrender Novena has a well-known traditional text: each day Jesus speaks a portion of His message to Fr. Dolindo Ruotolo, and every day repeats the refrain "O Jesus, I surrender myself to You; take care of everything" (the classic form repeats it ten times daily, with Day 9's variant "Mother, I am yours now and forever…"). Ember faithfully preserves the heart of the devotion — the surrendering refrain is the intention on all nine days, and the meditations paraphrase the authentic message (Day 1 "Why do you confuse yourselves by worrying?", the warning against self-reliance, miracles in proportion to surrender, the Marian conclusion on Day 9). The wording is a faithful paraphrase/summary rather than the full verbatim message, and Ember presents it as a single daily reading rather than the traditional 10× repetition of the refrain.
