# Any saint (generic) — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O God, who are wonderful in your saints, I come to honor [Saint N.], your faithful servant whom you crowned with glory. Through the merits and intercession of this holy one, hear the prayers I offer over these nine days. Kindle in me the same love for you that burned in their heart. Amen.

**Day 1 — The universal call to holiness:** Every saint was once a sinner who answered God's call to become more. Their holiness was not reserved for a special few but is the destiny offered to me as well, if I will only let grace have its way.  _(Intention: For the grace to take seriously God's call to become a saint.)_

**Day 2 — A friend before God:** The saints are not distant statues but living friends who stand before the face of God and pray for us by name. To invoke them is to gain a companion on the road who has already finished the journey I am walking.  _(Intention: For a true friendship with the saint I now invoke.)_

**Day 3 — Imitating their faith:** What made this saint great was not their gifts but their faith, which held fast to God when everything seemed against them. I am called not merely to admire that faith but to imitate it in the ordinary trials of my own day.  _(Intention: For a stronger faith that holds fast to God in every trial.)_

**Day 4 — Imitating their charity:** The saints loved God above all and their neighbor for His sake, often spending themselves for the poorest and least lovable. Their charity rebukes my comfort and invites me to a love that costs something.  _(Intention: For a generous charity toward those most in need.)_

**Day 5 — My particular intention:** The saints know our struggles, for they once carried such burdens themselves before God lifted them up. Today I lay before this holy friend the particular need that brought me here, trusting it to their powerful prayer.  _(Intention: For the particular intention I now entrust to this saint's prayers.)_

**Day 6 — Patience in suffering:** No saint reached glory without the cross, and many bore illness, rejection, or martyrdom with serene trust. They teach me that suffering joined to Christ is never wasted but becomes the very road to holiness.  _(Intention: For patience and trust in carrying my own crosses.)_

**Day 7 — Faithful in small things:** Holiness was woven into the saints' ordinary days through small acts done with great love. I need not wait for heroic moments; sanctity is offered to me in the duties and people right in front of me.  _(Intention: For faithfulness in the small duties of each ordinary day.)_

**Day 8 — Hope of heaven:** The saints kept their eyes fixed on heaven, and that hope made them free amid the world's losses and fears. Their joy now is the promise held out to me, and it loosens my grip on lesser things.  _(Intention: For a living hope of heaven that frees me from earthly fears.)_

**Day 9 — Companions on the way home:** We belong to one Church spanning heaven and earth, and the saints cheer us on as we run the race they have finished. On this last day I ask to walk the rest of my life in their company until I join them in the joy of God.  _(Intention: For perseverance until I am united with the saints in glory.)_

(After every day: one Our Father, one Hail Mary, one Glory Be.)

**Closing prayer:** O [Saint N.], I thank God for the grace He poured out in your life and for the example you left to guide me home. Take the petitions of this novena and lay them before the Lord, and obtain for me the one thing necessary: to become a saint as you are a saint. Pray for me now and at the hour of my death. Amen.

## Fidelity note
This is a deliberately generic, fill-in-the-name template with no single authoritative source to transcribe; the closest standard form is the classic "O God, who are wonderful in your saints…" collect, which Ember adopts almost verbatim as its opening, and the traditional "pray for me now and at the hour of my death" close. The nine meditations are an original, theologically careful arc — call to holiness, communion of saints, imitation of the saint's faith and charity, the personal intention, suffering, fidelity in small things, hope of heaven, perseverance — fully consonant with Catholic teaching on the saints. With no fixed traditional text to match and the PrayMoreNovenas page not resolving to a generic template, Ember's version stands as a sound original composition rather than a comparison against a specific source.
