# Novena to St. Bernard of Clairvaux — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O St. Bernard, mellifluous Doctor and faithful servant of Mary, your every word breathed love for Jesus Christ. Teach me to love God for his own sake, and to find in his Holy Name the sweetness that drew your heart heavenward. Amen.

**Day 1 — Leaving the world:** The young Bernard renounced wealth and a brilliant future to enter the poor and rigorous monastery of Cîteaux, persuading even his brothers to follow. He teaches us that the love of God is worth every earthly sacrifice.  _(Intention: For the courage to seek God above worldly success.)_

**Day 2 — Loving God for His own sake:** In his treatise 'On Loving God,' Bernard asked why we should love God and answered that God himself is the reason, and the measure is to love without measure. He calls us beyond fear and reward to a love that seeks God alone.  _(Intention: For a love of God that asks for nothing but Himself.)_

**Day 3 — The sweetness of the Holy Name:** Bernard wrote that the name of Jesus is honey in the mouth, melody in the ear, and gladness in the heart. He teaches us to call upon that Name often, as a remedy in temptation and a comfort in sorrow.  _(Intention: For the habit of calling on the name of Jesus throughout the day.)_

**Day 4 — Devotion to Our Lady:** Bernard called Mary the aqueduct through which every grace flows down to us, and counseled, 'If the winds of temptation arise, look to the star, call upon Mary.' His tender confidence in her is the root of the Memorare tradition.  _(Intention: For confident recourse to Mary in every need.)_

**Day 5 — Never despair of Mary's help:** From Bernard's teaching springs the prayer that no one who fled to Mary's protection was ever left unaided. He assures the most discouraged sinner that the Mother of Mercy turns no one away.  _(Intention: For hope and trust that Our Lady never abandons her children.)_

**Day 6 — Zeal for the Church:** From his quiet cloister Bernard was drawn into the great affairs of his age, healing a papal schism and counseling rulers with fearless honesty. He shows that the contemplative life bears fruit in love and service of the whole Church.  _(Intention: For courage to serve the truth even when it is unwelcome.)_

**Day 7 — The bride and the Bridegroom:** In his sermons on the Song of Songs, Bernard described the soul as a bride longing for union with Christ her Bridegroom. He teaches that prayer is not mere duty but the ardent friendship of love.  _(Intention: For a prayer life enkindled by love rather than habit alone.)_

**Day 8 — Humility, the true foundation:** Bernard wrote at length on the degrees of humility and pride, knowing that no virtue stands without a lowly heart. He teaches us that self-knowledge is the doorway to the love of God.  _(Intention: For honest self-knowledge and a truly humble heart.)_

**Day 9 — Longing for the vision of God:** All Bernard's labors flowed from one desire: to see and to enjoy God forever, the final degree of love when the soul loves itself only for God's sake. He invites us to live each day reaching toward that blessed vision.  _(Intention: For the grace to long above all for the vision of God in heaven.)_

**Closing prayer:** St. Bernard, who taught us never to despair of Mary's help, present to her the petition I bring in this novena. Obtain for me a heart on fire with love of God, and lead me at last to contemplate forever the One whom you so longed to see. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas hosts a St. Bernard of Clairvaux novena. Each day is bracketed by the Sign of the Cross and follows a fixed template: thanksgiving to God for giving Bernard as an example, an address to the saint touching one aspect of his life, the request "Please bring my petitions before God…", a petition for the reader's own spiritual growth (e.g., "Pray for me, that I may always seek to serve God worthily in my life, as you did. Pray that I may be eager to give my life to serving Him and His Church"), a space for personal intentions, and the refrain "St. Bernard of Clairvaux, pray for us!"

## Fidelity note
Both are nine-day novenas built on episodes of Bernard's life, but the wording is independent. PMN keeps its daily focus fairly general (service to God and the Church); Ember draws far more directly on Bernard's own writings and reputation — "On Loving God" and its "love without measure," the Holy Name as "honey in the mouth," Mary as the aqueduct and the "look to the star, call upon Mary" counsel behind the Memorare, the Song of Songs sermons, and the degrees of humility. These are accurate to the Doctor's corpus, making Ember the richer and more sourced text. It is a faithful original composition that shares only the common novena scaffolding with the PMN source.
