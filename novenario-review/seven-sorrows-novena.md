# Septenary of the Seven Sorrows of Mary — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O sorrowful Mother of God, who stood faithfully beside your Son through every wound of His Passion, turn your eyes of mercy upon me. By the seven swords that pierced your heart, obtain for me a true sorrow for sin and a share in your unfailing love. Amen.

**Day 1 — First Sorrow: The prophecy of Simeon:** When Mary presented the Infant Jesus in the Temple, the aged Simeon foretold that a sword would pierce her own soul. From that day she carried the weight of all that her Son would suffer, treasuring it in faith.  _(Intention: For the grace to accept the sorrows God permits in our lives.)_

**Day 2 — Second Sorrow: The flight into Egypt:** Warned of Herod's murderous rage, Mary fled by night into a foreign land to save her Child. She knew the fear of the refugee and the hardship of exile, yet trusted God to guard them on an unknown road.  _(Intention: For all refugees, exiles, and families forced from their homes.)_

**Day 3 — Third Sorrow: The loss of Jesus in the Temple:** For three days Mary searched in anguish for her lost Son, until she found Him in His Father's house. She teaches us the sorrow of seeming abandonment by God, and the joy of seeking Him until He is found.  _(Intention: For all who feel they have lost God and long to find Him again.)_

**Day 4 — Fourth Sorrow: Mary meets Jesus on the way to Calvary:** On the road to Golgotha the eyes of the Mother met the eyes of her wounded, burdened Son. No word was needed; in that glance she joined her whole heart to His sacrifice, refusing to look away.  _(Intention: For the courage to accompany the suffering and not turn away.)_

**Day 5 — Fifth Sorrow: The Crucifixion and death of Jesus:** Mary stood at the foot of the Cross and watched her Son die, hearing Him give her to John as the mother of us all. She did not flee the horror but remained, her compassion uniting her to His redeeming love.  _(Intention: For the dying, and for all who keep vigil at a deathbed.)_

**Day 6 — Sixth Sorrow: Jesus taken down from the Cross:** Into the arms of His Mother the lifeless body of Jesus was laid, and she held once more the Son she had cradled at Bethlehem. In that embrace of grief she shows us the dignity of mourning what we love.  _(Intention: For all who grieve the loss of someone dear.)_

**Day 7 — Seventh Sorrow: The burial of Jesus:** Mary saw her Son sealed within the tomb and returned home in silence, holding in her heart a hope no darkness could extinguish. She teaches us to keep faith through the long Saturday, certain that the Lord will rise.  _(Intention: For perseverance in hope through every dark and waiting hour.)_

**Closing prayer:** Our Lady of Sorrows, who became Mother of us all at the foot of the Cross, keep me close to your wounded heart. Teach me to stand with you in the sufferings of this life, that I may also stand with you in the joy of the Resurrection. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
FishEaters has a "Novena in Honor of the Dolors of the Blessed Virgin" (anchor #dolors). It is a single unified prayer said once daily for nine days — it does NOT enumerate the seven sorrows individually. Full verbatim text: "O most holy and afflicted Virgin! Queen of martyrs! Thou who didst stand beneath the Cross, witnessing the agony of thy divine Son -- through the unceasing sufferings of thy life of sorry [sorrow], and the bliss which now more than amply repays thee for thy past trials, look down with a mother's tenderness and pity on me, who kneel before thee to venerate thy dolors, and place my requests, with filial confidence, in the sanctuary of thy wounded heart; present them, I beseech thee on my behalf, to Jesus Christ. Through the merits of His most sacred death and passion, and through thy sufferings at the foot of the Cross, I hope to obtain the grant of my present petition. To whom shall I recur in my wants and miseries if not to thee, O Mother of mercy, who, having so deeply drunk of the chalice of thy Son, canst compassionate the woes of those who still sigh in the land of exile? Sancta Maria, Mater Dolorosa, ora pro me!"

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
A 9-day "Our Lady of Sorrows" novena. Each day: Sign of the Cross, an introductory prayer thanking God for Our Lady, an apostrophe to Our Lady of Sorrows, a day-specific intention, a request for intercession, closing Sign of the Cross. The nine daily intentions are petitions for groups: mothers; families; parents witnessing children's suffering; those grieving a death; those in emotional anguish; growth in trust in God; growth in devotion to Mary; uniting our sufferings to Christ's; a strong and vibrant Church.

## Fidelity note
Ember's version is an original composition structured as a true seven-day septenary keyed to the canonical Seven Sorrows (Simeon's prophecy → flight into Egypt → loss in the Temple → meeting on the way to Calvary → Crucifixion → deposition → burial), which is the historic Servite devotion. Neither source matches this structure: FishEaters is a single generic dolors prayer (no enumerated sorrows, and a 9-day novena rather than a 7-day septenary), and PMN is a 9-day novena of intercessory petitions for groups rather than meditations on the sorrows. Ember's "seven swords" opening and Servite framing are traditional and accurate; the wording is its own, and its seven-sorrow enumeration is actually more faithful to the classic devotion than either online source.
