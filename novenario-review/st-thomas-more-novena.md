# Novena to St. Thomas More — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O steadfast St. Thomas More, who held fast to God's law amid the favor and threats of kings, look upon me as I begin this novena. Win for me a clear conscience and the courage to keep it, whatever the cost. Amen.

**Day 1 — A holy life in the world:** Thomas More once considered the cloister but discerned his call to serve God as a layman, husband, and public servant. He teaches us that the path to holiness runs through the ordinary duties of family and work. _(Intention: For the grace to seek holiness in my daily state of life.)_ (every day)

**Day 2 — A man of prayer:** Amid the burdens of high office, More rose early for prayer and Scripture, wearing a hidden hair shirt beneath his fine robes. He shows us that the busiest life must be rooted in daily, hidden union with God. _(Intention: For fidelity to daily prayer amid a busy life.)_ (every day)

**Day 3 — Devoted husband and father:** More made his home a school of learning and faith, educating his daughters with the same care as his son. He teaches us that love of God begins in the patient, joyful love of one's own household. _(Intention: For Christian families and the education of children in the faith.)_ (every day)

**Day 4 — Integrity in public office:** As Lord Chancellor, More judged justly and refused the bribes that corrupted so many around him. He shows us that authority is a trust from God, to be exercised with honesty and the fear of the Lord. _(Intention: For honesty and justice among those who hold public office.)_ (every day)

**Day 5 — The voice of conscience:** When asked to swear against the truth, More chose silence and then refusal, unwilling to damn his soul to please a king. He teaches us that conscience formed by God's truth must never be sold, even under threat. _(Intention: For the courage to follow my conscience faithfully.)_ (every day)

**Day 6 — Loss of all things:** More gave up his office, his wealth, and his liberty rather than betray the Church, leaving his beloved family to want. He shows us that no honor or comfort is worth purchasing at the price of faith. _(Intention: For detachment from honor, security, and human respect.)_ (every day)

**Day 7 — Patience in prison:** During fifteen months in the Tower of London, More wrote tender letters and treatises on the Passion, finding consolation in Christ's own suffering. He reminds us that even a prison can become a place of peace when offered to God. _(Intention: For all who are imprisoned or persecuted for the faith.)_ (every day)

**Day 8 — Humor on the scaffold:** Climbing the unsteady scaffold, More jested with his guard and asked help going up, promising to see to his own coming down. He teaches us that a soul anchored in God can meet even death with serenity and good cheer. _(Intention: For peace and trust in the face of suffering and death.)_ (every day)

**Day 9 — The King's good servant, but God's first:** At the block More declared himself the king's good servant but God's first, then forgave his executioner and died in faith. He assures us that fidelity to God, though it cost everything, is never truly defeated. _(Intention: For the particular grace I now entrust to St. Thomas More.)_ (every day)

**Closing prayer:** O St. Thomas More, good servant of your king but first of God, present my petitions to the Lord for whom you laid down your life. Teach me to serve faithfully in the world without surrendering my soul, until I share your joy in heaven. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas has a St. Thomas More novena (https://www.praymorenovenas.com/st-thomas-more-novena). Each day opens with a Trinitarian blessing, thanks God for an aspect of More's witness, and adds a petition plus space for personal intentions, closing "through Christ Our Lord. Amen." with a Trinitarian blessing. Daily themes: Day 1 holiness in everyday lay life; Day 2 submission to God's will (his discernment to serve God in the world); Day 3 virtue in professional life (lawyer/Parliament, resisting corruption); Day 4 holiness in family life; Day 5 integrity and honesty; Day 6 the sanctity of marriage (refusing the king's unlawful marriage); Day 7 fortitude; Day 8 courage and commitment to the Church / papal authority; Day 9 joy in suffering (his humor before execution).

## Fidelity note
Ember's prayers are an original composition with no shared wording, yet they parallel the PrayMoreNovenas novena almost theme-for-theme: lay holiness, prayer/discernment, family life, integrity in office, conscience, fortitude/loss, fidelity to the Church, and joy/humor in death. Ember's Day 5–6 ("the voice of conscience," "loss of all things") and Day 8 ("humor on the scaffold") closely echo the source's conscience, sacrifice, and "joy in suffering" days. The biography (hair shirt, Tower imprisonment, "the king's good servant but God's first") is accurate. A faithful, closely parallel original; the FishEaters version could not be verified.
