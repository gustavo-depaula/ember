# Novena to St. Josemaría Escrivá — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** St. Josemaría, faithful priest and tireless apostle of holiness in ordinary life, intercede for us before the throne of God. You who saw the divine pathways hidden in the streets and workshops of the world, teach us to walk them. Obtain for us the grace we seek in this novena, if it be for God's glory. Amen.

**Day 1 — The universal call to holiness:** St. Josemaría preached tirelessly that holiness is not reserved for priests and religious but is the vocation of every baptized person. The married, the worker, the student — all are called to be saints where they are. Sanctity is meant to be lived in the middle of the street. _(Intention: For the grace to believe that God truly calls me to be a saint.)_

**Day 2 — Divine filiation:** At the root of St. Josemaría's spirit lay the joyful certainty of being a child of God. To know oneself a beloved son or daughter of the Father changes everything, banishing fear and filling the heart with confidence. This is the foundation on which his whole teaching rests. _(Intention: For the grace to live as a confident and beloved child of God.)_

**Day 3 — The sanctification of work:** St. Josemaría taught that ordinary work, done well and offered to God, becomes a prayer and a means of redemption. The hidden years of Jesus in the workshop of Nazareth gave dignity to all honest labor. Our desks and tools can be altars where we meet the Lord. _(Intention: For the grace to sanctify my work and offer it daily to God.)_

**Day 4 — Finding God in daily life:** He insisted that we can and must find God not apart from the world but within its ordinary circumstances. Family, friendships, and the tasks of each hour are the very material of our sanctification. There is something holy hidden in the most ordinary situations. _(Intention: For the grace to find God in the ordinary moments of each day.)_

**Day 5 — The heroism of small duties:** St. Josemaría taught that great holiness is built from small things done with great love and constancy. The hidden faithfulness of an ordinary day, repeated without applause, is its own quiet heroism. He who is faithful in little will be entrusted with much. _(Intention: For the grace to be faithful and generous in small daily duties.)_

**Day 6 — A contemplative in the midst of the world:** He desired that Christians in the world become contemplatives, turning their work and rest alike into ongoing conversation with God. Prayer need not be reserved for the chapel but can rise from the office, the home, and the road. A life can become an unbroken dialogue with the Lord. _(Intention: For the grace to keep a spirit of prayer throughout the whole day.)_

**Day 7 — Apostolate of friendship:** St. Josemaría saw genuine friendship as a privileged path for bringing souls closer to Christ. Through sincere affection and example, ordinary Christians can sow the love of God in those around them. We are called to be apostles among our friends, naturally and without pretense. _(Intention: For the grace to draw friends closer to Christ through genuine love.)_

**Day 8 — Cheerfulness and the cross:** He taught that Christian joy is born not from the absence of suffering but from embracing the cross with love. Difficulties accepted for God become a source of deep and lasting peace. The saint of the ordinary smiled because he knew himself held by his Father. _(Intention: For the grace to carry daily crosses with cheerfulness and peace.)_

**Day 9 — Love through the hands of Mary:** St. Josemaría had a tender filial love for Our Lady, to whom he entrusted all his work and the souls in his care. He taught that the way to Jesus is always made smoother by the hand of His Mother. With childlike confidence he placed everything in her keeping. _(Intention: For the grace to entrust our whole life to Jesus through Mary.)_

Each day also prays one Our Father, one Hail Mary, and one Glory Be.

**Closing prayer:** O God, who raised up St. Josemaría to proclaim the universal call to holiness and the apostolic value of ordinary work, grant that through his intercession we may make our daily labor a continual prayer. May we serve You faithfully in the small duties of each day. We ask this through Christ our Lord. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
A nine-day novena invoking St. Josemaría Escrivá, founder of Opus Dei, who taught people to "make their daily life and work holy." Daily opening: "In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen." Each day closes with a Hail Mary, a Glory Be, and the trinitarian blessing. Daily intentions: 1 Christian families and childbearing; 2 honest, suitable, steady employment; 3 finding Christian joy in pain and illness; 4 setting good examples for children's Christian formation; 5 understanding work as service, imitating Christ's carpenter years; 6 viewing pain as participation in Christ's Cross; 7 gratitude for children and their formation; 8 recognizing love as the source of work's value; 9 accepting the cross as spiritual purification.

## Fidelity note
Ember's novena is an original composition that faithfully captures the saint's actual spirituality, often more squarely than the source. Both rest on the same pillars — the universal call to holiness, the sanctification of ordinary work (Jesus' hidden years as carpenter), love as the value of work, and the redemptive cross — and Ember's day 3 (work), day 5 (love in small things), day 8 (cheerfulness and the cross) line up thematically with PrayMoreNovenas' days 5, 8, and 6. Ember additionally surfaces signature elements of St. Josemaría's teaching that the source's petitions leave implicit: divine filiation, being "a contemplative in the midst of the world," and the apostolate of friendship — all authentic to his message. The closing adapts the saint's liturgical collect ("the universal call to holiness and the apostolic value of ordinary work"). Independent wording; arguably a closer portrait of his charism than the source's intention list.
