# St. John Bosco — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O glorious St. John Bosco, father and teacher of the young, you spent yourself wholly for souls and never turned away a child in need. Draw near to me now as you drew near to your boys, and present my prayer before the throne of God. Amen.

**Day 1 — Day 1: A heart for abandoned youth:** In the streets and prisons of Turin, John Bosco saw boys with no one to love them and resolved to be their father. His first oratory began with a single orphan and grew into a refuge for hundreds. _(Intention: For every child who feels unwanted, that they may find someone to love them in Christ.)_

**Day 2 — Day 2: The dream at nine years old:** At nine, John dreamed of unruly children he was told to win not with blows but with gentleness, and of a Lady who would be his teacher. He pondered that dream all his life and let it shape his mission. _(Intention: For the grace to recognize and follow God's call in the events of my life.)_

**Day 3 — Day 3: Reason:** Don Bosco appealed first to the mind and conscience of the young, explaining rules patiently rather than imposing them by fear. He trusted that a boy who understands the good will more readily choose it. _(Intention: For teachers and parents, that they may guide the young with patience and reason.)_

**Day 4 — Day 4: Religion:** At the heart of his method stood frequent Confession, Communion, and devotion to Our Lady, for he knew no upbringing endures without God. He led his boys to the sacraments not as a duty but as a feast. _(Intention: For a hunger for the sacraments in the hearts of the young.)_

**Day 5 — Day 5: Loving-kindness:** Don Bosco insisted that it is not enough to love the young; they must know they are loved. He played their games, learned their names, and made the playground a place where grace could take root. _(Intention: That those I serve may truly know they are loved.)_

**Day 6 — Day 6: Mary Help of Christians:** Don Bosco attributed every grace of his work to Our Lady and built a great basilica in her honor as Help of Christians. He told his sons to spread her devotion and watch the wonders she would work. _(Intention: That I may entrust all my needs to Mary, Help of Christians.)_

**Day 7 — Day 7: Tireless work for souls:** He labored late into the night writing, teaching, and hearing confessions, with the motto 'Give me souls, take away the rest.' He spent his health without counting the cost, asking only that his boys reach heaven. _(Intention: For zeal in my own duties, offered for the good of souls.)_

**Day 8 — Day 8: Holiness in cheerfulness:** Don Bosco taught his boys, as he taught young St. Dominic Savio, that sanctity consists in being always joyful and doing one's duty well. He banished gloom from his houses and made holiness attractive. _(Intention: For the grace to serve God with a joyful heart.)_

**Day 9 — Day 9: A father even in death:** As he lay dying in 1888, Don Bosco's last counsel to his sons was to love and care for the young and to trust in Mary. From heaven he still watches over the work he began on earth. _(Intention: That, faithful to the end, I may one day rejoice with St. John Bosco in heaven.)_

**Closing prayer:** St. John Bosco, who taught that holiness is found in cheerfulness and daily duty, obtain for me the grace I ask in this novena, if it be for God's glory and my salvation. Make me, like your boys, a friend of God, devoted to Our Lady and faithful in small things. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas, "St. John Bosco Novena." A single prayer repeated identically each of the nine days (no distinct daily meditations). Each day opens with the Sign of the Cross and prays:

"Oh holy patron of the youth, I come to you with sincere confidence in your closeness to Jesus. Saint John Bosco, I need your help, I need your prayers, I need your intercession to God for His grace to help me with…" _(state intentions)_

followed by the three-fold appeal "With your love of Mary, our Mother, pray for me! With your love for Jesus in the Blessed Sacrament, pray for me! With your love for all those who suffer, pray for me!" and the concluding petition "Don Bosco, please pray fervently for the grace that I now ask from God our Father… that I may have a sincere acceptance of the Will of God and a perfect trust in Him." Each day ends with the Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be, and the trinitarian closing.

## Fidelity note
Ember is an independent composition and shares almost no wording with PrayMoreNovenas, whose text is a single repeated petition rather than daily meditations. Their common ground is structural (the Our Father / Hail Mary / Glory Be frame) and thematic (Don Bosco's closeness to youth, devotion to Mary Help of Christians, Eucharistic emphasis, acceptance of God's will). Ember is far richer and accurately biographical, organizing the nine days around the saint's real life and his Preventive System — reason, religion, loving-kindness (Days 3-5), the dream at nine, "Give me souls, take away the rest," St. Dominic Savio, and his death in 1888. All historical claims are sound; Ember is the fuller and more catechetical of the two.
