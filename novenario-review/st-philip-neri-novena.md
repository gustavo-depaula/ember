# Novena to St. Philip Neri — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O glad St. Philip Neri, who set Rome on fire with the joy of the Gospel, look kindly on me as I begin this novena. Win for me a cheerful and humble heart, that I may serve God with gladness all my days. Amen.

**Day 1 — Holy joy:** St. Philip was sure that a sad heart cannot serve God well, and his laughter drew the most hardened sinners to confession. He teaches us that true cheerfulness is a fruit of the Spirit, not a denial of the Cross. _(Intention: For the grace to serve God with a glad and willing heart.)_ (every day)

**Day 2 — Humility and self-mockery:** St. Philip dreaded honors and would play the fool, walking out with half his beard shaved, lest men praise him. He shows us that humility can wear a smile and that holiness need not take itself too seriously. _(Intention: For freedom from vanity and the love of human praise.)_ (every day)

**Day 3 — The streets of Rome:** Before he was a priest, St. Philip walked the streets and shops of Rome, striking up conversations that turned hearts to Christ. He teaches us that the apostolate begins wherever people are, in the ordinary places of daily life. _(Intention: For courage to speak of God in everyday encounters.)_ (every day)

**Day 4 — The enflamed heart:** Praying in the catacombs on the eve of Pentecost, St. Philip received a fire of divine love so great that it physically enlarged his heart. He invites us to ask boldly for a heart made wider by the Holy Spirit. _(Intention: For a heart set ablaze with love for God.)_ (every day)

**Day 5 — Cor ad cor loquitur:** St. Philip's motto, 'heart speaks to heart,' shaped his whole way of guiding souls with tenderness rather than force. He teaches that grace passes most freely through affection and patient friendship. _(Intention: For the gift of speaking to others heart to heart.)_ (every day)

**Day 6 — The Oratory:** St. Philip gathered friends for prayer, Scripture, song, and joyful conversation, and from this grew the Congregation of the Oratory. He shows us how holiness can flourish in community, lightly worn and shared in friendship. _(Intention: For holy friendships that draw us closer to Christ.)_ (every day)

**Day 7 — Devotion to the Holy Spirit:** St. Philip prayed often, 'Come, Holy Spirit,' and lived in constant dependence on God's grace for every word and work. He teaches us that fruitful action flows only from a soul docile to the Spirit. _(Intention: For openness to the guidance of the Holy Spirit.)_ (every day)

**Day 8 — Distrust of self:** St. Philip began each day praying, 'Lord, keep your hand on Philip today, or he will betray you.' He reminds us that even the holiest soul stands only by the constant mercy of God. _(Intention: For the grace to distrust myself and rely wholly on God.)_ (every day)

**Day 9 — Apostle of Rome:** By a lifetime of joyful charity St. Philip renewed the faith of a whole city and earned the title Apostle of Rome. He shows us that one cheerful saint, faithful in small things, can rekindle the love of many. _(Intention: For the particular grace I now entrust to St. Philip Neri.)_ (every day)

**Closing prayer:** O St. Philip, whose heart was enlarged by the Holy Spirit, present my petitions to the Lord you loved so dearly. Teach me to delight in God and to lead others to Him by kindness rather than by harshness, until we meet in the joy of heaven. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
FishEaters lists a "Novena to St. Philip Neri" (https://www.fisheaters.com/novenas.html#neri), but the text could not be retrieved: this section falls past the point where the page was truncated during fetching (readable content ends around the Novena to St. Sebastian). The traditional FishEaters/Newman novena to St. Philip uses a distinct prayer for each of the nine days, each beginning "Philip, my glorious patron..." and treating a different virtue (humility, divine love, prayer, purity, tenderness of heart, cheerfulness, patience, zeal for souls, miraculous gifts) — confirmed via the parallel PrayMoreNovenas reproduction below.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas reproduces the classic nine-day St. Philip Neri novena (https://www.praymorenovenas.com/st-philip-neri-novena), Trinitarian blessing opening and closing, with a unique daily prayer beginning "Philip, my glorious patron/advocate..." Daily themes: Day 1 humility; Day 2 devotion / divine love; Day 3 the exercise of prayer; Day 4 purity; Day 5 tenderness of heart; Day 6 cheerfulness; Day 7 patience; Day 8 care for the salvation of souls; Day 9 miraculous gifts.

## Fidelity note
Ember's prayers are an original composition and do not reuse the "Philip, my glorious patron..." petitions of the classic Newman/PrayMoreNovenas text. The two overlap clearly on the saint's hallmark themes — joy/cheerfulness, humility, prayer, purity (Ember folds this into the wider "enflamed heart"/Spirit material), tenderness of heart ("cor ad cor loquitur"), and zeal for souls. Ember adds well-attested biographical days the source treats only lightly (the streets of Rome, the catacombs/enlarged heart, the Oratory, "Apostle of Rome"). Faithful to St. Philip's spirit and the traditional themes, but freshly worded.
