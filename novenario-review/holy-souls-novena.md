# Holy Souls — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O God, the Creator and Redeemer of all the faithful, I come before You for the souls of the faithful departed who suffer in purgatory. By the Precious Blood of Your Son, wash away whatever still stains them, and lead them quickly into the light of Your presence. Amen.

**Day 1 — Out of the depths:** With the psalmist the Holy Souls cry from the depths, 'O Lord, hear my voice.' They can no longer help themselves, and they wait upon our prayers as a parched land waits for rain.  _(Intention: For all the souls who have no one on earth to remember them.)_  (every day)

**Day 2 — The mercy of God:** 'If You, O Lord, should mark iniquities, who could stand?' Yet with the Lord there is mercy and plentiful redemption. The fire of purgatory is the mercy of God completing what His grace began.  _(Intention: For the souls who trusted in God's mercy and now await its fullness.)_  (every day)

**Day 3 — A purifying love:** Purgatory is not punishment without hope but a love that burns away every imperfection. The souls there suffer gladly, for each pang draws them nearer to the God they already love.  _(Intention: For souls being purified of the last attachments that keep them from God.)_  (every day)

**Day 4 — The Holy Sacrifice of the Mass:** No gift relieves the dead like the Holy Mass, where the same Victim of Calvary is offered anew. To have a Mass said for a departed soul is to place the Blood of Christ between them and their debt.  _(Intention: That many Masses may be offered for the relief of the Holy Souls.)_  (every day)

**Day 5 — Works of mercy for the dead:** To pray for the living and the dead is a work of mercy the Church has never set aside. Our almsgiving, fasting, and indulgences are spiritual coin we may freely spend on souls who can no longer earn it themselves.  _(Intention: For the grace to offer my penances and good works for the faithful departed.)_  (every day)

**Day 6 — My own departed:** We owe a debt of love to our parents, friends, and benefactors who have gone before us. They taught us, fed us, and prayed for us; now they look to us in their need.  _(Intention: For the souls of my own family, friends, and benefactors.)_  (every day)

**Day 7 — The communion of saints:** The Church Militant on earth, the Church Suffering in purgatory, and the Church Triumphant in heaven are one body in Christ. Our prayers cross that threshold, and the souls we help will one day pray for us before the throne of God.  _(Intention: That the bond of charity between the living and the dead may grow strong.)_  (every day)

**Day 8 — The hope of the resurrection:** We do not grieve as those who have no hope, for Christ has conquered death and promised to raise up the bodies of the faithful. Every soul we pray for is destined for the glory of the resurrection.  _(Intention: For a deepened hope in the resurrection of the body and life everlasting.)_  (every day)

**Day 9 — Into eternal light:** At last the purified soul is summoned into the unveiled light of God, where mourning is ended and every tear is wiped away. May our prayers hasten that hour for the souls we have carried these nine days.  _(Intention: That the Holy Souls may soon behold the face of God in glory.)_  (every day)

**Closing prayer:** Eternal rest grant unto them, O Lord, and let perpetual light shine upon them. May they rest in peace, and may the gates of heaven open to receive them into the company of the saints. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
Nine days on a repeating framework with thematically varying daily petitions. Each day: "In the name of the Father..." → a shared opening: "Dear Lord, we trust in Your infinite goodness. We praise Your justice and implore Your mercy..." → a day-specific group/theme and petition → a personal-intention insertion point ("I especially ask in this novena...") → closing: "Lord, hear our prayers! In the name of the Father..." Daily themes target different groups of the dead: deceased loved ones (Days 1–2), priests (Day 3), religious (Day 4), those who died suddenly (Day 5), the repentant (Day 6), the holy (Day 7), the regretful (Day 8), and the forgotten (Day 9).

## Fidelity note
The FishEaters text was not retrievable, so the comparison rests on PrayMoreNovenas. Both share the Holy Souls devotion and several anchors — trust in God's mercy/justice, the forgotten souls (Ember Day 1 / PMN Day 9), and intercession for one's own departed. But the wording is independent and the organizing principle differs: PMN sorts the nine days by category of the dead (priests, religious, the sudden, the repentant, etc.), whereas Ember builds a doctrinal/devotional arc — De Profundis, the nature of purgatory as purifying love, the Mass, works of mercy, the communion of saints, the resurrection, entrance into light. Ember leans on Psalm 130 and the traditional "Eternal rest" prayer for its frame; it is faithful to Catholic teaching on purgatory and clearly an original composition rather than a reproduction of either source.
