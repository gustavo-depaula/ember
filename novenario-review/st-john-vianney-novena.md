# Novena to St. John Vianney — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** Glorious St. John Vianney, humble priest of Ars, who spent yourself wholly for the salvation of souls, obtain for me a like hunger for God. By your prayers, draw me away from sin and closer to the heart of Christ. Amen.

**Day 1 — Perseverance against all odds:** John Vianney struggled bitterly with Latin and study, yet he never gave up his hope of the priesthood. He teaches us that holiness depends not on talent but on a will that refuses to surrender.  _(Intention: For perseverance in pursuing what God asks despite my own weakness.)_

**Day 2 — Zeal for souls:** The Curé wept and pleaded with God for the salvation of his cold, indifferent parish. He shows us that true love for our neighbor begins with longing for their eternal salvation.  _(Intention: For zeal to desire and work for the salvation of those around me.)_

**Day 3 — The ministry of the confessional:** For up to sixteen hours a day the Curé sat in his confessional, reading hearts and pouring out God's pardon. He teaches us the immense power of the Sacrament of Penance to remake a soul.  _(Intention: For a sincere love of confession and the grace of true repentance.)_

**Day 4 — Penance and austerity:** The Curé lived on little more than boiled potatoes and fasted that sinners might be converted. He shows us that self-denial offered in love becomes a quiet power for the good of others.  _(Intention: For the grace to embrace small sacrifices for the love of God and souls.)_

**Day 5 — Love of the Real Presence:** The Curé spent long hours before the tabernacle and once described prayer as simply looking at God while God looks at us. He teaches us that the heart of every priest and every Christian is found before the Eucharist.  _(Intention: For a deep love of Jesus truly present in the Blessed Sacrament.)_

**Day 6 — Humility:** Honored by all of France, the Curé thought himself unworthy and longed only to hide in prayer. He teaches us that the greater God raises a soul, the more deeply that soul bows before Him.  _(Intention: For true humility that gives all glory to God and none to myself.)_

**Day 7 — Trust in St. Philomena:** The Curé attributed the cures and conversions at Ars to his beloved patroness, St. Philomena, the little saint. He teaches us to honor the saints and to trust the power of their intercession.  _(Intention: For confidence in the intercession of the saints in my needs.)_

**Day 8 — Spiritual combat:** The Curé endured years of torment from the devil, whom he wryly called the grappin, yet he was never shaken from his work. He shows us that the closer a soul draws to God, the more bravely it must fight.  _(Intention: For courage and steadfastness in the daily battle against temptation.)_

**Day 9 — A heart spent for God:** Worn out by labor and love, the Curé died with the simple confidence that it is good to die when one has lived for God. He leaves us his great desire: to love God and to bring as many souls as possible to Him.  _(Intention: For all priests, and for the grace to spend my life for the love of God.)_

**Closing prayer:** Holy Curé of Ars, friend of sinners and lover of the Eucharist, intercede for all priests and for the conversion of poor sinners. Obtain for me the grace I now implore, and a heart that loves God as you loved Him. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas hosts a St. John Vianney novena. Each day is wrapped in the Sign of the Cross and uses a fixed template: address to the saint touching that day's virtue, a petition for specific graces, then "Believing in the power of your kind intercession, I humbly ask you to pray for me…" (with space for a personal intention), and the refrain "St. John Vianney, Priest of Ars, pray for our priests, and pray for us." Its daily themes run: (1) courage/faith despite obstacles, (2) charitable service, (3) determination/perseverance, (4) moral renewal and compassion for sinners, (5) trust in God's will, (6) power of prayer and contemplation, (7) worthiness to receive the sacraments, (8) spiritual over material wealth, (9) zeal to share God's love.

## Fidelity note
Ember is not a copy of the PrayMoreNovenas text but an independent composition drawn from the same well-known biography of the Curé. Both open and close by addressing the saint and both frame nine daily virtue-meditations, but Ember's wording is wholly its own and its day-by-day themes are richer and more specific (confessional, austerity, Eucharist, Philomena, spiritual combat) where PMN's are broader. The shared structure (Sign of the Cross, daily virtue + intention, Our Father / Hail Mary / Glory Be) reflects the common novena form rather than borrowing. This is a faithful original devotion, accurate to the saint's life, not a paraphrase of the source.
