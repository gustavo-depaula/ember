# Sacred Heart of Jesus — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** _(none — the flow opens with the Sign of the Cross and goes directly into the day's meditation.)_

**Day 1 — The Goodness of the Sacred Heart:** O most Sacred Heart of Jesus, You revealed to St. Margaret Mary Your burning desire to be loved by all. Your Heart is the source of all goodness, overflowing with mercy for sinners and consolation for the afflicted. I come before You, conscious of my unworthiness, yet trusting in Your infinite goodness. _(Intention: For a deeper awareness of God's goodness in my life.)_

**Day 2 — The Love of the Sacred Heart:** O Sacred Heart of Jesus, Your love for us is beyond all understanding. You loved us even unto death on the Cross, and Your Heart was pierced so that we might find refuge within it. Teach me to love as You love — without counting the cost, without seeking reward, with a love that is patient, generous, and self-giving. _(Intention: For the grace to love God and neighbor more ardently.)_

**Day 3 — The Mercy of the Sacred Heart:** O merciful Heart of Jesus, You never turn away the repentant sinner. You welcomed the woman at the well, the good thief, and Peter after his denial. Your mercy is new every morning, and Your compassion never fails. I bring before You all my sins and failings, trusting that Your mercy is greater than my weakness. _(Intention: For the conversion of sinners and the souls in purgatory.)_

**Day 4 — The Humility of the Sacred Heart:** O humble Heart of Jesus, You who said 'Learn from Me, for I am meek and humble of heart,' teach me true humility. Though You are Lord of all, You chose to be born in a stable, to wash the feet of Your disciples, and to die the death of a criminal. Free me from pride, vanity, and the desire for human praise. _(Intention: For the grace of humility in all things.)_

**Day 5 — The Patience of the Sacred Heart:** O patient Heart of Jesus, how long You bore with the unbelief of the Pharisees, the slowness of Your disciples, and the ingratitude of those You healed. You endured the agony in the garden, the scourging, the crowning with thorns, and the way of the Cross — all without complaint. Grant me patience in my trials and sufferings. _(Intention: For patience and perseverance in trials.)_

**Day 6 — The Generosity of the Sacred Heart:** O generous Heart of Jesus, You gave everything — Your time, Your strength, Your very life — for the salvation of souls. You multiplied loaves for the hungry, gave sight to the blind, and poured out Your Precious Blood for the forgiveness of sins. Inspire me to give generously of myself, my time, and my resources for the good of others. _(Intention: For a generous heart, open to the needs of others.)_

**Day 7 — The Obedience of the Sacred Heart:** O obedient Heart of Jesus, You were obedient to the Father's will in all things, even unto death on a cross. In Gethsemane You prayed, 'Not My will, but Yours be done.' You submitted to the laws of Your own creation, obeying Mary and Joseph in the hidden life at Nazareth. Help me to embrace God's will with joy and trust. _(Intention: For docility to God's will in my life.)_

**Day 8 — The Sacred Heart, Source of Peace:** O peaceful Heart of Jesus, You said to Your disciples, 'Peace I leave with you; My peace I give to you.' In the midst of storms You slept peacefully, and after Your resurrection You greeted Your frightened Apostles with 'Peace be with you.' Grant me that deep interior peace which the world cannot give — peace born of trust in Your providence. _(Intention: For peace in families, communities, and the world.)_

**Day 9 — The Sacred Heart, Our Refuge:** O Sacred Heart of Jesus, You are our refuge and our strength, an ever-present help in trouble. Your Heart, pierced by the soldier's lance, opened wide to receive all who come to You. In every sorrow, every temptation, every moment of darkness, I take refuge in Your Sacred Heart. As this novena ends, I entrust to You all my needs and the needs of those I love. _(Intention: For all the intentions held in my heart during this novena.)_

**Closing prayer:** _(after one Our Father, Hail Mary, and Glory Be)_ O Sacred Heart of Jesus, I place all my trust in You. Most Sacred Heart of Jesus, have mercy on us. Sacred Heart of Jesus, I believe in Your love for me. Amen.

## Source — FishEaters
FishEaters gives the traditional Novena to the Sacred Heart (recited once daily for nine days, classically from Corpus Christi to the Feast of the Sacred Heart). Its single repeated prayer opens "O most holy Heart of Jesus, fountain of every blessing, I adore Thee, I love Thee and with a lively sorrow for my sins, I offer Thee this poor heart of mine." It asks for humility, patience, purity, and complete conformity to God's will, then for protection in danger, comfort in affliction, bodily health, help in temporal needs, blessing on all undertakings, and the grace of a holy death — entrusting all cares to the Sacred Heart and repeating the aspiration "Heart of Jesus, help me."

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas opens each day with the Sign of the Cross and prays the same framework all nine days, built on three Gospel-promise petitions: 1) "O my Jesus, you have said: 'Truly I say to you, ask and you will receive…' Behold I knock, I seek and ask for the grace of… [intention]"; 2) "…if you ask anything of the Father in my name, he will give it to you"; 3) "…heaven and earth will pass away but my words will not pass away." After each, the refrain "Sacred Heart of Jesus, I place all my trust in you." The closing prayer is: "O Sacred Heart of Jesus, for whom it is impossible not to have compassion on the afflicted, have pity on us miserable sinners and grant us the grace which we ask of you, through the Sorrowful and Immaculate Heart of Mary, your tender Mother and ours. Amen."

## Fidelity note
Ember's Sacred Heart novena is an original composition that does not reproduce the fixed text of either source — neither the FishEaters "fountain of every blessing… this poor heart of mine" prayer nor PMN's three "Truly I say to you…" Gospel-promise petitions. Instead it builds nine themed meditations on attributes of the Heart of Jesus (goodness, love, mercy, humility, patience, generosity, obedience, peace, refuge), which are authentic to the devotion and notably echo the virtues the FishEaters prayer petitions (humility, patience, conformity to God's will). It is well-grounded in the tradition: it names St. Margaret Mary and the revelation of Christ's "burning desire to be loved," and centers on the pierced Heart as refuge. Its closing aspiration — "O Sacred Heart of Jesus, I place all my trust in You" — directly matches PMN's repeated refrain. Faithful in spirit, imagery, and the trademark act of trust; paraphrased rather than verbatim.
