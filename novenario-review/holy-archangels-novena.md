# Holy Archangels — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** Holy Archangels Michael, Gabriel, and Raphael, who stand before the throne of God and carry out His commands, come to my aid in this novena. Defend me, enlighten me, and lead me safely on the way to heaven. Amen.

**Day 1 — St. Michael, defend us in battle:** St. Michael cast the rebel angels from heaven, crying out 'Who is like God?' against the pride of Lucifer. He is the captain of the heavenly host, raised up to defend God's people against every spiritual enemy.  _(Intention: For St. Michael's protection against the snares of the devil.)_  (every day)

**Day 2 — St. Michael, guardian of the Church:** Tradition honors St. Michael as the special guardian of the Church and the soul's defender at the hour of death. Leo XIII gave us the prayer that he 'thrust into hell Satan and all the evil spirits who prowl about the world.'  _(Intention: For the defense of the Church and her shepherds against evil.)_  (every day)

**Day 3 — St. Michael, champion of God's glory:** St. Michael's name is itself a confession of faith: there is none like God. He teaches us to refuse every idol of self and pride, and to give to God alone the worship and obedience He deserves.  _(Intention: For the grace to put God first and reject every false idol.)_  (every day)

**Day 4 — St. Gabriel, the messenger:** St. Gabriel was sent to announce the Incarnation, greeting Mary with 'Hail, full of grace' and awaiting her consent. He is the bearer of God's most joyful word, the herald of salvation drawing near.  _(Intention: For the grace to listen for God's word and answer with a generous 'yes.')_  (every day)

**Day 5 — St. Gabriel, herald of the Incarnation:** Gabriel also foretold to Zechariah the birth of John the Baptist, opening the great drama of redemption. He shows that God prepares His works in silence and announces them in His own appointed time.  _(Intention: For confidence that God fulfills His promises in His own time.)_  (every day)

**Day 6 — St. Gabriel, strength of God:** Gabriel's name means 'God is my strength,' and he steadied the prophet Daniel and gave courage to the fearful. He reminds us that the messages of heaven come with the grace to carry them out.  _(Intention: For strength and courage to do God's will without fear.)_  (every day)

**Day 7 — St. Raphael, the healer:** St. Raphael, whose name means 'God heals,' restored the sight of old Tobit and freed Sarah from affliction. He is the friend of the sick and the patron of all who seek healing of body and soul.  _(Intention: For healing of body and soul through St. Raphael's intercession.)_  (every day)

**Day 8 — St. Raphael, guide of travelers:** Disguised as a companion, Raphael walked the long road with young Tobias, guarding him on the journey and leading him to a holy marriage. He is the trusted guide of all who travel and all who seek a good and faithful spouse.  _(Intention: For safe journeys and God's guidance in life's important choices.)_  (every day)

**Day 9 — The angelic ministry:** Together the Archangels reveal a God who stoops to guard, instruct, and heal His children through His unseen servants. They invite us to join their ceaseless worship and to trust that heaven is near to us in every need.  _(Intention: For the grace to live aware of God's protecting hand through His angels.)_  (every day)

**Closing prayer:** Glorious Archangels, faithful servants of the Most High, present my prayers before God and obtain for me the grace I seek. May your protection accompany me until I join you in praising Him forever. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
FishEaters carries a Novena to St. Michael the Archangel (anchor "michael"), prayed Sep 20–28 before the Sep 29 feast. It is a single fixed prayer repeated each of the nine days — addressing St. Michael as "loyal champion of God and His Catholic people," asking his intercession against the evil spirit who tempts, protection "in all dangers of body and soul," help in daily needs, and a holy life and happy death — with a personal intention inserted. FishEaters' devotion is to St. Michael alone, not to all three named Archangels.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas offers a St. Michael the Archangel novena (also Michael-only). It opens with the Sign of the Cross and a fixed invocation ("we honor you as a powerful protector of the Church and guardian of our souls… inspire us with your humility, courage and strength"), then a distinct daily meditation/intention (Day 1 zeal for Christ's teaching, Day 3 protection from the devil's snares, Day 5 humility, Day 9 sharing eternity with God), and closes every day with the classic "St. Michael the Archangel, defend us in battle" prayer.

## Fidelity note
Ember's novena is its own composition and deliberately broader than both sources: it honors all three Scripturally-named Archangels (Michael days 1–3, Gabriel 4–6, Raphael 7–9) rather than Michael alone, so it does not reproduce either source's fixed daily prayer or the "defend us in battle" closing. It shares the same nine-day window, the personal-intention structure, and the Michael themes of protection against the devil and a happy death. The wording is original, not a paraphrase of either source.
