# St. Anthony of Sant'Anna Galvão — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O God, who raised up St. Anthony Galvão as a herald of Your peace among the people of Brazil, grant us through his intercession a humble and trusting heart. Through Our Lady, in whom he placed all his confidence, lead us closer to Your Son. Amen.

**Day 1 — Leaving all to follow Christ:** Born into a prominent family, Anthony renounced wealth and standing to clothe himself in the poor habit of St. Francis. He teaches us that the heart finds its true riches only when it gives itself to God.  _(Intention: For the grace to seek God above worldly honor and comfort.)_  (every day)

**Day 2 — A heart consecrated to Mary:** Anthony bound himself to Our Lady as her devoted son and signed himself her slave of love. His tender Marian devotion reminds us that the surest path to Jesus passes through the heart of His Mother.  _(Intention: For a deeper love and confidence in the Blessed Virgin Mary.)_  (every day)

**Day 3 — The healing pills of faith:** When a man came to him gravely ill, Anthony gave him a tiny paper pill inscribed with a prayer to Our Lady, and the sick were restored. His famous 'pílulas' teach us that God works wonders through humble means and confident faith.  _(Intention: For the healing of all who are sick in body or soul.)_  (every day)

**Day 4 — The patient confessor:** For long hours Anthony sat in the confessional, drawing souls back to God with gentleness and patience. He shows us that there is no greater work of mercy than to lead a sinner to the peace of forgiveness.  _(Intention: For sincere repentance and a grateful love of the sacrament of Confession.)_  (every day)

**Day 5 — Founder of the Recolhimento da Luz:** Anthony founded the Recolhimento de Nossa Senhora da Luz so that women might live consecrated to God in prayer and labor. He poured himself into this work, often laboring with his own hands, trusting Providence to supply what was needed.  _(Intention: For all who consecrate their lives to God in religious life.)_  (every day)

**Day 6 — Trust in divine Providence:** Though his works often lacked money and material, Anthony never wavered in trusting that God would provide. His serene confidence teaches us to lay our worries before the Father who feeds the birds of the air.  _(Intention: For the grace to trust God's providence amid our worries and needs.)_  (every day)

**Day 7 — A man of peace:** Known as a herald of Christ's peace, Anthony reconciled enemies and calmed troubled hearts wherever he went. He reminds us that peace is born of humility, and that the meek truly inherit the earth.  _(Intention: For peace in our homes, our communities, and our nation.)_  (every day)

**Day 8 — Humble service of his brothers:** Anthony gladly served as porter, preacher, and helper of the poor, never seeking honor for himself. His humility shows us that true greatness is found in serving others for the love of God.  _(Intention: For a humble and generous spirit in serving those around us.)_  (every day)

**Day 9 — Faithful to the end:** Anthony labored for souls until his old age and died beloved by the people of São Paulo, who proclaimed him a saint long before the Church did. His holy life assures us that quiet, faithful service is never forgotten by God.  _(Intention: For the grace of perseverance in serving God faithfully all our days.)_  (every day)

**Closing prayer:** St. Anthony Galvão, friend of the poor and instrument of healing, you spent yourself in serving the souls God placed before you. Obtain for us healing of body and spirit, peace in our trials, and an unshakable trust in the providence of God. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Fidelity note
As Brazil's first native-born saint, St. Anthony Galvão has no FishEaters or PrayMoreNovenas novena to compare against, so this is an original composition. It is faithful to his documented life: the renunciation of a prominent family for the Franciscans, his ardent Marian devotion (signing himself Mary's "slave"), the celebrated "pílulas" (paper pills with a Marian invocation linked to healings), his long hours as confessor and porter, his founding of the Recolhimento de Nossa Senhora da Luz in São Paulo, his trust in Providence, and his renown as a peacemaker beloved by the people. The themes are well chosen and historically accurate; the closing prayer's appeal for healing fittingly echoes the devotion's strongest popular association.
