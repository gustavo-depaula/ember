# St. Louis de Montfort — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** St. Louis Marie de Montfort, faithful slave of Jesus in Mary, you spent your life leading souls to Christ through His holy Mother. Take me by the hand as I begin this novena and teach me to belong entirely to Jesus through Mary. Obtain for me the grace I now ask, that I may love God with an undivided heart. Amen.

**Day 1 — To Jesus through Mary:** St. Louis taught that since Jesus came to us through Mary, it is through Mary that we most surely come to Him. He invites us to take the way the Eternal Word himself chose, trusting the path God has already blessed.  _(Intention: For the grace to come to Jesus through the hands of His holy Mother.)_

**Day 2 — Total Consecration:** He counseled giving Mary our body and soul, our goods both interior and exterior, and even the value of all our good actions. This complete gift, he promised, loses nothing but is multiplied a hundredfold in the hands of the Mother of God.  _(Intention: For the courage to give myself entirely to God, holding nothing back.)_

**Day 3 — Holy slavery of love:** St. Louis called the consecrated soul a willing slave of love, bound to Jesus in Mary not by force but by adoring freedom. This loving bondage, far from diminishing us, makes us truly free children of God.  _(Intention: For the grace to serve Christ freely, out of love rather than fear.)_

**Day 4 — Through her, with her, in her:** He taught us to do all our actions through Mary, with Mary, in Mary, and for Mary, so as to do them more perfectly through, with, in, and for Jesus Christ. Mary is never the destination but the swift and gentle way to her Son.  _(Intention: For the grace to unite all my works to those of Mary and her Son.)_

**Day 5 — The Holy Rosary:** Montfort preached the Rosary as a treasure that contains the whole gospel, calling it a chain that binds heaven to earth. He saw in the daily decades a school of contemplation that gradually conforms us to Christ.  _(Intention: For the grace to pray the Rosary faithfully and to love its mysteries.)_

**Day 6 — Wisdom of the Cross:** In his "Love of Eternal Wisdom" St. Louis taught that Christ crucified is the Wisdom of God, and that to possess Him we must embrace the Cross. He himself bore contradiction, poverty, and rejection without bitterness, kissing the Cross he carried.  _(Intention: For the grace to embrace the cross in my life as the way to true wisdom.)_

**Day 7 — The zealous missionary:** Tireless and often opposed, St. Louis crisscrossed western France preaching parish missions, hearing confessions, and renewing whole towns. His burning zeal for souls reminds us that love of God overflows into love for our neighbor's salvation.  _(Intention: For zeal to bring others closer to Christ by word and example.)_

**Day 8 — Mary, mold of the saints:** St. Louis compared Mary to the mold of God, in which souls are gently and surely formed into living images of Jesus. To be cast in her is to be shaped quickly into holiness with far less labor and far greater fruit.  _(Intention: For the grace to be formed by Mary into the likeness of her Son.)_

**Day 9 — Apostles of the latter times:** Montfort prophesied that God would raise up great saints, true children of Mary, to renew the Church in the last ages. He calls each of us to be one of these humble apostles, set on fire by Marian devotion for the glory of Christ.  _(Intention: For the grace to live my consecration faithfully for the renewal of the Church.)_

**Closing prayer:** St. Louis Marie, who proclaimed that Mary is the surest, shortest, and most perfect way to Jesus, lead me along that royal road. Help me to consecrate myself wholly to Christ through her Immaculate Heart and to take up my Rosary each day. By your prayers, may I be among the apostles of the latter times whom you foretold. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
**Opening (all days):** "In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen."

**Core prayer (repeated each day):** "St. Louis de Montfort, you consecrated yourself to Jesus through Mary in your earthly life and are even more fully devoted to them now in Heaven. I ask that you present my petitions before our most blessed Mother, who will in turn place them at the feet of her Son."

_(Space for personal petitions.)_

**Daily intercessions:**
- Day 1: Request for growth in humility and self-denial through Mary's example
- Day 2: Request for invigorated and apparent faith
- Day 3: Request for becoming "a more immediately obedient child of God"
- Day 4: Request for endurance to "pray without ceasing"
- Day 5: Request to "become ever purer" through God's refining work
- Day 6: Request for "a more intense love for God and neighbor"
- Day 7: Request for growth in patience, "especially in trying situations"
- Day 8: Request to practice kindness "with all, especially those whom I find most difficult to love"
- Day 9: Request for attentiveness to "heavenly wisdom"

**Closing (all days):** "Amen. In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen."

## Fidelity note
Ember's text is an original composition, not a transcription of any single published novena. It does not follow PrayMoreNovenas, which uses one fixed intercessory prayer with a generic per-day intention; Ember instead builds nine distinct catechetical meditations drawn faithfully from Montfort's own doctrine (Total Consecration, holy slavery of love, the "through, with, in, for Mary" formula, the Rosary, the Wisdom of the Cross, apostles of the latter times). The structural frame — Sign of the Cross, opening prayer, meditation, intention, Our Father / Hail Mary / Glory Be, closing prayer — is conventional and sound. As a Montfortian novena it is theologically accurate and substantive, but it should not be presented as matching the PrayMoreNovenas version, which it does not reproduce.
