# St. Jude — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** St. Jude, faithful servant and friend of Jesus, I place this novena before God through your intercession. Obtain for me a heart full of hope, perseverance in prayer, and trust in the mercy of Christ. Amen.

**Day 1 — Called by Christ and chosen as Apostle:** St. Jude was called personally by Jesus and sent to proclaim the Kingdom. His vocation reminds us that every Christian life begins with a divine call and a mission of love.  _(Intention: For the grace to hear Christ's call and respond with generosity.)_  (every day)

**Day 2 — Fidelity in hidden service:** Though less mentioned in the Gospels, St. Jude remained faithful to the Lord. He teaches us that holiness is often forged in hidden, humble, and persevering service.  _(Intention: For fidelity in daily duties, especially when unseen or unrecognized.)_  (every day)

**Day 3 — Confidence in difficult causes:** The Church invokes St. Jude in difficult needs because he points souls toward unwavering trust in God's power. No situation is beyond divine mercy and providence.  _(Intention: For urgent intentions that seem difficult or impossible in human eyes.)_  (every day)

**Day 4 — Perseverance in prayer:** St. Jude's intercession encourages us to pray without losing heart. Persevering prayer stretches our trust, purifies our desires, and keeps us close to Christ.  _(Intention: For constancy in prayer and deeper trust in God's timing.)_  (every day)

**Day 5 — Courage to witness the Gospel:** As an Apostle, St. Jude preached Christ with courage amid trials. He strengthens us to witness the Gospel with clarity, charity, and steadfast faith.  _(Intention: For boldness to live and share the faith in our homes, work, and communities.)_  (every day)

**Day 6 — Mercy for sinners and the afflicted:** St. Jude calls us to compassion for those burdened by sin, suffering, and discouragement. In Christ, mercy heals wounds and restores hearts to hope.  _(Intention: For those in spiritual, emotional, or material distress.)_  (every day)

**Day 7 — Unity and peace in families:** Through the intercession of St. Jude, we ask for reconciliation where there is division. Christ desires homes marked by forgiveness, patience, and mutual charity.  _(Intention: For peace, healing, and unity in our families and relationships.)_  (every day)

**Day 8 — Hope for the despairing and abandoned:** St. Jude is often sought by those who feel at the end of their strength. He reminds us that God never abandons His children and always opens a path of grace.  _(Intention: For all who are tempted to despair, that they may receive renewed hope.)_  (every day)

**Day 9 — Thanksgiving and mission:** As this novena concludes, we thank God for every grace already given. With St. Jude, we ask for perseverance so that our lives become a witness of gratitude, faith, and charity.  _(Intention: For final perseverance and a life that gives glory to God.)_  (every day)

**Closing prayer:** St. Jude, Apostle of Christ and helper in difficult needs, pray for us and for all who seek God's mercy. May we remain faithful to Jesus, serve our neighbor with charity, and give thanks for every grace received. Amen.

(Between the daily intention and the closing prayer, each day also prays one Our Father, one Hail Mary, and one Glory Be.)

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
Opening: Trinitarian formula ("In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen."), then a prayer addressing St. Jude as apostle, martyr, and friend of Jesus, requesting his intercession for stated intentions. Daily themes: (1) general petition for grace and mercy; (2) zeal to preach the Gospel; (3) patience during struggles; (4) help with impossible needs; (5) sacrifice like St. Jude; (6) to know God's will; (7) trust in God's providence; (8) to maintain hope; (9) to preserve faith. Recurring core line: "You are the patron of the impossible. Pray for me and my intentions! O St. Jude, pray that God's grace and mercy will cover my intentions." Each day closes with the Sign of the Cross.

## Fidelity note
Ember's novena keeps the same essential identity as the common St. Jude devotion — invoking the apostle as friend of Jesus and patron of difficult/impossible causes, framed around hope, perseverance, and trust in God's mercy — and its day themes overlap meaningfully with PrayMoreNovenas (difficult causes, perseverance in prayer, courage to witness, maintaining hope). The wording is entirely Ember's own composition rather than a copy of either source; it is more catechetical and biographically structured (call, hidden service, witness, thanksgiving) than PrayMoreNovenas' petition-list format. There is no verbatim borrowing, and the recurring "patron of the impossible" refrain from PrayMoreNovenas is not used. The FishEaters text could not be verified due to a truncated fetch, but the Ember version is plainly an original, doctrinally sound treatment rather than a transcription of any single source.
