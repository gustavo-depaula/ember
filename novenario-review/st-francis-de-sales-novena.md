# St. Francis de Sales — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O gentle St. Francis de Sales, friend of every soul, draw us to the sweetness of God's love. Calm our restless hearts and teach us to serve the Lord with patience, charity, and unwavering trust. Amen.

**Day 1 — Day 1: Devotion for everyone:** Francis taught that holiness is not reserved for cloisters but is meant for the soldier, the merchant, the parent, and the worker. He shows us that we can sanctify the very duties of our daily state. _(Intention: For the grace to seek holiness in our daily life.)_

**Day 2 — Day 2: Gentleness of heart:** Naturally inclined to anger, Francis labored for years to become meek, and he won more souls by sweetness than others by force. He teaches that a drop of honey draws more than a barrel of vinegar. _(Intention: For the grace of gentleness toward others.)_

**Day 3 — Day 3: Patience with ourselves:** Francis counseled us to correct our faults gently, without bitter self-reproach, rising again with confidence after every fall. He teaches that even our patience with our own weakness must be charitable. _(Intention: For patience and gentleness with our own faults.)_

**Day 4 — Day 4: Conformity to God's will:** Francis summed up the spiritual life in surrender: ask for nothing, refuse nothing. He teaches that perfect peace is found in wanting only what God wants for us. _(Intention: For the grace to conform our will to God's.)_

**Day 5 — Day 5: Living charity:** For Francis, devotion is simply charity grown active and prompt. He teaches that true love of God overflows into kindness, service, and patience toward our neighbor. _(Intention: For a charity active in word and deed.)_

**Day 6 — Day 6: The little virtues:** Francis prized humility, gentleness, and patience above showy heroics, calling them the virtues of everyday life. He teaches us to grow holy through small, hidden, faithful acts of love. _(Intention: For faithfulness in the small duties of each day.)_

**Day 7 — Day 7: Confidence in God:** Amid anxieties and trials Francis counseled boundless trust in the Father's tender care. He teaches us never to lose heart, for the same God who guides the world holds our small life as well. _(Intention: For confident trust in God's loving providence.)_

**Day 8 — Day 8: A zealous shepherd:** As bishop, Francis traveled, preached, and wrote tirelessly to bring straying souls home, never wearying of patience. His zeal shows us that love of souls is the heart of the apostolate. _(Intention: For zeal in leading others closer to God.)_

**Day 9 — Day 9: Live Jesus!:** Francis signed his letters 'Live Jesus,' that Christ might live and reign in every heart. He calls us to make Jesus the center and the love of our whole life. _(Intention: That Jesus may live and reign in our hearts.)_

**Closing prayer:** O holy Bishop and Doctor, who sought in all things only the good pleasure of God, obtain for us a heart wholly conformed to His will. May we, asking nothing and refusing nothing, find true peace in loving surrender. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas, "St. Francis de Sales Novena." Each day opens with the Sign of the Cross and follows a fixed format ending with a day-specific "Pray for me, that…" petition and "St. Francis de Sales, pray for us!" The nine days track his biography:
- Day 1: imitating his devotion to the priesthood — "that I may do all I can to serve you in my life"
- Day 2: submitting to God's will despite obstacles
- Day 3: missionary work converting Calvinists — "persevere against all difficulties to lead souls to God"
- Day 4: persevering through trials
- Day 5: growth during his episcopal service — "grow in my spiritual life each day"
- Day 6: his spiritual-direction work — "cultivate a close relationship with God"
- Day 7: his advocacy of lay holiness — "embrace the unique call to holiness of my vocation"
- Day 8: his Catholic writings — "strive for great holiness… each day"
- Day 9: his lifelong service — "always seek to serve God and His Church"

## Fidelity note
Ember is an independent composition, not a copy of either source. It shares strong thematic overlap with the PrayMoreNovenas arc — universal call to holiness in everyday life (Day 1 in both), conformity to God's will (Day 4 / Day 2), zeal for souls, lay holiness — all genuinely Salesian. Ember additionally draws directly on de Sales's own well-known sayings and signatures ("a drop of honey," "ask for nothing, refuse nothing," "Live Jesus!"), which makes it arguably more faithful to the saint's *Introduction to the Devout Life* than the PMN petitions. No factual divergence; the two are parallel devotions of the same spirit rather than the same text.
