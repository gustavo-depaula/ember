# St. Teresa of Ávila — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** St. Teresa of Jesus, valiant daughter of the Church and mistress of prayer, who found God dwelling within the castle of your soul, turn your gaze upon me. Teach me to converse with the Christ who loves me, and obtain for me the grace I ask in this novena. Amen.

**Day 1 — Prayer as friendship with Christ:** St. Teresa described mental prayer as nothing other than spending time alone with the One we know loves us. She teaches us that prayer is not a duty to perform but a friendship to cultivate with the living God.  _(Intention: For the grace to make prayer a daily friendship with Jesus.)_  (every day)

**Day 2 — Conversion of heart:** For years St. Teresa lived a tepid religious life until, before an image of the wounded Christ, she wept and surrendered herself wholly to Him. She teaches us that it is never too late to begin again with greater fervor.  _(Intention: For the grace of a deeper conversion and renewed fervor in our faith.)_  (every day)

**Day 3 — The interior castle:** St. Teresa saw the soul as a castle of crystal with many rooms, at whose center the King Himself dwells. She invites us to journey inward through prayer until we reach the chamber where God awaits us.  _(Intention: For the grace to seek God in the depths of our own soul.)_  (every day)

**Day 4 — The transverberation:** St. Teresa recounted how an angel pierced her heart with a golden dart of fire, leaving her aflame with love of God. This wound of love reveals that union with Christ is the deepest joy and the truest treasure of the soul.  _(Intention: For a heart set on fire with love for God above all things.)_  (every day)

**Day 5 — Reformer of Carmel:** Against great opposition, St. Teresa founded convents of strict poverty and deep prayer, restoring the original spirit of Carmel. She teaches us that genuine reform begins not with grand plans but with humble, persevering love.  _(Intention: For the courage to pursue holiness despite obstacles and opposition.)_  (every day)

**Day 6 — Determined determination:** St. Teresa urged souls to set out on the path of prayer with a determined determination never to turn back. She reminds us that perseverance, more than feelings, carries the soul to God.  _(Intention: For the grace of perseverance when prayer feels dry or difficult.)_  (every day)

**Day 7 — Humility and self-knowledge:** St. Teresa taught that humility is truth, and that we cannot grow in prayer without honest knowledge of ourselves before God. She shows us that the lowly soul is the one most ready to be raised by grace.  _(Intention: For the grace of true humility and honest self-knowledge.)_  (every day)

**Day 8 — Let nothing disturb you:** On a slip of paper in her breviary St. Teresa left the words, 'Let nothing disturb you, let nothing frighten you; all things pass, God never changes.' She teaches a serene peace rooted in the unchanging faithfulness of God.  _(Intention: For the grace of unshakable peace amid the trials of life.)_  (every day)

**Day 9 — God alone suffices:** St. Teresa concluded that whoever possesses God lacks nothing, for God alone suffices. She invites us to anchor our whole hope in Him and to find in His love the fulfillment of every desire.  _(Intention: For the grace to find in God alone the fulfillment of every longing.)_  (every day)

**Closing prayer:** St. Teresa, whose heart was wounded by the fire of divine love, intercede that nothing may ever disturb the peace of my soul. Keep me steadfast in patience and trust, knowing that God alone suffices and that whoever has God wants for nothing. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas opens each day with the Sign of the Cross and a fixed thanksgiving ("O most loving Heavenly Father! We thank you for the great gift you gave us through your beloved St. Teresa of Avila, virgin and doctor of the Church"), with a distinct daily intention, the recurring petition "Open wide the doors to the interior castle of our hearts and souls so that we may know how to pray," and concluding Our Father / Hail Mary / Glory Be plus "Saint Teresa of Avila, pray for us!" Daily themes: 1 gift of prayer, 2 greater trust, 3 recognizing God's plan, 4 love for Jesus, 5 listening to God, 6 strength in grief, 7 a consistent relationship with God, 8 awareness of God's presence, 9 recognizing answered prayers.

## Fidelity note
Ember's novena is its own composition that draws directly on Teresa's writings and life — prayer as friendship with the One who loves us, her conversion before the wounded Christ, the interior castle, the transverberation, the reform of Carmel, "determined determination," humility as truth, "let nothing disturb you," and "God alone suffices." It shares PrayMore's core themes (prayer, trust, conversion, perseverance) and the same Our Father / Hail Mary / Glory Be conclusion, while grounding each day in a specific Teresian text or episode rather than a generic petition. Wording is original, not paraphrased; the FishEaters source could not be checked.
