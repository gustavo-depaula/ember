# All Saints — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** Almighty and eternal God, who gladden us by the merits and intercession of all Your saints, grant that we who honor them on earth may one day share their joy in heaven. Make us, by Your grace, members of that great multitude that praises You forever. Amen.

**Day 1 — The universal call to holiness:** The saints were not a separate breed of men, but sinners who let God's grace transform them. Their lives proclaim that holiness is the vocation of every baptized soul, including our own.  _(Intention: For the grace to answer God's call to holiness in our own state of life.)_  (every day)

**Day 2 — The great multitude before the Lamb:** St. John saw a multitude beyond counting, from every nation and tongue, robed in white before the throne. This vision assures us that heaven is full and welcoming, and that there is room there for us.  _(Intention: For a living hope of heaven that shapes how we live each day.)_  (every day)

**Day 3 — Poor in spirit:** The saints lived the first Beatitude, holding loosely to the things of earth so as to be filled with God. Their poverty of spirit opened their hands to receive the kingdom of heaven.  _(Intention: For a detached heart that finds its treasure in God alone.)_  (every day)

**Day 4 — Meek and merciful:** The Beatitudes promise mercy to the merciful and the earth to the meek, and the saints made these promises their way of life. They overcame evil not with force but with gentleness and forgiveness.  _(Intention: For the grace to be gentle and merciful toward everyone we meet.)_  (every day)

**Day 5 — Pure of heart:** 'Blessed are the pure of heart, for they shall see God.' The saints guarded their hearts from divided loves so that nothing might cloud their vision of the One they sought.  _(Intention: For purity of heart and singleness of love for God.)_  (every day)

**Day 6 — The witness of the martyrs:** Countless saints poured out their blood rather than deny Christ, washing their robes white in the blood of the Lamb. Their courage shows us that no earthly loss can outweigh the gift of remaining faithful.  _(Intention: For courage to remain faithful to Christ in every trial.)_  (every day)

**Day 7 — The hidden saints:** Most of the blessed are unknown to the world — mothers and laborers, the poor and the forgotten, who loved God faithfully in ordinary days. Their hidden holiness teaches us that no quiet act of love is ever lost.  _(Intention: For the grace to grow holy in the ordinary duties of each day.)_  (every day)

**Day 8 — The communion of saints:** The saints in glory are not distant strangers but our family, bound to us in one Body and praying for us still. We are never alone on the journey, for heaven is full of friends who long to help us home.  _(Intention: For confidence in the prayers and friendship of the saints.)_  (every day)

**Day 9 — Pressing on toward the prize:** Surrounded by so great a cloud of witnesses, we are urged to run our race with perseverance, keeping our eyes on Jesus. The saints who finished the course now cheer us on toward the same crown of life.  _(Intention: For perseverance to the end and the grace of a holy death.)_  (every day)

**Closing prayer:** All you holy men and women, citizens of heaven and our elder brothers and sisters in the faith, pray for us who still walk the pilgrim road. Obtain for us perseverance in grace, hunger for holiness, and the hope of one day joining your unending song before the throne of God. Amen.

## Fidelity note
The FishEaters All Saints novena text could not be extracted, so a line-by-line comparison was not possible; what follows assesses Ember on its own merits. Ember's version is a coherent, theologically sound nine-day reflection on the feast, weaving together the universal call to holiness, the Revelation 7 vision of the great multitude, the Beatitudes (Days 3–5), the martyrs and the hidden saints, the communion of saints, and the Hebrews 12 "cloud of witnesses." It reads as an original composition; absent the FishEaters source text, its closeness to that source cannot be confirmed, but it is faithful to the spirit and Scripture of the solemnity.
