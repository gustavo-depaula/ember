# Novena for the Ascension — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** Lord Jesus, ascended in glory to the right hand of the Father, You did not leave us orphans but promised to send the Spirit of truth. Gather us with Your apostles and Your Mother in the Upper Room of our hearts, and teach us to wait in confident prayer. Prepare us to receive the gift You have promised. Amen.

**Day 1 — Christ ascends in glory:** Before the eyes of the apostles, the Lord was lifted up and a cloud took Him from their sight. He ascends not to leave us behind but to prepare a place for us where He has gone. Our true homeland is now hidden with Christ in heaven. _(Intention: For the grace to lift our hearts to heaven where Christ reigns in glory.)_

**Day 2 — Why do you stand looking up?:** Two men in white asked the gazing disciples why they stood looking up to heaven, for this same Jesus would come again. Their words turn longing into mission and waiting into hope. We are not to be paralyzed by His absence but to live ready for His return. _(Intention: For the grace to await Christ's coming with vigilant and hopeful hearts.)_

**Day 3 — The return to the Upper Room:** Obedient to the Lord's command, the apostles returned to Jerusalem and gathered in the room where He had given them His Body and Blood. There, in the very place of the first Eucharist, they began to wait. Obedience gathered them into the place where grace would be poured out. _(Intention: For the grace to obey the Lord and remain where He has placed us.)_

**Day 4 — Mary in the midst of the Church:** Scripture tells us the disciples persevered in prayer together with Mary, the Mother of Jesus. She who first received the Spirit at the Annunciation now intercedes for the newborn Church. With her at the center, the waiting community becomes a school of prayer. _(Intention: For the grace to pray as Mary prayed, in humble and constant trust.)_

**Day 5 — Persevering with one heart:** The apostles were gathered with one accord, united in heart and mind as they prayed. Their unity prepared them to receive a single Spirit poured upon them all. The Holy Spirit is given not to the isolated soul alone but to the Church gathered in charity. _(Intention: For the grace of unity and charity within Christ's Church.)_

**Day 6 — Waiting in faith without sight:** The Lord had ascended and the Spirit had not yet come; the apostles held nothing but His word. They waited in the darkness between promise and fulfillment, trusting what they could not see. So too we are called to believe before we feel, to hope before we receive. _(Intention: For the grace to trust God's promise in seasons of darkness and delay.)_

**Day 7 — Longing for the promised Spirit:** Each day in the Upper Room sharpened the apostles' hunger for the gift the Father had promised. Holy desire is itself the beginning of the Spirit's work, hollowing out the heart to be filled. The more we long, the more room we make for God. _(Intention: For the grace of a deep and burning desire for the Holy Spirit.)_

**Day 8 — Clothed with power from on high:** Jesus had promised that they would be clothed with power from on high and become His witnesses to the ends of the earth. The Spirit comes not to comfort us alone but to send us out in courage. What we receive in prayer we are meant to carry into the world. _(Intention: For the grace to become bold and faithful witnesses to Christ.)_

**Day 9 — On the threshold of Pentecost:** The nine days of waiting are complete, and the Church stands at the threshold of Pentecost. Soon the Spirit will descend as tongues of fire upon hearts made ready by prayer. Let us open ourselves wide, that the same fire may fall upon us. _(Intention: For a fresh outpouring of the Holy Spirit upon us and upon the whole Church.)_

Each day also prays one Our Father, one Hail Mary, and one Glory Be.

**Closing prayer:** Come, Holy Spirit, fill the hearts of Your faithful and kindle in us the fire of Your love. As You came upon the apostles who persevered with Mary, descend now upon us who have waited these nine days. Renew the face of the earth, and renew us. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
A nine-day novena framed around the Ascension. Daily opening: "In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen." followed by an address to Jesus: "we thank You and praise You for the mystery of Your Ascension into heaven. We ask You, dear Lord, to be with us always!" Each day meditates on the Ascension (forty days after the Resurrection, the instruction to the apostles, the promise of the Spirit), adds a daily focus, an intention insertion point, and closes "Lord, hear our prayers!" with the Sign of the Cross. Daily themes: 1 holy joy; 2 faith; 3 hope; 4 surrender to God's will; 5 intimacy with Jesus; 6 awareness of life's final purpose; 7 comfort amid loss; 8 bringing others to Christ; 9 strengthening the Church.

## Fidelity note
Ember's novena is an original composition, not a copy of PrayMoreNovenas. Both rightly anchor the devotion in the original "first novena" — the apostles and Mary praying in the Upper Room between Ascension and Pentecost — and Ember's day 8 (witnesses/"power from on high") and day 9 (the Church, Pentecost) align with PrayMoreNovenas' bringing-others and strengthening-the-Church themes. Ember's structure is more tightly narrative, following the Acts account step by step (ascent, the angels' words, return to the Upper Room, Mary, perseverance, waiting, longing, mission, threshold of Pentecost), where PrayMoreNovenas organizes around abstract virtues (joy, faith, hope). The closing draws on the traditional "Come, Holy Spirit" / "Renew the face of the earth" prayer. Faithful to the scriptural source; independently written.
