# Novena for the Assumption — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O Mary, assumed into heaven and crowned Queen of all creation, you are the first fruits of those who belong to Christ. Turn your merciful eyes upon me from your throne of glory, and draw me along the road that you have walked before me. Amen.

**Day 1 — The lowly handmaid:** She who was exalted above all creatures first called herself the handmaid of the Lord and rejoiced that God had looked upon her lowliness. Mary teaches us that the way up to glory passes through humility.  _(Intention: For a humble heart that magnifies the Lord in all things.)_

**Day 2 — Preserved from all sin:** Because Mary was kept free from the stain of sin, her body did not see corruption but was taken up whole into glory. Her purity reminds us that holiness is the seed of immortal glory.  _(Intention: For purity of heart and freedom from the bondage of sin.)_

**Day 3 — Faithful to the foot of the Cross:** Mary stood beside her dying Son when all but a few had fled, sharing his agony with unbroken faith. She who suffered most with Christ now shares most fully in his triumph.  _(Intention: For perseverance in faith through every trial and sorrow.)_

**Day 4 — The Woman clothed with the sun:** St. John beheld a great sign in heaven, a woman clothed with the sun, the moon beneath her feet, and a crown of twelve stars. The Church sees in her image Mary glorified, victorious over the ancient serpent.  _(Intention: For confidence in Mary's victory over evil in my own struggles.)_

**Day 5 — Taken up in body and soul:** At the end of her earthly course Mary was assumed whole and entire, body and soul, into the glory of heaven. Her Assumption proclaims that the body too is made for resurrection and eternal life.  _(Intention: For reverence toward my body as a temple destined for glory.)_

**Day 6 — Crowned Queen of heaven:** Christ the King welcomed his Mother to his side and set upon her a crown above all the saints and angels. Her queenship is not power for herself but mercy poured out for her children on earth.  _(Intention: For trust in Mary's queenly intercession for my needs.)_

**Day 7 — Our hope of resurrection:** In Mary assumed into heaven we behold the destiny promised to all the faithful, body and soul reunited in glory. She goes before us as a sign of sure hope and comfort to a pilgrim people.  _(Intention: For unshakable hope in the resurrection of the dead.)_

**Day 8 — Mother of the pilgrim Church:** From her place in glory Mary does not forget the Church still journeying through trial, but watches over us as a tender mother. She intercedes without ceasing until all her children are gathered home.  _(Intention: For the whole Church on earth and the souls in purgatory.)_

**Day 9 — To Jesus through Mary:** Every glory given to Mary leads back to her Son, for she is the first and finest fruit of his redemption. She invites us to let her carry us, as she carried him, into the heart of God.  _(Intention: For the grace to follow Mary all the way to her Son in heaven.)_

**Closing prayer:** O glorious Queen of heaven, who shares now in body and soul the joy of your risen Son, obtain for me the grace I implore in this novena. Keep me faithful unto death, that at the last I may rejoice with you in the glory you already possess. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas hosts an Assumption novena. Each day is bracketed by the Sign of the Cross and opens with the same fixed prayer (this is in fact the Collect of the feast): "Lord Jesus Christ, You have destroyed the power of death and given the hope of eternal life in body and soul. You granted your Mother a special place in your glory, and did not allow decay to touch her body. As we rejoice in the Assumption of Mary, give to us a renewed confidence in the victory of life over death. You live and reign forever and ever. Amen." Each day then adds a distinct intercessory prayer to Mary, a space for petitions, and the refrain "O Queen Assumed into Heaven, pray for us." Day 9 ends: "When our hour of death has come, lead us safely to the presence of Jesus to enjoy the vision of God for all eternity together with you."

## Fidelity note
Ember and PMN share the doctrinal core of the feast — Mary taken up body and soul, preserved from corruption, crowned Queen, the pledge of our own resurrection and hope at the hour of death — and Ember's closing ("Keep me faithful unto death… rejoice with you in the glory you already possess") echoes the same note as PMN's Day 9, though in Ember's own words. The structures differ: PMN's fixed daily prayer is the liturgical Collect addressed to Christ, whereas Ember addresses Mary throughout and organizes nine themed Marian meditations (handmaid, Immaculate, Calvary, Apocalypse 12, Assumption, Coronation, resurrection-hope, pilgrim Church, "to Jesus through Mary"). The result is a doctrinally faithful original composition that parallels the source's themes without reproducing its text.
