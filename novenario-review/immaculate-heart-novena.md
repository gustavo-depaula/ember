# Novena to the Immaculate Heart of Mary — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O Immaculate Heart of Mary, pure refuge of sinners and gentle Mother of mercy, we come to you in trust. You who kept the word of God and pondered it in your heart, draw us into the warmth of your love. Teach us to belong wholly to Jesus through you. Amen.

**Day 1 — The most pure Heart:** Preserved from all sin from the first moment of her conception, Mary's heart is wholly pure, untouched by any shadow. Her Immaculate Heart is a spotless mirror reflecting the goodness of God. In her purity we see what grace can make of a creature wholly given to the Lord. _(Intention: For the grace of purity of heart, mind, and body.)_

**Day 2 — A heart that pondered all things:** Scripture tells us that Mary kept all these things, pondering them in her heart. She received the mysteries of her Son in silence and treasured them in prayerful love. Her heart teaches us to listen deeply and to keep the word of God within us. _(Intention: For the grace to ponder God's word in a recollected heart.)_

**Day 3 — The Heart pierced by a sword:** Simeon foretold that a sword would pierce her soul, and at the foot of the Cross his prophecy was fulfilled. Mary's pure heart was wounded with the sufferings of her Son, yet she did not turn away. In her sorrow she became the Mother of all who suffer. _(Intention: For the grace to unite our sorrows to the suffering Heart of Mary.)_

**Day 4 — Refuge of sinners:** No one who has fled to her protection has been left unaided, for her heart is a sure refuge for sinners. The greater our misery, the more her mercy bends down to lift us. To run to her Immaculate Heart is to find shelter near the very Heart of God. _(Intention: For the conversion of sinners and refuge for those far from God.)_

**Day 5 — The call of Fatima:** At Fatima Our Lady revealed her Immaculate Heart and asked for prayer, penance, and consecration. She made known that God wished to establish devotion to her heart in the world. Her gentle request remains an invitation to peace through her maternal care. _(Intention: For the grace to answer Our Lady's call to prayer and penance.)_

**Day 6 — Consecration and entrustment:** To consecrate ourselves to the Immaculate Heart is to place our whole life, body and soul, into Mary's keeping. She receives all that we entrust to her and offers it to Jesus made pure by her hands. In her heart nothing we surrender is ever lost. _(Intention: For the grace to entrust ourselves wholly to the Immaculate Heart.)_

**Day 7 — Reparation to her Heart:** Our Lady asked for acts of reparation to console her Immaculate Heart, wounded by the sins and ingratitude of men. To make reparation is an act of love, repairing what offense has torn. We seek to comfort the Mother whose heart still aches for her children. _(Intention: For the grace to console the Immaculate Heart by lives of reparation.)_

**Day 8 — Through her Heart to the Heart of Jesus:** Mary's heart points always away from itself and toward her Son, saying still, 'Do whatever He tells you.' Her Immaculate Heart is the surest and shortest road to the Sacred Heart of Jesus. To love her is never to stop short of Christ, but to be led straight to Him. _(Intention: For the grace to be led through Mary's heart to the Heart of Jesus.)_

**Day 9 — In the end, her Heart will triumph:** Our Lady promised at Fatima that in the end her Immaculate Heart would triumph. This is no boast but a quiet confidence in the victory of grace over sin. We pray that her triumph may begin within our own hearts, surrendered fully to God. _(Intention: For the triumph of the Immaculate Heart in our hearts and in the world.)_

Each day also prays one Our Father, one Hail Mary, and one Glory Be.

**Closing prayer:** O Immaculate Heart of Mary, to you we consecrate ourselves and all that we are. Keep us close to your Son, guard us in the hour of temptation, and bring us safely home to heaven. Be our refuge now, and let your Immaculate Heart at last triumph in our souls. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
FishEaters gives a single extended prayer prayed each of the nine days, not a nine-meditation cycle. It opens: "O Heart of Mary, Mother of God and our Mother; Heart most worthy of love, in which the adorable Trinity is ever well-pleased..." and develops Mary's heart as the refuge of the Church, channel of grace, and protector in life's trials and at death, closing: "...open to us a safe refuge in that very fountain of mercy, whence we may come to praise Him with thee in paradise, world without end. Amen." Suggested to be prayed August 13–21, before the feast.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
A nine-day novena with the same prayers repeated daily: an opening invocation, a personal-intention section, and a formal Act of Consecration attributed to Pope Pius XII. Daily opening: "In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen." with the petition "Immaculate Virgin, by the holy will of your Son my Lord Jesus Christ, you are my Mother in Heaven," then a Hail Mary. The Act of Consecration ("Most Holy Virgin Mary, tender Mother of men...") consecrates self, families, and nations to Mary's heart and ends "Make our hearts and homes Your shrine, and through us, make the Heart of Jesus, together with your rule, triumph in every heart and home." Closes "Immaculate Heart of Mary. Pray for us!"

## Fidelity note
Ember's novena is an original composition rather than a transcription, and unlike both sources — which repeat one fixed prayer/consecration each day — Ember offers nine distinct daily meditations. It is nonetheless deeply faithful to the authentic devotion: the Immaculate Conception (pure heart), the Lucan "pondered in her heart," Simeon's sword, refuge of sinners, the Fatima call to prayer/penance/consecration/reparation, and Our Lady's promise "in the end my Immaculate Heart will triumph" all appear and are doctrinally accurate. Ember's consecration/triumph themes (days 6, 9, and the closing) directly echo PrayMoreNovenas' Pius XII consecration and "triumph in every heart and home," while the "refuge" language and Trinitarian framing align with FishEaters. Faithful in substance and tradition; freshly and independently written, with a richer day-by-day structure than either source.
