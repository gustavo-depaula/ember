# St. Padre Pio — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** St. Padre Pio, faithful servant of God and bearer of the wounds of Christ, you who spent yourself for sinners at the altar and in the confessional, draw near to me now. Obtain for me a deeper love of Jesus and confidence in His mercy. Amen.

**Day 1 — Pray, hope, and don't worry:** Padre Pio comforted anxious souls with five simple words: pray, hope, and don't worry. He had learned that worry is wasted energy and that God carries what we surrender to Him in prayer.  _(Intention: For the grace to hand all my anxieties to God and rest in His care.)_

**Day 2 — The wounds of Christ:** For fifty years Padre Pio bore in his own flesh the five wounds of the Crucified, a hidden agony he tried to conceal. He showed that to love Christ is to share His Cross without complaint.  _(Intention: For the grace to carry my own crosses in union with the suffering Christ.)_

**Day 3 — The Holy Mass:** Padre Pio's Masses often lasted hours, for he saw before him the very Sacrifice of Calvary renewed upon the altar. He taught that the world could more easily exist without the sun than without the Holy Mass.  _(Intention: For a renewed reverence for the Holy Sacrifice of the Mass.)_

**Day 4 — The confessional:** Day after day Padre Pio sat for long hours hearing confessions, reading hearts with a gaze that pierced excuses and softened the proud. He labored that no soul might leave the tribunal of mercy without true sorrow.  _(Intention: For the grace of a sincere and contrite confession of my sins.)_

**Day 5 — Suffering united to Christ:** Padre Pio embraced his illnesses and pains as a share in the redemptive work of Jesus, offering them for the salvation of souls. He called the Cross the bed on which the soul rests with its Spouse.  _(Intention: For the grace to offer my sufferings for the good of souls.)_

**Day 6 — Spiritual direction:** Padre Pio guided his spiritual children with firmness and tenderness, knowing each one by name and need. He urged them to advance step by step, never discouraged by falls, but always rising again toward God.  _(Intention: For wise guidance and steady progress in the spiritual life.)_

**Day 7 — Devotion to Our Lady:** Padre Pio loved the Rosary above all his other prayers, calling it the weapon for our times and rarely letting the beads leave his hands. He entrusted every soul and every need to the Mother of God.  _(Intention: For a faithful daily devotion to Our Lady through the Rosary.)_

**Day 8 — Charity and the House for the Relief of Suffering:** From his prayer flowed action, and Padre Pio built a great hospital, the Home for the Relief of Suffering, to serve the sick in body and soul. He wished it to be a temple of science and of prayer at once.  _(Intention: For the grace to let my prayer overflow into concrete works of mercy.)_

**Day 9 — Perseverance to the end:** Padre Pio died with the names of Jesus and Mary on his lips, having served at the altar nearly to his final hour. His long life of fidelity teaches us to remain faithful until God Himself calls us home.  _(Intention: For the grace of final perseverance and a holy death.)_

**Closing prayer:** St. Padre Pio, who taught us to pray, to hope, and not to worry, intercede for me before the throne of God. Win for me the grace I seek and, above all, the will to belong wholly to Christ. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
A 9-day novena with identical prayers every day (no day-specific meditations). Each day: Sign of the Cross; invocation "O St. Pio, I come to you today knowing that you are truly a miracle worker... I ask that you would pray for these my intentions: (State your intentions here)"; a main prayer attributed to Pope John Paul II beginning "Glorious, humble and beloved Padre Pio," asking for humility, faith, and devotion to Mary; a closing prayer attributed to Padre Pio beginning "O Lord, we ask for a boundless confidence and trust in Your divine mercy, and the courage to accept the crosses and sufferings..."; a doxology; "St. Padre Pio, Pray for us!"; closing Sign of the Cross.

## Fidelity note
Ember's version is an original composition and does not reproduce PMN's prayers (the JPII and Padre Pio texts). The two differ in form: PMN repeats one fixed set of prayers all nine days, while Ember writes nine distinct biographical meditations. Thematically Ember is well-grounded in Pio's life — his signature "Pray, hope, and don't worry," the stigmata (fifty years), his hours in the confessional, the long Masses, the Casa Sollievo della Sofferenza ("Home for the Relief of Suffering"), and his love of the Rosary — all historically accurate. PMN's "courage to accept the crosses and sufferings" theme is echoed in Ember's Day 2 and Day 5, but the match is thematic, not textual.
