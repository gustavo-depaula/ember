# Novena for the Immaculate Conception — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O Mary conceived without sin, all-beautiful and full of grace, I come to you who were never for a moment a slave of the enemy. Draw me into your purity, and obtain for me the grace to hate sin and to love God as you do. Amen.

**Day 1 — Conceived without sin:** From the first instant of her conception Mary was preserved free from all stain of original sin, a privilege granted to no other creature. God prepared a worthy dwelling for His Son before the world ever knew her name.  _(Intention: For a deeper gratitude for the gift of Mary's Immaculate Conception.)_

**Day 2 — Full of grace:** The angel greeted Mary as 'full of grace,' a vessel so filled with God that no room remained for sin. Her soul was a garden enclosed where the Lord delighted to dwell.  _(Intention: That God's grace may fill and transform my soul.)_

**Day 3 — You are all beautiful:** 'Tota pulchra es, Maria' — you are all beautiful, and the original stain is not in you. Mary's beauty is the radiance of a soul wholly given to God, undimmed by any shadow.  _(Intention: For the beauty of holiness to grow in my own soul.)_

**Day 4 — The new Eve:** Where the first Eve fell to the serpent, Mary crushed his head by her obedience and her sinless 'yes.' She is the woman foretold in Genesis, at enmity with the devil from the very beginning.  _(Intention: For Mary's protection against the temptations of the evil one.)_

**Day 5 — The dawn of redemption:** Mary's Immaculate Conception is the first light of the coming Redeemer, the dawn that heralds the rising Sun. In her preservation from sin we already see the saving power of the Cross at work.  _(Intention: That my heart may prepare for the coming of Christ.)_

**Day 6 — Saved by Christ's merits:** Mary was redeemed in the most perfect way, preserved from sin rather than rescued from it, in view of her Son's future merits. She is the first and greatest fruit of His redemption, and proof of all He longs to do in us.  _(Intention: For confidence in the redeeming power of Christ.)_

**Day 7 — I am the Immaculate Conception:** At Lourdes the Lady told Bernadette her name: 'I am the Immaculate Conception,' confirming from heaven what the Church had defined. To a poor and unlettered child she revealed the mystery the learned had long contemplated.  _(Intention: For childlike trust in the words of our heavenly Mother.)_

**Day 8 — Refuge of sinners:** Though she never knew sin, Mary is the tender refuge of sinners, drawing us out of the mire by her prayers. The sinless Mother is most moved by the misery of her wandering children.  _(Intention: For all sinners, that they may turn to Mary and be converted.)_

**Day 9 — Patroness and Mother:** Under the title of the Immaculate Conception, Mary is the patroness of many nations and the Mother of all the Church. She who is wholly God's takes us as her own and leads us to her Son.  _(Intention: That I may entrust myself wholly to the Immaculate Mother.)_

**Closing prayer:** O Mary conceived without sin, pray for us who have recourse to you. By your Immaculate Conception, purify my heart, defend me from the snares of the evil one, and lead me safely to your Son. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
Said once a day for nine days, especially November 29 through December 7 (the vigil of the feast). The daily prayer:

"Immaculate Virgin! Mary, conceived without sin! Remember, thou wert miraculously preserved from even the shadow of sin, because thou wert destined to become not only the Mother of God, but also the mother, the refuge, and the advocate of man; penetrated therefore, with the most lively confidence in thy never-failing intercession, we most humbly implore thee to look with favor upon the intentions of this novena, and to obtain for us the graces and the favors we request."

The novena continues with petitions for purity of heart, a spirit of prayer and self-denial, and concludes with versicles and a formal prayer for forgiveness and eternal blessedness through the Immaculate Conception. The same prayer is used each day (no per-day meditations).

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
Each day opens "In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen." The main prayer, repeated identically all nine days, begins "O most pure Virgin Mary conceived without sin, from the very first instant, you were entirely immaculate" and continues with requests for grace to imitate Mary's holiness and submission to God's will; closes with space to mention intentions, three petitions for increased faith, hope, and love in Jesus, and a concluding Hail Mary.

## Fidelity note
The theology is fully aligned: both sources and Ember center on Mary preserved free from all stain of original sin from the first instant of her conception, her role as refuge/advocate of sinners, and the call to imitate her purity — and Ember's dating (Nov 29–Dec 7) matches FishEaters exactly. Structurally they differ: both web sources repeat one fixed daily prayer, whereas Ember unfolds nine distinct meditation days (full of grace, Tota Pulchra, the new Eve, redemption "in view of Christ's merits," the Lourdes title, refuge of sinners), reflecting the dogma and Lumen Gentium more richly than either source's single prayer. Ember's "refuge and advocate" closing motif clearly echoes the FishEaters prayer, but the opening/closing texts are Ember's own wording. A faithful and notably well-catechized expansion rather than a transcription.
