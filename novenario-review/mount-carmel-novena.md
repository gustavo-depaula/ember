# Our Lady of Mt. Carmel — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O beautiful Flower of Carmel, Mother of God and Mother most loving, I place this novena in your hands. Obtain for me the grace to pray faithfully, love Jesus with an undivided heart, and persevere in holiness under your maternal care. Amen.

**Day 1 — Mother and beauty of Carmel:** Under the title of Mount Carmel, Mary is honored as the beauty of holiness and the Mother who leads us to Jesus. She invites us to begin this novena with trust and filial love. _(Intention: For a heart open to Mary's maternal guidance.)_

**Day 2 — School of prayer and contemplation:** Carmel teaches silence, recollection, and attentive listening to God. With Mary we learn to treasure the Word in our hearts and remain faithful in daily prayer. _(Intention: For perseverance in prayer and a contemplative spirit.)_

**Day 3 — Fidelity to Christ through Mary:** True Marian devotion always leads to deeper union with Jesus. Our Lady of Mount Carmel forms disciples who obey the Gospel with humility and courage. _(Intention: For fidelity to Jesus Christ in every state of life.)_

**Day 4 — The scapular as sign of consecration:** The Brown Scapular reminds us to belong to Mary and to live clothed in Christ. It is a call to conversion, sacramental life, and daily imitation of her virtues. _(Intention: For grace to live our Marian consecration with integrity.)_

**Day 5 — Purity of heart and conversion:** Mary's Immaculate Heart reflects total openness to God. She helps us turn away from sin and seek purity in mind, speech, and action. _(Intention: For ongoing conversion and purity of life.)_

**Day 6 — Strength in trials and darkness:** In moments of suffering, Mary remains close and steadfast. Under her mantle we learn to endure trials with hope, trusting that God is present even in the night of faith. _(Intention: For those who suffer, that they may receive hope and endurance.)_

**Day 7 — Charity and service to neighbor:** Contemplation bears fruit in charity. Our Lady of Mount Carmel teaches us to recognize Christ in others and to serve with humility, gentleness, and generous love. _(Intention: For growth in charity and concrete works of mercy.)_

**Day 8 — Comfort of the dying and hope for souls:** The Church invokes Our Lady of Mount Carmel for the dying and for the souls in purgatory. She accompanies her children at life's final hour and leads them toward the mercy of Christ. _(Intention: For the dying, the souls in purgatory, and final perseverance.)_

**Day 9 — Perseverance in holiness:** We conclude this novena by entrusting our whole life to Mary of Carmel. With her intercession, may we remain faithful to Christ until the end and rejoice forever in God's presence. _(Intention: For final perseverance and eternal union with God.)_

**Closing prayer:** Our Lady of Mount Carmel, pray for us. Cover us with your mantle, protect our families, strengthen the Church, and lead us always to your Son, Jesus Christ. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
FishEaters carries the classic "Flower of Carmel" novena, said daily July 7–15: Opening — "O most beautiful Flower of Mount Carmel, Fruitful Vine, Splendour of Heaven, Blessed Mother of the Son of God, Immaculate Virgin, assist me in this my necessity" (followed by the petitioner's intention). Main prayer — "O Star of the Sea, help me and show me in this that thou art my Mother. O holy Mary, Mother of God, Queen of Heaven and earth, I humbly beseech thee, from the bottom of my heart, to help me in this necessity; there are none that can withstand thy power. O show me in this that thou art my Mother!" Closing (3× each) — "O Mary, conceived without sin, pray for us who have recourse to thee." and "Sweet Mother, I place this cause in thy hands."

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas opens each day with the Sign of the Cross and closes with "Our Lady of Mount Carmel, pray for us!" Rather than one repeated prayer, it gives nine distinct daily themes with their own reflections and a space to state personal intentions: Day 1 scapular wearers' faithfulness; Day 2 Carmelite communities and vocations; Day 3 those in poverty and suffering; Day 4 understanding devotion to Mary; Day 5 comfort for the dying and deceased; Day 6 the holy souls in Purgatory; Day 7 healing for the sick; Day 8 surrendering to God's will; Day 9 drawing closer to Jesus through Mary.

## Fidelity note
Ember's version is an original composition rather than a transcription of either source. It does not reproduce the famous "Flower of Carmel"/"Star of the Sea" prayer (the most distinctive textual marker of the traditional FishEaters novena), instead opening with a freshly written invocation that echoes the "Flower of Carmel" image. Structurally it most resembles PrayMoreNovenas' model — nine thematic days each with a meditation and intention — and its day themes overlap meaningfully (scapular/consecration, prayer/contemplation, the suffering and dying, the souls in purgatory, perseverance), though the wording is paraphrased and reorganized. The closing "Our Lady of Mount Carmel, pray for us" matches PMN's refrain. Faithful in spirit and theme; not a verbatim rendering of the historic prayers.
