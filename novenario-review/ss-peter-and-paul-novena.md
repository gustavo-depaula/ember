# Novena to SS. Peter and Paul — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US

**Opening prayer:** Holy Apostles Peter and Paul, chosen pillars of Christ's Church, you laid down your lives in Rome for the faith you proclaimed. As I begin this novena, obtain for me a faith as firm as Peter's and a zeal as burning as Paul's. Watch over the Church you founded with your blood. Amen.

**Day 1 — Called from the nets:** Peter left his boat at once when Jesus said, 'Follow me, and I will make you a fisher of men.' He teaches us to answer the Lord's call without delay or condition. _(Intention: For the grace to follow Christ promptly wherever He leads.)_

**Day 2 — Thou art the rock:** When Peter confessed Jesus as the Christ, the Son of the living God, the Lord named him the rock and gave him the keys of the kingdom. On this confession the unity of the Church still rests. _(Intention: For unity in the Church around the successor of Peter.)_

**Day 3 — Peter's tears:** Peter denied his Master three times, yet wept bitterly and was restored when the risen Lord asked, 'Do you love me?' He teaches us that no fall is final for the soul that loves and repents. _(Intention: For the grace of tears over my sins and confidence in God's mercy.)_

**Day 4 — Paul on the Damascus road:** Saul, breathing threats against the disciples, was thrown to the ground by a light from heaven and heard, 'Why do you persecute me?' In an instant the persecutor became the apostle, proof that grace can master any heart. _(Intention: For the conversion of sinners and of those who oppose the faith.)_

**Day 5 — Apostle to the gentiles:** Paul carried the Gospel across sea and land, becoming all things to all men that he might win some for Christ. His tireless travels remind us that the faith is meant for every nation. _(Intention: For missionaries and the spread of the Gospel to all peoples.)_

**Day 6 — Strength in weakness:** Paul boasted of his weaknesses, for the Lord told him, 'My grace is sufficient for you; my power is made perfect in weakness.' He teaches that God works most when we rely least on ourselves. _(Intention: That I may trust God's grace in my own weakness and trials.)_

**Day 7 — The charity of Paul:** Paul wrote that without love he would be nothing, for love is patient and kind and bears all things. The Apostle places charity above every gift and every knowledge. _(Intention: For the gift of genuine charity in all my relationships.)_

**Day 8 — Feed my sheep:** To Peter the risen Lord entrusted His flock, saying, 'Feed my sheep.' He teaches us that to love Christ is to care for His people and to serve in His name. _(Intention: For the Pope, bishops, and all shepherds of souls.)_

**Day 9 — The good fight finished:** Both apostles witnessed in Rome unto death, Peter crucified and Paul beheaded, sealing the faith with their blood. Paul could write at the end, 'I have fought the good fight, I have kept the faith.' _(Intention: For the grace of perseverance and a holy death.)_

(Each day the user also prays one Our Father, one Hail Mary, and one Glory Be.)

**Closing prayer:** Glorious Apostles, Peter holding the keys and Paul wielding the sword of the Spirit, intercede for the Church and for the Holy Father who sits in Peter's chair. Keep me faithful to the apostolic faith you handed down. Lead me at last to the crown you now wear. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas has a Novena to Saints Peter and Paul. It is structured as nine distinct days (not one repeated prayer): each day opens and closes with the Sign of the Cross, presents a unique reflection on the two apostles, voices petitions to the saints, and provides a space for the petitioner's own intentions ("St. Peter and St. Paul, please pray for my intentions [state your intentions here]."). The day themes run: (1) opposition and courage, (2) strengthening family faith, (3) repentance and amendment, (4) growth in faith and support for clergy, (5) evangelization using personal talents, (6) deepening relationship with Christ, (7) community service and spreading the Gospel, (8) listening to God's voice, (9) trust in God during difficulties.

## Fidelity note
Ember and PMN share the same scaffold — nine distinct days, each a meditation plus a personal intention — which is the standard PMN shape, so the structural fidelity is high. The wording is wholly Ember's own: it is not a transcription of PMN's prayers but an independent composition built on the same Scripture-driven episodes of the apostles' lives. The thematic emphases also diverge: Ember organizes the nine days tightly around the biblical biography (call, confession, denial, Damascus, missions, weakness, charity, the keys, martyrdom), whereas PMN's themes lean more pastoral/applied (family, clergy, community service). Best read as an original Ember composition in the PMN format rather than a faithful copy of the PMN text.
