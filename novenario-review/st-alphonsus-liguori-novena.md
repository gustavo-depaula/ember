# Novena to St. Alphonsus Liguori — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US

**Opening prayer:** Glorious St. Alphonsus, who left the honors of the world to serve the souls most forgotten, obtain for me a heart on fire with love of God. Teach me, by your tenderness toward sinners, never to despair of God's mercy. Amen.

**Day 1 — Leaving the world for Christ:** A celebrated young lawyer, Alphonsus laid his sword upon Our Lady's altar and turned from worldly success to the priesthood. He teaches us that the loss of earthly honor can be the gateway to a far greater calling. _(Intention: For the courage to renounce vain ambitions for the sake of Christ.)_

**Day 2 — Love for the poor and abandoned:** Alphonsus founded the Redemptorists to carry the Gospel to the goatherds and country folk whom no one else reached. He shows us that Christ's love seeks out those whom the world overlooks. _(Intention: For a heart that reaches out to the poor and the forgotten.)_

**Day 3 — Tenderness toward sinners:** In the confessional Alphonsus was gentle and patient, drawing souls back to God by kindness rather than fear. He teaches us that mercy, not harshness, is the surest road to conversion. _(Intention: For confessors and for sinners, that mercy may lead them home to God.)_

**Day 4 — The Glories of Mary:** Alphonsus poured out his love for the Mother of God in writings that proclaim her as the refuge of sinners and gate of heaven. He teaches us to fly to Mary with the confidence of a child. _(Intention: For a deeper, childlike devotion to Mary, our refuge and hope.)_

**Day 5 — Love of the Eucharist:** Alphonsus called the visit to the Blessed Sacrament the dearest of all devotions and spent long hours before the tabernacle. He shows us that no friend awaits us so faithfully as Jesus truly present in the Eucharist. _(Intention: For a burning love for Jesus present in the Blessed Sacrament.)_

**Day 6 — Prayer, the great means of salvation:** Alphonsus taught that those who pray are surely saved and those who do not are surely lost. He urges us to ask God constantly, for the Lord has promised to grant what we humbly beg. _(Intention: For the grace to pray faithfully and never to neglect it.)_

**Day 7 — Conformity to God's will:** Through years of illness and trial, Alphonsus taught that all holiness consists in loving and embracing God's will. He shows us that peace is found not in changing our lot but in surrendering it to God. _(Intention: For the grace to accept God's will in joy and in suffering alike.)_

**Day 8 — Constancy in suffering:** In old age Alphonsus bore great physical pain and even inner darkness without losing his trust in God. He teaches us that fidelity in the long night of suffering is itself a hidden victory. _(Intention: For perseverance and trust in seasons of pain and spiritual darkness.)_

**Day 9 — Final perseverance:** Alphonsus taught his people to beg unceasingly for the grace of a holy death, which crowns every other grace. He reminds us that to die in the friendship of God is the one thing truly necessary. _(Intention: For the grace of a holy death in the friendship of God.)_

(Each day the user also prays one Our Father, one Hail Mary, and one Glory Be.)

**Closing prayer:** St. Alphonsus, doctor of prayer and herald of Mary, lead me ever closer to Jesus in the Blessed Sacrament and to His Mother, our hope. Obtain for me the grace I now ask, and above all the grace of final perseverance. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
FishEaters has a single prayer said once a day for nine days (24 July–1 August, eve of the feast), followed by the Litany of St. Alphonsus Liguori:

"O my glorious and well-beloved patron, Saint Alphonsus, thou who didst toil and suffer so abundantly to assure to men the fruits of the Redemption, behold the miseries of my poor soul and have pity on me. By thy powerful intercession with Jesus and Mary, obtain for me true repentance for my sins together with their pardon and remission, a deep hatred of sin, and strength evermore to resist all temptations. Share with me, I pray, at least a spark of that fire of love wherewith thy heart did ever burn; and grant that, following thy example, I may make the will of God the only rule of my life. Obtain for me likewise a fervent and lasting love of Jesus, and a tender and childlike devotion to Mary, together with the grace to pray without ceasing and to persevere in the service of God even to the end of my life, that so I may finally be united with thee in praising God and most holy Mary through all eternity. Amen."

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PMN's St. Alphonsus novena is nine days in an identical format (Sign of the Cross open/close, thematic content, "Please bring my petitions before God" with space for intentions, "St. Alphonsus, pray for us!"). Its day themes are organized around his biography: (1) faithfulness to vocation despite physical limitations, (2) balancing worldly success with the spiritual calling, (3) openness to God's will despite setbacks, (4) devotion through priesthood and perseverance, (5) service to the poor, (6) effective preaching, (7) missionary work, (8) renouncing material wealth, (9) theological study and writing.

## Fidelity note
Ember does not transcribe the FishEaters prayer, but its themes are strikingly faithful to it: the FishEaters text's "fire of love," "the will of God the only rule of my life," "fervent and lasting love of Jesus," "tender and childlike devotion to Mary," "grace to pray without ceasing," and "persevere... to the end" map almost one-for-one onto Ember's days on the Eucharist, conformity to God's will, devotion to Mary, prayer, and final perseverance — so it reads as a faithful expansion of that single prayer into nine meditations. Against PMN it shares the nine-day meditation format and overlaps on leaving the world, the poor, and God's will, but Ember weights the days toward Alphonsus's writings and spiritual doctrine (The Glories of Mary, the Eucharist, "those who pray are saved," final perseverance) rather than PMN's biographical/ministry sequence. Wording is Ember's own throughout. Faithful, well-sourced, and notably close in spirit to the traditional FishEaters prayer; an original composition.
