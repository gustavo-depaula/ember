# St. Thomas Aquinas — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O ineffable Creator, who gave to St. Thomas Aquinas a burning love of truth, send forth Your light into our minds and Your love into our hearts. By his prayers grant us to seek, to find, and to rest in You, the Truth that never fails. Amen.

**Day 1 — Day 1: Love of truth:** From his youth Thomas was consumed by one question: 'What is God?' His whole life was a search for the Truth who is God Himself, drawing all knowledge back to its divine source. _(Intention: For a sincere love of truth above all opinions.)_

**Day 2 — Day 2: Faith and reason:** Thomas showed that faith and reason are not enemies but two paths from the one God of truth. He teaches us to honor the mind God gave us while bowing before mysteries beyond it. _(Intention: For harmony between our faith and our reason.)_

**Day 3 — Day 3: Study as prayer:** Thomas began his work with the prayer 'Creator ineffabilis,' asking light to learn and grace to teach. He shows us that study undertaken for God becomes itself a form of worship. _(Intention: That our learning and work may glorify God.)_

**Day 4 — Day 4: Adoring the hidden God:** In the hymn Adoro te devote Thomas knelt before the Eucharist, where sight, touch, and taste fail but faith alone suffices. He teaches us to adore Christ truly present beneath the veils of bread and wine. _(Intention: For a deep, adoring faith in the Holy Eucharist.)_

**Day 5 — Day 5: The bread of angels:** In the sequence Lauda Sion Thomas sang of the Eucharist as the bread of angels given to the children of men. He teaches us to receive Holy Communion with reverence, wonder, and love. _(Intention: For greater reverence in receiving Holy Communion.)_

**Day 6 — Day 6: Humility of the wise:** Though the most learned of his age, Thomas was gentle, silent, and unassuming, mocked in youth as the 'dumb ox.' His humility teaches us that true wisdom never puffs up but bows before God. _(Intention: For humility joined to whatever knowledge we possess.)_

**Day 7 — Day 7: Drawing wisdom from the Cross:** When the crucified Lord asked what reward he desired, Thomas answered, 'Only Yourself, Lord.' He teaches that all learning is empty unless it leads us to love God alone. _(Intention: That we may desire God above every other gift.)_

**Day 8 — Day 8: All is straw:** After a profound vision, Thomas laid down his pen, saying that all he had written seemed like straw beside what he had glimpsed of God. He teaches us that no thought can contain the One we are made to behold. _(Intention: For longing for the vision of God above all things.)_

**Day 9 — Day 9: Truth in love:** Thomas died in peace, receiving Viaticum with words of fervent faith in the Sacrament he had so loved. He intercedes that we may seek the truth, live it in charity, and rest at last in God. _(Intention: For the grace to seek and live the truth in charity.)_

**Closing prayer:** O Angelic Doctor, who adored the hidden God in the Blessed Sacrament and counted all learning as straw before His glory, obtain for us a humble and ardent faith. May we love truth for God's sake and adore Him with all our heart. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas, "St. Thomas Aquinas Novena." Each day opens with the Sign of the Cross and "Dear Lord, you chose St. Thomas Aquinas to write about Your Word and the Christian faith, help me to be educated by the faith that is revealed in his writings," then a day-specific intercession, closing with a Glory Be and the trinitarian blessing. The nine days are organized as petitions:
- Day 1: for students (those who learn easily and those who struggle), openness to truth
- Day 2: for the Church's growth, more priests and vocations, strength for the Pope, theologians, missionaries
- Day 3: deeper love and respect for the Holy Eucharist and Christ's presence in Communion
- Day 4: humility when facing insults and attacks on one's character
- Day 5: patience in discerning vocations (priests, religious, married, single)
- Day 6: greater reliance on prayer amid theological/personal difficulty and confusion
- Day 7: increased charity toward those who disagree with us
- Day 8: dedication to seeking and teaching truth; prayers for those lost in falsehood
- Day 9: protection from sin, chastity, and against abortion, abuse, pornography, infidelity

## Fidelity note
Ember is an independent composition, not a copy of the PMN petitions. Both rightly center Thomas's love of truth, his Eucharistic devotion, and humility, and there is genuine overlap (Day 3/4 Eucharist; the humility theme). But Ember is markedly more biographical and devotional, weaving in authentic Thomistic detail the PMN version omits — the "What is God?" question, the *Creator ineffabilis* study-prayer, the *Adoro te devote* and *Lauda Sion* hymns, the "dumb ox" mockery, the crucifix's "Only Yourself, Lord," and the "all is straw" vision. These are accurate to the saint's life and writings, making Ember the fuller and more faithful portrait of the two. No factual divergence noted.
