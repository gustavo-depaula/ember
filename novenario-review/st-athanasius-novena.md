# St. Athanasius — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** St. Athanasius, intrepid bishop and pillar of the true faith, you confessed Christ as true God when nearly the whole world turned away. Strengthen me as I begin this novena, that I may hold fast to the truth without fear. Obtain for me from God the grace I now ask and a heart firmly fixed on Christ. Amen.

**Day 1 — Day 1: Christ is true God:** Athanasius insisted that the Son is of one substance with the Father, true God from true God, not a creature however exalted. He saw clearly that our salvation depends on this truth, for only God can make us partakers of His own life.  _(Intention: For the grace to adore Jesus Christ as true God and true man.)_

**Day 2 — Day 2: On the Incarnation:** In his great treatise Athanasius wrote that God became man so that man might become a sharer in God. He marveled that the Word who upholds all things took a body to renew the image of God in us.  _(Intention: For wonder and gratitude before the mystery of the Word made flesh.)_

**Day 3 — Day 3: The Council of Nicaea:** As a young deacon, Athanasius assisted at the Council of Nicaea, where the Church confessed the Son as consubstantial with the Father. He spent the rest of his life defending what that council had so clearly proclaimed.  _(Intention: For fidelity to the faith handed down by the Church through the ages.)_

**Day 4 — Day 4: Athanasius against the world:** When emperors, bishops, and crowds embraced Arianism, Athanasius stood almost alone for the truth, earning the title "Athanasius against the world." He teaches us that truth is not decided by numbers, and that one faithful soul can hold the line.  _(Intention: For courage to stand for the truth even when I stand nearly alone.)_

**Day 5 — Day 5: Patience in exile:** Five times Athanasius was driven from his see, spending long years in exile, hiding, and the desert. He bore each banishment without bitterness, trusting that God would vindicate the truth in His own time.  _(Intention: For patience to endure injustice without bitterness or compromise.)_

**Day 6 — Day 6: Friend of the monks:** During his exiles Athanasius found refuge among the desert monks and wrote the famous Life of St. Antony, spreading the monastic ideal across the Church. He valued the hidden prayer of the desert as a wellspring of the Church's strength.  _(Intention: For all who seek God in prayer and hidden fidelity.)_

**Day 7 — Day 7: Shepherd of his people:** Even from hiding, Athanasius shepherded his flock by letters, instruction, and unfailing care for their faith. He never abandoned the people of Alexandria, who loved him and welcomed him home with joy after each return.  _(Intention: For bishops and pastors, that they may guard their flocks with courage.)_

**Day 8 — Day 8: Defender against error:** Athanasius answered every subtle argument of the Arians with the plain witness of Scripture and the Church's worship of Christ. He shows us that sound doctrine guards the prayer and life of the faithful from being led astray.  _(Intention: For the grace to know my faith well and never be led into error.)_

**Day 9 — Day 9: Perseverance to the end:** After decades of struggle, Athanasius died at peace in Alexandria, having seen the tide begin to turn toward the truth he had defended. His life proclaims that perseverance in the faith, however costly, is never in vain.  _(Intention: For the grace of final perseverance in the faith of Christ.)_

**Closing prayer:** St. Athanasius, who endured exile and slander rather than betray the faith, teach me your steadfast love of the truth. Help me to adore the eternal Son, consubstantial with the Father, and to confess Him before the world. By your prayers, may I persevere in the faith of Nicaea until I see God face to face. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas publishes a complete 9-day novena to St. Athanasius. Each day opens with the Sign of the Cross ("In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen.") and a fixed thanksgiving: "Dear Lord, we thank You for giving us Your servant, St. Athanasius, as an example of holiness…" Each day then addresses St. Athanasius directly on a distinct theme of his life — Day 1: "St. Athanasius, you grew up with the benefit of devout Christian parents. They ensured that you received a strong education in the Faith, and you eagerly grew in your Faith and service to God." — followed by a petition ("Pray for me, that I may always seek ways to grow in holiness in my life…"), a space to mention personal intentions, the acclamation "St. Athanasius, pray for us!", and the closing Sign of the Cross.

The nine themes are: Day 1 — childhood and holiness; Day 2 — service as deacon; Day 3 — theological writings; Day 4 — preaching against heresy; Day 5 — holding fast through false accusations; Day 6 — perseverance through exile; Day 7 — fortitude amid trials; Day 8 — service as bishop; Day 9 — lifelong devotion to the Church.

## Fidelity note
Ember's novena is an original composition, not a copy of the PrayMoreNovenas text — the wording of every opening, daily, and closing prayer differs entirely. Both follow the same nine-day arc through the saint's life and share several themes (Incarnation/writings, defending against heresy, exile, the episcopate, perseverance), so they are thematically close though structured differently. Ember separates meditation from a single short intention and folds in the Our Father / Hail Mary / Glory Be, whereas PMN embeds petitions in each day's prayer and repeats a fixed thanksgiving. This is a faithful devotional treatment of St. Athanasius rather than a reproduction of any specific published source.
