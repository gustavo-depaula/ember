# Divine Mercy — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** (No single inline opening prayer; the flow opens with the Sign of the Cross, a heading, and the rubric "Offer today's intention and prayer, then pray the Divine Mercy Chaplet." Each day then carries its own intention + petition prayer.)

**Day 1 — For all mankind, especially sinners:** Jesus opens His Heart to every soul and invites us to trust in His mercy. Today we begin by placing the whole world before Him, especially those most wounded by sin.  _(Intention: For all mankind, especially sinners, that they may receive conversion and mercy.)_  (every day)

**Day 2 — For priests and religious:** The Lord gives shepherds and consecrated souls to nourish and guide His people. We pray that they may be strengthened in holiness, zeal, and fidelity.  _(Intention: For priests, deacons, and all religious, that they may be living witnesses of Divine Mercy.)_  (every day)

**Day 3 — For devout and faithful souls:** Faithful souls sustain the Church through hidden prayer and sacrifice. We ask that those who already seek God may persevere and draw many others toward His Heart.  _(Intention: For devout and faithful souls, that they may grow in trust, charity, and perseverance.)_  (every day)

**Day 4 — For those who do not believe in Christ and those who do not know Him:** Christ desires that every person know the truth of His love. Today we entrust to Him all who are far from faith, asking for light, healing, and the grace of encounter.  _(Intention: For those who do not believe in Christ and those who have not yet known Him.)_  (every day)

**Day 5 — For separated brethren:** The Heart of Jesus longs for unity among all who call upon His name. We pray for healing of divisions, mutual charity, and the full communion willed by Christ.  _(Intention: For separated brethren, that all Christians may be led into deeper unity in truth and love.)_  (every day)

**Day 6 — For meek and humble souls, and little children:** The meek and humble resemble the Heart of Jesus and are precious in His sight. We ask for childlike trust and a gentle spirit for ourselves and for all souls.  _(Intention: For meek and humble souls, and for little children, that they may remain under God's loving care.)_  (every day)

**Day 7 — For souls who especially venerate and glorify Divine Mercy:** Those who proclaim Divine Mercy become signs of hope in the world. We pray that their witness may spread confidence in Jesus and ignite works of charity.  _(Intention: For souls devoted to Divine Mercy, that they may be faithful messengers of trust and compassion.)_  (every day)

**Day 8 — For the souls in purgatory:** In charity we remember the faithful departed who await the vision of God. We commend them to the Precious Blood of Christ and to the maternal intercession of the Church.  _(Intention: For the souls in purgatory, that they may soon enter the joy of heaven.)_  (every day)

**Day 9 — For lukewarm souls:** Lukewarmness weakens love and dulls the desire for holiness. We ask Christ to rekindle in every soul the fire of divine charity and wholehearted conversion.  _(Intention: For lukewarm souls, that they may be renewed by grace and set ablaze with love for God.)_  (every day)

**Closing prayer:** Eternal God, in whom mercy is endless and the treasury of compassion inexhaustible, look kindly upon us and increase Your mercy in us, that in difficult moments we might not despair nor become despondent, but with great confidence submit ourselves to Your holy will, which is Love and Mercy itself. Amen.

(The flow also includes the full Divine Mercy Chaplet — Our Father, Hail Mary, Apostles' Creed, five decades of the "Eternal Father" and "For the sake of His sorrowful Passion" responses, and the threefold "Holy God" — before this closing prayer.)

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
Timing: "The Novena may be prayed any time, but Jesus asked that it especially be prayed beginning on Good Friday and ending the day before Low Sunday." Each day: an opening prayer specific to that day's intention, followed by the Divine Mercy Chaplet. Day-by-day intentions: (1) God's compassion toward sinners, asking Jesus to receive all into His "Most Compassionate Heart"; (2) grace to perform "worthy works of mercy"; (3) "faithful souls" as His inheritance; (4) pagan souls "who do not know" God; (5) heretics and schismatics drawn "into the unity of the Church"; (6) "meek and humble souls" and children who "bear the closest resemblance" to Christ; (7) souls "who glorify and venerate" His mercy; (8) souls in Purgatory "under Your just scrutiny"; (9) "lukewarm souls" through "the omnipotence of Your mercy."

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
Daily themes: (1) all mankind, especially sinners; (2) souls of priests and religious; (3) all devout and faithful souls; (4) those who don't believe in God or don't know Him; (5) souls separated from the Church; (6) meek, humble souls and little children; (7) souls who venerate and glorify Divine Mercy; (8) souls detained in Purgatory; (9) lukewarm souls. Each day: Trinitarian opening invocation, Jesus's daily intention quoted from St. Faustina's Diary, a prayer to Jesus, a prayer to the Eternal Father, a closing blessing, then the Divine Mercy Chaplet. Sample opening: "Most Merciful Jesus, whose very nature it is to have compassion on us and to forgive us, do not look upon our sins but upon our trust which we place in Your infinite goodness." Source noted as the copyrighted *Diary of St. Maria Faustina Kowalska* © 1987 Marian Fathers.

## Fidelity note
The nine daily intentions match the canonical Faustina novena exactly in order and theme (sinners, priests/religious, faithful souls, unbelievers, separated brethren, the meek/children, devotees of mercy, purgatory, lukewarm souls), and the structure faithfully pairs each day with the full Divine Mercy Chaplet, which is the defining element of the devotion. However, the meditations and petition prayers are Ember's own newly composed paraphrases rather than the verbatim prayers from St. Faustina's Diary — a deliberate choice, since the standard texts (e.g., "Today bring to Me all mankind, especially all sinners…") are under copyright by the Marian Fathers. The closing "Eternal God, in whom mercy is endless…" prayer is the one genuinely traditional concluding text and is reproduced accurately. Overall: structurally and thematically faithful, with original (non-copyright) wording standing in for the diary prayers.
