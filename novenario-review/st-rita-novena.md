# Novena to St. Rita — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O glorious St. Rita, faithful spouse, devoted mother, and patient widow, you embraced every cross with love for the crucified Lord. As I begin this novena, obtain for me a heart that trusts God in the darkest hour. Amen.

**Day 1 — Trust in God's plan:** From childhood St. Rita longed for the cloister, yet her parents gave her in marriage; she bowed to God's will without bitterness. She teaches us that the path we do not choose can still lead straight to holiness. _(Intention: For the grace to accept the life God has given me with trust.)_ (every day)

**Day 2 — Patience in a hard marriage:** For eighteen years St. Rita lived with a harsh and violent husband, answering his anger with gentleness and prayer. By her quiet constancy she softened his heart and won him at last to peace with God. _(Intention: For peace and conversion within troubled households.)_ (every day)

**Day 3 — Forgiveness of enemies:** When her husband was murdered, St. Rita forgave his killers and begged God to spare her sons from vengeance. She shows us that the chain of hatred is broken only by mercy freely given. _(Intention: For the grace to forgive those who have wronged me.)_ (every day)

**Day 4 — Surrender of her sons:** Fearing her sons would die avenging their father, St. Rita prayed they be taken rather than stain their souls; both died soon after, reconciled to God. Her sorrow teaches that we may entrust even our children wholly to Providence. _(Intention: For all parents anxious for the souls of their children.)_ (every day)

**Day 5 — Perseverance at the convent door:** Refused three times by the Augustinian sisters because of her past, St. Rita knocked until heaven itself carried her within the walls. She teaches us never to abandon a holy desire that meets closed doors. _(Intention: For perseverance when my good desires seem refused.)_ (every day)

**Day 6 — The wound of the thorn:** Praying before the crucifix, St. Rita asked to share Christ's suffering, and a thorn from His crown pierced her brow with a wound that remained until death. She invites us to love the Cross not from afar but in our own flesh. _(Intention: For the grace to unite my pains to the sufferings of Christ.)_ (every day)

**Day 7 — Hidden penance and prayer:** For forty years in the cloister St. Rita lived in fasting, vigil, and constant prayer, bearing her hidden wound without complaint. She reminds us that the holiest lives are often the least seen. _(Intention: For fidelity in the small, unseen duties of each day.)_ (every day)

**Day 8 — The rose in winter:** Near death, St. Rita asked for a rose from her family's garden, and one bloomed amid the January frost. The miracle shows that with God no situation is so barren that grace cannot make it flower. _(Intention: For hope in causes that seem entirely lost.)_ (every day)

**Day 9 — Advocate of the impossible:** In every trial St. Rita kept her gaze on the crucified Lord, and so the Church honors her as patroness of impossible causes. She assures us that no prayer offered in faith is ever truly beyond God's reach. _(Intention: For the particular grace I now place in St. Rita's hands.)_ (every day)

**Closing prayer:** O St. Rita, advocate of impossible causes, present my petitions before the throne of God. By the wound you bore from the crown of thorns, teach me to suffer with patience and to forgive without measure, until I rejoice with you in heaven. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas has a St. Rita novena (https://www.praymorenovenas.com/st-rita-novena-2). Each day opens with a Trinitarian blessing, an appeal to the Heavenly Father referencing St. Rita's holiness and her stigmata wound from the crown of thorns, a request to understand the paschal mystery through her example, and a day-specific petition, then a fixed intercession ("St. Rita, you are the patron of the impossible...") and a daily closing invocation. Daily closing phrases there: Day 1 compassion for the suffering of Christ; Day 2 generous in forgiving; Day 3 heroic in virtue; Day 4 our protector; Day 5 example of patience; Day 6 close to God through prayer; Day 7 example of Christian love; Day 8 full of hope; Day 9 steadfast in faith.

## Fidelity note
Ember's wording is original, not copied from PrayMoreNovenas, but the two are strongly aligned in substance: both build on St. Rita's biography (hard marriage, forgiveness, the thorn wound, patroness of the impossible) and use a fixed-opening + daily-theme structure. Ember's day themes track her life chronologically and capture the same pillars PrayMoreNovenas hits (forgiveness, patience, the wound of the thorn, hope in impossible causes), with Ember adding well-attested episodes (the convent door, the rose in winter). A faithful, well-grounded original composition; the FishEaters text could not be verified.
