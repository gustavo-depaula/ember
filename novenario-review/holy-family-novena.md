# Novena to the Holy Family — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O Holy Family of Nazareth, Jesus, Mary, and Joseph, in whose humble home heaven dwelt upon earth, I come before you as I begin this novena. Look with love upon my family, hear the petition I bring, and teach us to live united in faith, charity, and peace. Amen.

**Day 1 — The home as a school of holiness:** In the quiet house of Nazareth the Son of God learned to walk, to speak, and to pray at His mother's side. The Holy Family shows us that holiness is forged not chiefly in great deeds but in the daily love of an ordinary home. _(Intention: For my home to become a school of holiness.)_

**Day 2 — Mary, mother of the household:** Mary kept the house of Nazareth with a heart full of grace, pondering all things and serving without complaint. She teaches every mother and homemaker that humble service done in love is precious in the eyes of God. _(Intention: For all mothers, that they may serve their families with love.)_

**Day 3 — Joseph, the just man and protector:** St. Joseph guarded Jesus and Mary with a silent, faithful strength, providing for them by the work of his hands. He is the model of every father, showing that true headship is humble service and self-forgetful protection. _(Intention: For all fathers, that they may protect and provide in faith.)_

**Day 4 — Jesus obedient to His parents:** Though He was Lord of all, Jesus went down to Nazareth and was obedient to Mary and Joseph. He sanctifies the obedience of children and teaches us that submission offered in love is never small in the sight of God. _(Intention: For children to honor and obey their parents in the Lord.)_

**Day 5 — The sanctity of work:** At Joseph's bench Jesus labored with wood and tools, hallowing the daily work of human hands. The Holy Family teaches us that honest work, offered to God, is itself a path of holiness and a share in His creating love. _(Intention: For the grace to offer my daily work to God.)_

**Day 6 — The hidden life:** For thirty years Jesus lived hidden in Nazareth, known to none as the Savior of the world. The Holy Family teaches us the value of a hidden life of fidelity, where God sees and treasures what the world overlooks. _(Intention: For faithfulness in the hidden, unseen duties of each day.)_

**Day 7 — Trials borne together:** The Holy Family knew poverty, exile in Egypt, and the sword that pierced Mary's heart, yet they remained united in love. They teach us that families are bound together not by the absence of suffering but by faith that endures it side by side. _(Intention: For families to remain united through every trial.)_

**Day 8 — A house of prayer:** In Nazareth prayer was woven through the ordinary hours, and the family went up together to worship God. They teach us to make our homes places of prayer, where God is honored at table, at work, and at rest. _(Intention: For my family to pray together faithfully.)_

**Day 9 — Jesus, Mary, and Joseph, I give you my heart:** The faithful have long prayed, 'Jesus, Mary, and Joseph, I give you my heart and my soul.' On this final day we entrust our whole family to their care, asking them to lead us all home to the family of heaven. _(Intention: For my whole family to be one day united in heaven.)_

Each day also prays one Our Father, one Hail Mary, and one Glory Be.

**Closing prayer:** Jesus, Mary, and Joseph, I give you my heart and my home. Watch over all who dwell beneath my roof, keep us faithful to God and gentle with one another, and grant that we may one day be united forever in the family of heaven. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
FishEaters gives a brief novena of repeated invocations: "Jesus, Mary, and Joseph, bless us and grant us the grace of loving Holy Church, as we are bound to do, above every earthly thing, and of ever showing forth our love by the witness of our deeds. Recite one Our Father, one Hail Mary, and one Glory be." The pattern repeats five times with different graces requested, then closes by asking a special favor: "Jesus, Mary, and Joseph, we ask in particular this special favor..." This is the same prayer prayed identically each of the nine days — not a nine-meditation cycle.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
Opens "In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen." with the daily invocation: "Holy Family, you were close to our Heavenly Father on Earth, uniting your wills to His, and are now close to Him in Heaven. I ask that you intercede with Him on my behalf." Each day takes a distinct theme/intention: Day 1 trust in God's provision (journey to Bethlehem); Day 2 protection for families in exile (flight to Egypt); Day 3 working parents (Joseph's labor); Day 4 those who work in the home (Mary's vocation); Day 5 children's spiritual growth; Day 6 anxious parents (Jesus in the Temple); Day 7 vocations (Cana); Day 8 family support in trials; Day 9 families facing death and suffering. Closing each day: "Holy Family, you who once walked this Earth, accompany my family as we strive to follow your example by walking in your footsteps. Amen."

## Fidelity note
Ember's novena is an original composition, not a transcription of either source. It shares the universal themes both sources draw on — Nazareth's hidden life, the model roles of Mary, Joseph, and Jesus, work, trials, and the family's heavenward destiny — and its day-by-day structure mirrors PrayMoreNovenas more than the single-prayer FishEaters form. But the meditations and intentions are freshly written, organized around virtues (school of holiness, sanctity of work, hidden life, house of prayer) rather than PrayMoreNovenas' biographical episodes (Bethlehem, Egypt, Cana). The closing prayer deliberately echoes the traditional aspiration "Jesus, Mary, and Joseph, I give you my heart" that FishEaters and broader devotion both use. Faithful in spirit and tradition; independent in wording.
