# Our Lady of Guadalupe — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** Most Blessed Virgin of Guadalupe, Mother of the true God and our tender Mother, I place this novena in your hands. Intercede for me before your Son, Jesus Christ, and help me to welcome His will with faith, humility, and trust. Amen.

**Day 1 — Mother of the true God:** At Tepeyac, Mary revealed herself as the Mother of the true God through whom we live. She invites us to trust the living Lord in every need. Her presence reminds us that God draws close to His children with tenderness. _(Intention: For deeper faith in God and trust in His providence.)_

**Day 2 — Mother who comes to the humble:** Mary chose Saint Juan Diego, a simple and faithful man, to carry her message. God delights in working through little ones. She teaches us to offer ourselves with simplicity and obedience. _(Intention: For humility, obedience, and fidelity to God's call.)_

**Day 3 — Mother of consolation:** Mary spoke with maternal tenderness to Juan Diego in his sorrow and fear. She does not ignore our pain, but accompanies us in it. Under her mantle we find comfort and peace in trials. _(Intention: For the grieving, the anxious, and all who need consolation.)_

**Day 4 — Mother who gives signs of hope:** In winter, roses bloomed on Tepeyac as a heavenly sign. God can make grace blossom where we expect barrenness. Mary teaches us to hope even when situations seem impossible. _(Intention: For hope in difficult situations and trust in God's timing.)_

**Day 5 — Mother whose image calls to conversion:** The tilma image speaks without words, drawing hearts to Christ. Mary's mission is always to lead us to repentance, faith, and holiness. True devotion bears fruit in conversion of life. _(Intention: For conversion of heart and perseverance in confession and prayer.)_

**Day 6 — Mother and protectress of families:** Our Lady of Guadalupe gathers families under her mantle and teaches them to pray together. In a wounded world, Christian homes are called to be places of charity and fidelity. Mary strengthens spouses, parents, and children in love. _(Intention: For families, marriages, and healing of domestic wounds.)_

**Day 7 — Mother of the vulnerable:** In the Guadalupe image, Mary appears as a mother carrying new life. She teaches us to defend the dignity of every human person, especially the poor, the unborn, and those forgotten by society. _(Intention: For respect for life from conception to natural death.)_

**Day 8 — Mother of the Church in the Americas:** As Patroness of the Americas, Our Lady of Guadalupe calls the Church to unity and missionary zeal. She gathers diverse peoples into one family in Christ and encourages us to witness with charity and truth. _(Intention: For the Church in the Americas, for missionaries, and for new evangelization.)_

**Day 9 — Mother who leads us always to Jesus:** Mary's final work in every heart is to bring us to her Son. At Cana she said, 'Do whatever He tells you,' and this remains her maternal counsel. We end this novena by entrusting our lives to Jesus through her intercession. _(Intention: For final perseverance in faith and full fidelity to Jesus Christ.)_

**Closing prayer:** Our Lady of Guadalupe, Mother of the Americas, pray for us. Protect our families, defend the vulnerable, strengthen the Church, and lead all peoples to your Son, Jesus Christ. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
FishEaters gives the traditional Guadalupe novena (prayed Dec 3–11). It venerates Mary as "the Virgin Mother of the true God for whom we live, the Creator of all the world, Maker of heaven and earth," with the petitioner spiritually kneeling before the image on Juan Diego's tilma and presenting an intention. It echoes Our Lady's words to Juan Diego — that she is "a merciful mother to thee and to all thy people who love me and trust in me," promising to listen to lamentations and console all sorrows. The request is granted "if this should be the will of God," with the petitioner committing to bear witness to her love, compassion, help, and protection; it closes with three "Our Lady of Guadalupe, pray for us" and three Hail Marys.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas opens each day with the Sign of the Cross and closes with the Our Father, Hail Mary, and Glory Be. It uses nine per-day petitions to "Dearest Lady of Guadalupe": Day 1 "teach me your ways of gentleness and strength"; Day 2 "a lively faith to do your Son's holy will always"; Day 3 strength to "walk valiantly amid the sharp thorns strewn across my pathway"; Day 4 "a fortified will to imitate your divine Son's charity"; Day 5 "pardon of all my sins, abundant graces to serve your Son more faithfully"; Day 6 multiplication of "priestly vocations and religious houses"; Day 7 that "parents live a holy life and educate their children in a Christian manner"; Day 8 "grace to fulfill the duties of my state in life with faithfulness and constancy"; Day 9 "the happiness of seeing her face to face in heaven."

## Fidelity note
Ember's Guadalupe novena is an original composition rather than a transcription of either source, but it is strongly faithful to the Guadalupe event and its theology. The opening prayer deliberately echoes the signature FishEaters/Nican Mopohua phrasing — "Mother of the true God" — and the meditations are built squarely on the apparition narrative (Tepeyac, Juan Diego's humility and sorrow, winter roses, the tilma image, Patroness of the Americas). Its day-themes overlap with PMN's concerns (humility/obedience, conversion and pardon, families and the education of children, fidelity to one's state in life, perseverance to heaven), but wording is paraphrased and reorganized, and it adds a distinctly modern pro-life "Mother of the vulnerable" day grounded in the image's depiction of Mary as expectant. The closing matches the common "Our Lady of Guadalupe, pray for us" refrain. Faithful in event, imagery, and theme; not verbatim.
