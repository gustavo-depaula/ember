# Our Lady Aparecida — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** Most Holy Virgin, Mother Aparecida, I place myself under your mantle and ask your help in this novena. Present my intentions to Jesus and teach me to trust in God's providence with a humble and faithful heart. Amen.

**Day 1 — Mary, found among the humble:** The image of Our Lady Aparecida was found by simple fishermen. God often reveals His mercy through what the world considers small. Mary teaches us to welcome grace with humility and grateful faith. _(Intention: For a humble heart that recognizes God's presence in ordinary life.)_

**Day 2 — Mother who gathers what is broken:** The body and head of the image were found separately and made whole again. Under Mary's care, what is divided can be reconciled. She helps us seek unity in our families, communities, and hearts. _(Intention: For reconciliation in families and healing of relationships.)_

**Day 3 — Mother of Divine Providence:** After invoking Mary, the fishermen's empty nets became abundant. She points us to God's providence, which never fails those who trust Him. In spiritual and material needs, we learn to depend on the Lord. _(Intention: For all who lack work, bread, or daily necessities.)_

**Day 4 — Mother close to the poor:** Our Lady Aparecida is beloved by the poor and forgotten. Mary stands with those who suffer and reminds the Church to serve with tenderness and justice. In her school we learn charity that is concrete and faithful. _(Intention: For the poor, the abandoned, and all who seek dignity and hope.)_

**Day 5 — Mother and Patroness of Brazil:** As Patroness of Brazil, Our Lady of Aparecida gathers a diverse people under one maternal mantle. She calls the nation to conversion, peace, and fidelity to Christ. True renewal begins with prayer and holiness. _(Intention: For Brazil: for peace, justice, and fidelity to the Gospel.)_

**Day 6 — Mother of the Church:** At the foot of the Cross, Jesus gave Mary as Mother to all disciples. She intercedes for the Church so that we may remain united in truth and charity. Through her, we ask for faithful shepherds and holy vocations. _(Intention: For the Church, the Pope, bishops, priests, and all consecrated life.)_

**Day 7 — Mother of families:** Mary lived the hidden life of Nazareth with Jesus and Joseph. She understands the joys and trials of home life. Entrusting our households to her, we ask for patience, forgiveness, and persevering love. _(Intention: For families, marriages, children, and all who long for a holy home.)_

**Day 8 — Mother of consolation and healing:** Many faithful turn to Our Lady Aparecida in illness and affliction. Mary does not abandon her children in suffering; she leads them to the Heart of Jesus, source of mercy and healing. _(Intention: For the sick, the grieving, and all who need consolation.)_

**Day 9 — Mother who leads us to Jesus:** At Cana, Mary said, 'Do whatever He tells you.' Every true Marian devotion ends in deeper discipleship of Christ. At the end of this novena, we entrust our lives to Jesus through Mary's maternal hands. _(Intention: For perseverance in faith and total fidelity to Jesus Christ.)_

**Closing prayer:** Our Lady of Aparecida, Queen and Patroness of Brazil, pray for us. Protect our families, console the afflicted, strengthen the Church, and lead us always to your Son, Jesus Christ. Amen.

## Fidelity note
This is an original composition for a Brazilian devotion that neither English source covers. Its historical anchor is sound and well-told — the 1717 finding of the image by fishermen on the Paraíba do Sul (body then head, reunited; the abundant catch) drives Days 1–3, matching the traditional Aparecida narrative. The remaining days develop standard, doctrinally safe Marian themes (poor, Patroness of Brazil, Mother of the Church, families, the sick, leading to Jesus at Cana) appropriate to the title. Since the pt-BR side is the natural primary language for this devotion, the comparison baseline is internal; the text reads as a faithful, catechetically careful modern novena rather than a transcription of any specific published Aparecida novena.
