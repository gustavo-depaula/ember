# Novena to the Holy Face of Jesus — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O adorable Face of Jesus, marred by our sins yet radiant with love, I bow before You as I begin this novena. Receive my poor desire to console You and make reparation, and through Your suffering Face obtain for me the grace I now implore. Amen.

**Day 1 — The Face foretold by Isaiah:** The prophet Isaiah foresaw the Servant who would have no beauty to attract us, his face so marred as to be scarcely human. We adore in that disfigured Face the depth of a love that took our sins upon itself. _(Intention: For the grace to see God's love in Christ's suffering.)_

**Day 2 — The veil of Veronica:** On the road to Calvary a woman pressed through the crowd to wipe the Lord's face, and He left His image upon her cloth. Veronica teaches us that the smallest act of compassion offered to Christ is never forgotten by Him. _(Intention: For the courage to comfort Christ in the suffering.)_

**Day 3 — Reparation for blasphemy:** Our Lord revealed to Sr. Marie of St. Peter His sorrow at the blasphemies hurled against His name. He asks us to make reparation, to take up the defense of His honor where others curse Him, offering praise in place of insult. _(Intention: In reparation for blasphemies against the holy name of God.)_

**Day 4 — The Golden Arrow:** The prayer called the Golden Arrow blesses, praises, and adores the most holy name of God, wounding the Heart of Jesus with consolation rather than sorrow. We make it our own, sending up praise that pierces heaven with love. _(Intention: For the grace to praise God's name in reparation and love.)_

**Day 5 — Consoling the suffering Christ:** In Gethsemane an angel came to strengthen Jesus in His agony, and He still seeks souls who will console Him. Devotion to the Holy Face is a tender answer to that longing, a heart offered to comfort the abandoned Lord. _(Intention: To console the Heart of Jesus abandoned by sinners.)_

**Day 6 — The light of His countenance:** The psalmist prayed that the light of God's face would shine upon us, and in Jesus that face is revealed to the world. Even marred by suffering, His countenance radiates a mercy that heals all who gaze on it with faith. _(Intention: For the light of Christ's face to heal my soul.)_

**Day 7 — The Holy Face and St. Thérèse:** St. Thérèse of Lisieux took the Holy Face into her very name, finding in it the hidden, humble Christ whom she loved. She teaches us to seek the Lord not in splendor but in His humility, and to love Him most where He is least understood. _(Intention: For the grace to love the hidden and humble Christ.)_

**Day 8 — Reparation for impiety and the profaned Lord's Day:** Our Lord lamented to Sr. Marie of St. Peter the neglect of the Lord's Day and the spread of impiety in the world. He asks us to offer Him faithful worship and devout Sundays as a balm upon these wounds. _(Intention: In reparation for impiety and the profanation of Sundays.)_

**Day 9 — Imprint Your image on my soul:** As Christ left His face upon Veronica's veil, He desires to imprint His likeness upon every soul that loves Him. On this last day we ask to be conformed to Him, that He who looked on us in mercy may at last be seen in us. _(Intention: For the grace to be conformed to the image of Christ.)_

Each day also prays one Our Father, one Hail Mary, and one Glory Be.

**Closing prayer:** O Holy Face of Jesus, sweet and merciful, I offer You the homage of my whole heart in reparation for the blasphemies that wound You. May Your sacred countenance be my light in this life and my joy in eternity; imprint Your image upon my soul. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
FishEaters gives the classic Tours-tradition novena prayed identically each day. Its opening: "O Lord Jesus Christ, in presenting ourselves before Thine adorable Face, to ask of Thee the graces of which we stand in most need, we beseech Thee above all, to grant us that interior disposition of never refusing at any time what Thou requirest of us by Thy holy commandments and divine inspirations." A central petition: "Adorable Face of Jesus, my only love, my light, and my life, grant that I may know Thee, love Thee and serve Thee alone..." and the offering: "Eternal Father, I offer Thee the adorable Face of Thy Beloved Son for the honor and glory of Thy Name, for the conversion of sinners and the salvation of the dying." It is one fixed set of six prayer sections, not nine distinct meditations.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
A nine-day cycle that walks the Face of Jesus through the events of His life (approved by Leo XIII, 1885). Daily opening: "Lord, I desire to seek thy Face; do not Thou repel me far from it on account of my sins... Let the light of Thy Face shine upon me." Days: 1 Bethlehem; 2 among the people of Judea; 3 Mount Tabor (Transfiguration); 4 Gethsemane; 5 the house of Caiaphas; 6 Pilate's praetorium; 7 the road to Calvary (Veronica); 8 the Cross; 9 the Resurrection. Daily closing: "God all powerful and merciful, grant we entreat Thee, that, venerating the Face of Thy Christ, disfigured in his Passion because of our sins, we may deserve to contemplate it eternally in the splendour of the glory of Heaven."

## Fidelity note
Ember's version is an original composition that draws faithfully on the authentic Holy Face tradition rather than copying either source. It correctly grounds the devotion in Sr. Marie of St. Peter, the Golden Arrow, reparation for blasphemy and Sunday profanation, and the Veronica/Isaiah imagery — the historical core both sources share. Its nine-day arc is thematic (foretelling, reparation, consolation, conformity) and differs structurally from PrayMoreNovenas' chronological walk through Christ's life and from FishEaters' single fixed prayer. The closing echoes the tradition's reparation language without transcribing the Tours prayers. Theologically and historically accurate; wording independent.
