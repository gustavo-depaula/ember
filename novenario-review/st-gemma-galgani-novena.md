# St. Gemma Galgani — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O St. Gemma Galgani, who loved the crucified Jesus with a heart on fire and gave your whole life to His Passion, look kindly upon me from heaven. Take my petition into your wounded hands and lay it at the feet of your beloved Lord. Amen.

**Day 1 — Day 1: Orphaned and trusting:** Gemma lost her mother as a small child and her father in her teens, left poor and burdened with her younger siblings. In every loss she clung to God as her true and only Father.  _(Intention: For orphans and all who have lost those they love.)_

**Day 2 — Day 2: Love of the Crucified:** From her youth Gemma was drawn to the wounds of the crucified Jesus and could not look upon a crucifix without tears of love. The Passion was the book in which she read God's love for sinners.  _(Intention: For the grace to love the crucified Christ more deeply.)_

**Day 3 — Day 3: The stigmata:** On the eve of the Sacred Heart in 1899, the wounds of Christ appeared in Gemma's hands, feet, and side, returning each Thursday and Friday. She bore in her body what she carried first in her heart: the Passion of her Lord.  _(Intention: That I may unite my pains to the wounds of Christ.)_

**Day 4 — Day 4: Victim of love:** Gemma offered herself as a victim of love for the conversion of sinners and the souls in purgatory. She desired not suffering for its own sake but to console the wounded Heart of Jesus.  _(Intention: For the conversion of sinners and the holy souls in purgatory.)_

**Day 5 — Day 5: Friend of her guardian angel:** Gemma spoke with her guardian angel as with a familiar companion, who carried her messages and corrected her faults. Her trust reminds us that heaven is near to those who walk in childlike faith.  _(Intention: For a deeper devotion to my own guardian angel.)_

**Day 6 — Day 6: The closed convent door:** Gemma longed to be a Passionist nun, yet her frail health closed that door to her again and again. She surrendered her dearest desire into God's hands, finding her cloister in His will.  _(Intention: For the grace to surrender my dearest hopes to God's plan.)_

**Day 7 — Day 7: Trust amid temptation:** Gemma endured fierce assaults of the devil and dark trials of the soul, yet held fast to Jesus and Mary. She is a sure helper for all who are tempted and afflicted in spirit.  _(Intention: For all who are tempted, that they may not lose hope.)_

**Day 8 — Day 8: Patience in sickness:** Worn by tuberculosis, Gemma bore long months of pain with serene patience, asking only to do God's will. Her sickbed became an altar of loving sacrifice.  _(Intention: For the sick and dying, that they may suffer with patience and hope.)_

**Day 9 — Day 9: 'Jesus, I commend my soul':** On Holy Saturday of 1903, after long suffering, Gemma died at twenty-five with her eyes fixed on the crucifix. She passed into the embrace of the Lord she had loved through every wound.  _(Intention: For the grace of a holy death, with my heart fixed on Christ.)_

**Closing prayer:** St. Gemma, victim of love and friend of the suffering, obtain for me the grace I seek in this novena, if it serve God's glory and my salvation. Above all, teach me to love the Passion of Christ and to trust His will in every cross. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas has a St. Gemma Galgani novena, but its structure differs from Ember's. Each day opens and closes with the Sign of the Cross ("In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen.") and "St. Gemma Galgani, pray for us!" Every day follows the pattern: a thanksgiving to God ("Dear Lord, we thank You for giving us St. Gemma Galgani as an example of holiness. Help us to imitate the love of You she showed by remaining faithful amidst…"), an address to St. Gemma recalling an episode of her life, a request for her intercession, and space for a personal petition.

Its day-by-day themes are: Day 1 — perseverance through chronic health problems; Day 2 — faithfulness amid family loss; Day 3 — faith through grief; Day 4 — submission to God's will regarding her religious vocation; Day 5 — perseverance despite ridicule; Day 6 — devotion to Christ's Passion; Day 7 — patient endurance of suffering; Day 8 — charity toward the poor; Day 9 — wholehearted service to God.

## Fidelity note
Ember's novena is not a transcription of any single published source: FishEaters has no St. Gemma novena at all, and the PrayMoreNovenas text uses different wording, a different opening/closing, and a different sequence of daily themes. Ember's prayers and nine meditations are original compositions, though they are faithful to St. Gemma's life and to the broad devotional themes (her love of the Passion, the stigmata, victimhood of love, illness, holy death) that the PrayMoreNovenas version also touches. This should be treated as an original, devotionally sound novena inspired by — not copied from — the common sources, with several meditation topics (orphanhood, the guardian angel, the closed convent door) that PrayMoreNovenas does not emphasize.
