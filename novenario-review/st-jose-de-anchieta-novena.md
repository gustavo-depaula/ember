# Novena to St. José de Anchieta — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O holy José de Anchieta, missionary who carried Christ across the seas to a new land, look upon me as I begin this novena. Win for me your zeal for souls and your tender love for Our Lady, that I may labor for God wherever He sends me. Amen.

**Day 1 — The call across the sea:** Still a young Jesuit, José de Anchieta left the Canary Islands and crossed the ocean to a land he had never seen, trusting wholly in God. He teaches us that the Lord's call may lead far from home, and that love makes the journey light. _(Intention: For courage to follow God's call wherever it leads.)_ (every day)

**Day 2 — Founder of São Paulo:** In 1554 Anchieta helped raise the mission school of São Paulo de Piratininga, laying the spiritual foundation of a future city. He shows us that great works of God often begin in poverty, in a humble hut on a high plain. _(Intention: For all who build the Church in poor and forgotten places.)_ (every day)

**Day 3 — Learning the people's tongue:** Anchieta mastered the Tupi language and wrote its first grammar so the Gospel could be preached in the people's own words. He teaches us that true love takes the trouble to understand others as they are. _(Intention: For missionaries who labor to share the faith in every culture.)_ (every day)

**Day 4 — The poem in the sand:** Held as a hostage among the Tamoio, Anchieta composed a long Latin poem in praise of the Blessed Virgin, writing the verses each morning on the wet sand of the shore. He shows us that devotion to Mary can flower even in captivity and fear. _(Intention: For a deeper and more constant love for Our Lady.)_ (every day)

**Day 5 — Peacemaker among nations:** During the conflict with the Tamoio, Anchieta risked his life as a peacemaker, offering himself for the safety of others. He teaches us that the servant of Christ is sent to reconcile, not to conquer. _(Intention: For peace among peoples divided by enmity.)_ (every day)

**Day 6 — Healer and comforter:** Anchieta tended the sick with his own hands and was credited with many wonders of healing among the native peoples. He reminds us that the love of God is shown as much in care for the body as in care for the soul. _(Intention: For the sick and for those who care for them.)_ (every day)

**Day 7 — Teacher and dramatist:** To teach the faith, Anchieta wrote songs, catechisms, and sacred plays performed in Tupi and Portuguese. He shows us that the truths of God may be taught with beauty, and that art can be put wholly at His service. _(Intention: For teachers and for all who serve God through their gifts.)_ (every day)

**Day 8 — A life poured out:** For more than forty years Anchieta walked thousands of miles, often barefoot and ailing, never sparing himself for the souls entrusted to him. He teaches us that holiness is the patient gift of self, spent day after day. _(Intention: For perseverance in serving others without counting the cost.)_ (every day)

**Day 9 — Apostle of Brazil:** At his death the people mourned the loss of a father, and the Church now honors him as the Apostle of Brazil. He assures us that a life given wholly to God leaves a harvest that endures for centuries. _(Intention: For the particular grace I now entrust to St. José de Anchieta.)_ (every day)

**Closing prayer:** O St. José de Anchieta, Apostle of Brazil, present my petitions to the Lord for whom you spent your whole life. Teach me to love those of every tongue and people, and to seek God's glory above my own comfort, until I praise Him with you forever. Amen.

## Fidelity note
St. José de Anchieta (the Jesuit "Apostle of Brazil," canonized 2014) has no English-language novena on FishEaters or PrayMoreNovenas, so there is no comparison text. Ember's novena is an original composition that hews closely to the well-documented facts of his life: the voyage from the Canary Islands, the 1554 founding of São Paulo de Piratininga, his Tupi grammar, the Marian Latin poem composed on the sand during his Tamoio captivity, his peacemaking, healing, sacred plays, and the title Apostle of Brazil. The biography is accurate and the themes are devotionally apt; as an original work there is nothing to diverge from.
