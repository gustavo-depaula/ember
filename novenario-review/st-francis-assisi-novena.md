# St. Francis of Assisi — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** St. Francis of Assisi, poor and humble lover of the poor and humble Christ, look upon me with your fatherly kindness. Teach me to seek God above every earthly treasure and to follow the Gospel without reserve. Amen.

**Day 1 — Renouncing the world:** Before the bishop and his own father, Francis stripped off his fine clothes and gave them back, declaring that God alone was now his Father. He shows that true freedom begins when we let go of what we cannot keep.  _(Intention: For the grace to let go of what keeps me from God.)_  (every day)

**Day 2 — Lady Poverty:** Francis wedded himself to Lady Poverty, owning nothing so that he might possess Christ entirely. He found in having nothing a deep joy, for the poor man trusts in God's providence for his daily bread.  _(Intention: For a spirit of holy poverty and trust in God's providence.)_  (every day)

**Day 3 — Rebuild My Church:** Praying before the crucifix of San Damiano, Francis heard Christ say, 'Rebuild My Church, which is falling into ruin.' He began with stones and mortar, then understood that Christ meant the living temple of His people.  _(Intention: For the renewal of the Church and my own part in building her up.)_  (every day)

**Day 4 — Love of all creation:** In the Canticle of the Creatures, Francis praised God through Brother Sun, Sister Moon, and all created things. He saw the whole world as a family bound together in praise of its one Creator.  _(Intention: For the grace to praise God in the beauty of His creation.)_  (every day)

**Day 5 — The crib at Greccio:** At Greccio, Francis arranged a manger with hay and a living ox and ass, that the people might gaze upon the poverty of the newborn King. From his love sprang the Christmas crèche cherished by the whole Church.  _(Intention: For a tender love of the poverty and humility of the Christ Child.)_  (every day)

**Day 6 — Embracing the leper:** Once filled with disgust at lepers, Francis dismounted, embraced one, and found in that act the sweetness of conversion. He learned that the Lord is met most surely in those whom the world casts aside.  _(Intention: For the grace to embrace the suffering and the outcast with love.)_  (every day)

**Day 7 — Instrument of peace:** Francis greeted everyone with 'Peace and all good,' and even crossed enemy lines to speak peacefully with the Sultan. The prayer attributed to him still teaches us to sow love where there is hatred and pardon where there is injury.  _(Intention: For the grace to be an instrument of God's peace among others.)_  (every day)

**Day 8 — The stigmata:** On Mount La Verna, in deep prayer, Francis received in his hands, feet, and side the very wounds of the crucified Christ. His love had so conformed him to Jesus that his body itself was sealed with the Passion.  _(Intention: For the grace to be conformed to the crucified love of Christ.)_  (every day)

**Day 9 — Sister Death:** Dying, Francis welcomed 'Sister Bodily Death' as part of God's good creation and asked to be laid upon the bare earth. He met his end singing praise, showing that for the friend of God death is a homeward gate.  _(Intention: For the grace to meet death in peace, singing God's praise.)_  (every day)

**Closing prayer:** St. Francis, seraphic father marked with the wounds of Jesus, carry my prayers to the Lord whom you loved with all your heart. Obtain for me the grace I seek and make of me, like you, an instrument of His peace. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PrayMoreNovenas opens each day with the Sign of the Cross and a fixed invocation ("Holy Saint Francis, through your kind intercession we seek holiness above all things. We seek the faith, hope and love that allowed you to joyfully renounce the pleasures of earthly life and radically follow our Lord Jesus Christ"), followed by space for intentions and a distinct daily meditation. Its daily themes: 1 renouncing possessions, 2 trust in Divine Providence, 3 peacemaking and forgiveness, 4 care of creation, 5 surrender to God's will, 6 dedicating one's gifts to God, 7 mercy toward the poor, 8 overcoming doubt, 9 freedom from fear and anxiety.

## Fidelity note
Ember's novena is its own composition but tracks the well-known Franciscan biography closely and overlaps substantially with PrayMore's themes — poverty/renunciation (days 1–2), peacemaking (day 7), love of creation (day 4) all line up, while Ember adds historically grounded episodes PrayMore omits (San Damiano "Rebuild My Church," the Greccio crib, embracing the leper, the stigmata, Sister Death). The wording is original rather than paraphrased, and the framing is more narrative/biographical than PrayMore's virtue-petition format. The FishEaters source could not be checked.
