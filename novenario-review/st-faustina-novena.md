# St. Faustina — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** St. Faustina, faithful secretary of Divine Mercy, who hid the splendor of God's love beneath a poor religious habit, draw near to me now. Teach me to trust Jesus as you did, holding nothing back, and obtain for me the grace I seek in this novena. Amen.

**Day 1 — Hidden holiness:** St. Faustina spent her days in convent kitchens and gardens, unknown to the world, yet there she received the greatest of revelations. She teaches us that sanctity is not found in grand deeds but in humble fidelity to ordinary duties done for love of God.  _(Intention: For the grace to serve God faithfully in the small, hidden duties of each day.)_  (every day)

**Day 2 — Total trust in Jesus:** At Christ's command, St. Faustina had the words 'Jesus, I trust in You' inscribed beneath His Image. She lived these words in the darkest hours, surrendering her fears, her illness, and her future entirely into His hands.  _(Intention: For the grace to abandon all my anxieties into the merciful hands of Jesus.)_  (every day)

**Day 3 — The Diary of mercy:** Out of obedience, St. Faustina recorded in her Diary the words and graces Jesus gave her, though she felt unworthy of so great a task. Through those pages the Lord still speaks to countless souls of His unfathomable mercy.  _(Intention: For docility to God's will, even when it asks more than we feel able to give.)_  (every day)

**Day 4 — Apostle of Divine Mercy:** Jesus called St. Faustina the secretary and apostle of His mercy, charged to make it known to the whole world. She accepted this mission with joy, longing for every sinner to discover the ocean of mercy that awaits the contrite heart.  _(Intention: For the zeal to make God's mercy known to those who have lost hope.)_  (every day)

**Day 5 — Love for the suffering:** St. Faustina bore the long agony of tuberculosis with patience, offering her pain for the conversion of sinners. She saw in suffering not a curse but a hidden treasure united to the Passion of her Lord.  _(Intention: For the grace to bear my sufferings patiently and offer them for the salvation of souls.)_  (every day)

**Day 6 — Mercy in deed and word:** Jesus asked St. Faustina to show mercy in deed, in word, and in prayer, withholding it from no one. She understood that to receive God's mercy we must first become merciful toward others.  _(Intention: For a heart that is merciful to all I meet, in action, in speech, and in prayer.)_  (every day)

**Day 7 — Childlike simplicity:** St. Faustina approached God with the confidence of a little child, certain of His tenderness toward the lowly. She teaches us that the soul most pleasing to Jesus is the one that trusts simply, without calculation or pride.  _(Intention: For the grace of a simple, trusting heart before our heavenly Father.)_  (every day)

**Day 8 — Devotion to the Eucharist:** St. Faustina drew all her strength from Jesus present in the Blessed Sacrament, where the same mercy flows from His Heart. Before the tabernacle she found the courage to fulfill the great mission entrusted to her.  _(Intention: For a deeper love of Jesus truly present in the Holy Eucharist.)_  (every day)

**Day 9 — The hour of mercy:** Jesus revealed to St. Faustina the three o'clock hour as the hour of His death and of great mercy, when grace flows for the whole world. She invites us to pause in that hour and immerse our needs in the merciful Heart of the Savior.  _(Intention: For perseverance in trusting God's mercy until my last breath.)_  (every day)

**Closing prayer:** St. Faustina, apostle of the merciful Heart of Jesus, who promised that nothing is too great for the soul that trusts, intercede for me before the throne of mercy. May I, like you, repeat in every joy and every sorrow, 'Jesus, I trust in You,' until His mercy is my eternal home. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source
No FishEaters or PrayMoreNovenas source — original composition (modern or Brazilian devotion).

Note: St. Faustina is associated with the famous Divine Mercy Novena (the nine intentions Jesus dictated for the days before Divine Mercy Sunday), but that is a novena *to the Divine Mercy*, not a novena *to St. Faustina herself*. Ember's piece is a distinct devotion to the saint, built around her life and spirituality (hidden holiness, total trust, the Diary, mercy in deed/word/prayer, the Eucharist, the three o'clock hour), and is an original composition.

## Fidelity note
With no FishEaters or PrayMoreNovenas counterpart, there is nothing to measure verbal fidelity against. The content is doctrinally sound and faithful to St. Faustina's biography and spirituality as recorded in her Diary — "Jesus, I trust in You," the apostolate of mercy, the Image, the Hour of Mercy, her hidden convent life and death at thirty-three. Themes deliberately echo motifs of the canonical Divine Mercy devotion without copying its fixed daily intentions. Treat as an original, sourced-by-spirituality composition rather than a transcription.
