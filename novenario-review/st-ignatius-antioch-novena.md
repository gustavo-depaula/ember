# St. Ignatius of Antioch — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O God, who crowned the courage of St. Ignatius of Antioch with the palm of martyrdom, grant that we who honor his witness may share his ardent love for Your Son. Through his intercession, kindle in us the same longing to belong wholly to Christ. Amen.

**Day 1 — The wheat of God:** On the road to Rome, Ignatius wrote that he was God's wheat, to be ground by the teeth of beasts into the pure bread of Christ. He shows us that suffering offered in love is never wasted, but transformed into something that nourishes the whole Body.  _(Intention: For the grace to offer our sufferings in union with Christ, that they may bear fruit for souls.)_  (every day)

**Day 2 — Longing for union with Christ:** Ignatius wrote, 'My love has been crucified, and there is no fire in me desiring earthly things.' His whole heart strained toward Jesus, so that even life itself seemed small beside the joy of belonging to Him.  _(Intention: For the grace to desire Christ above every earthly comfort.)_  (every day)

**Day 3 — A bishop's faithful care:** As bishop of Antioch for nearly forty years, Ignatius shepherded his flock through persecution with vigilance and tenderness. Even in chains, his thoughts turned to the churches he loved, urging their pastors to guard the faith and feed the weak.  _(Intention: For our bishops and priests, that they may shepherd God's people with courage and love.)_  (every day)

**Day 4 — The Eucharist, medicine of immortality:** Ignatius called the Eucharist 'the medicine of immortality, the antidote against death,' and the flesh of Jesus Christ. He bears witness that from the very beginning the Church adored the Lord truly present in the breaking of the bread.  _(Intention: For a deeper reverence and love for Jesus truly present in the Holy Eucharist.)_  (every day)

**Day 5 — The unity of the Church:** Again and again Ignatius pleaded for the unity of the faithful around their bishop, 'as the strings to a harp.' He saw division as the great wound of the Body and prayed that all might be one in Christ.  _(Intention: For the unity of all Christians and the healing of every division in the Church.)_  (every day)

**Day 6 — Imitating the Master in suffering:** Ignatius asked the Romans, 'Allow me to be an imitator of the passion of my God.' He understood that the disciple is not above his Master, and that the cross is the truest school of love.  _(Intention: For the grace to carry our daily crosses in imitation of the suffering Christ.)_  (every day)

**Day 7 — Boldness in confessing the faith:** Tradition holds that Ignatius confessed Christ before the emperor Trajan without flinching, choosing chains over compromise. He reminds us that the name of Jesus is worth more than the favor of any earthly power.  _(Intention: For the courage to confess our faith openly, whatever it may cost.)_  (every day)

**Day 8 — Joy on the way to God:** Far from dreading his end, Ignatius wrote that only when he reached the beasts would he truly begin to be a disciple. His joy in the face of death reveals a soul already living in the presence of the Lord.  _(Intention: For the gift of supernatural joy that no trial can extinguish.)_  (every day)

**Day 9 — To live in Christ alone:** Ignatius declared that he sought the One who died for him and rose again, desiring nothing of this passing world. His final witness teaches us that Christ alone is the treasure worth every loss.  _(Intention: For the grace to make Christ the center and treasure of our whole life.)_  (every day)

**Closing prayer:** Glorious St. Ignatius, you ran to martyrdom as to a wedding feast, that you might be united forever with the Lord you loved. Obtain for us hearts undivided, a love stronger than fear, and a Church bound together in charity and truth. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
Nine days built on one repeated core prayer with a single changing petition line. Each day: "In the name of the Father..." → "Blessed holy servant of God, St. Ignatius of Antioch, we pray today for protection of the faithful throughout the world." (acknowledging his martyrdom and asking for spiritual growth) → a day-specific "Pray for us, that..." petition → "Glory be to the Father..." Daily petitions: (1) that persecution not bring hopelessness; (2) faith strengthened when tested; (3) disease not take away our joy; (4) courage to witness Christ's love; (5) offer suffering in union with Christ; (6) glorify God through trials; (7) hope of everlasting life; (8) hearts filled with tranquility and peace; (9) spiritual protection through faith in Jesus.

## Fidelity note
Both are nine-day novenas to the same martyr, but the resemblance is thematic rather than textual. Ember composes a distinct life-and-letters meditation for each day, anchored in Ignatius's own famous words ("wheat of God," "medicine of immortality," "imitator of the passion of my God," union around the bishop "as strings to a harp") — material the PrayMoreNovenas version does not draw on; PMN instead repeats a single generic core prayer with a rotating one-line petition. Ember's content is markedly richer and more historically grounded in Ignatius's seven letters, and is plainly an original composition rather than a reworking of the PMN text. The shared notes are the martyr's joy in suffering and the call to belong wholly to Christ.
