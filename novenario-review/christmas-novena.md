# Christmas Novena — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O Lord Jesus, eternal Word of the Father, who for love of us willed to be born of the Virgin Mary, prepare my heart to receive You. As I keep these nine days of watching, kindle in me a holy longing, and come to be born anew in my soul. Amen.

**Day 1 — The Word was made flesh:** The eternal Son, through whom all things were made, chose to become a child for our sake. We marvel that the One whom the heavens cannot contain willed to be held in a mother's arms, that we might be lifted to God.  _(Intention: For wonder at the mystery of God becoming man.)_

**Day 2 — O Wisdom, come and teach us:** The first of the O Antiphons hails Christ as the Wisdom of God, who orders all things with strength and gentleness. We ask Him to come and teach us the way of prudence, that we may walk wisely toward His coming.  _(Intention: For the wisdom to order my life toward Christ.)_

**Day 3 — Christian, recognize your dignity:** St. Leo the Great cried out, 'Christian, recognize your dignity,' for in the Incarnation God shares our nature and raises it to His own. The Christmas we await is not only God's descent to us but our calling upward into the life of God.  _(Intention: For the grace to live worthy of my dignity as a child of God.)_

**Day 4 — O Root of Jesse, sign for the nations:** The prophets foretold a shoot springing from the stump of Jesse, raised as a sign for all peoples. In Christ the long ages of waiting find their fulfillment, and the hope of every nation is gathered into one.  _(Intention: For all peoples to find their hope fulfilled in Christ.)_

**Day 5 — The humility of the manger:** There was no room in the inn, and the King of glory was laid in a feeding trough for animals. The poverty of Bethlehem teaches us that God comes not to the proud and crowded heart but to the lowly and the open.  _(Intention: For humility to make room for Christ within me.)_

**Day 6 — O Dayspring, light to those in darkness:** The Antiphon hails Christ as the Dayspring, the radiant dawn who comes to shine on those who sit in darkness and the shadow of death. We who wait in the long December night long for His light to break over us.  _(Intention: For Christ's light to shine on all who sit in darkness.)_

**Day 7 — Mary's expectant faith:** Our Lady carried the Christ within her and pondered in her heart the promise drawing near. She is the model of every soul that waits in faith, and she teaches us to receive Jesus as she did, with a quiet and trusting yes.  _(Intention: For Mary's faith as I await the birth of her Son.)_

**Day 8 — O Emmanuel, God with us:** The last great Antiphon names Christ Emmanuel, God-with-us, our King and Lawgiver, the longing of all peoples. Tomorrow the promise is kept; tonight we cry from the heart for the One who comes to dwell among us.  _(Intention: For the grace to welcome God who comes to dwell with us.)_

**Day 9 — This holy night:** On this last night the waiting is over, and heaven bends low over the stable of Bethlehem. With the shepherds and angels we kneel before the newborn King and offer Him the only gift He desires: our hearts made ready to love.  _(Intention: For a heart made ready to welcome the newborn Savior.)_

**Closing prayer:** Come, Lord Jesus, and do not delay; let the night of waiting give way to the dawn of Your coming. Make my heart a poor and lowly manger, swept clean and ready, that when You are born at Bethlehem You may find a home in me. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
FishEaters lists two Christmas novenas — "Christmas Novena I (St. Andrew Novena)" (anchor #christmas1) and "Christmas Novena II" (#christmas2) — in its index, but the section text could not be retrieved: the fetched page consistently truncates partway through an earlier novena (St. Sebastian) before reaching these, so the actual prayer text was unavailable for comparison. (The #christmas1 anchor on FishEaters is the St. Andrew Christmas Novena, a single repeated prayer beginning "Hail and blessed be the hour and moment…," prayed fifteen times daily from the feast of St. Andrew, Nov 30, to Christmas — a different devotion in form from Ember's nine-day meditation cycle.)

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
Each day opens "In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen." followed by a prayer to the Word of God ("O Lord, Word of God, You, whose glory is complete, came to us in perfect humility as a child in the womb…"). Each of the nine days makes a distinct request of the infant Jesus: Day 1 "fill us with Joy"; Day 2 "grant us great humility"; Day 3 "give us the gift of Faith"; Day 4 "bring us the Hope that saves"; Day 5 "inspire in us Your selfless love"; Day 6 "give us Your peace"; Day 7 "move us to forgive others as you forgive us"; Day 8 "inspire us to be repulsed by sin"; Day 9 "bring us to life eternal with You." Each day closes with the Glory Be, "May Your Holy Will be done in my life and with these intentions," and space for personal intentions.

## Fidelity note
All three center on the same devotion — the nine days before Christmas, longing for the Word made flesh — and Ember opens, as PMN does, by addressing Jesus as the eternal Word who came in humility. But the daily frameworks differ: PMN structures each day around a virtue requested of the Christ-child (joy, humility, faith, hope, love, peace, forgiveness, sorrow for sin, eternal life), while Ember builds its arc around the seven O Antiphons (Wisdom, Root of Jesse, Dayspring, Emmanuel) interwoven with St. Leo the Great's "recognize your dignity," the humility of the manger, and Mary's expectant faith — a more explicitly liturgical/patristic structure. The FishEaters #christmas1 is the distinct St. Andrew repeated-prayer form and could not be verified directly. Ember's wording is original throughout; faithful to the season's spirit but an independent, liturgically grounded composition rather than a port of either source.
