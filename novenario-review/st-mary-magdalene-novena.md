# Novena to St. Mary Magdalene — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US

**Opening prayer:** St. Mary Magdalene, faithful disciple who loved the Lord with an ardent and undivided heart, you sought Him in tears and found Him risen. As I begin this novena, kindle in me your love for Jesus and your longing to be near Him. Teach me to seek the Lord above all things. Amen.

**Day 1 — Healed and set free:** The Gospel tells us that Jesus freed Mary Magdalene from seven demons, and from that hour she followed Him. She teaches us that the soul restored by Christ responds with grateful and faithful love. _(Intention: For freedom from whatever binds my heart away from God.)_

**Day 2 — Following the Lord:** Mary Magdalene was among the women who followed Jesus and helped provide for Him out of their own means. She teaches us that discipleship is shown in faithful accompaniment and humble service. _(Intention: For the grace to follow and serve Christ faithfully each day.)_

**Day 3 — A heart that loves much:** The Lord praised the woman who loved much because much had been forgiven her, and the Church reads this love in Mary Magdalene's devotion. She teaches us that grateful love is the fruit of mercy received. _(Intention: That gratitude for God's mercy may set my heart on fire with love.)_

**Day 4 — Sitting at the Lord's feet:** Tradition has long contemplated Mary at the feet of Jesus, listening to His word as the one thing necessary. She teaches us to choose the better part and to rest in His presence. _(Intention: For the grace to sit at Christ's feet and listen to His word.)_

**Day 5 — Faithful at the Cross:** When most of the disciples had fled, Mary Magdalene stood by the Cross of Jesus to the very end. She teaches us to remain near the Lord in His suffering and not to abandon Him in our own dark hours. _(Intention: For fidelity to Christ in times of suffering and trial.)_

**Day 6 — Seeking Him in tears:** At the empty tomb Mary wept, saying, 'They have taken away my Lord, and I do not know where they have laid Him.' She teaches us that holy longing keeps seeking even when consolation seems gone. _(Intention: That I may persevere in seeking God even in desolation.)_

**Day 7 — He calls me by name:** The risen Jesus said but one word, 'Mary,' and she knew Him at once, answering 'Rabboni,' Teacher. She teaches us that the Lord knows each of us personally and calls us by our own name. _(Intention: For the grace to recognize the Lord's voice speaking to my heart.)_

**Day 8 — Apostle to the Apostles:** Jesus sent Mary Magdalene to tell the apostles, 'I have seen the Lord,' and so she became the first herald of the Resurrection. She teaches us that those who encounter Christ are sent to proclaim Him. _(Intention: For the courage to bear witness to the risen Christ.)_

**Day 9 — Do not cling, but go and announce:** The Lord told her not to cling to Him but to go to His brethren, for love must become mission. Mary Magdalene teaches us that true devotion overflows from the tomb into the joyful proclamation of the Gospel. _(Intention: That my love for Christ may overflow into joyful witness to others.)_

(Each day the user also prays one Our Father, one Hail Mary, and one Glory Be.)

**Closing prayer:** St. Mary Magdalene, first witness of the Resurrection and herald of the risen Christ, obtain for me a heart that loves much and clings to the Lord. May I, like you, seek Him faithfully in this life and rejoice to hear Him call my name. Lead me to the joy of Easter that never ends. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
FishEaters has a single prayer prayed once a day for nine days (13–21 July, eve of the feast), followed by one Our Father, Hail Mary, and Glory Be:

"O Most merciful Jesus, I give Thee thanks for that work of piety which the blessed Mary Magdalen wrought on Thee when she washed Thy feet with her tears, and wiped them with the hair of her head, and kissed them and anointed them with fragrant ointment; whereby she obtained from Thee such signal grace that Thou didst pour into her heart and soul so great love of Thee that she could love nothing apart from Thee: beseeching Thee that by her merits and intercessions Thou wouldst vouchsafe to grant me tears of true repentance, and pour into my heart Thy divine love. Amen."

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PMN's St. Mary Magdalene novena runs nine days in a fixed format: Sign of the Cross; a thanksgiving prayer to God for Mary Magdalene as an example; a direct address to her on a day-specific aspect of her life; the petition "Please bring my petitions before [Christ/God]"; a meditation; personal intercessory requests; space for intentions ("Please also pray for [mention your intentions here]"); and the close "St. Mary Magdalene, pray for us!" with the Sign of the Cross. Day themes: (1) repentance, (2) devotion / leaving the old life, (3) embracing God's mercy, (4) witnessing to others, (5) friendship with Jesus, (6) faith and devotion, (7) courageous proclamation, (8) faithful service in solitude, (9) steadfast service throughout life.

## Fidelity note
Ember does not reuse FishEaters' single traditional prayer (which centers on the washing/anointing of Christ's feet and the gift of tears) — in fact Ember consciously builds on the modern Gospel portrait (Luke 8's seven demons, John 20's "Mary"/"Rabboni" and "do not cling"), and treats the foot-washing only obliquely via "loved much." Against PMN it is structurally similar (nine meditation-plus-intention days framed by the Sign of the Cross) and overlaps in themes (mercy/forgiveness, witness, faithful service), but Ember's day-by-day arc is more tightly chronological and Scripture-anchored (healing → discipleship → love → Bethany → Cross → tomb → recognition → mission → "go and announce"). The wording throughout is Ember's own. Faithful to the saint and to the genre; an original composition rather than a copy of either source.
