# Novena to SS. Anne and Joachim — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US

**Opening prayer:** Glorious Saints Anne and Joachim, blessed parents of the Mother of God, you raised in your home the one who would bear the Savior of the world. Look kindly upon my family and obtain for us the grace of a household rooted in faith, patience, and love. Amen.

**Day 1 — Faithful waiting:** Anne and Joachim grew old without the child their hearts desired, yet they never ceased to trust in God. Their long waiting teaches us that God's gifts ripen in His own time, not ours. _(Intention: For the patience to wait upon God without losing hope in His promises.)_

**Day 2 — Persevering prayer:** Year after year this holy couple lifted their prayer to heaven, refusing to grow bitter or discouraged. They show us that perseverance in prayer is itself a kind of faith. _(Intention: For the grace to pray steadfastly even when no answer seems near.)_

**Day 3 — A holy marriage:** Anne and Joachim loved one another in God and bore their sorrow together as one heart. Their union reminds us that marriage is meant to be a shared road toward holiness. _(Intention: For all married couples, that they may grow holy together in good times and in trial.)_

**Day 4 — The gift of Mary:** When God at last gave them a daughter, He gave them the purest creature ever to live. Anne and Joachim received Mary as a treasure entrusted to their care. _(Intention: For parents awaiting a child, that they may receive new life as a gift from God.)_

**Day 5 — Raising a child for God:** In their home Mary first learned to pray, to serve, and to love the Lord. Anne and Joachim teach us that the first school of holiness is the family hearth. _(Intention: For parents, that they may form their children first of all in the love of God.)_

**Day 6 — The blessing of grandparents:** As grandparents of Jesus, Anne and Joachim stand for all who pass on the faith to a new generation. Their memory honors the quiet wisdom and prayers of the old. _(Intention: For grandparents, that their faith and prayers may bless their families for generations.)_

**Day 7 — Trust in God's providence:** Their barren years gave way to a joy beyond all they had dared to ask. Anne and Joachim assure us that God's providence often works most wondrously in our seasons of poverty. _(Intention: For trust in God's providence when our hopes seem long unfulfilled.)_

**Day 8 — Holiness in ordinary life:** Anne and Joachim became great saints not by extraordinary deeds but by faithfulness in a humble, ordinary life. They remind us that sanctity is woven from the daily duties done in love. _(Intention: For the grace to seek holiness in the ordinary duties of each day.)_

**Day 9 — Intercessors for the family:** From heaven, the grandparents of Jesus watch over every household that calls upon them. They love to present our family needs to their daughter Mary and to her divine Son. _(Intention: For the protection of my whole family under the care of Saints Anne and Joachim.)_

(Each day the user also prays one Our Father, one Hail Mary, and one Glory Be.)

**Closing prayer:** Holy grandparents of Jesus, you who trusted God through long years of waiting, teach us to hope when His answer is slow in coming. Present my petitions to your daughter Mary, that through her they may reach the throne of God. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
FishEaters carries these as two separate single-prayer novenas. The **St. Anne** novena (17–25 July) is one prayer said daily:

"O glorious St. Anne, filled with compassion for those who invoke thee and with love for those who suffer, heavily laden with the weight of my troubles, I cast myself at thy feet and humbly beg of thee to take under thy special protection the present affair which I commend to thee. (Mention your intentions here) Be pleased to commend it to thy daughter, the Blessed Virgin Mary, and lay it before the throne of Jesus, so that He may bring it to a happy outcome. Cease not to intercede for me until my request is granted. Above all, obtain for me the grace of one day beholding my God face to face, and, with thee and Mary and all the Saints, of praising and blessing Him for all eternity. Amen." — followed three times by "Good St. Anne, mother of her who is our life, our sweetness and our hope, pray to her for us and obtain our request," and "Good St. Anne, pray for us. Jesus, Mary, Anne."

The separate **St. Joachim** novena (Aug 7–15) is likewise one repeated prayer: "O Great patriarch St. Joachim, by that singular privilege by which thou wast chosen by Divine Providence to present to the world that Immaculate Queen... we thy devout clients rejoice with thee over this beautiful privilege, and we implore thy special protection for ourselves and our families..." (asking protection from the devil, sin, and worldly seduction, and a firm faith).

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
PMN has a combined Saints Joachim and Anne novena, nine days in one fixed format: opening Sign of the Cross; gratitude to God for the saints as examples; an address to them on a day-specific virtue; "Please bring my petitions before God"; a reflection; a petition for grace mirroring their virtue; space for intentions; and the close "Sts. Joachim and Anne, pray for us!" with the Sign of the Cross. Day themes: (1) faithfulness in marriage, (2) perseverance through the suffering of infertility, (3) hope despite prolonged childlessness, (4) faithful parenting and holy example, (5) deep trust in God's plan, (6) holy family life centered on God, (7) gratitude for answered prayer, (8) family as gift, (9) humility/virtue in obscure lives.

## Fidelity note
Ember combines Anne and Joachim into one novena (like PMN), rather than FishEaters' two separate single-prayer novenas, and uses nine distinct meditation-plus-intention days framed by the Sign of the Cross — structurally a very close match to PMN. The themes track PMN tightly: waiting, persevering prayer, holy marriage, the gift of the child, raising a child for God, providence, holiness in ordinary/obscure life, and family intercession all appear in both, in similar order; Ember adds an explicit "blessing of grandparents" day. Wording is Ember's own (no verbatim borrowing from either source), and the closing — petitions presented "to your daughter Mary" so they reach God — echoes the FishEaters Anne prayer's distinctive "commend it to thy daughter, the Blessed Virgin Mary, and lay it before the throne of Jesus." Faithful and well-grounded; an original composition in the PMN combined-novena format with a nod to the FishEaters Anne text.
