# St. Catherine of Siena — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** St. Catherine of Siena, bride of Christ and lover of His Precious Blood, you set the world on fire with the love that burned in your own heart. Be with me as I begin this novena and obtain for me a like ardor for God and His Church. Through your powerful intercession, grant me the grace I now humbly ask. Amen.

**Day 1 — Day 1: The cell of self-knowledge:** From childhood Catherine built within her soul an interior cell of self-knowledge, where she learned that she was nothing and God was all. There she found God dwelling within her and learned to abide with Him no matter where she went.  _(Intention: For the grace of true self-knowledge that I may rest humbly in God.)_

**Day 2 — Day 2: Mystical espousal to Christ:** Catherine received from the Lord the grace of a mystical marriage, a bond of pure and total love uniting her soul to Christ. She teaches us that the deepest vocation of every Christian is intimate friendship with Jesus.  _(Intention: For the grace of an intimate and faithful friendship with Christ.)_

**Day 3 — Day 3: Devotion to the Precious Blood:** Catherine was consumed with love for the Blood of Christ, often closing her letters with the words, "in the Blood of Christ." She saw in that Blood the price of every soul and the unfailing fountain of mercy for sinners.  _(Intention: For a deeper love of the Precious Blood that has redeemed me.)_

**Day 4 — Day 4: Charity to the poor and sick:** Catherine nursed plague victims that others fled, embracing the most repulsive wounds for love of Christ in the suffering. Her contemplation never closed her in upon herself but poured out in fearless service.  _(Intention: For the grace to serve Christ in the poor and the suffering without fear.)_

**Day 5 — Day 5: Courageous love of the Church:** Catherine loved the Church as the Bride of Christ and grieved over her wounds without ever abandoning her. She pleaded for reform from within, with reverence for the Holy Father even as she begged him to act.  _(Intention: For the renewal of the Church and love for her amid her trials.)_

**Day 6 — Day 6: Counsel to the Pope:** A young laywoman with no power but holiness, Catherine urged Pope Gregory XI to be courageous and return the papacy from Avignon to Rome. She shows that humble fidelity to truth can move even the highest in the Church.  _(Intention: For the Pope and all shepherds, that they may lead with courage and holiness.)_

**Day 7 — Day 7: The Dialogue and divine truth:** In her great work, The Dialogue, Catherine recorded God the Father's words about truth, mercy, and the bridge that is Christ crossing from earth to heaven. As a Doctor of the Church, she teaches that wisdom is born of love.  _(Intention: For the grace to love divine truth and let it transform my life.)_

**Day 8 — Day 8: Peacemaker amid division:** Catherine traveled and wrote to reconcile warring cities and to heal the divisions tearing at Christendom. Her zeal for peace flowed from a heart that longed to see all souls united in the one love of God.  _(Intention: For peace in our families, our communities, and our world.)_

**Day 9 — Day 9: Set the world on fire:** Catherine wrote that if we become who we are meant to be, we will set the whole world ablaze. She died at thirty-three, spent for Christ and His Church, leaving the fire of her love to kindle generations after her.  _(Intention: For the grace to become fully who God created me to be.)_

**Closing prayer:** St. Catherine, who counseled, "Be who God meant you to be and you will set the world on fire," kindle that flame in me. Help me to seek truth, to love the Church, and to spend myself for the souls Christ redeemed with His Blood. By your prayers, may I serve God boldly in my own time. Amen.

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
This source uses a single prayer recited identically on each of the nine days.

**Opening:** In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen.

**Main Prayer:** Heavenly Father, your glory is in your saints. We praise your glory in the life of the admirable St. Catherine of Siena, virgin and doctor of the Church. Her whole life was a noble sacrifice inspired by an ardent love of Jesus, your unblemished Lamb. In troubled times she strenuously upheld the rights of His beloved spouse, The Church. Father, honor her merits and hear her prayers for each of us, and for our whole parish family dedicated to her. Help us to pass unscathed through the corruption of this world, and to remain unshakably faithful to the church in word, deed, and example. Help us always to see in the Vicar of Christ an anchor in the storms of life, and a beacon of light to the harbor of your Love, in this dark night of your times and men's souls. Grant also to each of us our special petition . . . (State your own intentions) We ask this through Jesus, your Son, in the bond of the Holy Spirit. Amen.

**Closing:** St. Catherine of Siena, Pray for us. In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen.

## Fidelity note
This is not a textual reproduction of either published source. FishEaters lists the novena in its table of contents but the text was not present on the fetched page. PrayMoreNovenas uses a single fixed daily prayer with no distinct daily meditations, whereas Ember's version is an original nine-day structure with a unique meditation and intention for each day drawn from Catherine's life (self-knowledge, mystical espousal, the Precious Blood, service, love of the Church, counsel to the Pope, the Dialogue, peacemaking, and "set the world on fire"). Ember's composition is faithful to the saint's authentic biography and spirituality and shares the same Church/papacy themes as the PMN prayer, but it stands as an independent devotional text rather than a copy of these sources.
