# Epiphany — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O Lord Jesus, light of the nations, who revealed Yourself to the Magi by the brightness of a star, draw us also to Your manger. Open our eyes to seek You and our hearts to adore You as our King and our God. Amen.

**Day 1 — Day 1: The light of the nations:** The Epiphany proclaims that Christ came not for one people alone but for the whole world. In the Magi, all the nations of the earth come to kneel before the Savior. _(Intention: For the spread of the Gospel to all peoples.)_

**Day 2 — Day 2: The star that guides:** God placed a star in the heavens to lead the wise men to His Son. He still guides every soul who watches for His signs and follows the light He gives. _(Intention: For guidance in following God's call.)_

**Day 3 — Day 3: The seeking of the wise:** The Magi left their land and crossed deserts to find the newborn King. Their long journey teaches us that true wisdom is restless until it rests in Christ. _(Intention: For perseverance in the search for God.)_

**Day 4 — Day 4: The gift of gold:** The Magi offered gold to honor Christ as King. We too owe Him the homage of our lives, acknowledging His reign over all we possess. _(Intention: That Christ may truly reign as King of our lives.)_

**Day 5 — Day 5: The gift of frankincense:** The frankincense, burned in worship, confessed the Child to be true God. It calls us to a life of adoration, lifting our prayer to heaven like rising incense. _(Intention: For a spirit of true and constant adoration.)_

**Day 6 — Day 6: The gift of myrrh:** The myrrh, used to anoint the dead, foretold that this King would suffer and die for us. From the cradle, the shadow of the Cross already speaks of His redeeming love. _(Intention: For the grace to embrace the Cross with love.)_

**Day 7 — Day 7: They fell down and adored:** Finding the Child with Mary His mother, the Magi fell to their knees in worship. Greatness bows before the lowly manger, teaching us the humility of true faith. _(Intention: For the gift of humble, adoring faith.)_

**Day 8 — Day 8: Returning by another way:** Warned in a dream, the Magi went home by another road. Whoever truly meets Christ cannot return unchanged, but walks a new path of life. _(Intention: For true conversion of heart and life.)_

**Day 9 — Day 9: Light to those in darkness:** Christ the Epiphany is the dawn that scatters every darkness. He desires to shine in our own hearts and through us upon all who have not yet seen His light. _(Intention: That Christ's light may shine through us to others.)_

**Closing prayer:** O Christ, manifested to the gentiles, let Your light shine upon all who walk in darkness. As the Magi returned home by another way, lead us by the path of faith until we behold You in the eternal day. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — FishEaters
FishEaters, "Novena to the Magi" (anchor #magi). A single prayer repeated each of the nine days, prayed in anticipation of the Epiphany so as to end the day before the feast. Each day prays the three-fold "ask and you shall receive" prayer:

"O Jesus, who has said ask and you shall receive, seek and you shall find, knock and it shall be opened to you, through the intercession of Mary, Thy Most Holy Mother, I knock, I seek and I ask that my prayer may be granted. _(Mention your intentions here.)_

O Jesus, who has said all that you ask of the Father in My Name, He will grant you, through the intercession of Mary, Thy Most Holy Mother, I humbly and urgently ask Thy Father in Thy Name, that my prayer be granted. _(Mention your intentions here.)_

O Jesus, who has said 'Heaven and Earth shall pass away, but My word shall not pass,' through the intercession of Mary, Thy Most Holy Mother, I feel confident that my prayer will be granted."

## Fidelity note
The two are essentially unrelated in wording. The FishEaters "Novena to the Magi" is a generic petition formula (the classic "ask and you shall receive" novena prayer through Mary's intercession), with no daily meditations on the Epiphany mystery. Ember instead composes nine original catechetical meditations following the Matthew narrative — the star, the journey, the three gifts (gold/frankincense/myrrh), the adoration, the return by another way — which is the traditional Epiphany devotional pattern, but the text is original, not drawn from FishEaters. Ember matches the feast faithfully and is far richer thematically; it does not reproduce the FishEaters source.
