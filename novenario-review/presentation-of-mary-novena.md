# Novena for the Presentation of Mary — fidelity comparison

## Ember (this app) — en-US
**Opening prayer:** O Mary, presented in the temple as a little child and given wholly to God, draw me into your spirit of total consecration. Teach me to belong to the Lord without reserve, as you did from your earliest years. Amen.

**Day 1 — The promised child:** Joachim and Anne, long childless, received Mary as the fruit of persevering prayer, and in gratitude they gave her back to God. Her whole life began as a gift returned to the Giver.  _(Intention: For parents who consecrate their children to God.)_

**Day 2 — Offered to God:** While still a small child Mary was brought to the temple and placed in the service of the Lord. She teaches us that no soul is too young, and no hour too early, to give itself entirely to God.  _(Intention: For the grace to give myself to God without holding anything back.)_

**Day 3 — The living temple:** Mary entered a temple of stone, yet she herself was the truer temple, conceived without sin and filled with grace. In her the Holy Spirit found a dwelling more glorious than any sanctuary built by hands.  _(Intention: That my soul may become a fitting temple of the Holy Spirit.)_

**Day 4 — Consecration from the first moment:** From the very first instant of her existence Mary was wholly the Lord's, untouched by sin and turned entirely toward Him. Her example invites us to make every moment, from rising to rest, an offering to God.  _(Intention: For the grace to renew my consecration to God each day.)_

**Day 5 — The hidden years:** Tradition pictures Mary in the temple growing in silence, prayer, and humble work, unknown to the world. She shows that holiness is forged not in display but in faithfulness to the hidden duties of each day.  _(Intention: For the grace to love the hidden, ordinary duties of my life.)_

**Day 6 — A pure and undivided heart:** Mary kept her heart wholly for God, undivided by any rival love. Her purity was not mere absence of stain but a single, burning desire for the One she served.  _(Intention: That my heart may be cleansed of every divided affection.)_

**Day 7 — Self-offering in the everyday:** Mary made of her whole life a quiet, continual gift, offering even small acts with great love. We too can lay our work, our trials, and our joys upon the altar as an unbroken offering.  _(Intention: For the grace to offer each task of the day with love.)_

**Day 8 — Awaiting the Lord:** In the temple Mary waited upon God with watchful, expectant love, her whole being a 'yes' before the Annunciation was ever spoken. Her hidden readiness prepared her to receive the Word made flesh.  _(Intention: For a heart ever ready to answer God's call.)_

**Day 9 — Wholly the Lord's:** All that began at Mary's presentation reached its flower in her fiat and her place beneath the Cross. She who gave herself completely as a child gave herself completely to the end, and so became Mother of all the living.  _(Intention: That I may belong to the Lord completely, like Mary, all my days.)_

**Closing prayer:** Holy Virgin, living temple of the Most High, obtain for me the grace to offer myself daily to God as you offered yourself in the temple. Keep my heart pure and undivided, that I too may be a dwelling place worthy of the Holy Spirit. Amen.

_(A pt-BR version also exists in the app.)_

## Source — PrayMoreNovenas
Each of the nine days opens with "In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen." and closes with personal intentions and thanks for Mary's faithfulness. Day-specific content:
- Day 1: Mary's presentation; "help me grow in desire to serve God. Help me to say yes" to His will even when fearful.
- Day 2: St. Anne, who "had just suffered through a period of infertility"; blessings on children worldwide; "Guide their parents to dedicate their children's lives" to God.
- Day 3: For those struggling with infertility; guidance toward "medical help" and openness to "adoption, spiritual parenthood, or some other ministry."
- Day 4: For priests; "Crush the devil's head" and "Defend your holy priests."
- Day 5: Scripture study; help to "better understand the Bible" and be "more dedicated to spending time reading God's word."
- Day 6: "We do not worship you" — clarifies Mary's intercessory role.
- Day 7: Temptation and sin; "help me to resist temptation."
- Day 8: The dying and suffering; "be with all who are ill" and grant "a peaceful death."
- Day 9: Mary's lifelong "yes"; help in trusting God's will and dedicating oneself to Him.

## Fidelity note
Ember and PrayMoreNovenas share the core devotion and a couple of anchoring motifs — Mary as a child given wholly to God, the role of Joachim and Anne, and the lifelong "yes" culminating in the fiat (Ember's Days 1 and 9 echo PMN's Days 1–2 and 9). Beyond that the daily content diverges substantially: PMN builds its days around modern intercessory topics (infertility, priests, Bible study, the dying), while Ember pursues a contemplative arc on consecration, the living temple, the hidden years, and self-offering. The opening and closing prayers are Ember's own composition, not PMN's wording. Faithful to the feast and spirit, but an independent meditation set rather than a port of the PMN text.
